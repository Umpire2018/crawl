Early parliamentary elections were held in [[Bulgaria]] on 2 April 2023 to elect members of the [[National Assembly (Bulgaria)|National Assembly]].<ref name=":1" /> These were initially scheduled to be held before November 2026;<ref>{{Cite web |title=Bulgaria: National Assembly |url=https://data.ipu.org/node/26/elections |access-date=24 January 2023 |website=Inter-Parliamentary Union Parline |language=en |archive-date=18 March 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230318180344/https://data.ipu.org/node/26/elections?chamber_id=13351 |url-status=live }}</ref> however, as no government was approved by the [[48th National Assembly of Bulgaria|48th Parliament]], [[president of Bulgaria|Bulgarian President]] [[Rumen Radev]] announced in January 2023 that he would call a [[snap election]].<ref name=":1" />

The [[GERB—SDS]] placed first with 69 seats, closely followed by [[PP–DB]] which won 64. Tied for third was the far-right [[Revival (Bulgarian political party)|Revival]] and the minority interests party [[Movement for Rights and Freedoms|DPS]], with 37 and 36 seats, respectively. [[There is Such a People|ITN]] also entered the parliament after having fallen out in the 2022 election.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-04-03 |title=Bulgarian elections: Pro-Russia party may 'force' a pro-EU coalition |url=https://www.euractiv.com/section/politics/news/bulgarian-elections-pro-russia-party-may-force-a-pro-eu-coalition-2/ |access-date=2023-04-03 |website=Euractiv |language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=2023-04-03 |title=Ex-PM Borissov's GERB nudges ahead in Bulgarian election, partial results show |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/ex-pm-borissovs-gerb-nudges-ahead-bulgarian-election-partial-results-2023-04-03/ |access-date=2023-04-03}}</ref> 

On 15 May President Rumen Radev granted GERB—SDS a mandate to form a government, and the party nominated Bulgaria's European Commissioner, [[Mariya Gabriel]], for prime minister. GERB—SDS sought to form a government with rival PP–DB to end the political deadlock that had resulted in numerous inconclusive elections. Although initially reluctant, with co-leader and former prime minister [[Kiril Petkov]] pledging to rule out working with GERB—SDS, the PP–DB later agreed to negotiate. However, the talks between the two parties broke down on 27 May after the leak of a video in which the leaders of the PP–DB expressed their intentions to reduce GERB—SDS' influence within the civil service. President Radev subsequently handed a mandate on 29 May to the PP–DB to form a government, which nominated [[Nikolai Denkov]] for prime minister. However, Radev recommended Denkov return the mandate due to the video's release, which sparked protests and criticism from PP–DB, arguing the president’s statement was unconstitutional. GERB—SDS agreed to restart negotiations with PP–DB to form a "government of experts." Denkov announced on 2 June that the two parties had reached an agreement on the composition of a cabinet with certain modifications that removed "inflammatory political figures". Parliament voted to approve the new GERB—SDS–PP–DB government on 6 June with Denkov as prime minister.

==Background==
{{See also|2021–present Bulgarian political crisis|2022 Bulgarian parliamentary election}}
The [[National Assembly (Bulgaria)|National Assembly]] remained fragmented following the [[2022 Bulgarian parliamentary election|2022 election]], the third snap election since 2021, and no party was able to form a governing coalition.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news |title=Bulgaria To Hold Early Election On April 2 |url=https://www.rferl.org/a/bulgaria-fifth-election-government-talks-fail/32237552.html |access-date=24 January 2023 |website=RadioFreeEurope/RadioLiberty |language=en |archive-date=24 January 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230124143512/https://www.rferl.org/a/bulgaria-fifth-election-government-talks-fail/32237552.html |url-status=live }}</ref> [[GERB]] and the [[Movement for Rights and Freedoms]] (DPS) did not have a majority, holding only 103 of the 121 seats needed. [[Bulgarian Rise]] (BV) and [[Revival (Bulgarian political party)|Revival]], which held a combined 39 seats, are considered [[Eurosceptic]] and sympathetic to Russia,<ref>{{Cite news |date=3 October 2022 |title=Former Bulgarian premier faces struggle to build coalition |work=Financial Times |url=https://www.ft.com/content/81bf2a8f-4b8c-4014-a9b5-2d5f6af0b539 |access-date=6 October 2022 |archive-date=6 October 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221006143204/https://www.ft.com/content/81bf2a8f-4b8c-4014-a9b5-2d5f6af0b539 |url-status=live }}</ref> similarly to the [[Bulgarian Socialist Party]] (BSP) with 25 seats. Although generally [[pro-EU]], the remaining parties and alliances with seats opposed [[Boyko Borisov]]'s past government and refused any possibility of a coalition with GERB due to disagreements over corruption.<ref>{{Cite news |date=4 October 2022 |title=Bulgaria's former-PM Borissov seeks path to coalition in fractured parliament |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/bulgarias-former-pm-borissov-seeks-coalition-talks-after-election-win-2022-10-04/ |access-date=6 October 2022 |archive-date=6 October 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221006182613/https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/bulgarias-former-pm-borissov-seeks-coalition-talks-after-election-win-2022-10-04/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Electoral system ==
The 240 members of the National Assembly are elected by [[open list]], [[proportional representation]] from 31 multi-member constituencies ranging in size from 4 to 19 seats. The electoral threshold is 4% for parties, with seats allocated according to the [[largest remainder method]] using a Hare quota.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Republic of Bulgaria Election for Narodno Sabranie (Bulgarian National Assembly) |url=https://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/3595/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210504022432/https://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/3595/ |archive-date=4 May 2021 |access-date=25 February 2022 |website=ElectionGuide}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Electoral system for national legislature – Bulgaria |url=https://www.idea.int/answer/ans1303551645461599 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210707030119/https://www.idea.int/answer/ans1303551645461599 |archive-date=7 July 2021 |access-date=25 February 2022 |website=International IDEA |publisher=International Institute for Democracy and Electoral Assistance}}</ref> Radev dissolved the National Assembly on 3 February to schedule the election for 2 April 2023.<ref name=":0" />

=== Magnitsky sanctions announcement ===
On 10 February 2023, the United States announced a new group of sanctions based on the [[Magnitsky Act]]. The sanctions targeted former finance minister in the [[Second Borisov Government|Second]] and [[Third Borisov Government|Third]] Borisov Governments, [[Vladislav Goranov]], former energy minister in the [[Stanishev Government]], [[Rumen Ovcharov]], the leader of Russophiles for the Revival of the Fatherland, Nikolay Malinov, as well as two former heads of the [[Kozloduy Nuclear Power Plant]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=14 February 2023 |title=Джеймс О'Брайън: Санкците по "Магнитски" посочват къде потъват милиони, вместо да отиват при гражданите |url=https://frognews.bg/novini/sasht-iskat-vidiat-fokus-varhu-sadebna-reforma-nas.html |access-date=13 February 2023 |website=frognews.bg |language=bg |archive-date=15 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230215022222/https://frognews.bg/novini/sasht-iskat-vidiat-fokus-varhu-sadebna-reforma-nas.html |url-status=live }}</ref> The group were accused of corruption, financial mismanagement and increasing Russian influence.<ref>{{Cite web |date=10 February 2023 |title=Първо по bTV: нов список със сакционирани българи по 'Магнитски' |url=https://btvnovinite.bg/bulgaria/nov-spisak-sas-sankcionirani-balgari-po-magnitski.html |access-date=20 February 2023 |website=btvnews.bg |language=bg |archive-date=20 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230220102116/https://btvnovinite.bg/bulgaria/nov-spisak-sas-sankcionirani-balgari-po-magnitski.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

The announcement served as a reminder of endemic corruption, as well as the political class's close ties to Russia.<ref>{{Cite web |date=13 February 2023 |title="Да се сещат няколко магарета едновременно". == Results ==
According to parallel counts of sample polling stations by several polling agencies, the final result was a close race between GERB—SDS and PP–DB, with all projecting GERB—SDS to be narrowly in the lead. All also suggest that V, DPS, and BSPzB would make it into the parliament, and there was conflicting data on whether ITN would win seats as well.<ref>{{Cite web |title="Маркет линкс" и "Алфа Рисърч" дават минимална преднина на ГЕРБ-СДС пред ПП-ДБ |url=https://bnr.bg/horizont/post/101803720 |access-date=2023-04-02 |website=bnr.bg |language=bg}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-04-02 |title=Bulgarian election too close to call after fifth vote in two years |url=https://www.politico.eu/article/bulgaria-election-too-close-to-call-after-fifth-vote-in-two-years-boyko-borissov-kiril-petkov/ |access-date=2023-04-02 |website=POLITICO |language=en}}</ref>

The following table outlines the results by party. The national [[Electoral threshold|threshold]] at 4% is calculated using total votes for parties and [[Independent politician|independent]] candidates only, and not the total of valid votes, which include "[[None of the above]]" votes. As such, ITN wins seats despite seemingly falling below 4%.

== Aftermath ==
=== Allegations of count irregularities ===

According to the Central Electoral Commission, 22% of election protocols were entered incorrectly,<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-04-07 |title=Всеки пети изборен протокол е бил грешно въведен |url=https://www.segabg.com/hot/category-izbori-2023/vseki-peti-izboren-protokol-e-bil-greshno-vuveden |access-date=2023-04-08 |website=Всеки пети изборен протокол е бил грешно въведен - Новини СЕГА |language=en}}</ref> and some members of PP–DB have shown examples of votes being counted incorrectly.<ref>{{Cite tweet |author=Venko Sabrutev |user=venko_sabrutev |title=Another curious case - section 30601065 in Varna... |number=1643862313298980864 |date=2023-04-06 |language=bg |access-date=2023-04-08 |link=https://twitter.com/venko_sabrutev/status/1643862313298980864}}</ref>

=== Government formation ===
As per the [[Constitution of Bulgaria|constitution]], the [[President of Bulgaria|president]] is required to hand a mandate for government formation to the largest parliamentary group, GERB–SDS. If they fail to propose a government within seven days, or if that government is rejected by [[National Assembly (Bulgaria)|the Bulgarian Parliament]], the president is required to hand the second mandate to the second-largest parliamentary group, PP–DB. If the second mandate also fails to produce a government, the president grants a third mandate to a party of their choice. If no government is approved by parliament after all three mandates have been returned, fresh elections will be scheduled.

Andrius Tursa, a political risk consult from [[Teneo]], commented that "a temporary technocratic cabinet or another [sixth] vote remain the most likely outcomes of the election" as the margin of GERB's victory was too narrow for them to rule but large enough that no coalition could govern without them, effectively preserving the years-long political deadlock. On 22 May, GERB–SDS and PP–DB announced that they had reached a surprise power-sharing deal (pending approval by MPs of both entities) for at least 18 months, whereby the premiership would be rotated between the two.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-05-22 |title=ГЕРБ и ПП-ДБ правят правителство, Денков и Габриел ще са премиери за по 9 месеца (видео) |url=https://www.mediapool.bg/gerb-i-pp-db-pravyat-pravitelstvo-denkov-i-gabriel-shte-sa-premieri-za-po-9-mesetsa-video-news347932.html |access-date=2023-05-24 |website=Mediapool.bg |language=bg-BG}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-05-22 |title=ГЕРБ и новата коалиция тръгват заедно към втория мандат със заявка за кабинет с ротационен премиер |url=https://www.dnevnik.bg/politika/2023/05/22/4486212_shte_ima_li_pravitelstvo_gerb_vrushta_mandata_dokato/ |access-date=2023-05-24 |website=Dnevnik |language=bg}}</ref> According to the [[Financial Times]], "under the power-sharing agreement, the PP–DB’s [[Nikolai Denkov]], a former education minister, will become prime minister, with [Mariya] Gabriel serving as his deputy and foreign minister. Gabriel will take over the premiership after nine months as the parties rotate the leadership." The deal was motivated by a desire to end the gridlock that had resulted in multiple inconclusive snap elections over two years, to move closer to fulfilling requirements for membership in the Eurozone, and to implement electronic voting as well as constitutional and comprehensive judicial reform.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Dunai |first=Marton |date=2023-05-23 |title=Bulgaria breaks political deadlock with proposed power-sharing deal |work=Financial Times |url=https://www.ft.com/content/298fb6e6-40bc-4769-b04b-88dbff3971f4 |access-date=2023-05-24}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-05-22 |title=Bulgaria agrees government with rotating PMs to tackle corruption |url=https://www.politico.eu/article/bulgaria-finds-government-agreement-with-rotating-pm/ |access-date=2023-05-24 |website=POLITICO |language=en}}</ref> [[Kiril Petkov]], one of the leaders of PP–DB, nonetheless apologized to those who had voted for him based on a promise of not dealing with GERB.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Petkov on the Agreement with GERB: We didn't Keep our Promise, I'm Sorry. This is the Best we could do |url=https://www.novinite.com/articles/220212/Petkov+on+the+Agreement+with+GERB%3A+We+didn%27t+Keep+our+Promise%2C+I%27m+Sorry.+This+is+the+Best+we+could+do |access-date=2023-05-24 |website=Novinite}}</ref>

On 26 May 2023, Radostin Vasilev, a former [[There is Such a People|ITN]] MP who founded the Civic Movement Strong Bulgaria, released a video snippet seemingly showing Kiril Petkov and Asen Vasilev working towards keeping Borisov out of a court case and agreeing to the appointments of key security services with approval of foreign organizations (particularly the EU and US).<ref>{{Cite web |title=Радостин Василев пусна онзи запис. Петков се разбрал с Урсула за еврозоната, Асен Василев с посолствата за службите (Видео, снимки) |url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14543577|access-date=2023-05-26 |website=24chasa}}</ref> He further announced that he was leaving the PP–DB group and would vote against the Denkov-Gabriel rotational government, urging fellow MPs from PP to vote against it as well.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Radostin Vasilev shows remarks from alleged political haggling over the formation of a cabinet |url=https://bnr.bg/en/post/101828982/radostin-vasilev-shows-remarks-from-alleged-political-haggling-over-the-formation-of-a-cabinet |access-date=2023-05-26 |website=bnr.bg |language=en}}</ref> The release of the tapes has triggered calls by GERB MPs to renegotiate certain elements of the agreements with PP–DB, especially about the composition of Denkov's Cabinet.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Тома Биков: ПП трябва да преосмисли кабинета си|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14551252 |access-date=2023-05-27 |website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Росен Желязков: Мария Габриел да получи втория мандат от президента|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14551169|access-date=2023-05-27 |website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref> On 27 May, Mariya Gabriel, GERB's PM candidate who had led negotiations with PP, announced that government negotiations between GERB and PP were suspended due to a loss of trust because of the contents of the video tape, which included the leaders of PP admitting they wished to lower GERBs influence within the civil service.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Мария Габриел: Замразяваме преговорите. Борисов: По-честно е избори, ако не се поправят (видео)|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14552863|access-date=2023-05-27 |website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref> GERB announced they would be willing to restart government talks with PP, if they pledged to form a government composed of "experts, which both parties could trust".<ref>{{Cite web |title=Деница Сачева: Искаме експертен кабинет и повече смиреност от ПП)|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14556690|access-date=2023-05-28|website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref> On 27 May, Nikolay Denkov (the PM candidate for PP) confirmed that despite the suspension of talks, he was still in talks with Mariya Gabriel, and that he hoped to re-start official negotiations when the second mandate was officially submitted.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Акад. Денков обясни за службите, очаквал да има проблеми с Радостин Василев|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14558651|access-date=2023-05-29|website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref>

On 28 May, [[Democrats for a Strong Bulgaria]] announced their full support for Nikolay Denkov as PM, indicating that Democratic Bulgaria was inclined to support the government.<ref>{{Cite web |title=ДСБ: Подкрепяме Николай Денков за премиер|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14561240|access-date=2023-05-29|website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref> 

On 29 May, President Rumen Radev handed the second mandate to PP–DB's Prime Minister candidate, Nikolay Denkov, urging Denkov to return the second mandate unfulfilled due to the loss of trust caused by the leaked tapes.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Румен Радев към Денков: Връчвам мандата, защото съм длъжен, но той е компрометиран (Видео)|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14565442|access-date=2023-05-29|website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref> This statement was criticised by PP–DB as unconstitutional<ref>{{Cite web |title=Денков отговори на президента: Не може да дава съвети на НС дали да състави кабинет|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14566259|website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref> and led to a protest outside the Presidential building, with calls for Radev to be impeached, as well as to curtail the powers of the Presidency.<ref>{{Cite web |title=tuk ne e moskva provezhda se protest sreshtu-prezidenta rumen radev snimki|url=https://btvnovinite.bg/bulgaria/tuk-ne-e-moskva-provezhda-se-protest-sreshtu-prezidenta-rumen-radev-snimki.html|website=btv.bg|language=bg}}</ref> Counter protests in support of President Radev were also held.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Протест в подкрепа на Румен Радев се провежда пред Народното събрание|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14578417|website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref>

On 31 May, GERB and PP–DB announced that they re-opened talks about government formation, with meetings taking place between key PP–DB and GERB leaders.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Размразяването е факт: Денков и Габриел пак подхванаха разговорите|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14579960|access-date=2023-05-29|website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref>

On 2 June, Nikolay Denkov (PP–DB's PM candidate) confirmed that an agreement had been reached about the composition of the cabinet, with some changes taking place to "take out inflammatory political figures and give the government a more expert look". He also confirmed the new composition was approved by all the constituent members of PP–DB.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Денков потвърди списъка с министри, но с ГЕРБ си нямали доверие (Видео)|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14595646|access-date=2023-06-02|website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref> A meeting was also held on that day with the [[Movement for Rights and Freedoms]], where changes to the constitution were discussed.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Двучасов разговор между ПП-ДБ, ГЕРБ-СДС и ДПС, сред тях Кирил Петков и Делян Пеевски)|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14596899|access-date=2023-06-02|website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref> After this meeting, DPS confirmed that they would not "impede" the formation of the Denkov-Gabriel government, and that they would support continued talks about making changes to the constitution.<ref>{{Cite web |title=ДПС потърсени за конституционно мнозинство, обещаха да "не пречат" на кабинета (Видео)|url=https://www.24chasa.bg/bulgaria/article/14597210|access-date=2023-06-02|website=24chasa.bg|language=bg}}</ref>

Denkov's [[Denkov-Gabriel Government|government]] was approved by the parliament on 6 June.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-06-06 |title=Bulgaria's National Assembly elects Denkov government |url=https://sofiaglobe.com/2023/06/06/bulgarias-national-assembly-elects-denkov-government/ |access-date=2023-06-06 |website=The Sofia Globe |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=The New Prime Minister of Bulgaria: Who is Nikolay Denkov? |url=https://www.novinite.com/articles/220415/The+New+Prime+Minister+of+Bulgaria%3A+Who+is+Nikolay+Denkov%3F |access-date=2023-06-06 |website=Novinite}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Bulgaria Finally has a Regular Government (CHRONOLOGY OF EVENTS) |url=https://www.novinite.com/articles/220414/Bulgaria+Finally+has+a+Regular+Government+%28CHRONOLOGY+OF+EVENTS%29 |access-date=2023-06-06 |website=Novinite}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Who's Who in Proposed Government Lineup |url=https://www.bta.bg/en/news/bulgaria/467714-who-s-who-in-proposed-government-lineup |access-date=2023-06-06 |website=Bulgarian News Agency |language=en}}</ref>
