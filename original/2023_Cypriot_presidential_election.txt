Presidential elections were held in [[Cyprus]] on 5 February 2023.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Disy leader to seek party nomination for presidency |url=https://cyprus-mail.com/2021/12/22/disy-leader-to-seek-party-nomination-for-presidency/ |accessdate=26 December 2021 |website=Cyprus Mail |date=22 December 2021 |language=en-GB |archive-date=22 December 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211222105724/https://cyprus-mail.com/2021/12/22/disy-leader-to-seek-party-nomination-for-presidency/ |url-status=live }}</ref> No candidate received a majority of the vote in the first round, so a [[Two-round system|runoff]] was held on 12 February.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Kambas |first=Michele |date=2 February 2023 |title=Cyprus poll draws record 14 candidates, unlikely to produce clear winner |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/cyprus-poll-draws-record-14-candidates-unlikely-produce-clear-winner-2023-02-02/ |access-date=2 February 2023 |archive-date=2 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230202123451/https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/cyprus-poll-draws-record-14-candidates-unlikely-produce-clear-winner-2023-02-02/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Incumbent president [[Nicos Anastasiades]] of the [[Democratic Rally]] (DISY), who won the presidential elections in [[2013 Cypriot presidential election|2013]] and [[2018 Cypriot presidential election|2018]], was ineligible to run due to the two-term limit mandated by the [[Constitution of Cyprus]].<ref>{{cite web |date=6 April 2021 |title=Αναστασιάδης: "Δεν θα επαναδιεκδικήσω Προεδρία-Δεν είμαι βασιλιάς να δίνω το δαχτυλίδι" |url=https://dialogos.com.cy/anastasiadis-den-tha-epanadiekdikiso-proedria-vasilias-na-dino-to-dachtylidi/ |access-date=11 January 2022 |archive-date=19 April 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210419070030/https://dialogos.com.cy/anastasiadis-den-tha-epanadiekdikiso-proedria-vasilias-na-dino-to-dachtylidi/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

In the first round, [[Independent politician|independent]] candidate [[Nikos Christodoulides]], supported by the [[Democratic Party (Cyprus)|Democratic Party]] (DIKO), [[Movement for Social Democracy]] (EDEK), [[Democratic Alignment (Cyprus)|Democratic Alignment]] (DIPA) and [[Solidarity Movement (Cyprus)|Solidarity]], received 32.04% of the vote, coming first.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web |title=Προεδρικές Εκλογές 2023: Επίσημα Αποτελέσματα |url=http://live.elections.moi.gov.cy/ |access-date=6 February 2023 |website=live.elections.moi.gov.cy |archive-date=5 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230205193554/http://live.elections.moi.gov.cy/ |url-status=live }}</ref> [[Independent politician|Independent]] candidate [[Andreas Mavroyiannis]], supported by the left-wing [[Progressive Party of Working People]] (AKEL), came second with 29.59% of the vote. [[Averof Neofytou]], the president of the centre-right [[Democratic Rally]] (DISY), received 26.11% of the votes, finishing third in the first round.<ref name=":32">{{Cite web |date=5 February 2023 |title=Εκλογές στην Κύπρο / Τι έδειχναν οι δημοσκοπήσεις και τι έγινε τελικά |url=https://www.avgi.gr/diethni/438621_ti-edeihnan-oi-dimoskopiseis-kai-ti-egine-telika |access-date=6 February 2023 |website=Αυγή |language=el |archive-date=6 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230206065504/https://www.avgi.gr/diethni/438621_ti-edeihnan-oi-dimoskopiseis-kai-ti-egine-telika |url-status=live }}</ref>

Christodoulides and Mavroyiannis advanced to the second round. The incumbent president Anastasiades then endorsed Christodoulides, while DISY declined to endorse either of the remaining candidates.<ref name=":023">{{Cite web |date=12 February 2023 |title=Ex-minister Christoulides wins Cyprus presidential election |url=https://apnews.com/article/cyprus-government-united-states-8f85f71fe4584c1a1638fb4240dd673a |access-date=12 February 2023 |website=AP NEWS |language=en |archive-date=12 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230212101714/https://apnews.com/article/cyprus-government-united-states-8f85f71fe4584c1a1638fb4240dd673a |url-status=live }}</ref> Christodoulides won the second round with 51.97% of the vote to Mavroyiannis' 48.03%. The margin of victory of less than 4% made this the closest presidential election in Cyprus [[1998 Cypriot presidential election|since 1998]].

==Electoral system==
The [[President of Cyprus]] is elected using the [[two-round system]]; if no candidate receives over 50% of the vote in the first round, a second round is held between the top two candidates.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/4017/|title=Republic of Cyprus: Election for Presidency of Cyprus|website=IFES Election Guide|access-date=15 February 2023|archive-date=15 February 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230215214004/https://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/4017/|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Andreas Mavroyiannis===
[[File:Nikos Dendias with Andreas Mavroyiannis.jpg|thumb|Former Greek Minister for Foreign Affairs Nikos Dendias meets with Andreas Mavroyiannis during his presidential campaign.]]
[[Andreas Mavroyiannis|Andreas Mavoryiannis]] was appointed [[Negotiation|negotiator]] for the [[Greek Cypriots|Greek Cypriot]] side in the talks on the [[Cyprus problem]], by the [[president of Cyprus]] [[Nicos Anastasiades]], on 1 September 2013. Mavroyiannis announced his resignation on 15 April 2022, due to the absence of progress in negotiations for the Cypriot problem. Despite being a close collaborator of the outgoing president as the official negotiator of Greek Cypriots, he became an [[independent candidate]] for the presidential elections, promising a progressive change of the governance of Cyprus.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Staikos |first=Apostolos |date=9 February 2023 |title=Ανδρέας Μαυρογιάννης στο euronews: "Είμαι η υποψηφιότητα της προοδευτικής αλλαγής" |url=https://gr.euronews.com/2023/02/09/andreas-mavrogiannis-sto-euronews-eimai-i-ypopsifiotita-tis-proodeutikis-allagis |access-date=10 February 2023 |website=euronews |language=el |archive-date=10 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230210103204/https://gr.euronews.com/2023/02/09/andreas-mavrogiannis-sto-euronews-eimai-i-ypopsifiotita-tis-proodeutikis-allagis |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Επίσημη εξαγγελία Μαυρογιάννη για Προεδρικές |url=https://www.brief.com.cy/politiki/episimi-exaggelia-mayrogianni-gia-proedrikes |access-date=10 February 2023 |website=www.brief.com.cy |language=en |archive-date=10 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230210103210/https://www.brief.com.cy/politiki/episimi-exaggelia-mayrogianni-gia-proedrikes |url-status=live }}</ref>

Mavroyiannis was supported by the main opposition party, the [[communist party]] [[Progressive Party of Working People|AKEL]]. AKEL decided not to run in the elections with a party candidate, but to support an independent politician. An internal election was carried out between Mavroyiannis and the lawyer Achilleas Demetriades, the president of the Human Rights Committee of the Cyprus Bar Association. Mavroyiannis won the election with 52 votes (53%). Demetriades gained 37 votes (38%) and 9 people preferred not to vote for either candidate (9%).<ref>{{Cite web |title=403 unauthorized |url=https://www.philenews.com/eidiseis/politiki/article/1463335 |access-date=10 February 2023 |website=www.philenews.com |language=en |archive-date=13 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230213035654/https://www.philenews.com/eidiseis/politiki/article/1463335 |url-status=live }}</ref> He was also supported by the centre-left party [[Generation Change]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Καλωσόρισε τη στήριξη της Άννας Θεολόγου ο Μαυρογιάννης |url=https://www.kathimerini.com.cy/gr/politiki/kalosorise-ti-stirixi-tis-annas-theologoy-o-mayrogiannis |access-date=10 February 2023 |website=Kathimerini.com.cy |language=el |archive-date=24 June 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220624101835/https://www.kathimerini.com.cy/gr/politiki/kalosorise-ti-stirixi-tis-annas-theologoy-o-mayrogiannis |url-status=live }}</ref>

The [[Political campaign|presidential campaign]] of the candidate focused on the change in governance, for a green and fair state, with equal opportunities for everyone. His program included the abolition of double taxation on fuel, decreasing [[Value-added tax|VAT]] of electricity from 19% to 9%, implementing compulsory all day schooling, equipment of all houses with solar panels on the basis of a graduated subsidy, which will reach up to 100%, increasing taxation on excess profit of energy companies and 100% return of [[Consumer price index|ATA]], based on the variation in inflation and its extension for all workers, with no exceptions.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Οι πυλώνες Μαυρογιάννη και το όραμά του για τον τόπο-"Θα είμαστε στον β' γύρο" |url=https://www.reporter.com.cy/proedrikes-2023/article/1018818/oi-pylones-mavroganni-kai-to-orama-toy-ga-ton-topo-tha-eimaste-ston-b-gro |access-date=10 February 2023 |website=ΡΕΠΟΡΤΕΡ |archive-date=10 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230210103209/https://www.reporter.com.cy/proedrikes-2023/article/1018818/oi-pylones-mavroganni-kai-to-orama-toy-ga-ton-topo-tha-eimaste-ston-b-gro |url-status=live }}</ref> He also criticised the minimum wage of €940 per month, that was implemented by Nicos Anastasiades, promising to increase it and include everyone, by abolishing all the exceptions, like the exception for house maids. He also criticised the 12% penalty imposed to anyone that decides to retire at the age of 63, instead of 65. He promised to abolish the penalty, allowing everyone to choose to retire earlier, with no punishment.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Εργασία – Ανδρέας Μαυρογιάννης |url=https://mavroyiannis.cy/programma-diakivernisis/ergasia/ |access-date=10 February 2023 |language=en-US |archive-date=10 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230210103202/https://mavroyiannis.cy/programma-diakivernisis/ergasia/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

[[Averof Neofytou]] denied the independency of Mavroyiannis, insisting that he is "totally dependent by [[Progressive Party of Working People|AKEL]]", the main [[opposition party]] that Neofytou and [[Democratic Rally|DISY]] blame for the [[2012–2013 Cypriot financial crisis]]. Mavroyannis denied these claims and insisted that he is an independent candidate, proud to be supported by a significant progressive political force.<ref>{{Cite web |title=403 unauthorized |url=https://www.philenews.com/eidiseis/politiki/article/1634701/me-arketes-entaseis-oloklirothike-to-debate-ton-epta |access-date=13 February 2023 |website=www.philenews.com |date=11 January 2023 |language=en |archive-date=13 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230213113936/https://www.philenews.com/eidiseis/politiki/article/1634701/me-arketes-entaseis-oloklirothike-to-debate-ton-epta |url-status=live }}</ref>

===Nikos Christodoulides===
[[File:Nikos Dendias with Nikos Christodoulides.jpg|thumb|Former Greek Minister for Foreign Affairs Nikos Dendias meets with Nikos Christodoulides during his presidential campaign.]]
[[Nikos Christodoulides]] is an academic and career diplomat, who served as Spokesman of the Government from 2014 to 2018 and [[List of Ministers of Foreign Affairs of Cyprus|Minister of Foreign Affairs]] from 2018 to 2022. Despite being a member of [[Democratic Rally|DISY]] and one of the closest collaborators of the outgoing president [[Nicos Anastasiades]], he entered the presidential elections as an [[independent candidate]], without the support of his party. Christodoulides used to be the absolute favourite to win the elections, with around 50% of the public willing to vote for him in May 2022 and an astonishing 30-point lead from any other candidate in the first round.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Δημοσκόπηση REPORTER: Η πρόθεση ψήφου Α γύρου, τα σενάρια του Β και το φαβορί |url=https://www.reporter.com.cy/proedrikes-2023/article/954023/dimoskopisi-reporter-i-prothesi-psifoy-a-groy-ta-senaria-toy-b-kai-to-fabori |access-date=10 February 2023 |website=ΡΕΠΟΡΤΕΡ |archive-date=25 December 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221225144549/https://www.reporter.com.cy/proedrikes-2023/article/954023/dimoskopisi-reporter-i-prothesi-psifoy-a-groy-ta-senaria-toy-b-kai-to-fabori |url-status=live }}</ref> Although his popularity decreased significantly with time, in the last polls he maintained a healthy 6-point-lead.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Οι "τρεις" στη ζυγαριά των αναποφάσιστων - Averof Neofytou was clear that [[Democratic Rally|DISY]] would not take part in any form of government with Christodoulides, since, if they were to lose the elections, the only responsible action would be to respect people's will and enter a responsible [[political opposition]] .<ref>{{Cite web |title=Αβέρωφ: Αν εκλεγεί ο Νίκος, ο ΔΗΣΥ θα είναι αντιπολίτευση {{!}} Offsite |url=https://www.offsite.com.cy/eidiseis/politiki/aberof-eklegei-o-nikos-o-disy-tha-einai-antipoliteysi |access-date=10 February 2023 |website=www.offsite.com.cy |language=en |archive-date=10 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230210103210/https://www.offsite.com.cy/eidiseis/politiki/aberof-eklegei-o-nikos-o-disy-tha-einai-antipoliteysi |url-status=live }}</ref> He also used to deny the independency of Christodoulides and constantly criticising him for being dependent by parties with completely opposing [[Political ideologies|ideologies]] on vital issues such as the economy and the [[Cyprus problem]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Επιτελείο Αβέρωφ: "Ο αχταρμάς των κομμάτων που στηρίζουν Χριστοδουλίδη, δεν έχει ούτε αρχή, ούτε μέση, ούτε τέλος" |url=https://www.tothemaonline.com/Article/335647/epiteleio-aberwf-o-ahtarmas-twn-kommatwn-poy-sthrizoyn-hristodoylidh-den-ehei-oyte-arhh-oyte-mesh-oyte-telos |access-date=10 February 2023 |website=tothemaonline.com |language=el |archive-date=10 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230210103158/https://www.tothemaonline.com/Article/335647/epiteleio-aberwf-o-ahtarmas-twn-kommatwn-poy-sthrizoyn-hristodoylidh-den-ehei-oyte-arhh-oyte-mesh-oyte-telos |url-status=live }}</ref> Christodoulides did not accept the accusations, saying that his candidacy is completely independent. He proved his independency by insisting that he had not offered or promised anything to any political party to win their support. This was confirmed by the president of [[Democratic Party (Cyprus)|DIKO]], [[Nikolas Papadopoulos]].

Christodoulides's goals include connecting the [[educational system]] with the economy, creating deputy ministries of immigration and of sports, giving financial help to vulnerable groups through modifications of [[Consumer price index|ATA]] and the instalment of financial literacy programs. He also envisioned perfecting the [[Digital economy|digital]] and [[green economy]], achieving a [[tax reform]] and a [[pension reform]], upgrading the network of [[Electricity Authority of Cyprus|EAC]] to increase the use of renewable energy sources and electrical interconnection of Cyprus with Greece, Israel and Egypt. ===Second round endorsements===

However, Neofytou expressed his opposition to Christodoulides, indirectly endorsing Mavroyiannis as his preferred candidate.<ref name=":13">{{Cite web |date=2023-02-09 |title=Κύπρος: Ο β' γύρος των προεδρικών εκλογών "τριχοτόμησε" τον ΔΗΣΥ |url=https://www.protothema.gr/politics/article/1338029/kupros-o-v-guros-ton-proedrikon-eklogon-trihotomise-ton-disu/ |access-date=2024-02-23 |website=ProtoThema |language=el}}</ref> On the other hand, the outgoing president and former president of DISY, Nicos Anastasiades, indirectly endorsed Christodoulides by cautioning his party members against the possibility of an [[Progressive Party of Working People|AKEL]]-backed government.

==Analysis==
In the first round, [[Independent politician|independent]] candidate [[Nikos Christodoulides]], supported by [[Democratic Party (Cyprus)|DIKO]], [[Movement for Social Democracy|EDEK]], [[Democratic Alignment (Cyprus)|DIPA]] and [[Solidarity Movement (Cyprus)|Solidarity]], secured 32.04%, coming first.<ref name=":2"/> [[Independent politician|Independent]] candidate [[Andreas Mavroyiannis]], supported by the left-wing party [[Progressive Party of Working People|AKEL]], outperformed polls to gain 29.59% of the votes. [[Averof Neofytou]], the president of the centre-right [[Democratic Rally]], secured 26.11% of the votes, finishing third in the first round. Christodoulides was thereafter backed by the incumbent president Anastasiades, while DISY declined to endorse any of the remaining candidates.<ref name=":0232">{{Cite web |date=12 February 2023 |title=Ex-minister Christoulides wins Cyprus presidential election |url=https://apnews.com/article/cyprus-government-united-states-8f85f71fe4584c1a1638fb4240dd673a |access-date=12 February 2023 |website=AP NEWS |language=en |archive-date=12 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230212185514/https://apnews.com/article/cyprus-government-united-states-8f85f71fe4584c1a1638fb4240dd673a |url-status=live }}</ref> Christodoulides won the second round with 51.92% of the votes, against Mavroyiannis who received 48.08% of the votes, to become president of Cyprus. Mavroyiannis conceded and sent a congratulatory message to Christodoulides.<ref name=":1232">{{Cite news |last=Kambas |first=Michele |date=12 February 2023 |title=Former Cyprus foreign minister wins presidential election |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/cypriot-diplomats-face-off-cliffhanger-presidential-vote-2023-02-12/ |access-date=12 February 2023 |archive-date=12 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230212123454/https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/cypriot-diplomats-face-off-cliffhanger-presidential-vote-2023-02-12/ |url-status=live }}</ref>
