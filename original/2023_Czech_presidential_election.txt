Presidential elections were held in the [[Czech Republic]] in January 2023, resulting in the election of [[Petr Pavel]].
Incumbent president [[Miloš Zeman]] was not eligible to run due to the [[two-term limit]].

The first round took place on 13 and 14 January. [[Petr Pavel]], former [[chair of the NATO Military Committee]], ran as an independent on a [[pro-Western]], [[pro-European]] platform,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Tait |first1=Robert |title=Pro-western Petr Pavel sweeps to landslide win in race for Czech presidency |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/jan/28/petr-pavel-wins-landslide-victory-in-czech-presidential-elections |access-date=31 January 2023 |work=The Guardian }}</ref> and was one of three candidates backed by the centre-right governing alliance [[Spolu (Czech Republic)|Spolu]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |first=Daniela|last=Lazarová|date=28 January 2023 |title=Retired army general Petr Pavel elected next president of Czechia |url=https://english.radio.cz/retired-army-general-petr-pavel-elected-next-president-czechia-8773546 |access-date=28 January 2023 |website=Radio Prague International}}</ref> He won the first round of the election with 35.40% of the popular vote, ahead of [[Andrej Babiš]], the former [[Czech prime minister]] running as the candidate of [[ANO (political party)|ANO]], who finished second with 34.99%.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web |last=Di Sario |first=Frederica |date=14 January 2023 |title=Pro-Western retired general leads first round in Czech presidential election |url=https://www.politico.eu/article/pro-western-retired-general-leads-first-round-in-czech-presidential-election/ |access-date=28 January 2023 |website=Politico}}</ref> Babiš had expressed opposition to Czech support for Ukraine after the [[2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine|Russian invasion]] and was characterised in the media as using [[Populism|populist]] rhetoric.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Cameron |first1=Rob |title=Petr Pavel: Ex-general beats populist rival in Czech election |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-64438955 |access-date=31 January 2023 |work=BBC News }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Bauerova |first1=Ladka |last2=Rauhala |first2=Emily |title=Retired Gen. Pavel defeats billionaire Babis in Czech election |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2023/01/28/czech-election-results-pavel-babis/ |access-date=31 January 2023 |newspaper=The Washington Post }}</ref> For the runoff, Pavel was backed by most eliminated candidates and by the incumbent prime minister [[Petr Fiala]], while Babiš received an endorsement from the [[Communist Party of Bohemia and Moravia]]<ref>{{Cite news |last=Rambousková |first=Michaela |date=19 January 2023 |title=Volte Babiše, Pavel je peklo, vyzvala KSČM své příznivce |language=cs |work=Seznam Zprávy |url=https://www.seznamzpravy.cz/clanek/volby-prezidentske-volte-babise-pavel-je-peklo-vyzvala-kscm-sve-priznivce-223813 |access-date=1 February 2023}}</ref> and the outgoing president Zeman.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web |date=27 January 2023 |title=Pro-Western war hero Petr Pavel looks set to sweep Czech presidential vote |url=https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20230127-pro-western-former-general-pavel-set-to-sweep-czech-president-vote |access-date=28 January 2023 |website=France 24}}</ref>

The second round was held on 27 and 28 January. Pavel won the runoff against Babiš with 58.33% of the vote to become the [[Czech Republic president]]-elect. He [[Inauguration of Petr Pavel|assumed office]] on 9 March 2023, replacing Zeman.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web |last=Pirodsky |first=Jason |date=28 January 2023 |title=Petr Pavel elected President of the Czech Republic |url=https://www.expats.cz/czech-news/article/petr-pavel-elected-president-of-the-czech-republic |access-date=28 January 2023 |website=Expats}}</ref><ref name="inaugurace">{{cite web |date=28 January 2023 |title=Inaugurace nově zvoleného prezidenta Petra Pavla proběhne 9. března, oznámila předsedkyně Sněmovny |url=https://www.irozhlas.cz/volby/petr-pavel-inaugurace-slib-snemovna-pekarova-adamova-senat-prezident-volby_2301281549_pat |access-date=28 January 2023 |website=iRozhlas |language=cs}}</ref> Babiš conceded defeat and congratulated Pavel.<ref name="Reuters">{{Cite news |last1=Muller |first1=Robert |last2=Lopatka |first2=Jan |date=28 January 2023 |title=Retired Czech general Pavel wins presidential election|work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/czech-retired-general-aims-beat-tycoon-ex-pm-presidential-race-2023-01-27/ |access-date=28 January 2023}}</ref> Voter turnout in the second round was a little above 70%, the highest in a direct Czech presidential election and the highest in any national Czech election since 1998.<ref>{{cite news |title=Prezidentské volby 2023 |language=cs |publisher=Seznam zprávy |url=https://www.seznamzpravy.cz/tag/volebni-ucast-54700 |access-date=28 January 2023}}</ref>

The campaign was widely described in the media as divisive. The outcome of the election was viewed as a sign of support for the [[Western World|West]] in the context of the War in Ukraine,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gosling |first1=Tim |title=Czech Republic secures pro-West direction as ex-NATO general wins |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2023/1/29/czechia-cements-west-direction-as-former-nato-general-wins |access-date=31 January 2023 |work=Al Jazeera }}</ref> and is expected to strengthen Czech ties with the [[European Union]] and the [[United States]].<ref>{{cite news |agency=AFP |title=New Czech president expected to foster EU, Ukraine ties |url=https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20230129-new-czech-president-expected-to-foster-eu-ukraine-ties |access-date=31 January 2023 |work=France 24}}</ref>

==Background==
===Before 2020===
Initial speculation about the 2023 election began after the [[2013 Czech presidential election|first direct presidential election in 2013]]. Political scientist Ladislav Cabada said that he expected the new president to be younger than [[Miloš Zeman]] when he was elected in 2013. [[Jiří Dienstbier Jr.]] was often mentioned as a potential left-wing candidate. Cabada mentioned [[Petr Fiala]] as a possible right-wing candidate.<ref name="Příštího prezidenta zvolí Husákovy děti, předpovídá politolog Cabada">{{cite web |date=14 November 2013 |title=Příštího prezidenta zvolí Husákovy děti, předpovídá politolog Cabada |url=http://www.rozhlas.cz/cesko2023/radiozurnal/_zprava/pristiho-prezidenta-zvoli-husakovy-deti-predpovida-politolog-cabada--1281088 |accessdate=15 May 2017 |website=Rozhlas |language=cs |archive-date=7 September 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170907212524/http://www.rozhlas.cz/cesko2023/radiozurnal/_zprava/pristiho-prezidenta-zvoli-husakovy-deti-predpovida-politolog-cabada--1281088 |url-status=live }}</ref> There was also speculation that [[ANO 2011]] leader [[Andrej Babiš]], who later became [[Prime Minister of the Czech Republic]], might run for president in 2023.<ref>{{cite web |date=14 April 2015 |title=Babiš bude českým prezidentem, předpovídá list |url=http://www.tyden.cz/rubriky/domaci/babis-bude-ceskym-prezidentem-predpovida-list_339573.html |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170812060142/http://www.tyden.cz/rubriky/domaci/babis-bude-ceskym-prezidentem-predpovida-list_339573.html |archive-date=12 August 2017 |accessdate=15 May 2017 |website=TÝDEN |language=cs}}</ref> At the [[2018 Czech presidential election|previous election]] in January 2018, Zeman was elected for his second and final term.<ref>{{cite web |date=27 January 2018 |title=Slavím poslední politické vítězství, řekl Zeman. In summer 2019, the leaders of the opposition [[Civic Democratic Party (Czech Republic)|Civic Democratic Party]], [[Czech Pirate Party|Pirate Party]], [[KDU-ČSL]], [[TOP 09]] and [[Mayors and Independents]] met to discuss a possible common candidate. The most discussed candidate was retired army general [[Petr Pavel]]. Other names included [[Miroslava Němcová]] and [[Věra Jourová]]. ===After 2020===
Pavel launched an initiative called "Stronger Together" in April 2020, aimed at helping those working in the fight against the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the Czech Republic|coronavirus pandemic]], especially in crowdfunding financial support for volunteers helping in hospitals or creating medical tools.<ref>{{cite web |date=6 April 2020 |title=Generál Pavel založil iniciativu, která pomůže lidem v boji s koronavirem |url=https://www.idnes.cz/zpravy/domaci/general-petr-pavel-iniciativa-boj-proti-koronaviru-covid-19.A200406_144300_domaci_chtl |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210321071430/https://www.idnes.cz/zpravy/domaci/general-petr-pavel-iniciativa-boj-proti-koronaviru-covid-19.A200406_144300_domaci_chtl |archive-date=21 March 2021 |access-date=6 April 2020 |website=iDNES |language=cs}}</ref> Political commentators including Petr Holec and Ondřej Leinert suggested that the initiative was a launch of Pavel's presidential bid, noting the similarity with [[Hillary Clinton]]'s slogan during the [[2016 United States presidential election]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Holec |first=Petr |date=6 April 2020 |title=Holec: Všechno má svůj čas. == Candidates ==
To qualify for the ballot, a candidate had to gather 50,000 signatures from citizens or the support of twenty [[Chamber of Deputies of the Parliament of the Czech Republic|deputies]] or ten [[Senate of the Parliament of the Czech Republic|senators]]. The candidate must file their application and signatures 66 days before the election, following which the [[Ministry of the Interior (Czech Republic)|Interior Ministry]] will verify a sample of the signatures.<ref name=signs>{{cite news |url= http://praguemonitor.com/2012/08/02/klaus-signs-direct-presidential-election-bill |title= Klaus signs direct presidential election bill |agency= [[ČTK]] |work= [[Prague Daily Monitor]] |date= 2 August 2012 |access-date= 21 September 2012 |url-status= dead |archive-url= https://archive.today/20130201040841/http://praguemonitor.com/2012/08/02/klaus-signs-direct-presidential-election-bill |archive-date= 1 February 2013 |df= dmy-all }}</ref>

=== Disqualified ===

Some commentators described these claims as the largest online [[disinformation campaign]] in Czech history.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Honzejk |first1=Petr |title=Skrze Babišovu kampaň sledujeme největší dezinformační operaci v historii. Pavel denied that he had sent them and accused Babiš's campaign of organising them.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Khatsenkova |first=Sophia |date=19 January 2023 |title=Police investigate bizarre Czech texts allegedly sent by candidate |url=https://www.euronews.com/2023/01/19/czech-elections-police-investigating-bizarre-texts-allegedly-sent-by-candidate-petr-pavel |access-date=22 January 2023 |website=euronews}}</ref>{{Citation needed|date=September 2023}} Pavel's website was attacked by hackers linked to Russia multiple times.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Vojáček |first1=Lukáš |title=Útoky ruských hackerů na Petra Pavla budou ve druhém kole voleb intenzivnější, varují experti |url=https://www.e15.cz/volby/prezidentske-volby/prezidentske-volby-2023/utoky-ruskych-hackeru-na-petra-pavla-budou-ve-druhem-kole-voleb-intenzivnejsi-varuji-experti-1396177 |access-date=1 February 2023 |work=E15.cz |agency=CZECH NEWS CENTER a.s.}}</ref> Meanwhile, Babiš and his family were targeted by several death threats.<ref>{{Cite web |date=28 January 2023 |title= Former NATO general Petr Pavel wins Czech presidential vote |url=https://www.rte.ie/news/world/2023/0128/1352413-petr-pavel/ |access-date=3 February 2023 |website= RTÉ.ie}}</ref> 

Babiš's statement during a debate questioning Czech assistance to NATO allies triggered strong criticism from governments of several NATO countries, notably [[Poland]] and of the [[Baltic states]],<ref name="assistance"/> and prompted Pavel to pledge that if elected he would visit Poland to reassure it that the Czech Republic would provide military support for its allies if they were attacked.<ref>{{cite news |title=Czech Rep: Pavel rejects Babiš' declaration over assistance for Baltics in event of attack |url=https://tvpworld.com/65812572/czech-rep-pavel-rejects-babi-declaration-over-assistance-for-baltics-in-event-of-attack |access-date=1 February 2023 |agency=TVP |publisher=Telewizja Polska S.A}}</ref>

==Endorsements==
{{main|Endorsements in the 2023 Czech presidential election}}
ANO and SPD were the only parties to nominate their own candidates. ANO nominated its leader Babiš, while SPD nominated Bašta, a member of the Chamber of Deputies.<ref>{{cite web |title=Kandidátem hnutí SPD na prezidenta bude poslanec Bašta |url=https://www.novinky.cz/clanek/domaci-kandidatem-hnuti-spd-na-prezidenta-bude-jaroslav-basta-40408327 |access-date=10 September 2022 |website=novinky.cz |date=10 September 2022 |language=cs |archive-date=10 September 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220910134752/https://www.novinky.cz/clanek/domaci-kandidatem-hnuti-spd-na-prezidenta-bude-jaroslav-basta-40408327 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Hnutí SPD zvolilo Jaroslava Baštu za svého kandidáta na prezidenta |url=https://www.e15.cz/volby/prezidentske-volby/hnuti-spd-zvolilo-jaroslava-bastu-za-sveho-kandidata-na-prezidenta-1393034 |access-date=1 January 2023 |website=E15.cz |language=cs |archive-date=1 January 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230101095504/https://www.e15.cz/volby/prezidentske-volby/hnuti-spd-zvolilo-jaroslava-bastu-za-sveho-kandidata-na-prezidenta-1393034 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Předsednictvo hnutí ANO podpořilo Babišovu kandidaturu. ODS, KDU-ČSL and TOP 09 confirmed in September 2022 that they would not nominate a joint candidate, and decided to endorse three candidates: Fischer, Nerudová and Pavel.<ref name=PetrPavelSpolu>{{cite web |title=Koalice SPOLU podpoří v prezidentských volbách tři kandidáty. The [[Czech Pirate Party]] launched a survey of party members to determine which candidate to endorse.<ref>{{cite web |title="Nehledáme Havla ani Masaryka." Piráti vybírají prezidenta, Babiš dostal jediný hlas |url=https://zpravy.aktualne.cz/domaci/pirati-prezident-volby/r~3725a4cc7d2a11eda3c0ac1f6b220ee8/ |website=Aktuálně.cz |access-date=1 January 2023 |language=cs |date=16 December 2022 |archive-date=1 January 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230101095502/https://zpravy.aktualne.cz/domaci/pirati-prezident-volby/r~3725a4cc7d2a11eda3c0ac1f6b220ee8/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Pavel led the ballot with 37% of votes. Nerudová finished second with 30% while Hilšer was third with 15%. The party decided not to endorse any candidate, but advised its supporters to vote for a candidate who would bring dignity to the institution of the presidency, and unite people and honour the constitution and law instead of dividing society.<ref>{{cite web |title=V pirátské anketě k prezidentské volbě získali největší podporu Petr Pavel, Danuše Nerudová a Marek Hilšer |url=https://www.pirati.cz/jak-pirati-pracuji/v-piratske-ankete-k-prezidentske-volbe-ziskali-nejvetsi-podporu-petr-pavel-danuse-nerudova-a-marek-hilser/ |website=Hlavní web Pirati.cz |access-date=7 January 2023 |language=cs |archive-date=6 January 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230106232047/https://www.pirati.cz/jak-pirati-pracuji/v-piratske-ankete-k-prezidentske-volbe-ziskali-nejvetsi-podporu-petr-pavel-danuse-nerudova-a-marek-hilser/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

The [[Communist Party of Bohemia and Moravia]] nominated Skála, who failed to gather enough signatures and withdrew.<ref>{{cite web |title=Josefa Skálu si KSČM zvolilo za kandidáta na prezidenta |url=https://www.novinky.cz/clanek/volby-prezidenta-josef-skala-kandidat-kscm-do-prezidentskych-voleb-2023-40394894 |website=Novinky.cz |date=25 April 2022 |access-date=1 January 2023 |language=cs |archive-date=1 January 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230101095503/https://www.novinky.cz/clanek/volby-prezidenta-josef-skala-kandidat-kscm-do-prezidentskych-voleb-2023-40394894 |url-status=live }}</ref> Party leader [[Kateřina Konečná]] subsequently stated that the party would only endorse a candidate who asked for support.<ref>{{cite web |title=Konečná: Skála bude v boji o Hrad chybět. The [[Tricolour Citizens' Movement]], [[Alliance of National Forces (Czech Republic)|Alliance of National Forces]] (ANS), [[PES Movement]] and [[Manifest.cz]] endorsed Bašta.<ref>{{cite web |title=Pseudovlastenci vytvořili na podporu Bašty téměř Národní frontu. == Results ==
[[File:2023 Presidential Elections in Czechia.jpg|thumb|Voting in 2023 Czech presidential election, electoral district no. 70, [[Olomouc]]]]
The first round of the Czech presidential election took place on 13 and 14 January. Petr Pavel, former [[chair of the NATO Military Committee]], ran as an independent with the endorsement of the governing alliance SPOLU.<ref name=":1" /> He won the first round with 35.4 per cent of the vote, while former prime minister Babiš running as the candidate of ANO 2011 finished second with 35 per cent.<ref name=":2" /> Pavel was subsequently endorsed by four of the eliminated candidates, while Bašta and Zima did not endorse either candidates. Pavel was also endorsed by the incumbent prime minister Petr Fiala, while Babiš received endorsements from the [[Communist Party of Bohemia and Moravia|Communist Party]], the [[Tricolour Citizens' Movement|Tricolour Movement]], and outgoing president Zeman.<ref name=":3" /> 

The runoff was held on 27 and 28 January. Pavel defeated Babiš with 58 per cent of the vote, and became the president-elect. He assumed office on 9 March, replacing Zeman.<ref name="inaugurace" /><ref name=":4" />

On 29 January, an electoral district in Prague-[[Bohnice]] was reported to have accidentally submitted its reported tallies for Babiš and Pavel the wrong way round, resulting in about 100 votes being erroneously counted in favour of Babiš. However, the legal period during which electoral commissions can still correct the submitted results had already expired when the error was noticed, after which only the Supreme Administrative Court can order the correction of the official results.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Chyba při sčítání voleb. Komise v pražských Bohnicích prohodila hlasy pro Pavla a Babiše |url=https://www.irozhlas.cz/volby/bohnice-chyba-scitani-pavel-babis-8090-prezidentske-volby_2301291844_ako |access-date=29 January 2023 |website=iROZHLAS |date=29 January 2023 |language=cs}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=29 January 2023 |title=V Bohnicích komise při sčítání chybovala, místo Babiše zvítězil Pavel |url=https://www.idnes.cz/volby/volby-mestska-cast-praha-8-vitezove-prvni-kolo-pavel-druhe-ziskal-babis-okrsek-cislo.A230129_122737_volby_vank |access-date=29 January 2023 |website=iDNES.cz |language=cs}}</ref>

==Aftermath==
Babiš conceded defeat in a speech at his headquarters, and congratulated Pavel, expressing hope that Pavel would be "everyone's president".<ref>{{cite news |first1=Miroslava|last1=Sloupová|first2=Terezie|last2=Wasserbauerová|title=Babiš popřál Pavlovi, ať je prezidentem všech. Budu tady pro lidi dál, vzkázal |url=https://www.idnes.cz/volby/andrej-babis-prezidentske-volby-volebni-stab-hnuti-ano.A230127_170953_volby_misl |access-date=28 January 2023 |publisher=iDnes |date=28 January 2023|language=cs}}</ref> Former Minister of Finance [[Alena Schillerová]] called Babiš's result a "historic result for ANO" which the party would try to replicate in the next parliamentary election.<ref name="after">{{cite news |first1=Drahoslav|last1=Zeman|first2=Dominika|last2=Glaserová|title=Jsme svědky konce Andreje Babiše, komentuje Fiala volby. Kampaň označil za odpornou |url=https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/3560881-jsme-svedky-konce-andreje-babise-komentuje-fiala-volby-kampan-oznacil-za-odpornou |access-date=28 January 2023 |publisher=ČT24 |date=28 January 2023 |language=cs}}</ref> Prime Minister [[Petr Fiala]] also sent a congratulatory message,<ref name="Reuters"/> and criticised Babiš's campaign during the election, calling it "the most abhorrent in our new era" and hailed "the end of Babiš's era in our country". Outgoing president [[Miloš Zeman]] expressed surprise at the scale of Pavel's victory, and stated that Babiš "should not be written off prematurely".<ref name="after" /> Pavel was also congratulated by Senate leader [[Miloš Vystrčil]], Chamber of Deputies speaker [[Markéta Pekarová Adamová]], and Deputy Prime Minister [[Marian Jurečka]]. [[Ivan Bartoš]], the leader of the [[Czech Pirate Party]], called upon Babiš to apologise for what he described as his "dishonest" campaign.<ref name="after" /> On 3 February 2023 [[Karel Janeček]] submitted a complaint to the Supreme Administrative Court over the presidential election process.<ref>{{cite news|title=Janeček podal stížnost k NSS kvůli průběhu voleb|url=https://denikn.cz/minuta/1072957/|access-date=3 February 2023 |publisher=Deník N|website=denikn.cz|date=3 February 2023 |language=cs}}</ref>
