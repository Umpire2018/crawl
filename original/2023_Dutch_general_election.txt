The result was seen internationally as a victory for [[Trumpism]], Wilders being an ally of former US President [[Donald Trump]] and being described as promoting [[right-wing populism]].<ref>{{cite web |date=2023-11-22 |title=Dutch election winner Geert Wilders is an anti-Islam firebrand known as the Dutch Donald Trump |url=https://apnews.com/article/wilders-netherlands-islam-trump-8efc30fa18dd9c4a0da21b25eca9e01d |access-date=2024-09-17 |publisher=AP |language=en-GB}}</ref>

After the election, [[2023–2024 Dutch cabinet formation|a cabinet formation]] began to determine which parties would form the next government. Subsequently on 16 May 2024, a coalition agreement was settled upon by PVV, VVD, NSC and BBB.<ref>{{cite web |title=Dutch populist Wilders reaches government deal after six months |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2qvqwq0gxlo |access-date=2024-05-17 |publisher=BBC News |language=en-GB}}</ref> Immigration policy was heavily prioritised on the incoming government's agenda,<ref>{{cite news |date=16 May 2024 |title=New Dutch Government to Crack Down on Migration, Green Measures |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-05-16/new-dutch-government-to-crack-down-on-migration-green-measures |publisher=[[Bloomberg News|Bloomberg]]}}</ref> as coalition representatives stated they would embrace the "strictest" asylum policy.<ref>{{cite web |title=Netherlands: New coalition pitches 'strictest' asylum policy – DW – 05/16/2024 |url=https://www.dw.com/en/netherlands-new-coalition-pitches-strictest-asylum-policy/a-69092023 |access-date=2024-05-17 |website=dw.com}}</ref> Numerous reforms were also expected across areas such as welfare and health, as emphasised by NSC leader [[Pieter Omtzigt]] during the election campaign, similar to addressing issues having surfaced in the aftermath of the [[Dutch childcare benefits scandal]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Sanou |first=Hanneke |date=2024-05-14 |title=Childcare scandal compensation may cost €14 billion: NOS |url=https://www.dutchnews.nl/2024/05/childcare-scandal-compensation-may-cost-e14-billion-nos/ |access-date=2024-05-17 |website=DutchNews.nl |language=en-GB}}</ref> Several observers have described the new government as the most right-wing in recent history.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-05-16 |title=Netherlands set for most right-wing government in years |url=https://www.yahoo.com/news/netherlands-set-most-wing-government-004948423.html |access-date=2024-05-17 |website=Yahoo News |language=en-US}}</ref>

Following further negotiation amongst parties, [[Dick Schoof]], an intelligence director, was then nominated for Prime Minister.<ref>{{cite web |date=2024-07-22 |title=Dutch ex-spy chief Dick Schoof named as PM-candidate |url=https://www.yahoo.com/news/netherlands-set-most-wing-government-004948423.html |access-date=2024-07-22 |publisher=BBC News |language=en-GB}}</ref> Shortly afterwards, all participating parties agreed to the nomination.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-07-22 |title=Former spy chief Dick Schoof proposed as Netherlands PM |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/5/28/former-spy-chief-dick-schoof-proposed-as-dutch-pm |access-date=2024-07-22 |website=Al Jazeera |language=en-GB}}</ref> He then became Prime Minister in a [[Schoof cabinet|new cabinet formation]] over a month later on 2 July after being formally appointed by [[Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands|the King]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-07-22 |title=Schoof government sworn in |url=https://www.government.nl/latest/news/2024/07/02/schoof-government-sworn-in |access-date=2024-07-22 |website=www.government.nl |language=en-GB}}</ref>

== Background ==
The [[2021 Dutch general election|2021 general elections]] resulted in the formation of the [[fourth Rutte cabinet]], consisting of a coalition of the [[People's Party for Freedom and Democracy]] (VVD), [[Democrats 66]] (D66), [[Christian Democratic Appeal]] (CDA) and the [[Christian Union (Netherlands)|Christian Union]] (CU). [[Mark Rutte]], leader of VVD, continued in his role as prime minister.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://nos.nl/collectie/13942/artikel/2481946-huwelijk-van-kabinet-rutte-iv-was-gedoemd-te-mislukken |title=Huwelijk van kabinet-Rutte IV was gedoemd te mislukken |website=NOS |language=nl |date=7 July 2023 |access-date=9 July 2023 |archive-date=15 July 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230715163630/http://nos.nl/collectie/13942/artikel/2481946-huwelijk-van-kabinet-rutte-iv-was-gedoemd-te-mislukken |url-status=live}}</ref>

After 2019 the government had the intention to limit the [[human impact on the nitrogen cycle]]. However, its nitrogen bill met resistance from several opposition parties, including the [[Farmer–Citizen Movement]] (BBB), which was founded in 2019 and entered the [[House of Representatives (Netherlands)|House of Representatives]] after winning one seat in the 2021 elections. Its popularity grew quickly, and the BBB emerged as the largest party in the [[2023 Dutch provincial elections|2023 provincial elections]], which also saw heavy losses for the ruling coalition. As the provincial councils indirectly elect the [[Senate (Netherlands)|Senate]],<ref>{{cite news |last=Henley |first=Jon |date=16 March 2023 |title=Rural populist party emerges as big winner in Dutch elections |language=en-GB |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/mar/16/rural-populist-party-farmer-citizen-movement-big-winner-dutch-elections |access-date=23 November 2023 |issn=0261-3077 |archive-date=23 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231123030146/https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/mar/16/rural-populist-party-farmer-citizen-movement-big-winner-dutch-elections |url-status=live}}</ref> it meant that the ruling coalition would face more difficulty passing legislation.<ref>{{cite web |date=24 March 2023 |title=Spreidingswet naar Tweede Kamer, maar nieuwe Eerste Kamer lijkt serieuzer obstakel |url=https://nos.nl/artikel/2468677-spreidingswet-naar-tweede-kamer-maar-nieuwe-eerste-kamer-lijkt-serieuzer-obstakel |access-date=23 November 2023 |website=nos.nl |language=nl |archive-date=5 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230605234642/https://nos.nl/artikel/2468677-spreidingswet-naar-tweede-kamer-maar-nieuwe-eerste-kamer-lijkt-serieuzer-obstakel |url-status=live}}</ref>

The government resigned on 7 July 2023 after the four parties failed to agree on a proposed limitation of [[family reunification]] for refugees fleeing armed conflict.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite web |url=https://nos.nl/collectie/13942/artikel/2482044-hoe-rutte-vriend-en-vijand-verraste-reconstructie-van-de-val-van-rutte-iv |title=Hoe Rutte vriend en vijand verraste: reconstructie van de val van Rutte IV |website=NOS |language=nl |date=8 July 2023 |access-date=9 July 2023 |archive-date=8 July 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230708205819/https://nos.nl/collectie/13942/artikel/2482044-hoe-rutte-vriend-en-vijand-verraste-reconstructie-van-de-val-van-rutte-iv |url-status=live}}</ref> The coalition government led by Mark Rutte collapsed ahead of the anticipated November elections due to irreconcilable disagreements on migration issues.<ref>{{cite web |date=12 November 2023 |title=Dutch election candidates make migration a key campaign issue in the crowded Netherlands |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/politics/dutch-ap-ter-apel-freedom-party-mark-rutte-b2445867.html |access-date=23 November 2023 |website=The Independent |archive-date=23 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231123211531/https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/politics/dutch-ap-ter-apel-freedom-party-mark-rutte-b2445867.html |url-status=live}}</ref> The dispute arose from Rutte's proposal to tighten restrictions on the reunification of asylum seekers' families, aiming to reduce the number of migrants following a previous scandal involving overcrowded migration centers. CDA supported Rutte's proposal, while the opposition of CU and D66 led to a breakdown in negotiations. ==Electoral system==
{{main|Elections in the Netherlands}}
[[File:Tweede Kamerverkiezingen 2023 in Delft (009).jpg|thumb| Ballot of the 2023 Dutch general election ]]
Pursuant to articles C.1, C.2 and C.3 of the electoral law, elections for the [[House of Representatives (Netherlands)|House of Representatives]] take place every four years in March, unless a [[snap election]] is called. The 150 members of the House of Representatives are elected by [[open list]] [[proportional representation]]. The number of seats per list is determined using the [[D'Hondt method]]. A list must receive a number of votes equal to or exceeding the [[Hare quota]] (1 full seat) in order to qualify for seat distribution, meaning there is an [[electoral threshold]] of 0.67%.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.kiesraad.nl/verkiezingen/tweede-kamer/uitslagen/zetelverdeling-over-partijen |title=Zetelverdeling over partijen |website=Kiesraad |date=22 April 2016 |language=nl |access-date=9 July 2023 |archive-date=9 July 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230709100010/https://www.kiesraad.nl/verkiezingen/tweede-kamer/uitslagen/zetelverdeling-over-partijen |url-status=live}}</ref> Voters have the option to cast a preferential vote. The seats won by a list are first allocated to the candidates who, in preferential votes, have received at least 25% of the Hare quota (effectively ¼ of a seat or 0.17% of the total votes), regardless of their placement on the electoral list. If multiple candidates from a list pass this threshold, their ordering is determined based on the number of votes received. Any remaining seats are allocated to candidates according to their position on the electoral list.<ref>{{cite report|title=Nederland, Parlementsverkiezingen, 15 maart 2017: Eindrapport|url=http://www.osce.org/nl/odihr/elections/netherlands/322761?download=true|publisher=OSCE/ODIHR|date=7 June 2017|access-date=28 October 2017|archive-date=24 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201024093005/http://www.osce.org/nl/odihr/elections/netherlands/322761?download=true|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.kiesraad.nl/verkiezingen/tweede-kamer/uitslagen/zetelverdeling-over-kandidaten |title=Zetelverdeling over kandidaten |website=Kiesraad |date=22 April 2016 |language=nl |accessdate=9 July 2023 |archive-date=9 July 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230709100008/https://www.kiesraad.nl/verkiezingen/tweede-kamer/uitslagen/zetelverdeling-over-kandidaten |url-status=live}}</ref>

=== NOS op 3 debates ===
In addition to the conventional debates, a series of debates between two or three party leaders were hosted by news program [[NOS op 3]]. The debates are broadcast through the programme's media outlets, such as radio and YouTube. The debates were broadcast in real-time and were held with a select group of young people present, who, in addition to the viewers on the YouTube live stream, asked the candidates various questions.<ref>{{cite web |last=Michel |first=Thomas |date=12 October 2023 |title=Alles over de verkiezingsuitzendingen van de NOS |trans-title=Everything about the NOS election broadcasts |url=https://over.nos.nl/nieuws/alles-over-de-verkiezingsuitzendingen-van-de-nos/ |access-date=31 October 2023 |website=Over NOS |language=nl |archive-date=2 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231102114432/https://over.nos.nl/nieuws/alles-over-de-verkiezingsuitzendingen-van-de-nos/ |url-status=live}}</ref>

===Political analysis===

|last2=Casert |first2=Raf |date=22 November 2023 |title=Anti-Islam populist Wilders heading for a massive win in Netherlands in a shock for Europe [[landslide victory]] for the [[Party for Freedom]] (PVV).<ref name=":12">{{cite news |last=Moses |first=Claire |date=22 November 2023 |title=Far-Right Icon Predicted to Win Dutch Elections |access-date=26 November 2023 |archive-date=27 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231127150232/https://unherd.com/thepost/are-the-dutch-set-for-a-sharp-turn-to-the-right/ |url-status=live}}</ref>

In addition, the leader of the [[GroenLinks–PvdA]] coalition, [[Frans Timmermans]], was disliked by many right-wingers, and since the [[People's Party for Freedom and Democracy|VVD]] and [[New Social Contract|NSC]] did not directly rule out working with either PVV or GL-PvdA, this led to right-wing voters consolidating around Wilders in order to prevent a Timmermans premiership.<ref>{{cite web |last=Stöckl |first=Benedikt |date=2023-07-21 |title=Timmermans candidacy for Dutch PM angers right-wing |url=https://www.euractiv.com/section/politics/news/timmermans-candidacy-for-dutch-pm-angers-right-wing/ |access-date=2023-11-25 |website=www.euractiv.com |language=en-GB |archive-date=25 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231125213954/https://www.euractiv.com/section/politics/news/timmermans-candidacy-for-dutch-pm-angers-right-wing/ |url-status=live}}</ref> The [[Farmer–Citizen Movement]] saw a decline from their peak in the provincial elections in March, with many of their voters going towards NSC or PVV, for issues around nitrogen emissions were only a minor topic in the campaign.<ref>{{cite news |last=Tullis |first=Paul |date=16 November 2023 |title=Nitrogen wars: the Dutch farmers' revolt that turned a nation upside-down |language=en-GB |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2023/nov/16/nitrogen-wars-the-dutch-farmers-revolt-that-turned-a-nation-upside-down |access-date=2023-11-25 |issn=0261-3077 |archive-date=25 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231125213953/https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2023/nov/16/nitrogen-wars-the-dutch-farmers-revolt-that-turned-a-nation-upside-down |url-status=live}}</ref> 

The Dutch Parliamentary Election Study, a cooperation between 38 political scientists and sociologists, confirmed that migration was the largest concern amongst voters, resulting in the election win of [[Issue ownership|issue owner]] PVV. Furthermore, it concluded a third of the electorate had changed their favored party during the campaign, including across different ideological blocks. The latter was partly caused by low trust in politics. According to the study, many voters – especially those voting for the PVV – favored a strong leader as a result of past political crises.<ref>{{Cite news |url = https://www.nrc.nl/nieuws/2024/07/10/kiezers-verlangen-naar-een-sterke-leider-en-nog-4-lessen-uit-het-nationaal-kiezersonderzoek-a4859338 |url-access = subscription |date = 10 July 2024 |last1 = Valk |first1 = Guus |first2 = Lamyae |last2 = Aharouay |access-date = 11 July 2024 |language = nl |website = [[NRC (newspaper)|NRC]] |title = Meer en meer mensen in Nederland verlangen naar een sterke leider – en nog 4 lessen uit het Nationaal Kiezersonderzoek |trans-title = An increasing part of the Dutch population wish for a strong leader – and 4 more conclustions from the Dutch Parliamentary Election Study }}</ref>

===Reactions===
Following his party's performance, Wilders was praised by Hungary's Prime Minister, [[Viktor Orbán]]. He was also praised by far-right leaders across Europe such as [[Marine Le Pen]], [[Matteo Salvini]], [[André Ventura]], [[Alice Weidel]], [[Tom Van Grieken]], and [[Santiago Abascal]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Sullivan |first=Helen |date=23 November 2023 |title=European far-right leaders celebrate Geert Wilders' party leading Dutch election exit polls |language=en-GB |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/nov/23/european-far-right-leaders-celebrate-geert-wilders-leading-exit-polls-dutch-elections |access-date=23 November 2023 |issn=0261-3077 |archive-date=23 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231123182430/https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/nov/23/european-far-right-leaders-celebrate-geert-wilders-leading-exit-polls-dutch-elections |url-status=live |agency=Reuters}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Hülsemann |first1=Laura |last2=Camut |first2=Nicolas |last3=Ngendakumana |first3=Pierre Emmanuel |title=Euroskeptics applaud shock Wilders win in Dutch election |website=POLITICO | date=23 November 2023 |url=https://www.politico.eu/article/euroskeptic-cheer-shock-geert-wilders-dutch-election/ |access-date=26 November 2023 |archive-date=26 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231126170846/https://www.politico.eu/article/euroskeptic-cheer-shock-geert-wilders-dutch-election/ |url-status=live}}</ref>

All parties of the [[Fourth Rutte cabinet|incumbent coalition government]] lost seats in this election.<ref name="coalition punished"/> Incumbent coalition party Democrats 66 leader [[Rob Jetten]] blamed the senior coalition party VVD and its leader [[Dilan Yeşilgöz]] for the successes of Wilders and the PVV in the election, saying on election night that "Yeşilgöz left the door wide open for Wilders" and blaming her party's campaign strategy for allowing "the politics of intolerance [to be] normalized while it should never be normal". Yeşilgöz denied her party's strategy was to blame for the PVV's success, saying: "It was not the VVD that made the PVV great. ===Government formation===
{{Main|2023–2024 Dutch cabinet formation}}
In February, [[Kim Putters]] (PvdA) was appointed informateur to determine the best structure for a new cabinet. He recommended forming an extra-parliamentary cabinet with PVV, VVD, NSC, and BBB. Under the guidance of informateurs [[Elbert Dijkgraaf]] and [[Richard van Zwol]], these parties reached an agreement on 15 May. Minister selection began on 23 May with [[formateur]] Van Zwol. On 28 May, [[civil servant]] [[Dick Schoof]] ([[Independent politician|independent]]) was nominated as [[Prime Minister of the Netherlands|Prime Minister]] after [[Ronald Plasterk]] withdrew over integrity concerns. The 29 cabinet members were sworn in on 2 July.
