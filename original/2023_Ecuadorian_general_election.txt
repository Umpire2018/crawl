[[Snap election|Snap]] [[general election]]s were held in [[Ecuador]] on 20 August 2023 to elect the [[President of Ecuador]], members of the [[National Assembly (Ecuador)|National Assembly]], and to decide on two [[referendum]]s. The elections were triggered by the invocation of {{Lang|es|[[muerte cruzada]]}}, which [[2023 Ecuadorian political crisis|dissolved]] the [[National Assembly (Ecuador)|National Assembly]] on 17 May 2023.<ref>{{Cite web|date=22 February 2022|title=Lasso quisiera gobernar nuevamente en 2025|url=https://radiolacalle.com/guillermo-lasso-no-descarta-una-reeleccion-en-2025/|access-date=19 March 2022|website=Radio La Calle |language=es|archive-date=22 February 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220222213523/https://radiolacalle.com/guillermo-lasso-no-descarta-una-reeleccion-en-2025/|url-status=live}}</ref> A run-off election was held on 15 October 2023 to elect the President after no candidate secured the required threshold to win in the first round. Incumbent president [[Guillermo Lasso]] was eligible for a second term,<ref>{{Cite web|date=22 February 2022|title=Guillermo Lasso no descarta buscar la reelección en el 2025 – Radio Pichincha|url=https://www.pichinchacomunicaciones.com.ec/presidente-lasso-no-descarta-buscar-la-reeleccion-en-el-2025/|access-date=19 March 2022|website=Pichincha Comunicaciones EP |language=es|archive-date=23 February 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220223043618/https://www.pichinchacomunicaciones.com.ec/presidente-lasso-no-descarta-buscar-la-reeleccion-en-el-2025/|url-status=dead }}</ref> but he announced on 18 May that he would not seek re-election in response to his impeachment.<ref name=not>{{cite news|title=Presidente Guillermo Lasso reveló que no será candidato en estas elecciones |url=https://www.eltelegrafo.com.ec/noticias/actualidad/44/presidente-guillermo-lasso-revelo-que-no-sera-candidato-en-estas-elecciones|access-date=19 May 2023|work=El Telégrafo |date=19 May 2023|archive-date=19 May 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230519142505/https://www.eltelegrafo.com.ec/noticias/actualidad/44/presidente-guillermo-lasso-revelo-que-no-sera-candidato-en-estas-elecciones|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=stand>{{cite news|title=After dissolving legislature, Ecuador's president says he's leaving, too|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2023/05/19/guillermo-lasso-interview-ecuador/|last1=Torres|first1=Arturo|last2=Schmidt|first2=Samantha|date=19 May 2023|newspaper=The Washington Post|access-date=19 May 2023|archive-date=10 August 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230810164116/https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2023/05/19/guillermo-lasso-interview-ecuador/|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 17 May 2023, a day after he presented his defense in an impeachment process against him, President Lasso invoked the {{Lang|es|[[muerte cruzada]]}} mechanism provided for in Article 148 of the [[2008 Constitution of Ecuador|2008 Constitution]] to [[2023 Ecuadorian political crisis|dissolve]] the [[National Assembly (Ecuador)|National Assembly]], bringing forward legislative and presidential elections.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Valencia|first=Alexandra|date=17 May 2023|title=Ecuador president dissolves legislature, bringing elections forward|language=en |work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/ecuador-president-dissolves-legislature-bringing-elections-forward-2023-05-17/|access-date=17 May 2023|archive-date=18 May 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230518214809/https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/ecuador-president-dissolves-legislature-bringing-elections-forward-2023-05-17/|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 18 May, [[Diana Atamaint]], the president of the [[National Electoral Council (Ecuador)|National Electoral Council]] (CNE), announced 20 August 2023 as the tentative date for the legislative election and the first round of the presidential vote. The CNE had until 24 May to finalize the electoral calendar.<ref>{{cite news|title=CNE tiene el 20 de agosto como fecha tentativa para realizar las elecciones generales anticipadas |url=https://www.eluniverso.com/noticias/politica/cne-tiene-el-20-de-agosto-como-fecha-tentativa-para-realizar-las-elecciones-generales-anticipadas-nota/|access-date=18 May 2023|work=El Universo|date=18 May 2023|archive-date=18 May 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230518160951/https://www.eluniverso.com/noticias/politica/cne-tiene-el-20-de-agosto-como-fecha-tentativa-para-realizar-las-elecciones-generales-anticipadas-nota/|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Electoral system==
[[File:Quito Parque del Arbolito Supporters of blank voting 2023.jpg|thumb|Demonstration in Quito supporting blank voting ("Vota no")]]
The president is elected using a modified [[two-round system]], with a candidate required to get over 50% of the vote, or get over 40% of the vote and be 10 points ahead of their nearest rival, to be elected in the first round.<ref name="guide"/> The president is limited to two consecutive four-year terms.<ref name="guide">{{Cite web|url=https://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/2265/|title=IFES Election Guide &#124; Elections: Ecuador Pres 2013|website=www.electionguide.org|access-date=23 June 2023|archive-date=31 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190331044326/http://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/2265/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="PDBA">{{Cite web|url=https://pdba.georgetown.edu/ElecSys/Ecuador/ecuador.html|title=Ecuador: Sistemas Electorales / Electoral Systems|website=pdba.georgetown.edu|access-date=23 June 2023|archive-date=19 April 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230419091911/https://pdba.georgetown.edu/ElecSys/Ecuador/ecuador.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

Members of the [[National Assembly (Ecuador)|National Assembly]] are elected by three methods.<ref name="PDBA"/> Fifteen are elected by [[closed list]] [[proportional representation]] in a nationwide constituency.<ref name="57lkF"/> Six are elected by overseas voters (two each from Canada/United States, Latin America/Caribbean/Africa and Asia/Europe/Oceania).<ref name="57lkF"/> The remaining 116 members are elected from multi-member constituencies by closed list proportional representation, with all seats allocated using the [[Webster/Sainte-Laguë method|Webster method]].<ref name="57lkF">{{Cite web|title=La Asamblea aprobó reformas al Código de la Democracia|url=http://www.elcomercio.com/actualidad/reformas-elecciones-asamblea-codigo-democracia.html|access-date=15 January 2021|website=El Comercio| archive-date = 28 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210128084532/https://www.elcomercio.com/actualidad/reformas-elecciones-asamblea-codigo-democracia.html|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="IJBs7">{{Cite web|title=Electores no podrán votar por candidatos de distintas listas|url=http://www.elcomercio.com/actualidad/cne-electores-vota-candidatos-listas.html|access-date=15 January 2021|website=El Comercio|archive-date=9 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210109054952/https://www.elcomercio.com/actualidad/cne-electores-vota-candidatos-listas.html|url-status=live}}</ref> Members of the National Assembly are limited to two four-year terms, regardless if they are consecutive or not.<ref name="IJBs7"/> There are gender quotas for the party lists, meaning there is [[Zipper system|alternation between men and women]]. There are no quotas for minority representation.<ref name="PDBA"/>

however, the party declined to nominate a candidate and instead endorsed businessman [[Jan Topić]]'s candidacy on 21 May.<ref name=psc/>

On 24 May, former National Assembly member [[Daniel Noboa]], son of businessman and former presidential candidate [[Álvaro Noboa]], announced his candidacy and was endorsed by the Democrática Nacional Alliance.<ref name=Noboa/> The next day, former [[Azuay Province|Azuay Provincial Prefect]] and [[2021 Ecuadorian general election|2021]] presidential candidate [[Yaku Pérez Guartambel]] announced his candidacy and was endorsed by the Claro Que Se Puede Alliance and [[Pachakutik]].<ref name=Yaku/> On 29 May, activist [[Bolívar Armijos Velasco]] announced his campaign along with the endorsement of the [[AMIGO Movement]].<ref name=Bolivar/>

On 6 June, President Lasso's party [[Creating Opportunities]] announced that they would not endorse a presidential candidate.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.teleamazonas.com/creo-no-presentara-candidatos-presidencia-asamblea/|title=CREO no presentará candidatos a la Presidencia ni a la Asamblea|accessdate=2023-06-15|date=2023-06-06|publisher=Teleamazonas|language=es|archive-date=23 June 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230623184643/https://www.teleamazonas.com/creo-no-presentara-candidatos-presidencia-asamblea/|url-status=live}}</ref> On 8 June, [[Quito]]-based businessman and 2021 presidential candidate [[Xavier Hervas]] announced his candidacy and was endorsed by the [[RETO Movement]].<ref name=Xavier/> On 10 June, the [[Rafael Correa]]-funded [[Citizen Revolution Movement]] party sought to nominate former Vice President [[Jorge Glas]] as their nominee; Glas declined the nomination, however.<ref name=Glas/> 2021 presidential runner-up [[Andrés Arauz]] also declined to run and instead sought the vice presidential candidacy.<ref name=":0"/> The party instead nominated former Assemblywoman [[Luisa González]] as its presidential nominee and Arauz as her running mate.<ref name=":0"/>

On 13 July 2023, the National Electoral Council (CNE) officially confirmed the eight candidacies of Armijos, González, Hervas, Noboa Azín, Pérez Guartambel, Sonnenholzner, Topić and Villavicencio.<ref name=CTEcon>{{Cite web|url=https://www.eltelegrafo.com.ec/noticias/actualidad/44/campana-electoral-para-binomios-presidenciales-arrancara-este-13-de-julio|title=Campaña electoral para binomios presidenciales arrancará el jueves, 13 de julio de 2023|accessdate=2023-07-13|publisher=El Telégrafo|date=2023-07-13|language=Spanish|archive-date=13 July 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230713131411/https://www.eltelegrafo.com.ec/noticias/actualidad/44/campana-electoral-para-binomios-presidenciales-arrancara-este-13-de-julio|url-status=live}}</ref> The CNE also said that the official campaign period would run from 13 July until 20 August.<ref name=CTEcon/>

On 13 August, journalist [[Christian Zurita]] was named as Villavicencio's replacement following his assassination.<ref name=Time/>

==Aftermath==
===First round===
On 20 August, [[Luisa González]] advanced to the run-off election after finishing in first place, winning 33% of the vote.<ref name=reutersresults/> Her first place finish was predicted.<ref name=CNN>{{cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/videos/world/2023/08/21/exp-ecuador-election-luisa-gonzalez-daniel-noboa-runoff-rafael-romo-lklv-082101aseg1-cnni-world.cnn|title=Luisa Gonzalez will face Daniel Noboa in Ecuador's presidential runoff election|date=21 August 2023|publisher=CNN|accessdate=21 August 2023|archive-date=21 August 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230821145247/https://www.cnn.com/videos/world/2023/08/21/exp-ecuador-election-luisa-gonzalez-daniel-noboa-runoff-rafael-romo-lklv-082101aseg1-cnni-world.cnn|url-status=live}}</ref> She was noted by commentators as a protege to former President [[Rafael Correa]].<ref name=reutersresults/> Also advancing to the run-off was businessman [[Daniel Noboa]], whose second place finish was seen as a surprise as his polling numbers were low in the days before the election.<ref name=WaPo>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2023/08/21/ecuador-presidential-election-candidate-killed/2e6d37e6-3fe4-11ee-9677-53cc50eb3f77_story.html|title=A presidential runoff is likely in Ecuador between an ally of ex-president and a banana tycoon's son|newspaper=The Washington Post|accessdate=21 August 2023|archive-date=25 August 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230825191703/https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2023/08/21/ecuador-presidential-election-candidate-killed/2e6d37e6-3fe4-11ee-9677-53cc50eb3f77_story.html|url-status=live}}</ref> His debate performance days before the election was seen as a key factor in his second place finish.<ref name=aol>{{cite web|url=https://www.aol.com/news/factbox-ecuadors-gonzalez-noboa-second-035037573.html|title=Factbox-Ecuador's Gonzalez and Noboa go to second round in presidential vote|date=21 August 2023|publisher=AOL|accessdate=21 August 2023|archive-date=21 August 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230821145808/https://www.aol.com/news/factbox-ecuadors-gonzalez-noboa-second-035037573.html|url-status=live}}</ref> Violence, political stability and the assassination of Fernando Villavicencio were heavily noted a key factors for voters.<ref name=WaPo/> If González would have been elected, she would have been the first woman to be elected president.<ref name="Daniel-Luisa" /> In contrast, if Noboa was elected, he would be the youngest president in Ecuador's history, at 35 years old when he took office.<ref name="Daniel-Luisa">{{Cite web|url=https://www.eluniverso.com/noticias/politica/segunda-vuelta-2023-ecuador-tendra-a-la-primera-mujer-presidenta-electa-en-las-urnas-o-al-presidente-mas-joven-de-la-historia-nota/|title=Segunda vuelta 2023: Ecuador tendrá a la primera mujer presidenta electa en las urnas o al presidente más joven de la historia|accessdate=9 October 2023|date=20 August 2023|publisher=El Universo|language=es|archive-date=11 October 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231011220214/https://www.eluniverso.com/noticias/politica/segunda-vuelta-2023-ecuador-tendra-a-la-primera-mujer-presidenta-electa-en-las-urnas-o-al-presidente-mas-joven-de-la-historia-nota/|url-status=live}}</ref>

In the [[National Assembly (Ecuador)|National Assembly]], the [[Citizen Revolution Movement]] won the most votes, coming in the first place at nearly 40% of the popular vote.<ref name=reutersresults/> In second place, Villavicencio's [[Movimiento Construye]] won 20% of the popular vote.<ref name=reutersresults>{{cite web|url=https://www.msn.com/en-gb/news/world/ecuador-candidate-backed-by-correa-will-face-banana-heir-in-second-round/ar-AA1fxUKk|title=Ecuador candidate backed by Correa will face banana heir in second round|publisher=Reuters|accessdate=21 August 2023|archive-date=16 October 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231016054528/https://www.msn.com/en-gb/news/world/ecuador-candidate-backed-by-correa-will-face-banana-heir-in-second-round/ar-AA1fxUKk|url-status=live}}</ref> Also on the ballot were votes on a [[2023 Ecuadorian Yasuní National Park oil exploitation referendum|referendum]] on [[Yasuní National Park#Oil reserves|oil exploitation]] in the [[Yasuní National Park]] and a local one on metallic [[mining]] in the Chocó Andino.<ref name=refone/><ref name=reftwo/> Voters voted to approve both measures, banning both oil exploitation in the Yasuní National Park, and metallic mining in the Chocó Andino.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://elecciones2023.cne.gob.ec/Consultas/choco|title=Consulta Popular Chocó Andino / Pichincha / Quito|publisher=CNE|accessdate=21 August 2023|archive-date=20 August 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230820222121/https://elecciones2023.cne.gob.ec/Consultas/choco|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://elecciones2023.cne.gob.ec/Consultas/yasuni|title=Consulta Popular Yasuní|publisher=CNE|accessdate=21 August 2023|archive-date=20 August 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230820222122/https://elecciones2023.cne.gob.ec/Consultas/yasuni|url-status=live}}</ref>

During the first round of the election, the overseas votes had several complications and a final count could not be established.<ref name=repeat/> Several citizens{{who|date=October 2023}} criticized the delay in counting overseas ballots with the [[Organization of American States]] vowing to assist in addressing the problem.<ref>{{cite Atamaint said that the delay in counting the oversea ballots were attributed to "cyber attacks" and that new security measures were being implemented to deter further attacks.<ref>{{cite Demonstrations were held both outside the CNE headquarters in Quito and in several cities abroad, demanding a repeat vote.<ref>{{cite 25 August, the CNE decided to annul the results of the oversea ballots, ordering a repeat of the legislative votes on the day of the runoff.<ref name=repeat>{{cite ===Second round===
González, the runner-up in the second round of the presidential election, conceded to Noboa, saying "the candidate they [Noboa's voters] chose has won and as Ecuadoreans we also embrace them".<ref>{{cite news|last1=Glatsky|first1=Genevieve|title=Daniel Noboa, Scion of a Banana Empire, Wins Ecuador's Presidency|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2023/10/15/world/americas/ecuador-election-president.html|work=The New York Times|date=15 October 2023 |access-date=15 October 2023|archive-date=15 October 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231015092119/https://www.nytimes.com/2023/10/15/world/americas/ecuador-election-president.html|url-status=live}}</ref> At age 35, Noboa became the youngest person elected to the presidency.<ref name=youngest/> Following his victory, Noboa thanked voters for believing in "a new political project, a young political project, an improbable political project".<ref name=vow>{{cite web|url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/news/world/daniel-noboa-political-neophyte-and-heir-to-banana-empire-elected-president-in-ecuador/ar-AA1ihIOr|title=Daniel Noboa, political neophyte and heir to banana empire, elected president in Ecuador|publisher=MSN|accessdate=16 October 2023|archive-date=17 October 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231017102232/https://www.msn.com/en-us/news/world/daniel-noboa-political-neophyte-and-heir-to-banana-empire-elected-president-in-ecuador/ar-AA1ihIOr|url-status=live}}</ref> He vowed "to return peace to the country, to give education to the youth again, to be able to provide employment to the many people who are looking for it".<ref name=vow/>
