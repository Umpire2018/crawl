Parliamentary elections were held in [[Finland]] on 2 April 2023 to elect members of the [[Parliament of Finland]].

Following the [[2019 Finnish parliamentary election|2019 election]], the [[Social Democratic Party of Finland|Social Democratic Party]] (SDP) formed [[Rinne Cabinet|a governing coalition]] with the [[Centre Party (Finland)|Centre Party]], [[Green League|Greens]], [[Left Alliance (Finland)|Left Alliance]], and the [[Swedish People's Party of Finland|Swedish People's Party]], with [[Antti Rinne]] serving as the [[prime minister of Finland]]. Later that year, Rinne was involved in [[2019 Finnish postal strike|a political scandal]] regarding the [[Posti Group|Finnish postal service]], after which he resigned and was succeeded by [[Sanna Marin]]. [[Marin Cabinet|Marin's government]] focused on issues regarding climate change, while her government also faced the [[COVID-19 pandemic]] and the [[Russian invasion of Ukraine]]. The invasion prompted her to announce that Finland would submit its candidacy to join [[NATO]], which it did in May 2022.

Issues regarding the economy, the budget deficit, climate change, and education were discussed during the campaign period. The election programs or the opposition [[National Coalition Party]] (NCP) and [[Finns Party]] (Finns) called for budget cuts and austerity to balance the budget, while the SDP's election program focused on economic growth through investments in employment and education. Marin supported more education and public health spending, while the NCP called for balancing the budget, reducing spending on unemployment and housing benefits, and constructing more nuclear plants. The Finns campaigned on an [[anti-immigration]] and [[Euroscepticism|anti-European Union]] platform, while the Greens focused on issues regarding mental health and [[universal basic income]]. The Centre Party campaigned on [[Regionalism (politics)|regional politics]].

The National Coalition Party won the election with 20.8% of the national popular vote, winning 48 seats in parliament, the third-highest result in the party's history. The Finns Party came in a close second place with the ruling [[Social Democratic Party of Finland]] under Prime Minister Marin in third place. The National Coalition Party then formed a coalition with the Finns Party, the Swedish People's Party, and the [[Christian Democrats (Finland)|Christian Democrats]], with [[Petteri Orpo]] as prime minister. For the first time in Finland's history, the Centre Party did not win a plurality of votes in any regional voting districts.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Lehtonen |first=Antti |date=2023-04-02 |title=Kaikkien vaalipiirien päivittyvät vaalitulokset |url=https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000009491635.html |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230402202018/https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000009491635.html |archive-date=2 April 2023 |access-date=2023-04-02 |website=Ilta-Sanomat |language=fi}}</ref> Five government parties and four opposition parties finished with parliamentary seats split at 100–100. This was the first time since [[1983 Finnish parliamentary election|1983]] that the top-three finishing parties did not lose a percentage share of votes.

== Electoral system ==
{{See also|Politics of Finland}}

The 200 members of the [[Parliament of Finland]] (''Eduskunta'', ''Riksdagen'') were elected using [[open list]], [[proportional representation]] in 13 multi-member [[Electoral districts of Finland|electoral districts]], with seats allocated according to the [[D'Hondt method]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web |title=Electoral system of Finland |url=http://archive.ipu.org/english/parline/reports/2111_B.htm |access-date=2 April 2023 |website=Inter-Parliamentary Union Parline |archive-date=2 April 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230402163946/http://archive.ipu.org/english/parline/reports/2111_B.htm |url-status=live }}</ref> The number of elected representatives is proportional to the population in the district six months prior to the elections. [[Åland]] has a single-member electoral district and its own party system.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web |title=Electoral Districts |url=http://www.vaalit.fi/en/index/onelections/parliamentaryelections/electoraldistricts.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150603211636/http://www.vaalit.fi/en/index/onelections/parliamentaryelections/electoraldistricts.html |archive-date=3 June 2015 |access-date=2 April 2023 |website=Vaalit}}</ref> Every citizen who is at least 18 years old has the right to vote.<ref>{{Cite web |title=On elections |url=https://vaalit.fi/en/parliamentary-elections |access-date=2 April 2023 |website=Vaalit |archive-date=28 September 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200928095731/https://vaalit.fi/en/parliamentary-elections/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Voters also had the option to vote either during the advance voting period, from March 22 to 28 March 2023,<ref>{{Cite web |date=22 March 2023 |title=Early voting gets off to lively start in Finland |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20023596 |access-date=2 April 2023 |website=Yle News |language=en |archive-date=23 March 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230323173616/https://yle.fi/a/74-20023596 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name=":4">{{Cite web |date=29 March 2023 |title=Advance voting hits record levels in Finnish election |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20024766 |access-date=2 April 2023 |website=Yle News |language=en |archive-date=2 April 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230402052014/https://yle.fi/a/74-20024766 |url-status=live }}</ref> or on election day.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web |title=Voting and Polling stations |url=https://vaalit.fi/en/voting-and-polling-stations1 |access-date=2 April 2023 |website=Vaalit |archive-date=5 December 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221205173818/https://vaalit.fi/en/voting-and-polling-stations1 |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Government formation ==
{{Main article|Orpo Cabinet}}
Before the election, the [[Social Democratic Party of Finland|Social Democratic Party]], [[Green League]], and the [[Left Alliance (Finland)|Left Alliance]] ruled out joining a coalition that involved the [[Finns Party]]. The [[Swedish People's Party of Finland|Swedish People's Party]] similarly stated that they were unlikely to align with the Finns but did not rule the possibility out completely. According to Swedish People's Party leader [[Anna-Maja Henriksson]], her party wanted to see how government talks would go before making a final decision.<ref name="coalition">{{Cite web |date=10 March 2023 |title=Current ruling coalition can't continue after elections, states Saarikko |url=https://www.helsinkitimes.fi/finland/finland-news/politics/23112-current-ruling-coalition-can-t-continue-after-elections-states-saarikko.html |access-date=2 April 2023 |website=Helsinki Times |archive-date=2 April 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230402165239/https://www.helsinkitimes.fi/finland/finland-news/politics/23112-current-ruling-coalition-can-t-continue-after-elections-states-saarikko.html |url-status=live }}</ref> The Left Alliance also stated that they were not ready to enter a coalition that involved the [[National Coalition Party]].<ref name="coalition" /> At the beginning of March 2023, [[Centre Party (Finland)|Centre Party]] leader [[Annika Saarikko]] stated that even though she was not ruling out a coalition with any party, she was not willing to continue remaining in the incumbent coalition government due to policy disputes with other coalition parties, especially the Green League.<ref name="coalition" /><ref name="coalition2">{{Cite web |date=1 April 2023 |title=Finland's far right aims to oust popular PM Sanna Marin in legislative elections |url=https://france24.com/en/europe/20230401-finland-s-far-right-aims-to-oust-popular-pm-sanna-marin |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230402002329/https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20230401-finland-s-far-right-aims-to-oust-popular-pm-sanna-marin |archive-date=2 April 2023 |access-date=2 April 2023 |website=France 24}}</ref> National Coalition Party leader [[Petteri Orpo]] said he was keeping his options open. News agencies predicted that if his party was the largest, he would need to team up with the Finns Party or the Social Democratic Party, along with smaller parties, to get a majority.<ref name="coalition2" />

After the immediate results of the election, the incumbent coalition did not have an absolute majority in parliament. The results showed that Orpo had to face difficult options, as his party differed from the Finns on immigration, climate, and EU membership status. Additionally, forming a coalition with them was said to be difficult because some parties had already ruled out a coalition with the Finns. On the other hand, he vowed to replace the economic policy of the Social Democrats. At that point, it was widely anticipated that the Centre Party, Green League, and Left Alliance would prefer to go into opposition rather than join a new coalition. This left the Swedish People's Party and [[Christian Democrats (Finland)|Christian Democrats]], which held their ground, as likely coalition partners.<ref name=":14">{{Cite web |title=Tulospalvelu {{!}} Eduskuntavaalit 2023 {{!}} yle.fi |url=https://vaalit.yle.fi/ev2023/tulospalvelu/fi/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230402202438/https://vaalit.yle.fi/ev2023/tulospalvelu/fi/ |archive-date=2 April 2023 |access-date=2023-04-02 |website=vaalit.yle.fi |language=fi}}</ref><ref name="coalition3">{{Cite web |date=3 April 2023 |title=Finland shifts to the right but could face weeks of fraught coalition talks |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/apr/03/finland-shifts-to-the-right-coalition-talks-petteri-orpo-ncp-sanna-marin-election |access-date=4 April 2023 |website=The Guardian}}</ref><ref name="coalition4">{{Cite web |date=3 April 2023 |title=Finnish centre-right eyes coalition talks as defeated Marin considers future |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/finland-election-idAFKBN2W00OU |access-date=4 April 2023 |website=Reuters}}</ref><ref name="coalition5">{{Cite web |date=3 April 2023 |title=Finnish election results predict two possible government coalitions |url=https://www.euractiv.com/section/politics/news/finnish-election-results-predict-two-possible-government-coalitions/ |access-date=4 April 2023 |website=EURACTIV}}</ref> The media reported that Orpo was leaning towards talks with the Finns Party first, but also that he was sending out a questionnaire to each of the other parties to assess their positions on various topics.<ref name=":15">{{Cite web |last=Karkkola |first=Minna |date=2 April 2023 |title=Näin Petteri Orpo laittaa hallitusneuvottelut käyntiin – Lähteekö viestejä SDP:lle tai PS:lle? |url=https://www.uusisuomi.fi/uutiset/nain-petteri-orpo-laittaa-hallitusneuvottelut-kayntiin-lahteeko-viesteja-sdplle-tai-pslle/d5fdecd9-d800-41b6-b61e-640322b60a77 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230402211115/https://www.uusisuomi.fi/uutiset/nain-petteri-orpo-laittaa-hallitusneuvottelut-kayntiin-lahteeko-viesteja-sdplle-tai-pslle/d5fdecd9-d800-41b6-b61e-640322b60a77 |archive-date=2 April 2023 |access-date=2023-04-02 |website=Uusi Suomi |language=fi}}</ref><ref name=":16">{{Cite web |title=Nimittäminen ja järjestäytyminen |url=https://valtioneuvosto.fi/tietoa/nimittaminen-ja-jarjestaytyminen |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230325033237/https://valtioneuvosto.fi/tietoa/nimittaminen-ja-jarjestaytyminen |archive-date=25 March 2023 |access-date=2023-04-02 |website=Valtioneuvosto |language=fi-FI}}</ref><ref name="coalition3"/>

On 4 April 2023, the leader of the Social Democratic Party, [[Sanna Marin]], announced that she had resigned as chairwoman of her party, but remained a lawmaker in parliament.<ref name="coalition6">{{Cite web |date=5 April 2023 |title=Finland's Marin steps down as party leader |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/finlands-marin-steps-down-party-leader-2023-04-05/ |access-date=5 April 2023 |website=Reuters}}</ref> Centre Party leader Annika Saarikko confirmed that the Centre Party's place was in the opposition following the party's election losses. Green League party leader [[Maria Ohisalo]] made similar statements, citing monetary cuts to education, insufficiently countering climate change, and nature loss as major concerns. Swedish People's Party leader Anna-Maja Henriksson said she did not rule out joining a coalition with the Finns Party, but stated that she opposed the policies and values that the Finns pursue. Christian Democratic leader [[Sari Essayah]] said she was open to joining a coalition led by the National Coalition if the coalition agreed to implement major structural reforms in the labour markets and social security system.<ref name="coalition7">{{Cite web |date=5 April 2023 |title=Centre and Greens set sights on opposition, adding difficulty to coalition talks |url=https://www.helsinkitimes.fi/finland/finland-news/politics/23307-centre-and-greens-set-sights-on-opposition-adding-difficulty-to-coalition-talks.html |access-date=5 April 2023 |website=Helsinki Times}}</ref> Based on the answers and initial talks with all parties, Orpo announced that he would negotiate forming a government with the Finns Party, Swedish People's Party, and the Christian Democrats.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Finnish far right in talks to join coalition government|url=https://www.politico.eu/article/finland-far-right-talks-coalition-government-petteri-orpo/amp/ |publisher=Politico |date=27 April 2023|access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref>

Petteri Orpo announced on 15 June that the four parties had agreed to establish a four-party [[Orpo Cabinet|coalition government]], which would include the far-right Finns Party.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Finland's conservatives to form coalition with far-right – DW – 06/15/2023 |url=https://www.dw.com/en/finlands-conservatives-to-form-coalition-with-far-right-finns-party/a-65933661 |access-date=2023-06-16 |website=dw.com |language=en}}</ref> The composition of the government, the assignment of nineteen ministerial posts, was announced on 17 June. The National Coalition received eight posts, the Finns Party seven, and the Swedish People's Party three, with the remaining one going to the Christian Democrats.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Finland’s conservative party picks ministers for right-wing coalition government – The Seattle Times |url=https://www.seattletimes.com/business/finlands-conservative-party-picks-ministers-for-right-wing-coalition-government/?amp=1 |access-date=2023-06-18 |website=www.seattletimes.com}}</ref>
