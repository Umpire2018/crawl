General elections were held in [[Guatemala]] on 25 June 2023 to elect the [[President of Guatemala|president]] and [[Vice President of Guatemala|vice president]], all 160 seats in [[Congress of Guatemala|Congress]], all 20 members of the [[Central American Parliament]], and mayors and councils for all  the country's 340 [[Municipalities of Guatemala|municipalities]]. Incumbent president [[Alejandro Giammattei]] was constitutionally prohibited from running for a second four-year term.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Oliva |first=William |date=7 March 2018 |title=Jimmy Morales 2023 |access-date=23 June 2023 |website=[[Prensa Libre (Guatemala)]] |language=es}}</ref> However, as no presidential candidate obtained over 50 percent of the vote in the first round on 25 June 2023, a second round was held between the top two finishers on 20 August 2023: Congressman [[Bernardo Arévalo]] (the son of former president [[Juan José Arévalo]]) of the [[Movimiento Semilla]] and [[Sandra Torres]], a former first lady representing the [[National Unity of Hope]] (UNE) party.<ref name="Abbott 2023 p558">{{cite web | last=Abbott | first=Jeff | title=Guatemala presidential election: Arévalo defeated Torres in the second round with nearly 61 percent of the vote in what was seen as a [[Landslide victory|landslide]].<ref name=":52">{{Cite web |title=Arevalo wins Guatemala presidency ruling [[Vamos (Guatemala)|Vamos]] party won the largest number of seats in Congress.<ref>{{cite The certification of the first round results by the Supreme Electoral Tribunal was delayed after the [[Constitutional Court of Guatemala|Constitutional Court]] granted an injunction to parties who challenged the results. On 12 July, the [[Guatemala Attorney General|Public Prosecution Service]] (MP) announced that [[Semilla (political party)|Semilla]] was suspended for alleged cases of false signatures to establish the party. This action would have prohibited Arévalo's participation in the second round. The following day, the Constitutional Court reversed the suspension, paving the way for the second round of the presidential election to proceed.

President [[Alejandro Giammattei]] congratulated Arévalo for his win in the runoff, promising an orderly transition once the results are certified. A key representative of the [[Organization of American States]] (OAS), which has a team of 86 election observers in Guatemala, said the runoff voting had gone smoothly. Torres and the National Unity of Hope did not accept the electoral result and alleged electoral fraud in favor of Arévalo.<ref>{{Cite web |title= UNE de Sandra Torres denuncia fraude electoral en Guatemala |url= https://www.dw.com/es/une-de-sandra-torres-denuncia-fraude-electoral-en-guatemala/a-66636637 |access-date= 2023-08-26 |website= Deutsche Welle |language= es |archive-date= 29 August 2023 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20230829151359/https://www.dw.com/es/une-de-sandra-torres-denuncia-fraude-electoral-en-guatemala/a-66636637 |url-status= live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=La UNE sigue sin aceptar resultados de elecciones y señala "ilegalidad" en su oficialización |url=https://www.prensalibre.com/guatemala/elecciones-generales-guatemala-2023/la-une-sigue-sin-aceptar-resultados-de-elecciones-y-senala-ilegalidad-en-su-oficializacion-breaking/ |access-date=2023-08-29 |website=Prensa Libre |date=28 August 2023 |language=es |archive-date=29 August 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230829144409/https://www.prensalibre.com/guatemala/elecciones-generales-guatemala-2023/la-une-sigue-sin-aceptar-resultados-de-elecciones-y-senala-ilegalidad-en-su-oficializacion-breaking/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 12 September 2023, the [[Guatemala Attorney General|Public Prosecution Service]] opened the boxes containing the electoral ballots. The Supreme Electoral Tribunal condemned the decision and described it as "illegal". The attorney general's office stated that there are "several investigations" against the electoral process.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Feci abre cajas electorales durante allanamiento en el Parque de la Industria y el fiscal Curruchiche justifica decisión pese al rechazo del TSE |url=https://www.prensalibre.com/guatemala/elecciones-generales-guatemala-2023/feci-abre-cajas-electorales-durante-allanamiento-en-el-parque-de-la-industria-y-el-fiscal-curruchiche-justifica-decision-pese-al-rechazo-del-tse-breaking/ |access-date=2023-09-12 |website=Prensa Libre |date=12 September 2023 |language=es |archive-date=12 September 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230912182952/https://www.prensalibre.com/guatemala/elecciones-generales-guatemala-2023/feci-abre-cajas-electorales-durante-allanamiento-en-el-parque-de-la-industria-y-el-fiscal-curruchiche-justifica-decision-pese-al-rechazo-del-tse-breaking/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Legally, it is not possible to open electoral boxes and it is only valid with those contested within the time established by law.<ref>{{Cite web |title=¿Se puede abrir las cajas electorales? |url=https://lalinterna.agenciaocote.com/2023/07/05/se-puede-abrir-las-cajas-electorales/ |access-date=2023-09-12 |website=Agencia Ocote |date=5 July 2023 |language=es |archive-date=10 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231010151813/https://lalinterna.agenciaocote.com/2023/07/05/se-puede-abrir-las-cajas-electorales/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 8 December 2023, the Attorney General's office found "irregularities" in the presidential election and requested that the results be annulled. Prosecutors José Rafael Curruchiche and Leonor Eugenia Morales Lazo announced that they found "altered vote counts" inside ballot boxes and claimed the boxes were not properly secured. Lazo said the irregularities should annul the election for president, Vice President, and Congress.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-12-08 |title=Guatemala Crisis Deepens as Officials Claim Election Invalid |url=https://news.yahoo.com/guatemala-prosecutors-claim-presidential-election-203740355.html |access-date=2023-12-08 |website=Yahoo News |language=en-US}}</ref> On 14 December, the Constitutional Court, in a 4–1 decision, upheld the results of the election, ordered the Congress to "guarantee" the inauguration of Arévalo, and rejected the moves of the Public Prosecutor to have the elections nullified.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-12-14 |title=Guatemala top court to Congress: 'guarantee' Arevalo inauguration |url=https://news.yahoo.com/guatemala-top-court-congress-guarantee-221552015.html |access-date=2023-12-15 |website=Yahoo News |language=en-US}}</ref> However, challenges in the Congress led to delays in the inauguration, which finally took place in the early morning of 15 January.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/guatemalas-arevalo-due-assume-presidency-with-difficult-path-ahead-2024-01-14/|title=Tensions high outside Guatemala Congress as inauguration sessions delayed|first=Sofia|last=Menchu|publisher=Reuters|date=2024-01-14|accessdate=2024-01-14}}</ref>

== Background ==
In July 2021, Attorney General [[María Consuelo Porras]] dismissed the head of the special prosecutor's office against impunity, [[Juan Francisco Sandoval]]. Sandoval left the country shortly afterwards to 'protect [his] life and integrity'. The Attorney General subsequently increased investigations on judges, lawyers and prosecutors linked to the fight against corruption; several former investigators of the Special Prosecutor's Office against Impunity (FECI) and the [[International Commission against Impunity in Guatemala]] (CICIG) were arrested in 2022 and others forced into exile or else continually harassed.<ref>{{cite news === Registration of candidates ===

== Campaign ==
Results from the 2020 [[Latinobarómetro]] survey indicated that Guatemala had one of the lowest levels of trust and support for democracy in the region among the electorate: only 37% of citizens considered democracy to be preferable to any other form of government, and 65% were not satisfied with how it operated in Guatemala. In terms of political rights, only 9% of Guatemalans considered that their right to participate in political life is fully guaranteed. The atmosphere of optimism for change that had been present for the elections in 2015 had faded by 2020.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web |last=Martínez |first=Andrés Arnal |date=2023-06-19 |title=Elecciones presidenciales en Guatemala: entre la desesperanza y la deriva autoritaria |url=https://www.eldiario.es/internacional/elecciones-presidenciales-guatemala-desesperanza-deriva-autoritaria_1_10233284.html |access-date= |website=elDiario.es |language=es |archive-date=25 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230625215006/https://www.eldiario.es/internacional/elecciones-presidenciales-guatemala-desesperanza-deriva-autoritaria_1_10233284.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

Of the 4.5 million registered citizens aged between 18 and 30, only 2.6 million were on the electoral roll and eligible to vote in 2023.<ref name=":2" />

A substantial number of candidates ran a right-wing campaign, highlighting their attachment to religious values and the defence of private property, as well as their opposition to the legalisation of [[Abortion in Guatemala|abortion]]. In particular, the security policies of Salvadoran President [[Nayib Bukele]] were held up as an example to follow. The [[Evangelical Churches|Evangelical churches]] continue to have a major influence on political life, having participated in the selection of Supreme Court and Constitutional Court judges, among other things. During the final debate of the campaign, the discussions focused in particular on gay marriage, while the issue of poverty, which affects at least 54% of the population, was ignored. On the contrary, "the discourse and ideas of the left have disappeared from the debate", due to the elimination of the most popular left-wing candidates by the electoral authorities.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |date=2023-06-25 |title=In Guatemala, elections against a background of authoritarian drift |url=https://globeecho.com/news/north-america/in-guatemala-elections-against-a-background-of-authoritarian-drift/ |access-date=2023-06-25 |website=Globe Echo |language=en |archive-date=25 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230625214541/https://globeecho.com/news/north-america/in-guatemala-elections-against-a-background-of-authoritarian-drift/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

The three favourites in the election (Ríos, Torres, and Mulet) made extensive use of [[Clientelism|clientelist]] practices. People who came to their meetings were offered various gifts, even a sum of money in the case of Ríos. Political party activists circulate among the ranks, taking down the names and addresses of those present as "primary beneficiaries" in the event of their candidate's victory. Vote buying is a common practice in Guatemalan elections and often goes unpunished.<ref name=":1" />

After proceeding to the run-off, Torres focused on winning over conservative voters, particularly supporters of Ríos. == Electoral system ==
=== President ===
The [[President of Guatemala]] is elected using the [[two-round system]].<ref name=EG/>

=== Congress ===
The 160 members of Congress are elected by two methods; 130 are elected from 22 multi-member constituencies based on the [[Departments of Guatemala|departments]], with the remaining 31 elected from a single nationwide constituency. Seats are elected using [[closed list]] [[proportional representation]], with seats allocated using the [[D'Hondt method]].<ref name=EG>[http://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/2419/ Congress of the Republic] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190418105018/http://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/2419/ |date=18 April 2019 }} IFES</ref>

== Aftermath ==
=== First round ===
[[File:2023 Guatemalan presidential election first round invalid and blank votes map by municipality.svg|thumb|upright=1.2|Map of the number of invalid and blank votes as a share of total votes by municipality in the first round]]
No presidential candidate obtained more than 50% of the vote in the first round, meaning a second round was required. In an upset, [[Bernardo Arévalo]] finished in second place, qualifying for the runoff along with one of the favorites to win, former first lady and 2015 and 2019 presidential runner-up [[Sandra Torres]]. [[Zury Ríos]] and [[Edmond Mulet]], two other candidates that ranked near the top of opinion polls, finished in 6th and 5th place, respectively.

The first round also saw a record number of invalid ballots, where more than 24% of voters either [[Spoilt vote|spoilt]] their ballots or left their ballots blank entirely. This was attributed to distrust among the electorate towards the electoral system, including the disqualification of some presidential candidates.<ref>{{Cite news |last1=Pérez D. |first1=Sonia |last2=Sherman |first2=Christopher |date=26 June 2023 |title=Guatemala voters send 2 presidential candidates on opposite sides of political spectrum to a runoff |work=[[Associated Press]] |url=https://apnews.com/article/guatemala-election-9398f441cb50792cfe82793c8065ff70 |url-status=live |access-date=11 August 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230719202404/https://apnews.com/article/guatemala-election-9398f441cb50792cfe82793c8065ff70 |archive-date=19 July 2023}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=26 June 2023 |title=Troubled Guatemala headed for runoff after inconclusive election |work=[[France 24]] |agency=[[Agence France-Presse]] |url=https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20230625-all-the-same-guatemalans-go-to-the-polls-with-little-hope |url-status=live |access-date=11 August 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230628182120/https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20230625-all-the-same-guatemalans-go-to-the-polls-with-little-hope |archive-date=28 June 2023}}</ref>

=== Second round ===
[[File:Presidente Giammattei emite su voto 20230820 - 53129624874 (cropped).jpg|thumb|150px|President Giammattei casts his ballot during the second round]]
Arévalo defeated Torres in the second round with nearly 61 percent of the vote in what was seen as a landslide.<ref name=":52"/> It is the first election since the return to democracy in 1985 in which the winning party does not have [[Clientelism|clientelist networks]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://elpais.com/internacional/2023-08-20/elecciones-en-guatemala-2023-el-pais-vota-en-unos-comicios-que-desafian-a-los-partidos-tradicionales.html|title=Guatemala vota en unas elecciones que desafían a los partidos tradicionales|trans-title=Guatemala votes in elections that challenge traditional parties|language=es|date=20 August 2023|access-date=20 August 2023|work=El País|first1=Lorena|last1=Arrollo}}</ref>

On the day of the second round of the election, there were several bomb explosions at voting centers across the country.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://diario.elmundo.sv/el-mundo/lanzan-dos-bombas-a-centros-de-votacion-en-guatemala-y-localizan-una-tercera|title=Lanzan Dos Bombas a Centros de Votación en Guatemala y Localizan Una Tercera|trans-title=Two Bombs Thrown at Voting Centers in Guatemala and a Third Found|language=es|date=20 August 2023|access-date=20 August 2023|work=[[El Mundo (El Salvador)|El Mundo]]|first1=Javier|last1=Maldonado|archive-date=21 August 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230821031913/https://diario.elmundo.sv/el-mundo/lanzan-dos-bombas-a-centros-de-votacion-en-guatemala-y-localizan-una-tercera|url-status=live}}</ref>

After the results were announced on the night of 20 August, hundreds of citizens congregated in the [[Obelisco (Guatemala City)|Obelisco]] in Guatemala City, as well as the central park of [[Quetzaltenango]] to celebrate Arévalo's victory.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Pineda |first1=Sandy |title=Imágenes {{!}} Ciudadanos salen a las calles a celebrar el triunfo de Bernardo Arévalo y Movimiento Semilla en las elecciones de Guatemala |url=https://www.prensalibre.com/fotogalerias/elecciones-generales-guatemala-2023/imagenes-ciudadanos-salen-a-las-calles-a-celebrar-el-triunfo-de-bernardo-arevalo-en-las-elecciones-de-guatemala/ |access-date=21 August 2023 |work=[[Prensa Libre (Guatemala)|Prensa Libre]] |date=20 August 2023 |language=es-GT |archive-date=21 August 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230821100313/https://www.prensalibre.com/fotogalerias/elecciones-generales-guatemala-2023/imagenes-ciudadanos-salen-a-las-calles-a-celebrar-el-triunfo-de-bernardo-arevalo-en-las-elecciones-de-guatemala/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 28 August, the TSE, in a conference, officialized the results of the second round.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-08-28 |title=TSE oficializa los resultados de la segunda vuelta electoral |url=https://www.prensalibre.com/ahora/guatemala/elecciones-generales-guatemala-2023/tse-oficializa-los-resultados-de-la-segunda-vuelta-electoral/ |access-date=2023-08-29 |language=es-GT |archive-date=29 August 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230829000621/https://www.prensalibre.com/ahora/guatemala/elecciones-generales-guatemala-2023/tse-oficializa-los-resultados-de-la-segunda-vuelta-electoral/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

[[Edmond Mulet]], who finished fifth in the first round, told [[Associated Press]] after Arévalo's victory that he sees at least two possible scenarios in the coming weeks and months, both of which see the cancellation of Semilla's registration. It would have dire effects on his party's representatives in Congress, however, who would be barred from holding leadership positions or leading committees and would already be in minority from the beginning. In the other scenario, the Attorney General's Office succeeds in cancelling the legal status of Semilla, and it makes the argument that because the party was improperly registered, everything that occurred afterward, including Arévalo's nomination, is nullified and he cannot assume the presidency.<ref name=":02" />

=== Political crisis ===

Shortly after announcing Arévalo as winner, documents were published from the citizens registry suspending [[Semilla (political party)|Semilla']]s registration once more, meaning that the members of Congress elected as part of the party would be forced to sit as independents. Arévalo said he would not "give up", and the move was condemned by the [[Organization of American States]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Menchu |first1=Sofia |title=Guatemala body ratifies presidential vote result amid new bid to thwart victor |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/guatemala-electoral-body-ratifies-arevalos-presidential-victory-2023-08-28/ |access-date=10 October 2023 |work=Reuters |issue=29 August |archive-date=29 September 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230929054850/https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/guatemala-electoral-body-ratifies-arevalos-presidential-victory-2023-08-28/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Following several raids on Semilla offices around the country committed by the Public Prosecutor's Office, Arévalo formally suspended his participation in the presidential transition, saying that the raids were "flagrant crimes of abuse of authority for electoral purposes". President [[Alejandro Giammattei]] disagreed with the move, stating that the executive branch had nothing to do with it.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Pulice |first1=Carolina |title=Guatemala President-elect Arevalo suspends participation in transition |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/guatemala-president-elect-arevalo-suspends-participation-transition-2023-09-13/ |access-date=10 October 2023 |work=Reuters |date=12 September 2023 |archive-date=26 September 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230926072421/https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/guatemala-president-elect-arevalo-suspends-participation-transition-2023-09-13/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

Following urging from the international community, Arévalo formally resumed the process on 4 October, despite [[Attorney General of Guatemala]] [[María Consuelo Porras]]' continued effort to deregister Semilla as a political party, including raids on the Supreme Electoral Tribune's offices.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Sofia |first1=Menchu |title=Guatemala restarts presidential transition with president-elect's team |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/guatemala-restarts-presidential-transition-with-president-elects-team-2023-10-05/ |access-date=10 October 2023 |work=Reuters |date=4 October 2023 |archive-date=10 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231010005953/https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/guatemala-restarts-presidential-transition-with-president-elects-team-2023-10-05/ |url-status=live }}</ref> An [[Organization of American States|OAS]] mediator was named to help manage the transition two days later,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Sofia |first1=Menchu |title=OAS names mediators for Guatemala conflict that threatens transfer of power |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/oas-mediate-guatemala-conflict-that-threatens-transfer-power-2023-10-07/ |access-date=10 October 2023 |work=Reuters |date=7 October 2023 |archive-date=9 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231009192319/https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/oas-mediate-guatemala-conflict-that-threatens-transfer-power-2023-10-07/ |url-status=live }}</ref> with the organisation's observation mission stating that it appeared the actions by prosecutors were intended to keep Arévalo from taking office. On 2 November, Movimiento Semilla was officially suspended by the Public Ministry. Indigenous movements vowed to continue to protest, and the United States government stripped the visas of 14 people in the Public Ministry on the same day. The U.S. State Department's Deputy Assistant Secretary for Western Hemisphere Affairs, Eric Jacobstein, stated that the suspension was worrisome as an apparent way to interfere with Arévalo's transition to office. The suspension also means that members of the party elected to the [[Congress of the Republic of Guatemala]] under the banner of the party cannot hold leadership positions or chair committees. It also means that the party cannot hold assemblies or carry out administrative procedures.<ref>{{cite news |title=Guatemala electoral authorities suspend President-elect Bernardo Arévalo's party |url=https://apnews.com/article/guatemala-election-arevalo-seed-movement-e51e3829bc8e227a926f3d7062758eb2 |access-date=6 November 2023 |publisher=Associated Press |date=2 November 2023 |archive-date=6 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231106152631/https://apnews.com/article/guatemala-election-arevalo-seed-movement-e51e3829bc8e227a926f3d7062758eb2 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Miranda |first1=Wilfredo |title=Guatemala electoral authorities suspend president-elect Bernardo Arévalo's Seed Movement |url=https://english.elpais.com/international/2023-11-03/guatemala-electoral-authorities-suspend-president-elect-bernardo-arevalos-seed-movement.html |access-date=6 November 2023 |work=El Pais |date=3 November 2023 |archive-date=5 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231105091735/https://english.elpais.com/international/2023-11-03/guatemala-electoral-authorities-suspend-president-elect-bernardo-arevalos-seed-movement.html |url-status=live }}</ref>
