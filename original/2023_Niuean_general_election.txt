General elections were held in [[Niue]] on 29 April 2023.<ref name=RNZ2023a/> The elections were originally planned to be held early, in late February or early March.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://islandsbusiness.com/news-break/niue-premier-indicates-an-early-general-election-in-2023/ |title=Niue Premier indicates an early general election in 2023 |website=Island Business |date=29 November 2022 |access-date=14 April 2023}}</ref> Eight new MPs and six women were elected.

Following the elections the Assembly elected [[Dalton Tagelagi]] as Premier. 

==Electoral system==
The 20 members of the [[Niue Assembly|Assembly]] are elected by two methods; 14 are elected from single-member consistencies using [[first-past-the-post voting]] and six are elected from a single nationwide constituency by [[multiple non-transferable vote]].<ref name=IFES>{{cite web|url=http://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/2998/|title=Niue|website=IFES Election Guide}}</ref>  There are currently no political parties. After the election, the Members of the Assembly elect a Speaker of the Assembly from outside parliament, and a Premier, who must be an MP. The Premier then chooses a Cabinet.

In 2023, the candidate nomination fee was increased from NZ$11 to NZ$200.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://tvniue.com/2023/03/new-changes-in-the-lead-up-to-the-2023-general-elections-including-200-candidates-nomination-fee/ |title=New changes in the lead-up to the 2023 general elections including $200 candidates nomination fee |website=TV Niue |author= Esther Pavihi  |date=March 2023 |access-date=14 April 2023}}</ref>

==Campaign==
A total of 42 candidates contested the election, of which 17 contested the six common roll seats.<ref name=RNZ2023a>{{cite web |url=https://www.rnz.co.nz/international/pacific-news/487946/candidate-list-for-niue-general-election-released |title=Candidate list for Niue General Election released |website=[[RNZ]] |date=14 April 2023 |access-date=14 April 2023}}</ref> 

==Results==
Three of the common-roll seats went to newcomers, with the initial results indicating that the final seat would be decided by a coin-toss.<ref name=TVNResults>{{cite web |url=https://tvniue.com/2023/04/several-new-members-will-be-sworn-in-to-the-18th-niue-legislative-assembly-after-the-general-election-yesterday/ |title=Several new members will be sworn in to the 18th Niue Legislative Assembly after the general election yesterday |website=TV Niue |author= Esther Pavihi  |date=30 April 2023 |access-date=1 May 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://pacificmedianetwork.com/articles/final-seat-in-niuean-elections-to-be-decided-by-hat-draw |title=Final seat in Niuean elections to be decided by hat-draw |website=Pacific Media Network |author=Matt Manukuo |date=1 May 2023 |access-date=3 May 2023}}</ref> The final results on 3 May did not show a tie, with [[Sonya Talagi]] winning the last seat on the common roll by two votes.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://tvniue.com/2023/05/sonya-talagi-duly-elected-6th-common-roll-member-while-lisimoni-togahai-has-filed-petition-for-a-recount/ |title=Sonya Talagi duly elected 6th Common Roll Member, while Lisimoni-Togahai has filed petition for a recount |website=TV Niue |author= Esther Pavihi  |date=3 May 2023 |access-date=3 May 2023}}</ref>

Six candidates, including Premier [[Dalton Tagelagi]], were elected unopposed.<ref name=RNZUnop>{{cite web |url=https://tvniue.com/2023/04/list-of-candidates-for-the-2023-general-elections-released-tagelagi-duly-elected-with-5-others/ |title=List of candidates for the 2023 General Elections released; Tagelagi duly elected with 5 others |website=TV Niue |author= Esther Pavihi  |date=14 April 2023 |access-date=14 April 2023}}</ref> Six women were elected.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rnz.co.nz/international/programmes/datelinepacific/audio/2018888245/six-women-mps-voted-into-niue-s-parliament |title=Six women MPs voted into Niue's parliament |website=[[RNZ]] |date=2 May 2023 |access-date=2 May 2023}}</ref> Three sitting MPs were unseated.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://tvniue.com/2023/05/two-long-serving-mps-terry-coe-and-opili-talafasi-will-not-be-returning-to-the-next-niue-legislative-assembly/ |title=Two long serving MPs Terry Coe and Opili Talafasi will not be returning to the next Niue Legislative Assembly |website=TV Niue |author= Esther Pavihi |date=1 May 2023 |access-date=2 May 2023}}</ref>

In a secret ballot, the Assembly re-elected [[Dalton Tagelagi]] as Premier in a 16-4 vote against [[O'Love Jacobsen]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rnz.co.nz/international/pacific-news/489641/dalton-tagelagi-re-elected-premier-of-niue |title=Dalton Tagelagi re-elected premier of Niue |publisher=[[RNZ]] |date=10 May 2023 |access-date=10 May 2023}}</ref> Tagelagi appointed his Cabinet on 12 May, the first gender-balanced Cabinet in Niuean history.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rnz.co.nz/international/pacific-news/489945/dalton-tagelagi-forms-niue-s-first-gender-balanced-cabinet |title=Dalton Tagelagi forms Niue's first gender-balanced Cabinet |publisher=[[RNZ]] |date=15 May 2023 |access-date=15 May 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://tvniue.com/2023/05/premier-tagelagi-announce-first-gender-balanced-cabinet/ |title=Premier Tagelagi announce first gender- balanced Cabinet |publisher=TV Niue |author= Esther Pavihi  |date=12 May 2023 |access-date=15 May 2023}}</ref>
