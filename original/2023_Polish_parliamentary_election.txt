Parliamentary elections were held in Poland on 15 October 2023 to elect members of the [[Sejm]] and [[Senate of Poland|Senate]]. A [[2023 Polish referendum|referendum]] containing four questions concerning economic and immigration policy of the government was held simultaneously.

In the previous [[2019 Polish parliamentary election]], the ruling right-wing [[Law and Justice (Poland)|Law and Justice Party]] (PiS) had held onto its majority in the Sejm with Prime Minister [[Mateusz Morawiecki]] forming [[Second Cabinet of Mateusz Morawiecki|a second government]]. The PiS sought to win a third term which would be unprecedented in Polish history. The opposition, including the [[Civic Platform|Civic Platform Party]] and others, secured a Senate majority. In the lead-up to the 2023 elections, opposition leader and former prime minister, [[Donald Tusk]], led the [[Civic Coalition (Poland)|Civic Coalition]] political alliance in opposition to the PiS.

The [[United Right (Poland)|United Right]] alliance placed first for the third straight election and won a plurality of seats but fell short of a Sejm majority. The opposition, consisting of the [[Civic Coalition (Poland)|Civic Coalition]], [[Third Way (Poland)|Third Way]], and [[The Left (Poland)|The Left]], achieved a combined total vote of 54%, managing to form a majority [[coalition government]].<ref>{{cite web |date=15 October 2023 |title=Opposition wins Polish election, according to exit poll |url=https://www.politico.eu/article/opposition-wins-polish-election-according-to-exit-poll-poland-kaczynski-duda-tusk-election-rule-of-law/ |access-date=15 October 2023 |website=POLITICO |archive-date=15 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231015191532/https://www.politico.eu/article/opposition-wins-polish-election-according-to-exit-poll-poland-kaczynski-duda-tusk-election-rule-of-law/ |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Picheta |first=Rob |date=15 October 2023 |title=Poland's opposition has path to oust populist ruling party, exit poll shows |url=https://www.cnn.com/2023/10/15/europe/poland-election-voting-intl/index.html |access-date=15 October 2023 |publisher=CNN |archive-date=15 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231015152206/https://www.cnn.com/2023/10/15/europe/poland-election-voting-intl/index.html |url-status=live}}</ref> In the Senate, the opposition electoral alliance [[Senate Pact 2023]] won a plurality of the vote and a majority of seats. Voter turnout was 74.4%, the highest in contested elections and the highest since the fall of the communist [[Polish People's Republic]], beating previous records set in 1989 and 2019.

==Background==

===2019 Polish parliamentary election===
{{further|2019 Polish parliamentary election|Second Cabinet of Mateusz Morawiecki}}
The 2019 parliamentary election saw a record high turnout, with over 60% of registered electors participating. It also saw the centre-left, in the form of [[The Left (Poland)|Lewica]], entering the [[Sejm]] after four years on the outside looking in. Conversely, the [[Far-right politics in Poland|far right]] united under the [[Confederation Liberty and Independence|Konfederacja]] (Kon) banner to enter one of the two chambers of parliament for the first time since the 2005 elections.<ref name=BBC>{{cite web|title=Poland election: Ruling Law and Justice party win poll|date=14 October 2019|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-50037654|work=BBC News|access-date=15 October 2023|archive-date=19 July 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230719195831/https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-50037654|url-status=live}}</ref>

[[Right-wing politics|Right-wing]] parties, coalesced around the ruling [[National conservatism|national-conservative]] [[Law and Justice (Poland)|Law and Justice]] (PiS) within the [[United Right (Poland)|United Right]] (ZP) alliance, won the highest percentage of votes ever received since the complete return to democracy in [[1991 Polish parliamentary election|1991]], maintaining their majority in the Sejm, but losing it in the [[Senate of Poland|Senate]]. The PiS party president, [[Jarosław Kaczyński]], thus saw his position as the country's strongman strengthened, despite occupying no governmental position. This result saw the second reelection of a majority government since the fall of the [[Eastern Bloc]]. Despite not defeating PiS, the main opposition party, the [[Liberalism in Poland|liberal]] [[Civic Platform]] (PO), itself within the [[Civic Coalition (Poland)|Civic Coalition]] (KO) alliance, progressed in the senate, though without winning a majority of seats on its own. The opposition altogether did win a majority of seats in the senate, thanks to Lewica, the [[Polish Coalition]] (PSL) and independent candidates' gains.<ref name=BBC/><ref name=WaPo>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2019/10/18/polands-parliament-is-now-divided-what-does-this-mean-ruling-law-justice-party/|first1=Mary|last1=Stegmaier|first2=Kamil|last2=Marcinkiewicz|title=Poland's Parliament is now divided. What does this mean for the ruling Law and Justice party?|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]] |date=18 October 2019|access-date=15 October 2023|archive-date=13 March 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220313173527/https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2019/10/18/polands-parliament-is-now-divided-what-does-this-mean-ruling-law-justice-party/|url-status=live}}</ref>

One month after the vote, the incumbent [[Prime Minister of Poland|Prime Minister]], [[Mateusz Morawiecki]], formed his second government. Its composition showed the so-called "moderate" right strengthened, which Morawiecki was part of, alongside a weakening of the "radical" right, led by the [[Ministry of Justice (Poland)|Justice minister]] [[Zbigniew Ziobro]]. This strategy was mainly to appeal to the more moderate electorate for the [[2020 Polish presidential election]].<ref>{{cite web |language=fr |first=Jakub |last=Iwaniuk |title=En Pologne, l'opposition conquiert le Sénat et freine la " révolution conservatrice " du PiS |trans-title=In Poland, the opposition wins the Senate and slows PiS' "conservative revolution" |work=[[Le Monde]] |date=14 November 2019 |url=https://www.lemonde.fr/international/article/2019/11/14/en-pologne-l-opposition-conquiert-le-senat-et-freine-la-revolution-conservatrice-du-pis_6019165_3210.html |access-date=15 October 2023 |archive-date=1 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230601160102/https://www.lemonde.fr/international/article/2019/11/14/en-pologne-l-opposition-conquiert-le-senat-et-freine-la-revolution-conservatrice-du-pis_6019165_3210.html |url-status=live}}</ref> Morawiecki's government received the Sejm's confidence on 19 November with 237 votes for, 214 against and three abstentions.<ref>{{cite web |title=Our goal is Polish prosperity state: PM |work=[[Telewizja Polska]] |date=19 November 2019 |url=https://polandin.com/45397689/our-goal-is-polish-prosperity-state-pm |access-date=15 October 2023 |archive-date=1 October 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201001124027/https://polandin.com/45397689/our-goal-is-polish-prosperity-state-pm |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=New government receives vote of confidence |work=[[Telewizja Polska]] |date=20 November 2019 |url=https://polandin.com/45413891/new-government-receives-vote-of-confidence |access-date=15 October 2023 |archive-date=26 June 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200626232312/https://polandin.com/45413891/new-government-receives-vote-of-confidence |url-status=live}}</ref>

===2020 presidential election===
{{further|2020 Polish presidential election}}
The 2020 presidential election saw the reelection of incumbent [[President of Poland|president]] [[Andrzej Duda]], a former member of [[Law and Justice (Poland)|Law and Justice]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=jk/pap |date=2015-05-26 |title=Andrzej Duda zrzekł się członkostwa w PiS |url=https://www.newsweek.pl/polska/andrzej-duda-zrzekl-sie-czlonkostwa-w-pis/3cnddk4 |access-date=2024-10-12 |website=Newsweek |language=pl}}</ref> Originally planned in May, the elections were very affected by the then ongoing [[COVID-19 pandemic in Poland|COVID-19 pandemic]]. The government's proposal to maintain the election in May but only through postal votes launched a strong polemic, with the opposition denouncing the unequal campaigning capacities of the incumbent president compared to other candidates within the context of the lockdown and quarantine measures. The election was then postponed to late June following a compromise within the ruling coalition and the opposition's approval, partly thanks to the latter's control of the Senate.<ref name=LaCroix>{{cite news |first=Jean-Baptiste |last=François |language=fr |title=En Pologne, la controverse du maintien de l'élection présidentielle |url=https://www.la-croix.com/Monde/Europe/En-Pologne-controverse-maintien-lelection-presidentielle-2020-03-27-1201086406 |work=la-croix.com |date=27 March 2020 |access-date=15 October 2023 |archive-date=11 April 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230411134646/https://www.la-croix.com/Monde/Europe/En-Pologne-controverse-maintien-lelection-presidentielle-2020-03-27-1201086406 |url-status=live}}</ref>

Despite the pandemic, both rounds of voting saw higher turnouts, with Duda facing the [[Mayor of Warsaw|Mayor]] of [[Warsaw]], [[Rafał Trzaskowski]], a member of [[Civic Platform]]. Duda beat Trzaskowski, gathering 51% of the votes against the latter's 49%. These results were the most closely fought presidential elections since the return of democracy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/jul/13/incumbent-andrzej-duda-wins-polish-presidential-election-commission|title=Duda narrowly re-elected in Poland in boost for ruling nationalists|first=Shaun|last=Walker|work=The Guardian|date=13 July 2020|access-date=15 October 2023|archive-date=15 May 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230515112714/https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/jul/13/incumbent-andrzej-duda-wins-polish-presidential-election-commission|url-status=live}}</ref>

Duda's victory allows PiS take advantage from his presidential veto in case of an opposition victory in the legislative elections, with the opposition needing a three-fifths majority, which currently amounts to 276 seats, to override one.<ref>{{cite web |language=pl |title=Konwencja PO. Budka proponuje Koalicję 276. "Tyle głosów potrzebnych do przejęcia władzy" |trans-title=PO Convention, Budka proposes a coalition for 276: "so many votes will be needed to get in power" |url=https://wiadomosci.gazeta.pl/wiadomosci/7,114884,26762104,konwencja-po-276-glosow-potrzebnych-do-przejecia-wladzy.html |website=gazetapl |date=6 February 2021 |access-date=15 October 2023 |archive-date=19 July 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230719173530/https://wiadomosci.gazeta.pl/wiadomosci/7,114884,26762104,konwencja-po-276-glosow-potrzebnych-do-przejecia-wladzy.html |url-status=live}}</ref>

===2023 Polish protests===
{{main|2023 Polish protests}}
In May 2023, a law previously passed by the Sejm with the votes of the governing parties came into force, which provides for the establishment of a commission that can, without a court order, exclude politicians from public office for a period of ten years if, in their opinion, the politician was influenced by Russian interests. According to the law, the commission must examine whether this applies to Polish government politicians from 2007 (after PiS' defeat in the [[2007 Polish parliamentary election|2007 election]]). According to critics, the law could have been used as an instrument to prohibit selected opposition politicians from taking part in the parliamentary elections.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tagesschau.de/ausland/europa/polen-gesetz-duda-100.html |title=Polen: Duda unterschreibt umstrittenes Gesetz zu Einflussnahme |work=tagesschau.de |date=29 May 2023 |language=de |access-date=30 May 2023 |archive-date=25 September 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230925150417/https://www.tagesschau.de/ausland/europa/polen-gesetz-duda-100.html |url-status=live}}</ref> Polish media therefore spoke of a "Lex Tusk" – a law aimed at the opposition leader and former prime minister [[Donald Tusk]] (2007–2014), who could have been excluded from the parliamentary elections in October 2023 as the potentially most promising opposition candidate.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tagesschau.de/ausland/europa/polen-protest-pis-100.html |title=Massenprotest gegen polnische Regierung |language=de |access-date=4 June 2023 |archive-date=25 September 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230925200421/https://www.tagesschau.de/ausland/europa/polen-protest-pis-100.html |url-status=live}}</ref> PiS party circles repeatedly accused Tusk of making Poland dependent on energy imports from Russia during his term as head of government. The law drew strong criticism from the United States and the European Union, which expressed concern that the law jeopardized freedom and fairness in elections in Poland. President Duda then softened the law by introducing an amendment to the Sejm on 2 June 2023, which deprived the proposed commission of the previously planned right to impose a ban on political activity.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://p.dw.com/p/4S7X2|title=Polens Präsident Andrzej Duda gibt klein bei|work=Deutsche Welle|date=2 June 2023|access-date=5 June 2023|language=de|archive-date=18 October 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231018131550/https://www.dw.com/de/polens-pr%C3%A4sident-andrzej-duda-gibt-klein-bei/a-65807484|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 4 June 2023 (the anniversary of the [[1989 Polish parliamentary election|first partially free elections in Poland in 1989]]) according to organizers, citing the city administration, half a million people took part in a "Great March for Democracy" organized by Tusk's Civic Platform in Warsaw to protest against the law.<ref>{{cite web |author=Viktoria Großmann |url=https://www.sueddeutsche.de/politik/polen-donald-tusk-pis-1.5900446 |title=500000 Menschen protestieren gegen Polens Regierung |date=4 June 2023 |language=de |access-date=4 June 2023 |archive-date=5 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230605081645/https://www.sueddeutsche.de/politik/polen-donald-tusk-pis-1.5900446 |url-status=live}}</ref> There were also protests with tens of thousands of participants in other cities, including Krakow, Szczecin and Częstochowa. The demonstration in Warsaw was joined by numerous civil rights movements, the Civic Platform spoke of the largest demonstration in Poland's history since the fall of communism in 1989. The protest march through the center of Warsaw was also led by the former Polish President [[Lech Wałęsa]].<ref>{{cite news |title="Europa, wir entschuldigen uns für die PiS": Zehntausende demonstrieren in Warschau gegen Polens Regierung |newspaper=Der Tagesspiegel Online |issn=1865-2263 |url=https://www.tagesspiegel.de/internationales/europa-wir-entschuldigen-uns-fur-die-pis-zehntausende-demonstrieren-in-warschau-gegen-polens-regierung-9926291.html |access-date=4 June 2023 |language=de |archive-date=20 September 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230920004343/https://www.tagesspiegel.de/internationales/europa-wir-entschuldigen-uns-fur-die-pis-zehntausende-demonstrieren-in-warschau-gegen-polens-regierung-9926291.html |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.stern.de/politik/ausland/demonstrationen--protest-in-warschau-gegen-polens-regierung--33527534.html |title=Protest in Warschau gegen Polens Regierung |date=4 June 2023 |language=de |access-date=4 June 2023 |archive-date=4 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230604170156/https://www.stern.de/politik/ausland/demonstrationen--protest-in-warschau-gegen-polens-regierung--33527534.html |url-status=live}}</ref>

==Electoral system==
{{Further|Elections in Poland}}
The [[President of Poland]] set the election day to be Sunday, 15 October 2023.<ref>{{cite web |title=Prezydent: podjąłem decyzję o zarządzeniu wyborów do Sejmu i Senatu na dzień 15 października 2023 roku |url=https://www.pap.pl/aktualnosci/news%2C1606184%2Cprezydent-podjalem-decyzje-o-zarzadzeniu-wyborow-do-sejmu-i-senatu-na |access-date=10 August 2023 |website=Polska Agencja Prasowa SA |language=pl |archive-date=9 August 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230809071130/https://www.pap.pl/aktualnosci/news,1606184,prezydent-podjalem-decyzje-o-zarzadzeniu-wyborow-do-sejmu-i-senatu-na |url-status=live}}</ref> This date was consistent with requirements posed in Article 98 Section 2 of the [[Constitution of Poland|Polish Constitution]], whereby the election is to take place within the final 30 days of the [[9th term Sejm and 10th term Senate of Poland|current term of Parliament]] (ending 11 November 2023). The vote ought to be held on a non-working day – a Sunday or a [[Public holidays in Poland|public holiday]]. Other possible dates included 22 October, 29 October, [[All Saints' Day#Poland|1 November]], 5 November and [[National Independence Day (Poland)|11 November]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://polityka.se.pl/wiadomosci/wybory-2023-cztery-mozliwe-terminy-wyjasniamy-kiedy-wybory-parlamentarne-2023-moga-sie-odbyc-02-02-aa-FJMa-bZd5-BzER.html |title=Znamy cztery daty, kiedy wybory 2023 mogą się odbyć. "Decyzję podejmie prezydent" |access-date=2 February 2023 |archive-date=4 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230204025140/https://polityka.se.pl/wiadomosci/wybory-2023-cztery-mozliwe-terminy-wyjasniamy-kiedy-wybory-parlamentarne-2023-moga-sie-odbyc-02-02-aa-FJMa-bZd5-BzER.html |url-status=live}}</ref>

The process of election for the Sejm is through [[party-list proportional representation]] via the [[D'Hondt method]] in multi-seat [[constituency|constituencies]],<ref name="electionresources">{{cite web |url=http://electionresources.org/pl/ |title=Election Resources on the Internet: Elections to the Polish Sejm, Part I |publisher=electionresources.org |access-date=3 August 2014 |archive-date=24 February 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210224021334/http://electionresources.org/pl/ |url-status=live}}</ref> with a 5% threshold for single party (KW) and citizen committees (KWW) and an 8% threshold for coalitions (KKW). National minority committees, such as the [[German minority in Poland|German minority]], can apply to be exempt from the nation-wide threshold, and in such case participate in the d'Hondt seat distribution within their constituency, in this specific case [[21st electoral district for the Sejm of the Republic of Poland|Opole]], regardless of the national share of votes.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://demagog.org.pl/wypowiedzi/jaki-jest-prog-wyborczy-dla-mniejszosci-narodowych/ |title=Jaki jest próg wyborczy dla mniejszości narodowych? |date=1 February 2023 |language=pl |website=demagog.org.pl |access-date=31 October 2023 |archive-date=31 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231031170035/https://demagog.org.pl/wypowiedzi/jaki-jest-prog-wyborczy-dla-mniejszosci-narodowych/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Contrary to popular belief, minority committees are <em>not</em> guaranteed seats in the parliament.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ogiolda |first1=Krzysztof |title=Mniejszość niemiecka bez reprezentacji w Sejmie. Tego jeszcze nie było |url=https://opolska360.pl/mniejszosc-niemiecka-bez-reprezentacji-w-sejmie-tego-jeszcze-nie-bylo/ |website=Opolska360 |access-date=28 October 2023 |language=pl-PL |date=16 October 2023 |archive-date=28 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231028141523/https://opolska360.pl/mniejszosc-niemiecka-bez-reprezentacji-w-sejmie-tego-jeszcze-nie-bylo/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

[[File:Senat RP okręgi blank map.svg|thumb|The 100 Senate constituencies]]

[[Senate of Poland|Senators]] are elected by [[First-past-the-post voting|first-past-the-post method]] in [[List of constituencies for the Senate of the Republic of Poland|100 constituencies]]. Most of the opposition (Civic Coalition, New Left and Third Way) signed a so-called ''[[Senate Pact 2023|senate pact]]'', under which the parties agreed to enter one commonly accepted candidate in each district.<ref>{{cite web |date=28 February 2023 |title=Opozycja zawarła pakt senacki. Co to konkretnie oznacza? |url=https://www.polityka.pl/tygodnikpolityka/kraj/2203490,1,opozycja-zawarla-pakt-senacki-co-to-konkretnie-oznacza.read |access-date=10 August 2023 |website=www.polityka.pl |language=pl |archive-date=10 August 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230810230359/https://www.polityka.pl/tygodnikpolityka/kraj/2203490,1,opozycja-zawarla-pakt-senacki-co-to-konkretnie-oznacza.read |url-status=live}}</ref> This strategy has previously granted them 51 seats despite losing the Sejm.<ref>{{cite web |date=25 July 2023 |title=Pakt senacki. Politycy opozycji mają "postawić kropkę nad i" |url=https://wiadomosci.onet.pl/kraj/pakt-senacki-politycy-opozycji-maja-postawic-kropke-nad-i/pd3n15r |access-date=10 August 2023 |website=Onet Wiadomości |language=pl |archive-date=10 August 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230810230626/https://wiadomosci.onet.pl/kraj/pakt-senacki-politycy-opozycji-maja-postawic-kropke-nad-i/pd3n15r |url-status=live}}</ref>

== Results ==
=== Sejm ===
{{See also|List of Sejm members (2023–2027)}}
PiS remained the largest party in the Sejm, but with about 35% of the vote, lost its majority and was unable to form a government. The three main opposition groups, Civic Coalition, Third Way and New Left, took 54% of the votes, winning enough seats to allow them to take power.<ref name=AP>{{cite news |last1=Gera |first1=Vanessa |last2=Scislowska |first2=Monika |title=Polish election marks huge win for Donald Tusk as ruling conservatives lose to centrist coalition |url=https://apnews.com/article/poland-election-tusk-results-f687cf22fee9d395e92a08d2b6ab0f03 |work=AP News |date=17 October 2023 |access-date=17 October 2023 |archive-date=17 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231017140837/https://apnews.com/article/poland-election-tusk-results-f687cf22fee9d395e92a08d2b6ab0f03 |url-status=live}}</ref> According to the final vote count by the [[National Electoral Commission (Poland)|National Electoral Commission]], Law and Justice won 194 seats, the Civic Coalition 157, the Third Way 65, The Left 26, and the Confederation Liberty and Independence 18.<ref>{{cite news |last=Cienski |first=Jan |date=17 October 2023 |title=Poland election results: Opposition secures win, final count shows |url=https://www.politico.eu/article/poland-election-results-opposition-donald-tusk-wins-final-count-civic-platform-pis/ |work=Politico |location=Warsaw |access-date=17 October 2023 |archive-date=17 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231017075052/https://www.politico.eu/article/poland-election-results-opposition-donald-tusk-wins-final-count-civic-platform-pis/ |url-status=live}}</ref>

Although the [[German Minority Electoral Committee]] did win 5.37% of the vote in the Opole region in this election, they came 6th instead of the expected 5th place, falling 5,372 votes short. The Opole Voivodeship represents a total of 12 Sejm seats, and as the 5th place was taken by the far-right [[Confederation Liberty and Independence]], the last 12th seat, which had previously been won by German Minority, went to them instead.<ref>{{cite web |author=Krzysztof Ogiolda |date=21 October 2023 |title=Mniejszość Niemiecka bez posła w Sejmie. Co poszło nie tak? |url=https://opolska360.pl/mniejszosc-niemiecka-bez-posla-w-sejmie-co-poszlo-nie-tak/ |website=opolska360.pl |language=pl |access-date=23 October 2023 |archive-date=24 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231024085649/https://opolska360.pl/mniejszosc-niemiecka-bez-posla-w-sejmie-co-poszlo-nie-tak/ |url-status=live }}</ref> As a result, the German Minority Electoral Committee failed to win any parliamentary seat for the first time in 32 years.<ref>{{cite web |date=17 October 2023 |title=German minority out of Polish parliament for the first time in 32 years |url=https://notesfrompoland.com/2023/10/17/german-minority-out-of-polish-parliament-for-the-first-time-in-32-years/}}</ref>

==Analysis ==
Turnout was 74.7% among women and 73.1% among men, with both giving similar levels of support for the government and two leading opposition parties, Civic Coalition and Third Way.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.dw.com/en/were-women-key-to-voting-out-polands-ruling-conservatives/a-67214867 | title=Were women key to voting out Poland's ruling conservatives? – DW – 10/25/2023 | website=[[Deutsche Welle]] | access-date=26 October 2023 | archive-date=26 October 2023 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231026042117/https://www.dw.com/en/were-women-key-to-voting-out-polands-ruling-conservatives/a-67214867 | url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://notesfrompoland.com/2023/10/19/record-number-of-female-mps-in-polands-parliament-after-elections/ | title=Record number of female MPS in Poland's parliament after elections | date=19 October 2023 | access-date=24 October 2023 | archive-date=25 October 2023 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231025171307/https://notesfrompoland.com/2023/10/19/record-number-of-female-mps-in-polands-parliament-after-elections/ | url-status=live }}</ref> Analysts identified a "youthquake" in which voting by Poland's young voters had a disproportionate impact on the election outcome. Turnout for ages 18–29 reached 68.8%, compared to 46.4% in the previous elections of 2019; among these voters, support for the ruling party fell to 14.9% from 26.3% four years earlier.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.euronews.com/2023/10/18/no-country-for-old-men-how-young-voters-helped-swing-the-elections-in-poland | title=Young voters' turnout in Poland showed it's 'No country for Old Men' | date=18 October 2023 | access-date=24 October 2023 | archive-date=23 October 2023 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231023191133/https://www.euronews.com/2023/10/18/no-country-for-old-men-how-young-voters-helped-swing-the-elections-in-poland | url-status=live }}</ref>

President [[Andrzej Duda]] later announced that he would hold consultations separately with every parliamentary party leader on 24 and 25 October.<ref>{{cite news |title=Poland's president to start consultations on forming new government |url=https://www.rfi.fr/en/international/20231020-poland-s-president-to-consult-with-party-leaders-on-forming-government |work=[[Radio France Internationale|RFI]] |date=20 October 2023 |access-date=23 October 2023 |archive-date=23 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231023080958/https://www.rfi.fr/en/international/20231020-poland-s-president-to-consult-with-party-leaders-on-forming-government |url-status=live }}</ref> On 24 October, leaders of the [[Civic Coalition (Poland)|Civic Coalition]], the [[Polish People's Party]], [[Poland 2050]], and [[The Left (Poland)|The Left]] stated they are ready to form a government with Donald Tusk as their candidate for prime minister.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://tvpworld.com/73661579/poland-president-concludes-consultations-with-party-leaders-on-forming-new-government | title=President met with all leaders of new parliament | access-date=26 October 2023 | archive-date=26 October 2023 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231026040058/https://tvpworld.com/73661579/poland-president-concludes-consultations-with-party-leaders-on-forming-new-government | url-status=live }}</ref> However, Duda had a maximum of 30 days to call parliament into session, especially if he wanted the ruling Law and Justice party to try to build a government. Opposition parties had called on Duda to allow them to form a government as soon as possible and respect the will of the voters. Representatives of Duda stated that he would do so within the timeframe that the Constitution demands and allows.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.nwaonline.com/news/2023/oct/25/premier-hopeful-emerges-in-poland | title=Premier hopeful emerges in Poland &#124; Northwest Arkansas Democrat-Gazette | date=25 October 2023 | access-date=26 October 2023 | archive-date=26 October 2023 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231026040039/https://www.nwaonline.com/news/2023/oct/25/premier-hopeful-emerges-in-poland/ | url-status=live }}</ref>

On 6 November, Duda named Law and Justice's incumbent prime minister [[Mateusz Morawiecki]] as his prime ministerial nominee.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Prezydent Andrzej Duda wskazał, komu powierzy misję tworzenia rządu |url=https://wiadomosci.onet.pl/kraj/prezydent-andrzej-duda-wskazal-komu-powierzy-misje-tworzenia-rzadu/7rsnxbv |access-date=6 November 2023 |website=wiadomosci.onet.pl |date=6 November 2023 |language=pl |archive-date=6 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231106191818/https://wiadomosci.onet.pl/kraj/prezydent-andrzej-duda-wskazal-komu-powierzy-misje-tworzenia-rzadu/7rsnxbv |url-status=live }}</ref> This move was criticized by the opposition, as the United Right was 40 seats short of a majority and no other bloc had agreed to join them for coalition talks.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Prezydent wszystkich Polaków (którzy głosowali na PiS) |url=https://wiadomosci.onet.pl/opinie/prezydent-wszystkich-polakow-ktorzy-glosowali-na-pis/3web5ys |access-date=6 November 2023 |website=wiadomosci.onet.pl |date=6 November 2023 |language=pl |archive-date=6 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231106192156/https://wiadomosci.onet.pl/opinie/prezydent-wszystkich-polakow-ktorzy-glosowali-na-pis/3web5ys |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 10 November, Civic Coalition, Poland 2050, Polish People's Party and New Left signed a coalition agreement with Tusk as their candidate for prime minister. The opposition parties wanted to sign the agreement before the Sejm's first sitting in order to show that they stood ready to govern. Morawiecki was required to secure the Sejm's [[Motion of no confidence|confidence]] within two weeks of being sworn in. Under the Constitution, if Morawiecki failed to do so, the Sejm would then designate its own candidate for prime minister, and Duda would be required to appoint that candidate before 11 December. Most commentators expected Morawiecki to come up short of the support needed to govern, as no other party willing to go into coalition with PiS would give it enough support to command the confidence of the Sejm.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-67381267.amp | title = Poland's Tusk-led pro-EU opposition signs deal and waits to govern | work = [[BBC]] | author = Easton, Adam | date = 10 November 2023 | access-date = 10 November 2023 | archive-date = 10 November 2023 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20231110171740/https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-67381267.amp | url-status = live }}</ref>

On 13 November, the newly elected Sejm held its first session. [[Szymon Hołownia]], leader of Poland 2050, was elected [[Marshal of the Sejm]], winning over the incumbent [[Elżbieta Witek]] of PiS.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.politico.eu/article/poland-parliament-speaker-holownia-as-the-opposition-takes-power/ | title = New broom in Poland's parliament as the opposition takes power | author = Wojciech Kość | date = 13 November 2023 | work = [[Politico Europe]] | access-date = 17 November 2023 | archive-date = 17 November 2023 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20231117052437/https://www.politico.eu/article/poland-parliament-speaker-holownia-as-the-opposition-takes-power/ | url-status = live }}</ref> Later that day, on the first meeting of the Senate, former Marshal of the Sejm [[Małgorzata Kidawa-Błońska]] of Civic Coalition was elected [[Marshal of the Senate of the Republic of Poland|Marshal of the Senate]].<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.rp.pl/polityka/art39396661-malgorzata-kidawa-blonska-wybrana-marszalkiem-senatu| title = Małgorzata Kidawa-Błońska wybrana marszałkiem Senatu| language = pl| date = 13 November 2023| access-date = 17 November 2023| work = Rzeczpospolita| archive-date = 17 November 2023| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20231117143355/https://www.rp.pl/polityka/art39396661-malgorzata-kidawa-blonska-wybrana-marszalkiem-senatu| url-status = live}}</ref>

On 27 November, Mateusz Morawiecki was sworn in by President Duda for an unprecedented [[Third Cabinet of Mateusz Morawiecki|third term]] as [[Prime Minister of Poland|Prime Minister]].<ref>{{Cite web
