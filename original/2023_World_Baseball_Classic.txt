The '''2023 World Baseball Classic''' ('''WBC''') was an international professional [[baseball]] tournament and the fifth iteration of the [[World Baseball Classic]]. It began on March 8, 2023, and ran until March 21.

It was originally scheduled to take place in 2021, four years after the [[2017 World Baseball Classic|previous event]], but was canceled in May 2020, as a result of the [[Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on sports|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name="Canceled">{{cite web |title=2021 World Baseball Classic canceled; event may not return until 2023, report says |url=https://www.cbssports.com/mlb/news/2021-world-baseball-classic-canceled-event-may-not-return-until-2023-report-says/ |website=CBS Sports |date=May 11, 2020 |access-date=February 3, 2021 |archive-date=November 26, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201126103656/https://www.cbssports.com/mlb/news/2021-world-baseball-classic-canceled-event-may-not-return-until-2023-report-says/ |url-status=live }}</ref> It was later announced that [[2023 World Baseball Classic qualification|qualifications]] for the Classic would start in September 2022, as agreed by [[World Baseball Classic Inc.]] (WBCI)<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.cbssports.com/mlb/news/hall-of-famer-mike-piazza-to-manage-italy-when-world-baseball-classic-returns-in-2023/amp/ | title=Hall of Famer Mike Piazza to manage Italy when World Baseball Classic returns in 2023 | access-date=May 19, 2022 | archive-date=May 19, 2022 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220519100548/https://www.cbssports.com/mlb/news/hall-of-famer-mike-piazza-to-manage-italy-when-world-baseball-classic-returns-in-2023/amp/ | url-status=live }}</ref> The tournament expanded from 16 to 20 national teams, with all teams that participated in the [[2017 World Baseball Classic|2017 edition]] automatically qualifying, plus four additional spots.<ref name=expanded>{{cite news |title=Qualifiers set for expanded 2021 World Baseball Classic |url=https://www.afp.com/en/news/824/qualifiers-set-expanded-2021-world-baseball-classic-doc-1oh6yy1 |accessdate=January 29, 2020 |agency=Agence France-Presse |date=January 28, 2020 |archive-date=January 29, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200129084728/https://www.afp.com/en/news/824/qualifiers-set-expanded-2021-world-baseball-classic-doc-1oh6yy1 |url-status=live }}</ref> Unlike in [[2009 World Baseball Classic rosters|2009]], [[2013 World Baseball Classic rosters|2013]] and [[2017 World Baseball Classic rosters|2017]], the U.S. lineup attracted some distinguished players and attracted significant attention for the tournament.<ref name="guardian lineup">{{cite news|title=Why is the US finally treating the World Baseball Classic like a World Cup?|date=March 6, 2023|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2023/mar/06/why-is-the-us-finally-treating-the-world-baseball-classic-like-a-world-cup|access-date=March 23, 2023|archive-date=March 23, 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230323063148/https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2023/mar/06/why-is-the-us-finally-treating-the-world-baseball-classic-like-a-world-cup|url-status=live}}</ref>

Japan won their record-extending third title after defeating defending champions United States 3–2 in the [[2023 World Baseball Classic championship|championship game]], becoming the second team since the [[Dominican Republic national baseball team|Dominican Republic]] in the [[2013 World Baseball Classic]] to win the WBC with an undefeated record. [[Shohei Ohtani]] was named the World Baseball Classic [[Most valuable player|Most Valuable Player]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=World Baseball Classic: Shohei Ohtani awarded WBC MVP after closing out championship in Japan's win over USA |url=https://www.cbssports.com/mlb/news/world-baseball-classic-shohei-ohtani-awarded-wbc-mvp-after-closing-out-championship-in-japans-win-over-usa/ |access-date=March 22, 2023 |website=CBSSports.com |date=March 22, 2023 |language=en |archive-date=March 22, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230322031240/https://www.cbssports.com/mlb/news/world-baseball-classic-shohei-ohtani-awarded-wbc-mvp-after-closing-out-championship-in-japans-win-over-usa/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

In January 2020, the WBC announced that the 16 national teams which participated at the [[2017 World Baseball Classic]] would automatically qualify for the 2023 tournament.<ref name="sportbusiness">{{cite web|url=https://www.sportbusiness.com/news/world-baseball-classic-to-expand-to-20-teams-for-2021-edition/|title=World Baseball Classic to expand to 20 teams for 2021 edition|date=January 30, 2020|first=Adam|last=Nelson|publisher=Sportbusiness.com|access-date=January 31, 2020|archive-date=January 31, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200131084811/https://www.sportbusiness.com/news/world-baseball-classic-to-expand-to-20-teams-for-2021-edition/|url-status=live}}</ref>

A qualifying tournament was scheduled for March 2020 in [[Tucson, Arizona]], [[United States]], to determine the last four teams.<ref name="sportbusiness"/> Twelve teams were split into two pools, and the top two teams in each pool would qualify.<ref name=expanded/> On March 12, 2020, Major League Baseball announced that the qualifying tournaments were being postponed due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.mlb.com/press-release/press-release-major-league-baseball-to-delay-2020-opening-day-by-at-least-two-we | title=Major League Baseball to delay 2020 Opening Day by at least two weeks | work=MLB.com Press Release | date=March 12, 2020 | accessdate=May 19, 2020 | archive-date=May 19, 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200519095637/https://www.mlb.com/press-release/press-release-major-league-baseball-to-delay-2020-opening-day-by-at-least-two-we | url-status=live }}</ref> The qualification tournament ended up postponing to September 16–21, 2022, for the [[2023 World Baseball Classic qualification#Pool A|Africa/Europe qualifiers]] and September 30 – October 5, 2022, for the [[2023 World Baseball Classic qualification#Pool B|Americas/Asia/Oceania qualifiers]].<ref>{{cite web |title=2023 World Baseball Classic qualification |url=https://www.mlb.com/world-baseball-classic/qualifiers |publisher=[[Major League Baseball]] |access-date=March 6, 2023 |archive-date=March 6, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230306234314/https://www.mlb.com/world-baseball-classic/qualifiers |url-status=live }}</ref>

The [[Czech Republic national baseball team|Czech Republic]], [[Great Britain national baseball team|Great Britain]], and [[Nicaragua national baseball team|Nicaragua]] made their first appearance in the World Baseball Classic, while [[Panama national baseball team|Panama]] returned after having missed out on two World Baseball Classic appearances. This was the third consecutive time that [[South Africa national baseball team|South Africa]], the only African team, did not qualify for the World Baseball Classic, and the second consecutive time that both [[Brazil national baseball team|Brazil]] and [[Spain national baseball team|Spain]] did not qualify. With Panama qualified for the World Baseball Classic for the first time since [[2009 World Baseball Classic|2009]], South Africa now has the longest active WBC appearance drought at 14 years, having not qualified since 2009.

===Draw===

The pool draw was announced by [[World Baseball Classic Inc.]] (WBCI) on July 7, 2022.<ref>{{cite web |title=World Baseball Classic 2023 venues, pools announced |url=https://www.mlb.com/news/world-baseball-classic-2023-dates-pools-revealed |publisher=[[Major League Baseball]] |access-date=March 6, 2023 |archive-date=July 8, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220708210601/https://www.mlb.com/news/world-baseball-classic-2023-dates-pools-revealed |url-status=live }}</ref> Organizers prioritized placement in separate pools of the four nations which reached the semifinals of the 2017 WBC (Japan, the Netherlands, Puerto Rico, and the United States) and the three hosts (Japan, the United States, and Taiwan). Remaining pool assignments were made based on [[WBSC World Rankings]], competitive balance, and commercial and geographic interest.<ref>{{cite web |title=2023 World Baseball Classic rules and regulations |url=https://www.mlb.com/news/world-baseball-classic-rules-and-regulations-2023 |website=MLB.com |access-date=February 8, 2023 |archive-date=February 7, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230207133128/https://www.mlb.com/news/world-baseball-classic-rules-and-regulations-2023 |url-status=live }}</ref>

===Attendance===
The first round of the tournament drew 1,010,999 fans across all four venues, nearly double the previous record for the WBC.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://sports.yahoo.com/world-baseball-classic-more-than-1-million-fans-attended-first-round-games-shattering-previous-record-182210957.html |title=World Baseball Classic: More than 1 million fans attended first-round games, shattering previous record |date=March 17, 2023 |access-date=March 25, 2023 |archive-date=March 23, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230323100829/https://sports.yahoo.com/world-baseball-classic-more-than-1-million-fans-attended-first-round-games-shattering-previous-record-182210957.html |url-status=live }}</ref> This included 361,976 fans in Tokyo for Pool B and 295,850 fans in Miami for Pool D. Total attendance for the tournament across all rounds was 1,306,414, the highest in WBC history.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/maurybrown/2023/03/23/from-record-breaking-television-attendance-and-merchandise-sales-2023-world-baseball-classic-was-a-home-run/ |title=From Record-Breaking Television, Attendance And Merchandise Sales, 2023 World Baseball Classic Was A Home Run |website=[[Forbes]] |access-date=March 25, 2023 |archive-date=March 25, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230325122254/https://www.forbes.com/sites/maurybrown/2023/03/23/from-record-breaking-television-attendance-and-merchandise-sales-2023-world-baseball-classic-was-a-home-run/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Prize money==
