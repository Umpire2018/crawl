The '''2024 Central European floods''' were a series of floods caused by a record heavy rainfall generated by Storm Boris, an extremely humid [[Genoa low]]. The flooding began in [[Austria]] and the [[Czech Republic]], then spread to [[Poland]], [[Romania]] and [[Slovakia]], and then onwards to [[Germany]] and [[Hungary]]. As of 28 September 2024, 27 fatalities have been reported. Munich Re estimates the total damage to have been ~4.2 billion euros ($4.3 billion) of which ~1.9 billion euros ($2.1 billion) were insured.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Climate change is showing its claws: The world is getting hotter, resulting in severe hurricanes, thunderstorms and floods {{!}} Munich Re |url=https://www.munichre.com/en/company/media-relations/media-information-and-corporate-news/media-information/2025/natural-disaster-figures-2024.html |access-date=2025-01-09 |website=www.munichre.com |language=en}}</ref>

=== Poland ===
[[File:Prudnik,_2024.09.14_38.jpg|thumb|left|Overflowing river in [[Prudnik]], Poland, 14 September]]

Some places saw more than {{cvt|400|mm|in}} of torrential rainfall, accompanied by thunderstorms and tornadoes. Boris struck [[Opole Voivodeship]] and [[Lower Silesian Voivodeship|Lower Silesia]], leading to flooding on 14–16 September. Polish tennis players [[Iga Świątek]] and [[Hubert Hurkacz]] assisted in recovery efforts for the flooding in their country of birth, with Hurkacz pledging to donate 100 euros for every [[Ace (tennis)|ace]] he served.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Davidov |first=Liya |date=September 25, 2024 |title=#AcesForAid: Hubert Hurkacz to donate 100 Euros for every ace to Poland after recent flooding |url=https://www.tennis.com/baseline/articles/acesforaid-hubert-hurkacz-to-donate-100-euros-for-every-ace-to-poland-after-recent-flooding |access-date=September 26, 2024 |website=[[Tennis.com]]}}</ref>

In January 2025, [[Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Bratislava|Archdiocese of Bratislava]] contributed 171,700 [[Euro|Euros]] to home and infrastructure renovations in Czech [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Ostrava-Opava|Diocese of Ostrava-Opava]]. The sum was raised as a part of ''Svätomartinská dobročinná zbierka'' ([[Martin of Tours|St. Martin]] charity fundraising) on 10 November 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |last=www.tkkbs.sk |title=Výťažok Svätomartinskej zbierky vo výške 171 700 eur pomôže rodinám postihnutým povodňami |url=https://www.tkkbs.sk/view.php?cisloclanku=20250225090 |access-date=2025-02-26 |website=www.tkkbs.sk}}</ref>
