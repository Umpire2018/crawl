Parliamentary elections were held in [[Croatia]] on 17 April 2024 to elect the members of the 11th [[Sabor]]. Prior to the elections, the government consisted of a coalition of the [[Croatian Democratic Union]] and [[Independent Democratic Serb Party]], with parliamentary support of five [[national minority]] MPs, two MPs from the [[Croatian Social Liberal Party]] and [[Croatian Demochristian Party]], and one independent MP, [[Silvano Hrelja]].

The ruling centre-right HDZ won the most seats for the fourth straight election, defeating the centre-left [[Rivers of Justice]] alliance. The right-wing populist [[Homeland Movement (Croatia)|Homeland Movement]] finished third, with the left-wing and green [[We can! (Croatia)|We can!]] in fourth place. The HDZ subsequently formed a right-wing coalition with the Homeland Movement and returned HDZ leader [[Andrej Plenković]] as Prime Minister for a third term.<ref>{{cite news |title=Croatia's Ruling Party Reaches Coalition Deal With Right Wing|url=https://www.barrons.com/news/croatia-conservatives-reach-coalition-deal-with-right-wing-spokesman-f0697844 |work=Barron's |date=May 8, 2024|author=Lajla Veselica}}</ref>

== Background ==
The pre-election period was marked by speculation about the possible date of the election, potential coalitions and unification of the opposition, as well as accusations of corruption and nepotism in the government led by Prime Minister [[Andrej Plenković]] and his changes in ministers.

=== Changes of ministers in the government ===
Plenković's two governments were marked by many changes in ministerial positions, a total of 30 ministers. Of its initial lineup in 2016, only [[Nina Obuljen Koržinek]] ([[Ministry of Culture and Media (Croatia)|Minister of Culture and Media]]), the Deputy Prime Minister [[Oleg Butković]] ([[Ministry of Maritime Affairs, Transport and Infrastructure|Minister of the Sea, Transport and Infrastructure]]), and the Deputy Prime Minister [[Tomo Medved]] ([[Ministry of Croatian Veterans|Minister of Croatian Veterans]]) remained.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |last=Deželić |first=Vanja |date=12 December 2023 |title=Ovo su svi ministri koji su otišli ili ih je Andrej Plenković smijenio |trans-title=These are all the ministers who left or were dismissed by Andrej Plenković |url=https://n1info.hr/vijesti/ovo-su-svi-ministri-koji-su-otisli-ili-ih-je-andrej-plenkovic-smijenio/ |access-date=18 February 2024 |website=N1 |language=hr |archive-date=18 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240218192556/https://n1info.hr/vijesti/ovo-su-svi-ministri-koji-su-otisli-ili-ih-je-andrej-plenkovic-smijenio/ |url-status=live }}</ref> [[The Bridge (Croatia)|The Bridge]] ministers [[Vlaho Orepić]] ([[Ministry of the Interior (Croatia)|Minister of the Interior]]), [[Ante Šprlje]] ([[Ministry of Justice and Public Administration (Croatia)|Minister of Justice]]), and [[Slaven Dobrović]] ([[Ministry of Environmental Protection and Energy (Croatia)|Minister of Environmental Protection and Energy]]) were the first to leave the [[Cabinet of Andrej Plenković I|government]]. They were dismissed by Plenković for refusing to reject the vote of no confidence in [[Zdravko Marić]] ([[Ministry of Finance (Croatia)|Finance Minister]]) requested by the opposition. Immediately afterwards, fourth The Bridge member [[Ivan Kovačić]] ([[Ministry of Public Administration (Croatia)|Minister of Administration]]) resigned from the government, as did [[Pavo Barišić]] ([[Ministry of Science and Education (Croatia)|Minister of Education]]) who was facing accusations of plagiarism which were later dismissed by the [[University of Augsburg]].<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1">{{Cite web |last=Srzić |first=Ante |date=13 December 2023 |title=Od neposlušnih mostovaca do zbunjenog Filipovića: Tko je sve odletio iz Plenkovićeve vlade |trans-title=From disobedient Most ministers to confused Filipović: Who flew away from Plenković's government |url=https://www.tportal.hr/vijesti/clanak/od-neposlusnih-mostovaca-do-zbunjenog-filipovica-tko-je-sve-odletio-iz-plenkoviceve-vlade-foto-20231213 |access-date=18 February 2024 |website=tportal.hr |archive-date=18 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240218191054/https://www.tportal.hr/vijesti/clanak/od-neposlusnih-mostovaca-do-zbunjenog-filipovica-tko-je-sve-odletio-iz-plenkoviceve-vlade-foto-20231213 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Scandals occurred in 2018, which began with the Borg affair that led to the departure of Economy Minister [[Martina Dalić]] and her replacement by [[Darko Horvat (politician)|Darko Horvat]], who was incarcerated in [[Remetinec prison]] in 2022 after being charged with illegally awarding 2.6 million Kuna.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> [[Davor Ivo Stier]] ([[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Croatia)|Minister of Foreign Affairs]]) resigned after a disagreement with the party leadership, and his successor [[Marija Pejčinović Burić]] left in 2019 after being elected [[Secretary General of the Council of Europe]]. In 2019, five ministers left the government following a series of scandals. [[Lovro Kušćević]] (Minister of Construction and Spatial Planning) after it was found that he conducted illegal real estate brokerage while he was the mayor of [[Nerežišća]] on [[Brač]], [[Goran Marić (politician)|Goran Marić]] (Minister of State Property) resigned after a series of real estate scandals. An indictment against [[Gabrijela Žalac]] ([[Ministry of Regional Development and EU Funds (Croatia)|Minister of Regional Development and European Union Funds]]) was brought for abuse of position and authority in the "Software affair", and her name was also mentioned in the Vjetroelektrana affair with [[Josipa Rimac]]. [[Tomislav Tolušić]] ([[Ministry of Agriculture (Croatia)|Minister of Agriculture]]) was mentioned in numerous scandals and was later indicted for embezzlement with EU funds, including 2.5 million Kuna for his winery, which he received under irregular circumstances.<ref name=":1" /> [[Nada Murganić]] ([[Ministry of Labour and Pension System, Family and Social Policy|Minister of Demography, Family, Youth and Social Policy]]) was dismissed for unsatisfactory performance.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |last=Ivandić |first=Ivana |date=31 December 2019 |title=Smjena Nade Murganić i povećanje porodiljnih naknada obilježili su socijalnu politiku |trans-title=The dismissal of Nada Murganić and the increase in maternity benefits marked social policy |url=https://www.tportal.hr/vijesti/clanak/smjena-nade-murganic-i-povecanje-porodiljnih-naknada-obiljezili-su-socijalnu-politiku-20191231 |access-date=18 February 2024 |website=tportal.hr |language=hr |archive-date=18 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240218191056/https://www.tportal.hr/vijesti/clanak/smjena-nade-murganic-i-povecanje-porodiljnih-naknada-obiljezili-su-socijalnu-politiku-20191231 |url-status=live }}</ref>

More controversies occurred in 2020, with [[Milan Kujundžić]] ([[Ministry of Health (Croatia)|Minister of Health]]) leaving the government following a series of real estate scandal. [[Damir Krstičević]] ([[Ministry of Defence (Croatia)|Minister of Defence]]) resigned after the crash of a military training plane in which two members of the [[Croatian Air Force]] died near Biljana Donje near [[Zadar]]. He was replaced by [[Mario Banožić]], who left after committing manslaughter while driving irresponsibly.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> [[Marko Pavić]] was removed as Ministry of Regional Development and EU Funds, along with [[Gari Cappelli]] ([[Ministry of Tourism and Sports (Croatia)|Minister of Tourism]]).<ref name=":0" /> [[Vesna Bedeković]] was replaced as Minister for Demography, Family, Youth and Social Policy by [[Josip Aladrović]], who himself was under investigation by [[USKOK]] and also resigned.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> [[Nataša Tramišak]] was replaced as Minister of Regional Development and European Union Funds by [[Šime Erlić]] as part of Plenković's personal intentions. [[Ivan Paladina]], Minister of Spatial Planning, Construction and State Property, was removed due to the slow pace of reconstruction after [[2020 Zagreb earthquake|Zagreb]] and [[2020 Petrinja earthquake|Petrinja earthquakes]].<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" />

=== Election date ===
In 2023, it was reported that the HDZ was preparing to hold elections in April 2024, two months before the [[2024 European Parliament election|elections for members of the European Parliament]]. From conversations with several high-ranking HDZ members, ''[[Jutarnji list]]'' reported that that Andrej Plenković would collect advantages with which he will go before the voters and ask for another prime ministerial mandate in the next year, so that the elections can be held before the [[Easter]] holidays in 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 January 2023 |title=HDZ se priprema za parlamentarne izbore u ožujku 2024. |trans-title=HDZ is preparing for parliamentary elections in March 2024. |url=https://dubrovacki.slobodnadalmacija.hr/dubrovnik/vijesti/hrvatska-i-svijet/hdz-se-priprema-za-parlamentarne-izbore-u-ozujku-2024-1259610 |access-date=26 January 2023 |website=Dubrovački vjesnik |language=hr |archive-date=26 January 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230126091826/https://dubrovacki.slobodnadalmacija.hr/dubrovnik/vijesti/hrvatska-i-svijet/hdz-se-priprema-za-parlamentarne-izbore-u-ozujku-2024-1259610 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Electoral system==
{{Main|Elections in Croatia#Parliamentary Elections|}}
[[File:Constituencies of Croatia (2023-present).svg|thumb|300x300px|Electoral districts in use from 2023]]
The 151 members of the [[Croatian Parliament]] are elected from 10 geographical and two special electoral districts:<ref>[http://www.ipu.org/parline-e/reports/2077_B.htm Electoral system] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160113112025/http://www.ipu.org/parline-e/reports/2077_B.htm |date=13 January 2016 }} IPU</ref>
*140 seats are elected in ten geographical 14-seat electoral districts ([[Croatian Parliament electoral districts|1st–10th Electoral Districts]]) by [[open list]] [[proportional representation]] (using a 5% [[electoral threshold]]) with seats allocated using the [[d'Hondt method]]
**A voter can give their "preference vote" to a single candidate on the list, but only candidates who have received at least 10% of the party's votes take precedence over the other candidates on the list.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.zakon.hr/z/355/Zakon-o-izborima-zastupnika-u-Hrvatski-sabor | title=Zakon o izborima zastupnika u Hrvatski sabor (Act on Election of Representatives to the Croatian Parliament) | language=hr | access-date=27 August 2018 | archive-date=28 August 2018 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180828035834/https://www.zakon.hr/z/355/Zakon-o-izborima-zastupnika-u-Hrvatski-sabor | url-status=live }}</ref>
*3 seats are elected in a special electoral district ([[Electoral district XI (Croatian Parliament)|11th Electoral District]]) for Croatian citizens [[Croatian diaspora|living abroad]]
*8 seats are elected from an electoral district for national minorities ([[Electoral district XII (Croatian Parliament)|12th Electoral District]]): 3 seats for Serbian, 1 seat for Italian, 1 seat for Hungarian, 1 seat for Czech and Slovak, 1 seat for Albanian, Bosniak, Macedonian, Montenegrin and Slovenian, and 1 seat for Austrian, Bulgarian, German, Jewish, Polish, Roma, Romanian, Rusyn, Russian, Turkish, Ukrainian and Vlach national minority
**Voters with the right to vote in the 12th Electoral District can choose to either vote for a candidate list on the ballot in the district they belong to according to their place of residence (one of the 10 geographical districts) or for a candidate of their minority in the 12th Electoral District.<ref>{{cite web |title=Zakon o izborima zastupnika u Hrvatski sabor (pročišćeni tekst) |url=https://www.zakon.hr/z/355/Zakon-o-izborima-zastupnika-u-Hrvatski-sabor |website=zakon.hr |access-date=2 April 2024 |archive-date=28 August 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180828035834/https://www.zakon.hr/z/355/Zakon-o-izborima-zastupnika-u-Hrvatski-sabor |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 28 October 2022, the [[Constitutional Court of Croatia|Constitutional Court]] issued a warning that it could declare the next elections unconstitutional unless the electoral law was changed, due to excessive differences in the number of voters for each constituency, leading to significant variations in vote weight.<ref>{{Cite web |date=27 October 2022 |title=Novo upozorenje Ustavnog suda: 'Ne bude li promjena izbornih jedinica, sljedeći izbori mogli bi biti proglašeni neustavnima' |trans-title=New warning from the Constitutional Court: 'If electoral units are not changed, the next }}</ref> On 7 February 2023, the Constitutional Court repealed the electoral law because the difference in vote weight between different electoral districts was too large to comply with the constitutional provision that each vote must be of equal weight.<ref>{{Cite web |date=7 February 2023 |title=Ustavni sud ukinuo Zakon o izbornim jedinicama: "Ovo nije nikakva hajka" |trans-title=The === Submission of candidate lists ===
Lists for the election of representatives may be proposed independently by one political party or by two or more political parties (coalition list) which are registered in the Republic of Croatia on the day the Decision to call the elections is announced, or by voters. Voters shall propose independent candidate lists on the basis of validly collected 500 signatures of residents of the Electoral District the list is running in.<ref>{{cite web |title=Elections of representatives to the Croatian Parliament |url=https://www.izbori.hr/site/en/about-elections-referenda/elections-of-representatives-to-the-croatian-parliament/1724 |website=izbori.hr |publisher=State Electoral Commission of the Republic of Croatia |access-date=2 April 2024 |archive-date=2 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240402234732/https://www.izbori.hr/site/en/about-elections-referenda/elections-of-representatives-to-the-croatian-parliament/1724 |url-status=live }}</ref>

===Female representation===
It is required by the election law that each list running in the election contains at least 40% of both men and women, to ensure equality. For the lists that do not adhere to this rule, the State Electoral Commission (DIP) will report them to The Ombudsperson for Gender Equality and the [[State's Attorney Office of the Republic of Croatia]] (DORH) when the parties and candidates proposing those lists can expect a fine of up to 33 000 [[euros]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Zakon o izborima zastupnika u Hrvatski sabor |trans-title=The Elections of representatives to the Croatian Parliament Act |url=https://www.zakon.hr/z/355/Zakon-o-izborima-zastupnika-u-Hrvatski-sabor |access-date=13 April 2024 |work=[[Narodne Novine]] |publisher=zakon.hr |date=1 January 2020 |language=Croatian |archive-date=28 August 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180828035834/https://www.zakon.hr/z/355/Zakon-o-izborima-zastupnika-u-Hrvatski-sabor |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Strankama koje neće imati dovoljno žena na listama prijeti i do 33.000 eura kazne |url=https://n1info.hr/vijesti/strankama-koje-nece-imati-dovoljno-zena-na-listama-prijeti-i-do-33-000-eura-kazne/ |website=n1info.hr |date=27 March 2024 |access-date=13 April 2024 |archive-date=9 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240409024622/https://n1info.hr/vijesti/strankama-koje-nece-imati-dovoljno-zena-na-listama-prijeti-i-do-33-000-eura-kazne/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

Thirty-two out of 165 lists did not respect the rule and will be reported. A number of the main parties respected the rule and have at least six women and men on all their lists, among them Focus/Republic (11 lists), MOST/HS (11), RF (10), the Rivers of Justice coalition (10) and the Our Croatia coalition (eight). On the other hand, the biggest offenders are HDZ, DP/PiP coalition and MP Karolina Vidović Krišto's party OiP. For both HDZ and OiP, the fine could be high as much as 60 000 euros all together. ==Results==
The election produced a fourth consecutive victory for the governing [[HDZ]]; however, the opposition parties denied it a one-party majority in the Sabor. The official results showed that HDZ's coalition won 61 seats, almost 20 seats more than the second placed [[Rivers of Justice]] coalition whose 42 candidates were elected.<ref name="apresult">{{Cite web |date=18 April 2024 |title=Croatia's ruling conservatives win parliamentary vote, but cannot rule alone |url=https://apnews.com/article/croatia-election-european-union-plenkovic-milanovic-b3a93b2598f60ebfdf663815dda2c0ad |work=Associated Press |url-status=live |access-date=18 April 2024 |archive-date=18 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240418044737/https://apnews.com/article/croatia-election-european-union-plenkovic-milanovic-b3a93b2598f60ebfdf663815dda2c0ad }}</ref> Despite HDZ's plurality of votes, in order to form a governing majority, they still required to rely on forming a coalition, with the most likely partners the third-placed far-right party, the [[Homeland Movement (Croatia)|Homeland Movement]], which won 14 seats.<ref name="HR elect 24 US News">{{cite news|url=https://www.usnews.com/news/world/articles/2024-04-17/croatia-votes-in-a-parliamentary-election-thats-a-showdown-between-its-president-and-prime-minister|title=Croatia's Ruling Conservatives Win Parliamentary Vote, but Cannot Rule Alone|publisher=US News|author=[[Associated Press]]|date=17 April 2024|access-date=23 April 2024|archive-date=17 April 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240417063939/https://www.usnews.com/news/world/articles/2024-04-17/croatia-votes-in-a-parliamentary-election-thats-a-showdown-between-its-president-and-prime-minister|url-status=live}}</ref> The Homeland Movement therefore has the [[kingmaker]] position,<ref name="HR elect 24 US News" /> although it declined to say whether they would join a left-leaning or right-leaning bloc and setting the condition that they will not enter into a coalition with [[Independent Democratic Serb Party|SDSS]] and [[We can! (Croatia)|Možemo]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Sprečić |first=Elvis |last2=Josipović |first2=Tatjana |last3=Grgin |first3=Matea |date=17 April 2024 |title=Penava dočekan ovacijama: 'SDSS u niti jednoj varijanti i ni pod kojim uvjetima. Ni stranka Možemo' |trans-title=Penava was greeted with a standing ovation: 'SDSS in no variant and under no conditions. Neither the We Can Party' |url=https://www.vecernji.hr/vijesti/uzivo-iz-stozera-domovinskog-pokreta-1762400 |access-date=25 April 2024 |website=Večernji list |language=hr |archive-date=25 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240425163535/https://www.vecernji.hr/vijesti/uzivo-iz-stozera-domovinskog-pokreta-1762400 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Among the other opposition parties, conservative [[The Bridge (Croatia)|The Bridge]] along with partners [[Croatian Sovereignists]] won 11 seats, and the green-left [[We can! (Croatia)|We Can!]] who won 10, both declared after the vote they intended to start talks with potential allies aimed at putting together some form of majority administration.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2024/apr/18/croatia-election-results-hdz-democratic-union-pm-andrej-plenkovic-without-majority-sdp-homeland-movement|date=18 April 2024|title=Political uncertainty in Croatia as ruling party wins most seats but no majority|work=[[The Guardian]]|author=Jon Henley|access-date=23 April 2024|archive-date=22 April 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240422190025/https://www.theguardian.com/world/2024/apr/18/croatia-election-results-hdz-democratic-union-pm-andrej-plenkovic-without-majority-sdp-homeland-movement|url-status=live}}</ref> The latter party also encouraged all smaller parties regardless of political leanings to come to an agreement in order to oust HDZ from power. The [[Workers' Front (Croatia)|Workers' Front]] failed to achieve the 5% [[electoral threshold]], leaving the party without a representative in the [[Sabor]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.vecernji.hr/vijesti/potop-radnicke-fronte-katarina-peovic-nije-presla-izborni-prag-1762451 |title=Potop Radničke fronte: Katarina Peović nije prešla izborni prag |trans-title=Descent of the Workers' Front: Katarina Peović did not cross the electoral threshold |newspaper=[[Večernji list]] |date=17 April 2024 |access-date=20 April 2024 |archive-date=20 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240420100557/https://www.vecernji.hr/vijesti/potop-radnicke-fronte-katarina-peovic-nije-presla-izborni-prag-1762451 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Aftermath==
[[Andrej Plenković]] claimed victory on behalf of the [[Croatian Democratic Union|HDZ]] and said that it would begin the process of forming a new parliamentary majority to form the HDZ's third government on 18 April 2024. He also congratulated other contesting parties.<ref name="apresult"/> [[Social Democratic Party of Croatia|SDP]] leader [[Peđa Grbin]] said that they had hoped for a better result but said that they would also start talks on the formation of a new government on 18 April.<ref>{{Cite web |date=18 April 2024 |title=Ruling HDZ party wins most seats, but no majority in Croatia election |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/4/17/croatia-ruling-party-heading-for-election-win-without-majority-exit-poll |work=Al Jazeera |url-status=live |access-date=18 April 2024 |archive-date=17 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240417210612/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/4/17/croatia-ruling-party-heading-for-election-win-without-majority-exit-poll }}</ref> During negotiation talks, the [[Homeland Movement (Croatia)|DP]] was labeled the kingmaker of the election.<ref>{{cite web |title=Croatia ruling party races to form coalition after election win |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/croatian-ruling-party-wins-polls-prepares-talks-forming-government-2024-04-18/ |website=[[Reuters]] |access-date=19 April 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Orovic |first1=Joe |last2=Higgins |first2=Andrew |title=A Far-Right Party Emerges as a Possible Kingmaker in Croatia |work=The New York Times |date=18 April 2024 |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2024/04/18/world/europe/croatia-election-far-right.html |access-date=19 April 2024 |archive-date=18 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240418164648/https://www.nytimes.com/2024/04/18/world/europe/croatia-election-far-right.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 19 April, the [[Constitutional Court of Croatia|Constitutional Court]] ruled that President Zoran Milanović was ineligible to become prime minister in the event that the SDP would be able to form a government, citing his statements and behavior during the electoral campaign. According to the ruling, he would be barred from being given the mandate to form a new government even if he previously resigned as President.<ref>{{cite web |title=Croatia's top court rules President Milanović cannot be prime minister because of campaign |url=https://apnews.com/article/croatia-election-president-constitution-milanovic-court-sdp-7cd3da5839c0e641a113a6f9d8b31a08 |website=[[Associated Press]] |date=19 April 2024 |access-date=19 April 2024 |archive-date=19 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240419204151/https://apnews.com/article/croatia-election-president-constitution-milanovic-court-sdp-7cd3da5839c0e641a113a6f9d8b31a08 |url-status=live }}</ref> Three of the Constitutional Court judges released a [[dissenting opinion]] pointing out there are no specific arguments or regulations to justify the decision. They cited "unconstitutional threats" of the Court and criticized the potential undermining of the will of the people.<ref>{{cite web |title=Troje sudaca protiv odluke o Milanoviću: "Ovo je prijetnja novom Saboru da paze što rade" |url=https://dnevnik.hr/vijesti/hrvatska/objavljeno-izdvojeno-misljenje-sudaca-ustavnog-suda---844058.html |website=dnevnik.hr |publisher=Nova TV |access-date=19 April 2024 |language=Croatian |date=19 April 2024 |archive-date=19 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240419204150/https://dnevnik.hr/vijesti/hrvatska/objavljeno-izdvojeno-misljenje-sudaca-ustavnog-suda---844058.html |url-status=live }}</ref> Milanović gave a statement in front of a photo showing "HDZ's man" [[Miroslav Šeparović]], the Court's President, hanging out together with HDZ ministers [[Oleg Butković]] and [[Branko Bačić]], accusing the Court of preparing a [[coup d'état]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Milanović šefu Ustavnog suda odgovorio fotografijom: "Pogledajte kako HDZ donosi odluke tzv. As one of the scenarios for the formation of a new government, Grbin presented the idea of an anti-corruption government. This would imply the constitution of a Parliament in which, at least temporarily, the majority will be all opposition parties (Možemo!, Rivers of Justice, IDS, Fokus, Most, and DP). Such a parliamentary majority could pass several important laws, including the electoral one, as well as annul the appointment of State Attorney Ivan Turudić. After that, in the event that the parties could not agree on the formation of a joint government, new elections could be held.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Škaro |first=Katarina |date=20 April 2024 |title=Grbin iznio detalje novog plana za Sabor. On 21 April, elections were repeated in two polling stations. The first was in [[Sračinec]], where an excess of ballots was found in the ballot boxes. The irregularity referred to the list of candidates in the [[Electoral district XII (Croatian Parliament)|XII electoral unit]] where representatives of the Albanian, Bosniak, Montenegrin, Macedonian and Slovenian national minorities were elected. The second polling station was in [[Markušica]] in the village of [[Ostrovo, Croatia|Ostrovo]], where it was discovered that by mistake 83 persons of Serbian nationality voted twice, namely for the [[Electoral district V (Croatian Parliament)|V electoral unit]] and for the candidates of the XII electoral unit of the Serbian national minority.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Vištica |first=Sanja |date=20 April 2024 |title=Ponavljaju se izbori u dva mjesta: Poništeni su zbog velikih nepravilnosti |trans-title=Elections are repeated in two places: They were annulled due to major irregularities |url=https://dnevnik.hr/vijesti/hrvatska/gradjani-ce-ponovno-na-izbore-saznajte-zasto-u-prilogu-sanje-vistice---844155.html?itm_source=TopNews&itm_medium=Dnevnik&itm_campaign=Naslovnica |access-date=21 April 2024 |website=Dnevnik.hr |language=hr |archive-date=21 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240421112051/https://dnevnik.hr/vijesti/hrvatska/gradjani-ce-ponovno-na-izbore-saznajte-zasto-u-prilogu-sanje-vistice---844155.html?itm_source=TopNews&itm_medium=Dnevnik&itm_campaign=Naslovnica |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 27 April, the DP held negotiation talks with both the HDZ and SDP, with Prime Minister [[Andrej Plenković]] announcing that the HDZ would form a formal coalition with the DP some time in "the coming days."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Badovinac |first1=Nikola |title=Homeland Movement meets with HDZ and SDP |url=https://glashrvatske.hrt.hr/en/politics/homeland-movement-meets-with-hdz-and-sdp-11506517 |website=glashrvatske.hrt.hr |access-date=2 May 2024 |archive-date=2 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240502125523/https://glashrvatske.hrt.hr/en/politics/homeland-movement-meets-with-hdz-and-sdp-11506517 |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 29 April, the State Electoral Commission published the final results, setting a deadline to 19 May for holding the first session of the Sabor with president Milanović deciding the exact date.<ref>{{Cite web |date=29 April 2024 |title=Objavljeni su konačni izborni rezultati: Počeo teći važan rok od 20 dana |trans-title=The final election results have been published: An important period of 20 days has begun |url=https://dnevnik.hr/vijesti/hrvatska/konacni-izborni-rezultati-parlamentarni-izbori-2024---845518.html?itm_source=SpecialNews&itm_medium=Dnevnik&itm_campaign=Naslovnica |access-date=29 April 2024 |website=Dnevnik.hr |language=hr}}</ref>

On 5 May, an agreement was reached on the HDZ and DP government, with Plenković as prime minister.
