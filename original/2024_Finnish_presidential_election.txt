Presidential elections were held in [[Finland]] on 28 January 2024, with a second round held on 11 February.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Schedule for the 2024 presidential election |url=https://vaalit.fi/en/schedule-in-the-presidential-election |access-date=2023-06-21 |website=Vaalit |archive-date=21 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230621122448/https://vaalit.fi/en/schedule-in-the-presidential-election |url-status=live }}</ref> Voters elected a [[President of Finland|president of the Republic]] for a six-year term. Incumbent president [[Sauli Niinistö]] was term-limited and ineligible to run for re-election, having served the maximum two terms, ensuring that the president elected would be the country's thirteenth.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Näkökulma: Punavihreä vs. porvari – onko jompikumpi heistä Suomen seuraava presidentti? |url=https://www.iltalehti.fi/politiikka/a/e64b3f46-fba1-48ab-be01-fac0d9261387 |access-date=2021-04-13 |website=Iltalehti |language=fi |archive-date=13 April 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210413165211/https://www.iltalehti.fi/politiikka/a/e64b3f46-fba1-48ab-be01-fac0d9261387 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Had the new president been elected in the first round on 28 January by winning more than half of valid votes cast, their term would have begun on 1 February. However, as no candidate received a majority of votes, [[Alexander Stubb]] and [[Pekka Haavisto]] contested in a runoff on 11 February with Stubb winning 51.6% to Haavisto's 48.4%. The result in the second round was the closest in the history of presidential elections in Finland, followed by the [[2000 Finnish presidential election|2000 presidential runoff]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Teivainen |first=Aleksi |date=2024-02-11 |title=Stubb beats Haavisto in Finland's closest-ever presidential election |url=https://www.helsinkitimes.fi/finland/finland-news/politics/24888-stubb-beats-haavisto-in-finland-s-closest-ever-presidential-election.html |access-date=2024-02-13 |website=Helsinki Times |language=en-gb |archive-date=11 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240211223637/https://www.helsinkitimes.fi/finland/finland-news/politics/24888-stubb-beats-haavisto-in-finland-s-closest-ever-presidential-election.html |url-status=live }}</ref> Stubb began his term as president on 1 March.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Schedule in the Presidential Election |url=https://vaalit.fi/en/schedule-in-the-presidential-election |access-date=2024-01-27 |website=Vaalit |language=en-US |archive-date=21 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230621122448/https://vaalit.fi/en/schedule-in-the-presidential-election |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Background==
The presidential election was the first in Finland since it joined the [[North Atlantic Treaty Organization]] (NATO) in 2023 in response to the [[Russian invasion of Ukraine]], ending decades of [[Finlandization|neutrality in foreign policy]]. The decision was supported by the outgoing president [[Sauli Niinistö]], whose constitutional position also makes him the commander-in-chief of the [[Finnish Defence Forces]] and gives him responsibility for defence and foreign policy affairs. Niinistö, who is the only Finnish president to have been elected with an outright majority (62.7 percent) in the first round of voting in a direct popular election during his reelection in 2018, enjoyed over 90 percent popularity ratings since 2021. He rejected efforts by a citizen movement to allow him to run for a third term.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Psaropoulos |first=John |date=2024-01-26 |title='Voters are looking for a mini-Niinisto': Finland braces for new president |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/1/26/voters-are-looking-for-a-mini-niinisto-finland-braces-for-new-president |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240126125646/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/1/26/voters-are-looking-for-a-mini-niinisto-finland-braces-for-new-president |archive-date=2024-01-26 |access-date=2024-01-28 |work=Al Jazeera |language=en-US}}</ref> An estimated 4.5 million people are eligible to vote in the 2024 election.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Finns go to the polls Sunday to elect a new president at a time of increased tension with Russia |url=https://apnews.com/article/finland-presidential-election-nato-russia-security-9cd07b3674b97f588b576404d1101cfa |access-date=2024-01-28 |website=Associated Press |date=26 January 2024 |language=en-US |archive-date=28 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240128023519/https://apnews.com/article/finland-presidential-election-nato-russia-security-9cd07b3674b97f588b576404d1101cfa |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Candidates ==
According to the [[Constitution of Finland]], the president must be a natural-born Finnish citizen.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2019 |title=Constitution of Finland |url=https://www.finlex.fi/en/laki/kaannokset/1999/en19990731_20180817.pdf |access-date=2024-01-27 |website=Finlex |archive-date=14 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231114111441/https://finlex.fi/en/laki/kaannokset/1999/en19990731_20180817.pdf |url-status=live }}</ref> In order to vote in the election, the person must be a [[Finnish nationality law|Finnish citizen]] and at least 18 years of age.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Right to Vote and Compilation of the Voting Register |url=https://vaalit.fi/en/right-to-vote-and-compilation-of-the-voting-register2 |access-date=2024-01-27 |website=Vaalit |language=en-US |archive-date=27 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240127132232/https://vaalit.fi/en/right-to-vote-and-compilation-of-the-voting-register2 |url-status=live }}</ref> A candidate may be nominated by a registered political party that has secured at least a single representative in the parliament in the preceding parliamentary election, by a constituency association that has gathered at least 20 000 signatures from eligible voters, or both. The whole country acts as a single constituency.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Eligibility and Nomination of Candidates |url=https://vaalit.fi/en/eligibility-and-nomination-of-candidates3 |access-date=2024-01-27 |website=Vaalit |language=en-US |archive-date=14 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240214154949/https://vaalit.fi/en/eligibility-and-nomination-of-candidates3 |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Campaign ==

=== Mika Aaltola ===
[[Mika Aaltola]], the director of [[Finnish Institute of International Affairs]], announced his candidacy on 3 August 2023.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20043730 | title=Mika Aaltola pyrkii presidentiksi: "Ajan Suomen etua, se on tärkeä erottava tekijä muihin ehdokkaisiin" – ei vastannut kritiikkiin panttaamisesta | date=April 3, 2023 | accessdate=April 4, 2023 | website=Yle | archive-date=3 August 2023 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230803143631/https://yle.fi/a/74-20043730 | url-status=live }}</ref> He is running as an independent candidate, and therefore must assemble 20,000 signatures from qualified voters to establish a constituency association in order to run for the presidency. Aaltola emerged as a surprise candidate in a presidential survey published by [[Yle]] on 28 March 2022.<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://yle.fi/a/3-12372173 | title = Konkarit jyräävät Ylen presidenttikyselyssä: Olli Rehn nousi kärkeen, Sanna Marin putosi kolmanneksi | date = March 28, 2022 | website = Yle | access-date = 21 June 2023 | archive-date = 21 June 2023 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20230621124207/https://yle.fi/a/3-12372173 | url-status = live }}</ref> On 27 May 2022, similar results were obtained from a survey conducted by ''[[Ilta-Sanomat]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000008841320.html|title=Yllätysnimi teki presidenttikyselyssä uskomattoman nousun – tutkija: "En muista uraltani vastaavaa"|date=May 27, 2022|website=Ilta-Sanomat|access-date=21 June 2023|archive-date=21 June 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230621123534/https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000008841320.html|url-status=live}}</ref> Aaltola commented that he was humbled by the results and that "In a situation where the candidates would solely represent [[Finlandization]] and behind-the-scenes dealings, I would feel a calling to attempt the impossible for the benefit of my homeland. Therefore, my stance would entirely depend on the candidate lineup."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000008843939.html|title=Mika Aaltola kommentoi kannatustaan presidentinvaaligallupissa: Vetää mielen nöyräksi|date=May 27, 2022|website=Ilta-Sanomat|access-date=21 June 2023|archive-date=21 June 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230621132433/https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000008843939.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

In a subsequent survey published by ''Ilta-Sanomat'' on 21 October 2022, Aaltola had unexpectedly risen to the top.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000009148729.html|title=IS:n presidenttikyselyssä yllätysykkönen: "Voi pyhä jysäys"|date=October 21, 2022|website=Ilta-Sanomat|access-date=23 October 2022|archive-date=23 October 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221023113607/https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000009148729.html|url-status=live}}</ref> After the results, Aaltola stated: "There is a one percent chance that I will become the President of Finland." He did not completely rule out his candidacy and stated that he would announce a possible candidacy at a later time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.is.fi/kotimaa/art-2000009152055.html|title=Mika Aaltola Ylellä: Yhden prosentin todennäköisyys, että minusta tulee presidentti|date=October 22, 2022|website=Ilta-Sanomat|access-date=21 June 2023|archive-date=21 June 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230621132433/https://www.is.fi/kotimaa/art-2000009152055.html|url-status=live}}</ref> On 31 October 2022, Aaltola tweeted: "I am not currently running for any position. My focus is on the ongoing war and the geopolitical challenges of the winter."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/MikaAaltola/status/1586978267969511424|title=Mika Aaltola's Twitter page|date=October 31, 2022|website=Twitter|access-date=21 June 2023|archive-date=17 March 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230317193245/https://twitter.com/MikaAaltola/status/1586978267969511424|url-status=live}}</ref> On 4 November 2022, ''[[Helsingin Sanomat]]'' published a comprehensive personal profile and interview with Aaltola. Many of the sources interviewed in the article noted that Aaltola no longer behaves like an expert but rather speaks like a preacher or a politician.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hs.fi/sunnuntai/art-2000009154696.html|title=Mikä Aaltola|date=November 4, 2022|website=Helsingin Sanomat|access-date=21 June 2023|archive-date=1 June 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230601192756/https://www.hs.fi/sunnuntai/art-2000009154696.html|url-status=live}}</ref> The same day Aaltola tweeted, that "I have no intention of running for the presidential elections."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/MikaAaltola/status/1588910113292967937|title=Mika Aaltola's Twitter page|date=November 5, 2022|website=Twitter|access-date=21 June 2023|archive-date=5 November 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221105235335/https://twitter.com/MikaAaltola/status/1588910113292967937|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 11 April 2023, Aaltola tweeted that "My support in presidential surveys remains persistently encouraging, even though I have stated a practical "no" on multiple occasions. This was based on the fact that it is practically impossible [to win] outside of political parties. Should I reconsider the matter in the autumn if the support persists?"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/MikaAaltola/status/1645697789177868288|title=Mika Aaltola's Twitter page|date=April 11, 2023|website=Twitter|access-date=21 June 2023|archive-date=4 June 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230604200218/https://twitter.com/MikaAaltola/status/1645697789177868288|url-status=live}}</ref> In the presidential poll commissioned by Yle in May 2023, Aaltola was the favorite as the next president of around 13% of the respondents.<ref name="Yle poll may 2023" /> On 10 October 2023 Mika Aaltola's support association announced that they had collected 25,000 endorsement cards for Aaltola, exceeding the required 20,000 endorsement card threshold for candidacy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hs.fi/politiikka/art-2000009906263.html|title=Mika Aaltola keräsi kannattajakortteja yli tarvittavan määrän|date=October 10, 2023|website=Helsingin Sanomat|access-date=10 October 2023|archive-date=11 October 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231011025403/https://www.hs.fi/politiikka/art-2000009906263.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Sari Essayah ===

=== Pekka Haavisto ===
[[Pekka Haavisto]], former Minister of Foreign Affairs and [[Parliament of Finland|Member of Parliament]] for [[Green League|the Green League]], announced his candidacy on 8 June 2023. Haavisto has run for the office twice before, in [[2012 Finnish presidential election|2012]] and [[2018 Finnish presidential election|2018]], both times finishing second behind the incumbent President [[Sauli Niinistö]]. Haavisto is running as an independent candidate in the presidential elections. The collection of signatures in support of Haavisto began immediately on the same day he announced his candidacy.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pekka Haavisto announces 2024 presidential bid|url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20035789|date=June 8, 2023|access-date=June 8, 2023|work=[[Yle]]|archive-date=8 June 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230608094153/https://yle.fi/a/74-20035789|url-status=live}}</ref>

According to multiple polls, Haavisto is currently the leading candidate for the presidency. He is the most popular candidate among women and younger voters, as well as across party lines. In the presidential poll commissioned by Yle in May 2023, Haavisto was the favorite as the next president of around 28% percent of the respondents.<ref name="Yle poll may 2023">{{Cite web |title=Ylen presidenttikysely: Haavisto on ykkösnimi ja nuorten suosikki, koko kolmen kärki lujitti etumatkaansa |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20031583 |date=May 16, 2023 |work=Yle |access-date=21 June 2023 |archive-date=21 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230621152509/https://yle.fi/a/74-20031583 |url-status=live }}</ref> In a presidential survey published by Ilta-Sanomat on 13 June 2023, which aimed to determine the potential result of a hypothetical second round in the presidential elections, Haavisto emerged as the winner when compared to all the other major potential candidates.<ref>{{Cite web |title=IS:n presidenttikysely: He pärjäisivät Haavistoa vastaan |url=https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000009650402.html |date=June 13, 2023 |work=Ilta-Sanomat |access-date=21 June 2023 |archive-date=21 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230621134450/https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000009650402.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 3 October 2023, Haavisto's campaign announced that they had so far collected 48,000 endorsement cards, exceeding the required 20,000 endorsement card threshold for candidacy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000009897895.html|title=Pekka Haavisto sai kasaan 48,000 kannattajakorttia – nappasi presidenttikampanjansa johtoon yllätysnimien kokoomuksesta|date=October 3, 2023|website=Ilta-Sanomat|access-date=10 October 2023|archive-date=4 October 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231004015408/https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000009897895.html|url-status=live}}</ref> Should Haavisto win the election, he would be the first openly gay person to become President of Finland.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/1/28/finns-head-to-the-polls-to-pick-new-president |title=Stubb narrowly wins first round of Finland's presidential election |date=January 29, 2024 |website=Al Jazeera |access-date=29 January 2024 |archive-date=29 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240129030009/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/1/28/finns-head-to-the-polls-to-pick-new-president |url-status=live }}</ref>

=== Jussi Halla-aho ===
[[Jussi Halla-aho]], the [[Speaker of the Parliament of Finland]] and a Member of Parliament for the [[Finns Party]], announced his candidacy on 7 July 2023.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000009704981.html|title=Jussi Halla-aho pyrkii presidentiksi – "Olen käytettävissä"|date=July 7, 2023|access-date=July 7, 2023|website=Ilta-Sanomat|archive-date=7 July 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230707162728/https://www.is.fi/politiikka/art-2000009704981.html|url-status=live}}</ref> His candidacy was confirmed by the party congress in August.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20044987|title=Jussi Halla-aho toivoo tiedotusvälineiden perkaavan presidenttiehdokkaiden aiempia lausuntoja Venäjästä|date=August 13, 2023|access-date=August 17, 2023|publisher=Yle|archive-date=17 August 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230817002318/https://yle.fi/a/74-20044987|url-status=live}}</ref> In a survey conducted by Uutissuomalainen in January 2023 among party delegates, over 80 percent of respondents considered Halla-aho the most suitable presidential candidate for the party. In a similar survey conducted in summer 2021, Halla-aho ranked behind [[Laura Huhtasaari]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.iltalehti.fi/politiikka/a/f3ec473d-685c-40de-9b46-ee945cc43f2a|title=USU: Lähes 80 prosenttia PS-valtuutetuista haluaa Halla-ahon presidenttiehdokkaaksi – Urpilainen ja Stubb puolueidensa suosikit|date=February 11, 2023|website=Iltalehti|access-date=22 June 2023|archive-date=20 March 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230320000959/https://www.iltalehti.fi/politiikka/a/f3ec473d-685c-40de-9b46-ee945cc43f2a|url-status=live}}</ref> In the presidential poll commissioned by Yle in May 2023, Halla-aho was the favorite as the next president of around 6% of the respondents.<ref name="Yle poll may 2023"/>

===Expenditure===
Financial disclosures released after the election showed that Alexander Stubb's campaign team spent 2.7 million euros during the election period and received 1.2 million euros in private donations, 610,000 euros from businesses and around 710,000 euros from the National Coalition Party. Pekka Haavisto's campaign was found to have spent 1.9 million and received about 1.3 million euros from individual donors, 310,000 euros from the corporate sector, and 160,000 euros from the Green League.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20083862 |title=Stubb's presidential campaign cost €2.7m |date=17 April 2024 |publisher=Yle |access-date=17 April 2024 }}</ref>

==Electoral issues==
The electoral campaign has mostly been dominated by international and security issues that have risen since the country's accession to NATO in 2023, particularly relations with Russia.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://lemonde.fr/en/international/article/2024/01/28/finnish-presidential-election-faced-with-russia-security-takes-center-stage_6472397_4.html |title=Finnish presidential election: Faced with Russia, security takes center stage |work=Le Monde |date=28 January 2024 |access-date=3 February 2024 |language=en |archive-date=3 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240203060805/https://www.lemonde.fr/en/international/article/2024/01/28/finnish-presidential-election-faced-with-russia-security-takes-center-stage_6472397_4.html |url-status=live }}</ref> All nine candidates in the first round expressed hard stances on relations with Moscow, with Jussi Halla-aho and Alexander Stubb supporting the idea of revoking Finnish citizenship from dual Russian-Finnish nationals. Halla-aho also supported pushing back migrants at the [[Finnish-Russian border]] in response to gatherings of migrants to the area that were suspected to have been orchestrated by the Russian government.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/finlands-presidential-hopefuls-vie-offer-toughest-stance-russia-2024-01-25/ |title=Finland's presidential hopefuls vie to offer toughest stance on Russia |work=Reuters |date=28 January 2024 |access-date=3 February 2024 |language=en |archive-date=25 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240125122636/https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/finlands-presidential-hopefuls-vie-offer-toughest-stance-russia-2024-01-25/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Pekka Haavisto supported the closure of the border with Russia, but expressed concern about how the influx of migrants has been used to justify rhetoric against asylum seekers.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2024/jan/16/pekka-haavisto-finland-must-be-united-against-external-threats-presidential-candidate |title=Finland must crack down on hate speech, says presidential hopeful |work=The Guardian |date=25 January 2024 |access-date=3 February 2024 |language=en |archive-date=2 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240202225551/https://www.theguardian.com/world/2024/jan/16/pekka-haavisto-finland-must-be-united-against-external-threats-presidential-candidate |url-status=live }}</ref> Stubb also expressed support for the deployment of nuclear weapons in Finland, while Haavisto objected to the idea as well as that of the [[Treaty of Paris (1856)|remilitarization]] of [[Åland]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20072688 |title=How do Pekka Haavisto and Alexander Stubb differ? |work=Yle |date=2 February 2024 |access-date=3 February 2024 |language=en |archive-date=2 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240202210325/https://yle.fi/a/74-20072688 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In response to an ongoing massive strike that began during the election campaign, Stubb distanced himself from his National Coalition Party's support for employers and employees' freedom to settle disputes locally and said he would not interfere with labor market issues as president. Haavisto said he would try to bring the parties together for behind-the-scenes negotiations.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://apnews.com/article/finland-presidential-election-runoff-stubb-haavisto-5827256bccbff2e435263b63c5a89d8d |title=As Finns choose a new president, one thing is certain: A hard line on Russia will continue |work=Associated Press |date=9 February 2024 |access-date=9 February 2024 |language=en |archive-date=9 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240209071755/https://apnews.com/article/finland-presidential-election-runoff-stubb-haavisto-5827256bccbff2e435263b63c5a89d8d |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Disinformation==
In the days leading up to the vote, some Finns Party supporters accused the public broadcaster [[Yle]] of being biased against Jussi Halla-aho, prompting Yle's CEO to respond that "Yle does not campaign against or for anyone." Several of Halla-aho's rivals also defended the broadcaster, with Jutta Urpilainen describing the allegations as the "international trend of right-wing populists questioning elections for their own gain arriving in Finland as well", and Pekka Haavisto saying that there were no "signs of outside interference despite concerns regarding that". Dimitri Qvintus, a Social Democrat party official and spokesperson for former prime minister Sanna Marin, said that the allegations "would fit perfectly with the new Finns Party playbook" of trying to promote doubt in the election results in the event that Halla-aho loses the election, adding that the government should have immediately addressed these claims.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.euronews.com/2024/01/28/finland-votes-in-tight-presidential-election-amid-hybrid-operation-claims |title=Finland votes in tight presidential election amid 'hybrid operation' claims |date=28 January 2024 |website=Euronews |access-date=28 January 2024 |archive-date=28 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240128132838/https://www.euronews.com/2024/01/28/finland-votes-in-tight-presidential-election-amid-hybrid-operation-claims |url-status=live }}</ref>

Fake posters appeared in [[Trams in Helsinki|tram stops]] in [[Helsinki]], in which images of Jussi Halla-aho were altered to include phrases such as "Fascist Halla-aho" and "Finland must be a safe space for Nazis." An altered poster of Haavisto was also displayed with a falsely-attributed quote.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20070529 |title=Fake election posters pop up at Helsinki tram stops |date=22 January 2024 |website=Yle |access-date=28 January 2024 |archive-date=28 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240128134406/https://yle.fi/a/74-20070529 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Voting==
===First round===
Early voting opened on 17 January,<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-01-28 |title=New NATO member Finland set to pick president after 12 years of Niinisto |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/1/24/new-nato-member-finland-set-to-pick-president-after-12-years-of-niinisto |access-date=2024-01-28 |website=Al Jazeera |language=en |archive-date=28 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240128052434/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/1/24/new-nato-member-finland-set-to-pick-president-after-12-years-of-niinisto |url-status=live }}</ref> with turnout estimated at 44 percent,<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-01-28 |title=Record advance voting numbers could slow down the count |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20071620 |access-date=2024-01-28 |website=Yle |language=en |archive-date=28 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240128030849/https://yle.fi/a/74-20071620 |url-status=live }}</ref> or about 1.88 million votes,<ref name="open">{{Cite web |date=2024-01-28 |title=Finns head to the polls; presidential voting turnout could set record |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20071703 |access-date=2024-01-28 |website=Yle |language=en |archive-date=28 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240128111559/https://yle.fi/a/74-20071703 |url-status=live }}</ref> an increase from the 36.7 percent recorded in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-01-24 |title=Advance voters set record in Finland's presidential election |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20071162 |access-date=2024-01-28 |website=Yle |language=en |archive-date=28 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240128134406/https://yle.fi/a/74-20071162 |url-status=live }}</ref> This continued until 23 January. Voting at overseas polling stations that were set up in Finnish diplomatic missions was conducted from 17 to 20 January,<ref name=guide/> during which 58,757 out of an estimated 263,000 eligible Finnish citizens abroad voted, an increase of 6,253 from 2018.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-01-28 |title=Nearly 60k Finns vote in presidential election at missions around the world |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20071393 |access-date=2024-01-28 |website=Yle |language=en |archive-date=28 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240128112130/https://yle.fi/a/74-20071393 |url-status=live }}</ref> In the first round of voting on 28 January, polling stations opened at 09:00,<ref name="open" /> and closed at 20:00.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Schedule for the 2024 presidential election |url=https://vaalit.fi/en/schedule-in-the-presidential-election |access-date=2024-01-28 |website=Vaalit |language=en |archive-date=21 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230621122448/https://vaalit.fi/en/schedule-in-the-presidential-election |url-status=live }}</ref> Initial results are expected at around midnight, while official results are expected to be confirmed on 30 January.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-01-28 |title=Finns go to the polls to elect a new president at an unprecedented time for the NATO newcomer |url=https://apnews.com/article/finland-presidential-election-cd3d168b50c72b0437f90c3c757a80e4 |access-date=2024-01-28 |website=Associated Press |language=en |archive-date=28 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240128111745/https://apnews.com/article/finland-presidential-election-cd3d168b50c72b0437f90c3c757a80e4 |url-status=live }}</ref>

===Second round===
For the runoff, early voting was held from 31 January to 6 February while overseas voting ran from 31 January to 3 February.<ref name=guide>{{Cite web |date=2024-01-28 |title=A really simple guide to Finland's 2024 presidential election |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20068250 |access-date=2024-01-28 |website=Yle |language=en |archive-date=28 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240128053008/https://yle.fi/a/74-20068250 |url-status=live }}</ref> Turnout in advanced voting reached 46 percent, or around 1.96 million voters.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-02-07 |title=Nearly half of Finns vote early in presidential election second round |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20073365 |access-date=2024-02-07 |website=Yle |language=en |archive-date=7 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240207162638/https://yle.fi/a/74-20073365 |url-status=live }}</ref> In the second round of voting on 11 February, polling stations opened at 09:00 and closed at 20:00. The result of the second round were confirmed on 14 February.<ref name=confirmed-results>{{Cite web |date=2024-02-14 |title=Confirmed results of the presidential election now available online |url=https://vaalit.fi/-/1410853/presidentinvaalin-vahvistettu-tulos-nyt-verkossa?languageId=en_US |access-date=2024-02-14 |website=Vaalit |language=en |archive-date=14 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240214181546/https://vaalit.fi/-/1410853/presidentinvaalin-vahvistettu-tulos-nyt-verkossa?languageId=en_US |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Aftermath==
[[File:Inauguration of President Alexander Stubb; Helsinki, Finland, 2024 March – 01.jpg|thumb|Inauguration of the President]]
[[File:Tasavallan presidentin virkaanastujaiset 1.3.2024 - 43.jpg|thumb|Inauguration of the President]]
Following the result of the first round, [[Alexander Stubb]] said: "We have at this stage gotten the kind of result for which we can be grateful and humble." [[Pekka Haavisto]] described his showing as "a really great result." [[Jussi Halla-aho]] said that he had to be "satisfied" with the result, noting that it had been "better" than what he had expected. It was the Finns party's best presidential election result in history. [[Olli Rehn]] expressed thanks to the voters. [[Mika Aaltola]] said that he was happy that foreign and security policy were on the electoral agenda but said that it would take some time before he would "take on something like this again." Rehn, [[Sari Essayah]], and Harry Harkimo declined to endorse any candidate in the runoff, with Essayah saying that "citizens can vote according to their preference," and Harkimo saying that both Stubb and Haavisto "have the same opinions about everything."<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-01-29 |title=As it happened: Stubb and Haavisto head for second round run-off in Finnish presidential election |url=https://yle.fi/a/74-20071743 |access-date=2024-01-29 |website=Yle |language=en |archive-date=29 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240129031612/https://yle.fi/a/74-20071743 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Following the result of the second round, Haavisto conceded defeat to Stubb and publicly shook hands with him at [[Helsinki City Hall]]. In response, Stubb expressed appreciation and pride at having been "able to run" with him.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-02-12 |title=Centre-right Alexander Stubb elected Finland's next president |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/2/11/centre-right-stubb-leads-finlands-presidential-vote-in-early-results |access-date=2024-02-12 |website=Al Jazeera |language=en |archive-date=12 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240212034549/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/2/11/centre-right-stubb-leads-finlands-presidential-vote-in-early-results |url-status=live }}</ref> He also called his victory as "the greatest honour" of his life.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-02-12 |title=Conservative ex-PM Alexander Stubb elected Finland president |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-68270804 |access-date=2024-02-12 |website=BBC |language=en |archive-date=12 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240212034938/https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-68270804 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Reactions==
Ukrainian president [[Volodymyr Zelenskyy]] congratulated Stubb on his victory and said that he was looking forward "to advancing our relations and our shared vision of a free, united, and well-defended Europe".<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-02-12 |title=Former PM Alexander Stubb wins Finnish presidency, narrowly defeating ex-top diplomat Pekka Haavisto |url=https://apnews.com/article/finland-presidential-election-runoff-stubb-haavisto-545f7942ec9855152c4d9ac8dc840c10 |access-date=2024-02-12 |website=Associated Press |language=en |archive-date=11 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240211115126/https://apnews.com/article/finland-presidential-election-runoff-stubb-haavisto-545f7942ec9855152c4d9ac8dc840c10 |url-status=live }}</ref>
