{{Politics of Indonesia}}
Presidential elections were held in [[Indonesia]] on 14 February 2024 with defence minister and former general [[Prabowo Subianto]] contesting the elections against the former governor of Jakarta, [[Anies Baswedan]] and the former governor of [[Central Java]], [[Ganjar Pranowo]]; incumbent president [[Joko Widodo]] was constitutionally barred from seeking a third term in office.

On 20 March, the [[General Elections Commission (Indonesia)|General Elections Commission]] (KPU) announced Prabowo's victory, having received over 96 million votes. Prabowo and his vice-presidential candidate, [[Gibran Rakabuming]], were [[Inauguration of Prabowo Subianto|sworn in on 20 October 2024.]]<ref>{{Cite web |title=Indonesia Decides: 2024 Elections |url=https://www.thejakartapost.com/election-2024 |access-date=2023-06-14 |website=The Jakarta Post |language=en |archive-date=3 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230603045413/https://www.thejakartapost.com/election-2024 |url-status=live }}</ref>

The presidential election was held together with the [[2024 Indonesian general election|legislative election]] for members of the [[House of Representatives (Indonesia)|House of Representatives]] (DPR), the [[Regional Representative Council|Senate]] (DPD), provincial legislative councils (DPRD Provinsi), and regency or municipal legislative councils (DPRD Kabupaten or DPRD Kota) throughout Indonesia.

The elevation of Prabowo Subianto to the presidency prompted concerns from scholars and observers about potential democratic backsliding in Indonesia.<ref>{{Cite journal |last=Slater |first=Dan |date=2024 |title=Indonesia's High-Stakes Handover |url=https://muse.jhu.edu/pub/1/article/922832 |journal=Journal of Democracy |volume=35 |issue=2 |pages=40–51 |issn=1086-3214}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Pepinsky |first=Thomas |date=2024 |title=Why Indonesia’s Democracy Is in Danger |url=https://www.journalofdemocracy.org/online-exclusive/why-indonesias-democracy-is-in-danger/ |website=Journal of Democracy |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Pepinsky |first=Thomas |date=2024 |title=Indonesia's election reveals its democratic challenges |url=https://www.brookings.edu/articles/indonesias-election-reveals-its-democratic-challenges/ |website=Brookings |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Tharoor |first=Ishaan |date=2024-02-14 |title=The world’s third-biggest democracy could be sliding backward |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2024/02/14/indonesia-elections-democracy-backsliding-prabowo-widodo/ |work=Washington Post |language=en-US |issn=0190-8286}}</ref>

== Background ==
[[File:Logo Pemilu 2024.svg|thumb|2024 presidential election promotional logo]]
The 2014 and 2019 presidential elections were contested by the same two candidates. These are [[Prabowo Subianto]], who was nominated by parties supporting the [[Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono]] administration, and [[Joko Widodo]], who was mostly supported by the opposition and subsequently won.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nomor urut pilpres: Prabowo-Hatta satu, Jokowi-JK dua|url=https://www.bbc.com/indonesia/berita_indonesia/2014/06/140601_pilpres_nomor_urut|work=BBC|language=id-ID|date=2014-06-01|access-date=2023-09-09|archive-date=2023-06-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230628102357/https://www.bbc.com/indonesia/berita_indonesia/2014/06/140601_pilpres_nomor_urut|url-status=live}}</ref> Jokowi won [[2019 Indonesian general election|reelection in 2019]] by winning 55.5% of the votes.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://pilpres.tempo.co/read/1207538/jokowi-menang-di-21-provinsi-ini-daftar-lengkap-rekapitulasi-kpu/full&view=ok|title=Jokowi Menang di 21 Provinsi, Ini Daftar Lengkap Rekapitulasi KPU|author=Nurita|publisher=Tempo.co|date=2019-05-21|accessdate=2019-05-21|work=[[Tempo.co]]|first=Dewi}}{{dead link|date=November 2023}}</ref>

The [[President of Indonesia]] is [[Direct election|directly elected]] every five years. As stipulated in Article 7 of the 1945 Constitution of the Republic of Indonesia, incumbent President Joko Widodo is ineligible to run for a third term. The presidential election was required to be held before the end of his current term on 20 October 2024.

In the 2019 election, Joko Widodo was re-elected with [[Ma'ruf Amin]] as his running mate. He chose his opponent in the election, Prabowo Subianto, as [[Ministry of Defense (Indonesia)|Minister of Defense]] after a reconciliation meeting in July 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sah! Prabowo Subianto Jadi Menteri Pertahanan|first1=Bangun|last1=Santoso|first2=Ummi|last2=Hadyah Saleh|url=https://www.suara.com/news/2019/10/23/083557/sah-prabowo-subianto-jadi-menteri-pertahanan|website=Suara.com|language=id-ID|date=2019-10-23|access-date=8 September 2023|archive-date=2023-09-08|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230908165643/https://www.suara.com/news/2019/10/23/083557/sah-prabowo-subianto-jadi-menteri-pertahanan|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Pertemuan Jokowi - Prabowo: Ucapan selamat Prabowo dan ajakan rekonsiliasi Jokowi|url=https://www.bbc.com/indonesia/indonesia-48910427|website=BBC|language=id-ID|date=2019-07-13|access-date=8 September 2023|archive-date=2023-09-08|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230908165639/https://www.bbc.com/indonesia/indonesia-48910427|url-status=live}}</ref> The meeting, which ended the political feud between both men, also resulted in [[Gerindra Party]] and the [[National Mandate Party]] (PAN) in joining the governing [[Onward Indonesia Coalition]] (Koalisi Indonesia Maju).<ref>{{Cite web |date=22 December 2020 |title=Jokowi Resmi Tunjuk Sandiaga Uno Jadi Menteri Parekraf |url=https://www.cnnindonesia.com/ekonomi/20201222102011-532-585069/jokowi-resmi-tunjuk-sandiaga-uno-jadi-menteri-parekraf |access-date=8 September 2023 |work=CNN Indonesia |language=id-ID |location=Jakarta |archive-date=11 September 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230911041258/https://www.cnnindonesia.com/ekonomi/20201222102011-532-585069/jokowi-resmi-tunjuk-sandiaga-uno-jadi-menteri-parekraf |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Electoral system ==
The general election period is regulated in Article 6A and Article 22E of the [[Constitution of Indonesia|Constitution of the Republic of Indonesia]] and by the [[Indonesian electoral law of 2017|Law on General Elections]]. The presidential and vice-presidential candidate pairs are proposed by [[List of political parties in Indonesia|political parties]] or coalitions of political parties that have at least 20% of the seats in the [[House of Representatives (Indonesia)|House of Representatives]] (DPR) or at least 25% of the national vote from the previous House of Representatives general election. Of the parties in the 2019–2024 DPR, only [[Indonesian Democratic Party of Struggle|PDI-P]] could nominate a pair of candidates without a coalition with other parties, given that the party met the presidential threshold requirement of at least 115 seats in the outgoing DPR."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Fitria Chusna|first=Farisa|date=2022-05-20|title=PDI-P Dapat Usung Capres Sendiri Tanpa Perlu 2023|archive-date=2023-09-08|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230908130903/https://nasional.kompas.com/read/2022/05/20/20543521/pdi-p-dapat-usung-capres-sendiri-tanpa-perlu-koalisi-bagaimana-bisa|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite Some non-parliamentary political parties, both parties participating in the previous election and new parties after 2019, could choose to be supporting parties for a presidential candidate.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Taher |first=Andrian Pratama |date=14 May 2023 |editor-last=Aziz |editor-first=Abdul |title=Peran Partai Non-Parlemen di Tengah Pembentukan Koalisi Parpol A runoff is held if no candidates obtains more than 50% of the votes with at least 20% of the votes in more than half of the [[Provinces of Indonesia|provinces in Indonesia]] in the first round. As of 2024, a runoff has only ever taken place in [[2004 Indonesian presidential election|2004]]. This election is the first election since [[2009 Indonesian presidential election|2009]] to have more than two candidates.

==== Ganjar–Mahfud ====
{{Main|Alliance of Political Parties Supporting Ganjar Pranowo}}
The end of Joko Widodo's presidency in 2024 led the [[Indonesian Democratic Party of Struggle]] (PDI-P) to search for a presidential candidate to replace him. [[Governor of Central Java|Central Java Governor]] (2013‒2023) and former member of the [[House of Representatives (Indonesia)|House of Representatives]] (2004‒2013), [[Ganjar Pranowo]], was nominated for president by PDI-P.<ref name="Emir Yanwardhana">{{Cite web|author=Emir Yanwardhana|date=21 April 2023|title=Sah! Megawati Umumkan Ganjar Pranowo Capres PDIP|url=https://www.cnbcindonesia.com/news/20230421141522-4-431603/sah-megawati-umumkan-ganjar-pranowo-capres-pdip|location=Jakarta|website=CNBC Indonesia|language=id|access-date=21 April 2023|archive-date=3 September 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230903183052/https://www.cnbcindonesia.com/news/20230421141522-4-431603/sah-megawati-umumkan-ganjar-pranowo-capres-pdip|url-status=live}}</ref> His name was announced by PDI-P Chair Megawati.<ref name="Emir Yanwardhana"/> Previously, there were rumours of PDI-P's nomination of [[Puan Maharani]] to be paired with Prabowo as a vice presidential candidate.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2022-09-04|title=Bicara Kemungkinan Berduet Dengan Puan, Prabowo: Ya Pasti Mungkin|url=https://www.viva.co.id/berita/politik/1516966-bicara-soal-kemungkinan-berduet-dengan-puan-prabowo-ya-pasti-mungkin|work=Viva|location=|language=id|access-date=2023-09-09|archive-date=2022-09-05|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220905212530/https://www.viva.co.id/berita/politik/1516966-bicara-soal-kemungkinan-berduet-dengan-puan-prabowo-ya-pasti-mungkin|url-status=live}}</ref> Puan was eventually nominated by the party to become a legislative candidate for the House of Representatives.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2023-05-11|first=M. Julnis|last=Firmansyah|editor=Febriyan|title=Batal Jadi Capres, Puan Maharani Kembali Masuk Daftar Bacaleg PDIP|url=https://nasional.tempo.co/read/1724534/batal-jadi-capres-puan-maharani-kembali-masuk-daftar-bacaleg-pdip|work=Viva|location=Jakarta|language=id|access-date=2023-09-09|archive-date=2023-05-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230523183614/https://nasional.tempo.co/read/1724534/batal-jadi-capres-puan-maharani-kembali-masuk-daftar-bacaleg-pdip|url-status=live}}</ref> In addition, NasDem also nominated Ganjar's name as one of the presidential candidates in its national working meeting, although his nomination was mandated to Anies. [[People's Conscience Party|Hanura]], a non-parliamentary party, also supported Ganjar as a presidential candidate.<ref name="hanuraganjar">{{Cite web|date=2023-04-22|title=Partai Hanura Deklarasikan Dukungan untuk Ganjar sebagai Capres 2024|url=https://nasional.kompas.com/read/2023/04/22/15091841/partai-hanura-deklarasikan-dukungan-untuk-ganjar-sebagai-capres-2024|work=Kompas com|location=Jakarta|language=id|access-date=2023-04-23|archive-date=2023-04-22|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230422173257/https://amp.kompas.com/nasional/read/2023/04/22/15091841/partai-hanura-deklarasikan-dukungan-untuk-ganjar-sebagai-capres-2024|url-status=live}}</ref> Hanura became the first supporting party after Ganjar's nomination by PDI-P that gave birth to political cooperation without a coalition.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.detik.com/pemilu/d-6900422/oso-tegaskan-hanura-pdip-kerja-sama-dukung-ganjar-kita-nggak-ada-koalisi|title=OSO Tegaskan Hanura-PDIP Kerja Sama Dukung Ganjar: Kita Nggak Ada Koalisi|author=Ng, Silivia|date=2023-08-28|work=Detik A few days later, the [[United Development Party]] (PPP) also endorsed Ganjar.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnbcindonesia.com/news/20230430160640-4-433341/pdip-dan-ppp-resmi-kerja-sama-usung-ganjar-di-pilpres-2024|title=PDIP dan PPP Resmi Kerja Sama, Usung Ganjar di Pilpres The two parties also collaborated in the legislative election.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2023-05-29|last=Martiar|first=Norbertus Arya Dwiangga|title=PDI-P dan PPP Pererat Kerja Sama ke Pemilu Afterwards, [[Perindo Party|Perindo]] also nominated Ganjar amid rumours that his party would join the Great Indonesia Awakening Coalition that would later support Prabowo.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2023-04-09|title=PAN, PBB, dan Perindo dukung Prabowo Subianto|url=https://jogja.antaranews.com/berita/610476/pan-pbb-dan-perindo-dukung-prabowo-subianto|work=Antara == General election ==
=== Campaign ===
Prior to joining Joko Widodo's government, [[Prabowo Subianto]] was known for being assertive and expressive as a reflection of his previous profession as a [[Indonesian Army|soldier]]. In each of his presidential campaigns, he criticised Jokowi's policies.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Prabowo Kritik Jokowi: Kerja, Kerja, Kerja Untuk Siapa?|url=https://www.cnnindonesia.com/nasional/20180901190044-32-326832/prabowo-kritik-jokowi-kerja-kerja-kerja-untuk-siapa|work=CNN Indonesia|language=id|date=1 September 2018|access-date=30 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231204055042/https://www.cnnindonesia.com/nasional/20180901190044-32-326832/prabowo-kritik-jokowi-kerja-kerja-kerja-untuk-siapa|url-status=live}}</ref> Likewise, his speeches showed Prabowo's firm stance that positioned himself as part of the opposition to Jokowi.<ref>{{Cite web|title=BPN Sebut Prabowo Gebrak Meja Marah dengan Kondisi Indonesia|url=https://www.cnnindonesia.com/nasional/20190410222421-32-385169/bpn-sebut-prabowo-gebrak-meja-marah-dengan-kondisi-indonesia|work=CNN Indonesia|language=id|date=10 April Afterwards, Prabowo appeared more cooperative, given that he was the [[Minister of Defence]] in the cabinet and made Jokowi's son and Mayor of [[Surakarta]], [[Gibran Rakabuming Raka|Gibran]] as his deputy.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gibran Rakabuming Raka Resmi Ditunjuk Jadi Cawapres Prabowo 2024, they expressed support for Jokowi's policies, including continuing the planning of the [[Nusantara (planned city)|Nusantara Capital City]] as one of the national strategic projects. In his campaign, Prabowo often discussed the programme of distributing food and milk to students.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Prabowo Janjikan Makan Siang, Angkutan Sampai Sekolah Gratis|url=https://www.cnbcindonesia.com/research/20231110135021-130-488063/prabowo-janjikan-makan-siang-angkutan-sampai-sekolah-gratis|work=CNBC Indonesia|language=id|date=10 November 2023|access-date=30 November 2023|archive-date=4 December 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231204055041/https://www.cnbcindonesia.com/research/20231110135021-130-488063/prabowo-janjikan-makan-siang-angkutan-sampai-sekolah-gratis|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2024, [[Anies Baswedan]] became a new rival for Prabowo, who had previously promoted Anies as a candidate for [[Governor of Jakarta]] in [[2017 Jakarta gubernatorial election|2017]], just as Jokowi, who was a rival for Prabowo in the two previous presidential elections, had been promoted by Prabowo as a candidate for governor in [[2012 Jakarta gubernatorial election|2012]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Prabowo: Jokowi dan Ahok Figur Pemimpin Bersih|url=https://nasional.kompas.com/read/2012/03/20/00351949/Prabowo.Jokowi.dan.Ahok.Figur.Pemimpin.Bersih|work=Kompas|language=id|date=20 March ===Debates===
Unlike in previous editions, the 2024 presidential and vice-presidential debates were attended by the candidate pairs.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Aprilia |first=Zefanya |date=2 December 2023 |title=Ini Beda Format Debat Capres-Cawapres Saat Ini dengan di 2019 |url=https://www.cnbcindonesia.com/news/20231202184759-4-494038/ini-beda-format-debat-capres-cawapres-saat-ini-dengan-di-2019 |access-date=21 December 2023 |work=CNBC Indonesia |language=id |location=Jakarta |archive-date=12 December 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231212152731/https://www.cnbcindonesia.com/news/20231202184759-4-494038/ini-beda-format-debat-capres-cawapres-saat-ini-dengan-di-2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> Five rounds of debates were held between the candidates, with participants alternating between the presidential and vice presidential candidates. The first round of debates, between the presidential candidates, was held on 12 December 2023. The second round of debates, between their running mates, was held on 22 December 2023. The third round of debates was held on 7 January 2024. The next two rounds were held on 21 January 2024 and 4 February 2024.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hidayat |first1=Dedi |title=Simak Jadwal, Tema, Stasiun TV Siarkan Debat Capres-Cawapres |url=https://www.rri.co.id/pemilu/478195/simak-jadwal-tema-stasiun-tv-siarkan-debat-capres-cawapres |access-date=22 December 2023 |work=rri.co.id |date=12 December 2023 |language=id |archive-date=22 December 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231222151313/https://www.rri.co.id/pemilu/478195/simak-jadwal-tema-stasiun-tv-siarkan-debat-capres-cawapres |url-status=live }}</ref>

The first debate saw three presidential candidates debating law, bureaucracy, and the democratic system. == Controversies ==
=== Constitutional court and Gibran's candidacy ===
The [[Constitutional Court of Indonesia]] (MK RI) made a controversial exception, allowing Gibran to run for vice president in the 2024 election.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web |date=2023-10-16 |title=Constitutional Court's Ruling Shakes Indonesian Politic |url=https://republika.co.id/share/s2m91o393 |access-date=2023-12-12 |website=Republika Online |language=id |archive-date=2023-12-12 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231212160804/https://republika.co.id/share/s2m91o393 |url-status=live }}</ref> As per the court ruling, candidates under the required age of 40 were eligible to seek the presidency or vice-presidency in the presidential ballot, given a condition that they held elected regional office in the past.<ref name=":3" /> Since Gibran, who is the current mayor of [[Surakarta]], is the son of [[Joko Widodo]], it led to speculation that the President influenced the ruling so that he could continue his legacy. It was also noted that Jokowi's brother-in-law and the [[Chief Justice of the Constitutional Court of Indonesia|Chief Justice]] of the MK, [[Anwar Usman]], was also involved in the ruling.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-03-16 |title=Profil Anwar Usman, Adik Ipar Jokowi yang Terpilih Kembali Jadi Ketua MK |url=https://nasional.kompas.com/read/2023/03/16/07304561/profil-anwar-usman-adik-ipar-jokowi-yang-terpilih-kembali-jadi-ketua-mk |access-date=2023-12-12 |website=KOMPAS.com |language=id |archive-date=2023-12-12 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231212160803/https://nasional.kompas.com/read/2023/03/16/07304561/profil-anwar-usman-adik-ipar-jokowi-yang-terpilih-kembali-jadi-ketua-mk |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=2023-10-17 |title=Joko Widodo's son can run for Indonesian vice-president after controversial court ruling |language=en |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2023/oct/17/joko-widodos-son-can-run-for-indonesian-vice-president-after-controversial-court-ruling |access-date=2023-10-26 |archive-date=2023-10-26 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231026090827/https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2023/oct/17/joko-widodos-son-can-run-for-indonesian-vice-president-after-controversial-court-ruling |url-status=live }}</ref> Anwar Usman was ultimately demoted by the Honorary Assembly of the Constitutional Court from the position of Chief Justice on 8 November 2023 after finding him guilty of conflict of interest on the ruling.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Top judge demoted in Indonesia after ruling favouring president's son |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2023/11/8/top-judge-demoted-in-indonesia-after-ruling-favouring-presidents-son |access-date=2023-12-12 |website=Al Jazeera |language=en |archive-date=2023-12-13 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231213225427/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2023/11/8/top-judge-demoted-in-indonesia-after-ruling-favouring-presidents-son |url-status=live }}</ref>

The KPU was also criticised for accepting Gibran's registration without following the proper procedures after the ruling from MK. Because of this on 5 February 2024, the Election Organization Ethics Council (DKPP) found that KPU chairman Hasyim Asy'ari, and all six of KPU's commissioners were guilty of an ethics violation for allowing Gibran to register his candidacy before the commission had adjusted the age minimum for candidates in its internal regulation.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web |title=KPU found guilty of ethics breach in handling of Gibran VP bid |url=https://www.thejakartapost.com/indonesia/2024/02/05/kpu-found-guilty-of-ethics-breach-in-handling-of-gibran-vp-bid.html |access-date=2024-02-11 |website=The Jakarta Post |language=en |archive-date=2024-02-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240205161437/https://www.thejakartapost.com/indonesia/2024/02/05/kpu-found-guilty-of-ethics-breach-in-handling-of-gibran-vp-bid.html |url-status=live }}</ref> Hasyim Asy'ari in particular received a "final stern warning" from the DKPP while the other six only received a "stern warning".<ref name=":4" /> Hasyim received that particular warning was due to the fact that Hasyim has already collected two stern warnings from DKPP due to ethical breaches committed in March and April 2023.<ref name=":4" />

A lawsuit was filed by the Indonesian Democracy Defenders (TPDI) and the Indonesian Advocates Movement (Perekat Nusantara) against Joko Widodo, Gibran Rakabuming, Anwar Usman and First Lady [[Iriana]] alleging nepotism and political dynasty on the part of the respondents, but was dismissed by the Jakarta State Administrative Court a day before the election.<ref>{{Cite web |date=February 15, 2024 |title=Court Dismisses Lawsuit against Jokowi over Political Dynasty, Nepotism |url=https://en.tempo.co/read/1833785/court-dismisses-lawsuit-against-jokowi-over-political-dynasty-nepotism |access-date=2024-02-15 |website=Tempo |language=en |archive-date=15 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240215144822/https://en.tempo.co/read/1833785/court-dismisses-lawsuit-against-jokowi-over-political-dynasty-nepotism |url-status=live }}</ref>

=== Social media usage and disinformation ===
Prabowo Subianto's campaign was noted for its efforts at rehabilitating his image from his association with human rights violations during the dictatorship of former President [[Suharto]] into a "cute and cuddly" (''gemoy'') grandfather figure among the youth, particularly first-time voters, going as far as to make an animated avatar of him on [[TikTok]] using [[artificial intelligence]]. Anies Baswedan's and Ganjar Pranowo's campaign also used interactive AI [[chatbots]] to engage with voters.<ref name="suharto"/><ref name="rfi">{{cite news|date=14 February 2024|title=Indonesia votes for president with ex-general Subianto the favourite|language=en|work=RFI|url=https://www.rfi.fr/en/international-news/20240213-indonesia-to-vote-with-ex-general-subianto-the-favourite|access-date=14 February 2024|archive-date=14 February 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240214043126/https://www.rfi.fr/en/international-news/20240213-indonesia-to-vote-with-ex-general-subianto-the-favourite|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="gemoy">{{cite news|date=15 February 2024|title=Ex-general Prabowo Subianto poised for sweeping presidential win in Indonesia|language=en|work=France 24|url=https://www.france24.com/en/asia-pacific/20240215-ex-general-prabowo-poised-for-sweeping-presidential-win-in-indonesia|access-date=15 February 2024|archive-date=15 February 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240215043241/https://www.france24.com/en/asia-pacific/20240215-ex-general-prabowo-poised-for-sweeping-presidential-win-in-indonesia|url-status=live}}</ref>

During the campaign, Anies Baswedan was targeted by a [[deepfake]] audio recording purportedly showing him being chastised by a political backer in January. Prabowo Subianto's campaign team used AI to depict children in a television advertisement in order to bypass laws prohibiting the appearance of minors in political advertisements.<ref name="suharto">{{cite news|date=11 February 2024|title=AI 'resurrects' long dead dictator in murky new era of deepfake electioneering|language=en|work=CNN|url=https://edition.cnn.com/2024/02/12/asia/suharto-deepfake-ai-scam-indonesia-election-hnk-intl/index.html|access-date=12 February 2024|archive-date=12 February 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240212085731/https://edition.cnn.com/2024/02/12/asia/suharto-deepfake-ai-scam-indonesia-election-hnk-intl/index.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

[[Golkar]], one of the parties supporting Prabowo for president, uploaded a viral [[Generative artificial intelligence|AI-generated]] deepfake video on social media of a simulation of Suharto, who had died in 2008, in which he appeared to urge voters to select the party's candidates in the upcoming election. This led some civil society organizations to urge the KPU to implement regulations on the usage of artificial intelligence.<ref>{{cite news|date=12 February 2024|title=Fake Suharto video fuels debate on AI use in Indonesian election campaign|language=en|work=Benar News|url=https://www.benarnews.org/english/news/indonesian/suharto-deepfake-used-in-election-campaign-01122024135217.html|access-date=12 February 2024|archive-date=12 February 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240212124751/https://www.benarnews.org/english/news/indonesian/suharto-deepfake-used-in-election-campaign-01122024135217.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Allegations of state support ===
On 12 February 2024, investigative journalist [[Dandhy Laksono]] released a documentary on YouTube directed by him, titled ''Dirty Vote'', alleging that Joko Widodo used state funds to support Prabowo Subianto's campaign, becoming viral within the day and prompting accusations of sabotage by Prabowo's campaign team.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Indonesia Documentary Claims Widodo Improperly Backed Election Favourite |url=https://www.barrons.com/news/indonesia-documentary-claims-widodo-improperly-backed-election-favourite-5ddc0df9 |access-date=2024-02-13 |website=Barron's |language=en |archive-date=13 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240213152004/https://www.barrons.com/news/indonesia-documentary-claims-widodo-improperly-backed-election-favourite-5ddc0df9 |url-status=live }}</ref> The presidential office denied the claims, while protests were held in reaction to the allegations.<ref name="abc"/>

=== Discrepancies in vote-counting ===
During the vote-counting, allegations emerged of votes appearing larger on the KPU-generated online application Sirekap (Recapitulation Information System) than what the actual results showed. The [[General Election Supervisory Agency]] (Bawaslu) attributed the issue to possible errors on part of the newly founded app, and welcomed an audit into Sirekap.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-02-15 |title=Bawaslu Welcomes Anyone to Audit Sirekap Amid Mounting Pressure |url=https://en.tempo.co/read/1834374/bawaslu-welcomes-anyone-to-audit-sirekap-amid-mounting-pressure |access-date=2024-02-15 |website=Tempo |language=en |archive-date=2024-02-17 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240217195145/https://en.tempo.co/read/1834374/bawaslu-welcomes-anyone-to-audit-sirekap-amid-mounting-pressure |url-status=live}}</ref> As of 20 February, Sirekap results from 1,223 polling stations were found to be mismatched with actual results.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Muliawati |first1=Anggi |title=Ditanya soal Kesalahan Baca Data di Sirekap, Ini Jawaban KPU |url=https://news.detik.com/pemilu/d-7202433/ditanya-soal-kesalahan-baca-data-di-sirekap-ini-jawaban-kpu |access-date=21 February 2024 |work=detiknews |date=20 February 2024 |language=id-ID |archive-date=21 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240221025550/https://news.detik.com/pemilu/d-7202433/ditanya-soal-kesalahan-baca-data-di-sirekap-ini-jawaban-kpu |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Release of results==
Official results were expected to be released in March, but [[quick count]]s from government-approved tabulators came out shortly after polling stations closed.<ref name="rfi"/> Initial tallies from Indikator Politik, ''[[Kompas]]'', and the Indonesian Survey Circle showed Prabowo Subianto receiving between 53.4 and 59.8 percent of votes cast, followed by Anies Baswedan, who received between 23.11 and 26.39 percent, and Ganjar Pranowo, who received between 16.72 and 17.12 percent.<ref>{{cite news|date=14 February 2024|title=Early results showing Prabowo on course for landslide win in Indonesia's presidential election, securing around 60% of votes cast|language=en|work=CNA|url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/asia/indonesia-elections-2024-unofficial-count-prabowo-lead-4122556|access-date=14 February 2024|archive-date=14 February 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240214090757/https://www.channelnewsasia.com/asia/indonesia-elections-2024-unofficial-count-prabowo-lead-4122556|url-status=live}}</ref> On 20 March, Prabowo was officially confirmed as the winner of the election by the KPU, having won about 96 million votes (59 percent), against Anies Baswedan who won 41 million (25 percent), and Ganjar Pranowo, who won 27 million (16 percent).<ref>{{cite news|date=20 March 2024|title=Indonesia election commission confirms Prabowo Subianto wins presidency |language=en|work=Al Jazeera |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/3/20/indonesia-election-commission-confirms-prabowo-subianto-wins-presidency |access-date=20 March 2024}}</ref>

== Aftermath ==

Indonesians."<ref>{{cite news |date=14 February 2024 |title=Prabowo Delivers Victory Speech: Grateful for the Safe Election |url=https://en.tempo.co/read/1833618/prabowo-delivers-victory-speech-grateful-for-the-safe-election |access-date=15 February 2024 |work=Tempo |language=en |archive-date=14 February 2024 |last1=Karmini |first1=Niniek |last2=Tarigan |first2=Edna |date=14 February 2024 |title=Indonesian defense chief linked to past rights abuses claims victory in presidential election |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240214023408/https://apnews.com/article/indonesia-presidential-election-voting-dd732adb2d0f3b674fc92aee4f547c6a |url-status=live }}</ref> Gibran Rakabuming also expressed thanks to Prabowo for "giving young people a chance."<ref name="abc">{{cite news |date=15 February 2024 |title=Prabowo Subianto claims victory in Indonesia's election, as counting continues in world's largest single-day vote |url=https://www.abc.net.au/news/2024-02-14/indonesia-election-key-takeaways/103456122 |access-date=15 February 2024 |work=ABC Australia |language=en Ganjar Pranowo's campaign team said that they were investigating reports of electoral violations and alleged "structural, systematic and massive fraud" during the voting.<ref>{{cite news |date=14 February 2024 |title=Prabowo Subianto claims victory in Indonesian presidential election |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/2/14/prabowo-subianto-claims-victory-in-indonesian-presidential-election |access-date=15 February 2024 |work=Al Jazeera |language=en |archive-date=14 February [[Hasto Kristiyanto]], the secretary-general of the PDI-P, said that election irregularities were enforced from the top down, beginning with the decision to allow Gibran Rakabuming Raka to run for vice president. [[Hamdan Zoelva]], former chief justice of the Constitutional Court and a member of Anies Baswedan's campaign team also said that there were "strong indications that violations occurred in a structured, systematic and massive way in the presidential election."<ref name="apfraud"/> Remarks by independent observers indicated there were "no signs of systemic fraud."<ref>{{cite news |title=Defeated Indonesian election candidates call for parliamentary probe |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/defeated-indonesian-election-candidates-call-parliamentary-probe-2024-02-20/ |access-date=21 February 2024 |work=Reuters |date=20 February 2024}}</ref> Prabowo again expressed thanks to the electorate after the official confirmation of the election results on 20 March.<ref>{{cite news |title=Indonesia's Prabowo Subianto confirmed as president after first-round win |url=https://www.france24.com/en/asia-pacific/20240320-indonesia-s-former-defence-minister-prabowo-subianto-wins-presidency |access-date=21 March 2024 |work=France 24 |date=21 March 2024}}</ref>

The [[Indonesia Stock Exchange]] on 15 February recorded its sharpest rise in two months as quick count results indicated Prabowo's victory, which analysts attributed to the removal of political uncertainty which would arise from a runoff election. Largest gains were made by banks, [[Nickel mining in Indonesia|nickel companies]], and infrastructure firms.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Wee |first1=Rae |title=Indonesian markets cheer as Prabowo's likely victory removes uncertainty |url=https://www.reuters.com/markets/asia/indonesian-markets-cheer-after-prabowo-claims-victory-2024-02-15/ |access-date=21 February 2024 |work=Reuters |date=15 February 2024}}</ref>

In total, the Constitutional Court received 297 lawsuits related to the results of the legislative election.<ref>{{cite news |title=MK Bacakan Putusan Sengketa Pileg 2024 mulai Besok hingga 10 Juni |url=https://nasional.kompas.com/read/2024/06/05/13330121/mk-bacakan-putusan-sengketa-pileg-2024-mulai-besok-hingga-10-juni |access-date=7 June 2024 |work=KOMPAS.com |date=5 June 2024 |language=id}}</ref>
