Early presidential elections in [[Iran]] were held on 28 June and 5 July 2024<ref name=arabiya>{{Cite web|title=Iran to hold presidential elections on June 28 after Raisi's death|url=https://english.alarabiya.net/News/middle-east/2024/05/20/iran-to-hold-presidential-elections-on-june-28-after-raisi-s-death|access-date=20 May 2024|publisher=[[Al Arabiya]]|archive-date=20 May 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240520205132/https://english.alarabiya.net/News/middle-east/2024/05/20/iran-to-hold-presidential-elections-on-june-28-after-raisi-s-death|url-status=live}}</ref> following [[2024 Varzaqan helicopter crash|the death]] of incumbent president [[Ebrahim Raisi]] in a [[2024 Varzaqan helicopter crash|helicopter crash]] on 19 May.<ref>{{Cite web |last=McNamee |first=Michael Sheils |date=20 May 2024 |title=Iran helicopter crash: President Ebrahim Raisi killed in helicopter crash |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cpwwql21747o |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240520171420/https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cpwwql21747o |archive-date=20 May 2024 |access-date=20 May 2024 |website=BBC News |publisher= |language=en-GB}}</ref>

Four candidates contested the first round of the election, in which Masoud Pezeshkian won 44%, [[Saeed Jalili]] won 40%, [[Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf]] won 14% and [[Mostafa Pourmohammadi]] won less than 1% of the vote. Pezeshkian was the only reformist candidate on the ballot.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Aktaş |first=Alperen |date=6 July 2024 |title=Profile: Masoud Pezeshkian, new reformist president of Islamic Republic of Iran |url=https://www.aa.com.tr/en/middle-east/profile-masoud-pezeshkian-new-reformist-president-of-islamic-republic-of-iran/3267636 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240706152418/https://www.aa.com.tr/en/middle-east/profile-masoud-pezeshkian-new-reformist-president-of-islamic-republic-of-iran/3267636 |archive-date=6 July 2024 |access-date=2024-07-07 |website=Anadolu Ajansı}}</ref> As no candidate won a majority in the first round, a run off vote was held on 5 July between Jalili and Pezeshkian,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Gambrell |first1=Jon |last2=Karimi |first2=Nasser |date=29 June 2024 |title=Iran to hold runoff election with reformist Pezeshkian and hard-liner Jalili after low-turnout vote |url=https://apnews.com/article/iran-presidential-election-jalili-pezeshkian-qalibaf-189a89c3a9c04be1af83ab684e213558 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240629040242/https://apnews.com/article/iran-presidential-election-jalili-pezeshkian-qalibaf-189a89c3a9c04be1af83ab684e213558 |archive-date=29 June 2024 |access-date=29 June 2024 |website=Associated Press |agency=}}</ref> which the latter won with 53.7% of the vote. On 6 July 2024, the [[Ministry of Interior (Iran)|Ministry of Interior]] declared Pezeshkian the winner of the election,<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Cassini|first1=Farnaz|last2=Vinograd|first2=Cassandra|date=5 July 2024|title=Reformist Candidate Wins Iran's Presidential Election|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2024/07/05/world/middleeast/iran-election-reformist-wins.html|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240706034614/https://www.nytimes.com/2024/07/05/world/middleeast/iran-election-reformist-wins.html|archive-date=6 July 2024|access-date=5 July 2024|website=The New York Times}}</ref> with Jalili conceding defeat shortly thereafter.<ref>{{cite news|date=6 July 2024|title=Centrist Masoud Pezeshkian will be Iran's next president|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/7/6/irans-reformist-masoud-pezeshkian-wins-run-off-presidential-vote-reports|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240706050835/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/7/6/irans-reformist-masoud-pezeshkian-wins-run-off-presidential-vote-reports|archive-date=6 July 2024|access-date=6 July 2024|publisher=Al Jazeera|language=en}}</ref>

With a 39.93% turnout, the first round of the election saw the lowest participation for a presidential election in the Islamic Republic's history.<ref name="aljazeera-result">{{Cite web |last=Motamedi |first=Maziar |date=29 June 2024 |title=Iran heads to presidential run-off amid record low turnout |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/6/29/iran-poised-for-presidential-run-off-in-tight-race |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240629074240/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/6/29/iran-poised-for-presidential-run-off-in-tight-race |archive-date=29 June 2024 |access-date=29 June 2024 |website=Al Jazeera |publisher=}}</ref> The turnout improved in the second round to 49.68%. Pezeshkian was seated as president after the ballot certification process was completed. His inauguration was held on 28 July.

== Electoral system ==
{{Main|Elections in Iran}}

The [[president of Iran]] is usually elected every four years by a "direct vote of the people", as set out by Article 114 of the [[Constitution of Iran|Iranian Constitution]].<ref name=":0" /> The upcoming presidential election was scheduled to take place on or before 18 June 2025, but was held earlier due to the death of the president. Under Iran's political system, the President is the country's highest directly elected official, the chief of the [[Executive (government)|executive branch]], and the second most important position after that of the [[Supreme Leader of Iran|Supreme Leader]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|date=19 June 2021|title=Ebrahim Raisi, ultra-conservative judiciary chief, wins Iran's presidential vote amid historically low turnout|url=https://www.cnn.com/2021/06/19/middleeast/ebrahim-raisi-iran-election-intl-hnk/index.html|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210621094529/https://www.cnn.com/2021/06/19/middleeast/ebrahim-raisi-iran-election-intl-hnk/index.html|archive-date=21 June 2021|access-date=19 June 2021|publisher=CNN}}</ref> The minimum voting age is 18.

According to the Islamic Republic of Iran's constitution, any Iranian citizen who believes in [[Shia Islam]], is loyal to the Constitution, the ideology of [[Guardianship of the Islamic Jurist]] and the Islamic Republic can participate in the election as a presidential candidate. The Election Monitoring Agency (EMA), managed by the [[Guardian Council]], vets registered candidates and selects a handful to run in the election.<ref>{{Cite web|author=Iranian provisional government of Mehdi Bazargan|title=Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Iran|url=https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Islamic_Republic_of_Iran|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210305102724/https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Islamic_Republic_of_Iran|archive-date=5 March 2021|access-date=20 June 2021|via=Wikisource}}</ref>

The Guardian Council does not publicly announce the reason for rejections of particular candidates, although those reasons are privately explained to each candidate. Women are not constitutionally restricted from running, but all who have registered as candidates have been disqualified by the Guardian Council.<ref>{{Cite web|date=10 October 2020|title=ثبت نام زنان در انتخابات ریاست جمهوری بلامانع است/ نظر فقها هیچ تغییری نکرده است|url=https://www.isna.ir/news/99071913710/ثبت-نام-زنان-در-انتخابات-ریاست-جمهوری-بلامانع-است-نظر-فقها-هیچ|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210515112608/https://www.isna.ir/news/99071913710/%D8%AB%D8%A8%D8%AA-%D9%86%D8%A7%D9%85-%D8%B2%D9%86%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%AF%D8%B1-%D8%A7%D9%86%D8%AA%D8%AE%D8%A7%D8%A8%D8%A7%D8%AA-%D8%B1%DB%8C%D8%A7%D8%B3%D8%AA-%D8%AC%D9%85%D9%87%D9%88%D8%B1%DB%8C-%D8%A8%D9%84%D8%A7%D9%85%D8%A7%D9%86%D8%B9-%D8%A7%D8%B3%D8%AA-%D9%86%D8%B8%D8%B1-%D9%81%D9%82%D9%87%D8%A7-%D9%87%DB%8C%DA%86|archive-date=15 May 2021|access-date=14 May 2021|website=ایسنا|language=fa}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Karimov|first=F|date=8 May 2013|title=First female candidate registered for Iranian presidential elections|url=http://en.trend.az/regions/iran/2148306.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130607174407/http://en.trend.az/regions/iran/2148306.html|archive-date=7 June 2013|access-date=18 June 2013|work=Trend}}</ref> The council has officially denied rejecting a woman's candidacy because of her gender.<ref>{{cite news|date=17 May 2013|title=Iran's ban on female presidential candidates contradicts Constitution|url=https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2013/05/irans-ban-on-female-presidential-candidates-contradicts-constitution/|access-date=18 June 2013|publisher=Amnesty International}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref>

Candidates approved by the Guardian Council are put to a public vote. The winner is the candidate who receives a majority (50% plus one) of votes. If no candidate receives enough votes, a run-off election is held between the two candidates with the most votes the following Friday.<ref name=":12">{{Cite web|date=28 July 1989|title=Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Iran, 1979 (amended 1989)|url=https://www.constituteproject.org/constitution/Iran_1989.pdf?lang=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191222001540/https://www.constituteproject.org/constitution/Iran_1989.pdf?lang=en|archive-date=22 December 2019|access-date=18 June 2021|website=Constitute Project}}</ref> Iranians who vote during the election receive a stamp that indicates so on their birth certificates.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Gambrell|first=Jon|date=19 June 2021|title=Hard-line judiciary head wins Iran presidency as turnout low|url=https://apnews.com/article/iran-election-ebrahim-raisi-judiciary-turnout-middle-east-4d60554fd608a5dea2c76a303ec352d8|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210621100607/https://apnews.com/article/iran-election-ebrahim-raisi-judiciary-turnout-middle-east-4d60554fd608a5dea2c76a303ec352d8|archive-date=21 June 2021|access-date=19 June 2021|location=[[Dubai]], [[United Arab Emirates]]|agency=Associated Press}}</ref>

According to the constitution, once the result is known, the Supreme Leader must sign the decree of the elected president, and if he refuses to sign, the elected president will not assume the presidency. As of 2024, Supreme Leaders have always signed the decree of the elected president.<ref>{{Cite web|date=3 August 2013|title=بازخوانی تنفیذ احکام روسای جمهور+عکس و متن احکام|url=https://www.isna.ir/news/92051206128/بازخوانی-تنفیذ-احکام-روسای-جمهور-عکس-و-متن-احکام|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210514051134/https://www.isna.ir/news/92051206128/%D8%A8%D8%A7%D8%B2%D8%AE%D9%88%D8%A7%D9%86%DB%8C-%D8%AA%D9%86%D9%81%DB%8C%D8%B0-%D8%A7%D8%AD%DA%A9%D8%A7%D9%85-%D8%B1%D9%88%D8%B3%D8%A7%DB%8C-%D8%AC%D9%85%D9%87%D9%88%D8%B1-%D8%B9%DA%A9%D8%B3-%D9%88-%D9%85%D8%AA%D9%86-%D8%A7%D8%AD%DA%A9%D8%A7%D9%85|archive-date=14 May 2021|access-date=14 May 2021|website=ایسنا|language=fa}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=مراسم تنفیذ حکم حسن روحانی برگزار شد|url=https://www.bbc.com/persian/world-40811127|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210514052647/https://www.bbc.com/persian/world-40811127|archive-date=14 May 2021|access-date=14 May 2021|publisher=BBC News فارسی|language=fa}}</ref> After that, the elected president must recite and sign an oath in a session of the [[Islamic Consultative Assembly]], in the presence of the members of the Guardian Council and the head of the [[Supreme Court of Iran|Supreme Court]].<ref name=":12" />

For this election, more than 61 million citizens were eligible to vote, with about 18 million of them between 18 and 30 years old.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Iranians head to the polls to replace president killed in helicopter crash|url=https://www.france24.com/en/middle-east/20240628-iranians-head-to-the-polls-to-replace-president-killed-in-helicopter-crash|work=France 24|language=en|date=28 June 2024|access-date=28 June 2024|archive-date=28 June 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240628073839/https://www.france24.com/en/middle-east/20240628-iranians-head-to-the-polls-to-replace-president-killed-in-helicopter-crash|url-status=live}}</ref> Voters were required to present [[Iranian identity booklet|identity booklets]], passports or smart [[Iranian identity card]]s<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.irna.ir/amp/85528336/|title=Office of Protecting Irans Interests against Washington's statement about second round of election|access-date=3 July 2024|archive-date=7 July 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240707041102/https://www.irna.ir/amp/85528336/|url-status=live}}</ref> The Ministry of Intelligence warned that presidential nominees were under constant monitoring.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.etemadonline.com/%D8%A8%D8%AE%D8%B4-%D8%B3%DB%8C%D8%A7%D8%B3%DB%8C-9/662527-%D9%87%D8%B4%D8%AF%D8%A7%D8%B1-%D9%88%D8%B2%DB%8C%D8%B1%D8%A7%D8%B7%D9%84%D8%A7%D8%B9%D8%A7%D8%AA-%D9%86%D8%B8%D8%A7%D8%B1%D8%AA|title=هشدار وزیر اطلاعات نسبت به نامزدهای انتخاباتی: مستمر نظارت می‌شوند|date=7 June 2024|website=اعتمادآنلاین|access-date=6 June 2024|archive-date=11 June 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240611022710/https://www.etemadonline.com/%D8%A8%D8%AE%D8%B4-%D8%B3%DB%8C%D8%A7%D8%B3%DB%8C-9/662527-%D9%87%D8%B4%D8%AF%D8%A7%D8%B1-%D9%88%D8%B2%DB%8C%D8%B1%D8%A7%D8%B7%D9%84%D8%A7%D8%B9%D8%A7%D8%AA-%D9%86%D8%B8%D8%A7%D8%B1%D8%AA|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Dates ===
Following the announcement of Raisi's death on 20 May, authorities announced that voting would be held on 28 June.<ref name=arabiya/> Registration of candidates began on 30 May and ran until 3 June,<ref>{{Cite news|last=Gambrell|first=Jon|date=30 May 2024|title=Iran opens registration period for the presidential election after a helicopter crash killed Raisi|url=https://apnews.com/article/iran-presidential-election-raisi-helicopter-crash-38421a7f0db72a0e3086a8c1b1db6f9f|access-date=30 May 2024|location=[[Dubai]], [[United Arab Emirates]]|agency=Associated Press|archive-date=30 May 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240530045225/https://apnews.com/article/iran-presidential-election-raisi-helicopter-crash-38421a7f0db72a0e3086a8c1b1db6f9f|url-status=live}}</ref> while campaigning lasted from 12 June until 27 June.<ref name=arabiya/>

===Rejected===
A total of 74 aspirants had their candidacies rejected by the Guardian Council.<ref name="final"/> These included all four women who applied to run in the election.<ref>{{Cite news|date=9 June 2024|title=Iran announces six candidates approved for June 28 presidential election|url=https://www.france24.com/en/middle-east/20240609-iran-announces-six-candidates-approved-for-june-28-presidential-election|access-date=9 June 2024|agency=[[France 24]]}}</ref> The applications of least 30 candidacies were rejected on 30 May for failure to meet the "basic conditions for qualification".<ref>{{Cite news|date=30 May 2024|title=Iran registers presidential candidates for snap elections after Raisi's death|url=https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20240530-iran-opens-registration-for-presidential-candidates|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240530090852/https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20240530-iran-opens-registration-for-presidential-candidates|archive-date=30 May 2024|access-date=30 May 2024|agency=[[France 24]]}}</ref> Former President [[Mahmoud Ahmadinejad]] was the most notable to be disqualified by the Guardian Council, having been disqualified previously in 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|date=3 June 2024|title=Iran OKs 6 candidates for presidential race, but again blocks Ahmadinejad|url=https://apnews.com/article/iran-election-raisi-ahmadinejad-557dd8e41a9abf78e0ad02e0e21d4e00.html|access-date=14 June 2024|agency=Associated Press|language=en-US|archive-date=7 July 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240707034551/https://apnews.com/article/iran-election-raisi-ahmadinejad-557dd8e41a9abf78e0ad02e0e21d4e00|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Approved===
Six candidates were allowed by the Guardian Council to run for president, namely Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, Saeed Jalili, Masoud Pezeshkian, Mostafa Pourmohammadi, Amirhossein Ghazizadeh Hashemi and Alireza Zakani.<ref name="final"/> On 26 June, Hashemi withdrew his candidacy and called on other candidates to follow "so that the front of the revolution will be strengthened". He was followed on 27 June by Zakani, who did so citing the need to "block the formation of a third administration" of former President [[Hassan Rouhani]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=28 June 2024|title=Two candidates drop out of Iran presidential election, due to take place Friday amid voter apathy|url=https://apnews.com/article/iran-presidential-election-ebrahim-raisi-mideast-tensions-6081dc2b8a9ff4c310e148edacf51b29|access-date=28 June 2024|agency=Associated Press|language=en|archive-date=27 June 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240627232625/https://apnews.com/article/iran-presidential-election-ebrahim-raisi-mideast-tensions-6081dc2b8a9ff4c310e148edacf51b29|url-status=live}}</ref> Both candidates, as well as Ghalibaf, subsequently endorsed Jalili in the second round.<ref>{{Cite web|date=29 June 2024|title=Reformist to face ultraconservative in Iran presidency runoff|url=https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20240629-reformist-ultraconservative-lead-iran-presidential-vote-1|access-date=29 June 2024|website=France 24|language=en|archive-date=29 June 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240629083640/https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20240629-reformist-ultraconservative-lead-iran-presidential-vote-1|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Campaign platforms==

===Second round===
During a two-hour televised debate on IRIB on 1 July, Pezeshkian criticized Jalili over his lack of managerial experience, his stance on nuclear negotiations and his pledge to achieve an economic growth rate of 8% while in office, adding that authorities should be allowed to "execute" Jalili if his target was not met. Jalili accused Pezeshkian of having no plans to manage the country and instead lead it to a "backward position". Both candidates pledged to address issues involving the poor, workers, women, ethnic groups and religious minorities, as well as improve internet services. They also called for an investigation into the low turnout in the first round.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2 July 2024|title=Iranian presidential candidates accuse each other of having no plan or experience ahead of runoff|url=https://apnews.com/article/iran-debate-runoff-election-4c4e05cae4f6cf75473f21f8b954d9a4|access-date=2 July 2024|agency=Associated Press|language=en|archive-date=2 July 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240702011911/https://apnews.com/article/iran-debate-runoff-election-4c4e05cae4f6cf75473f21f8b954d9a4|url-status=live}}</ref> Pezeshkian called a situation in Iran a deadlock and said that no government will grow in a cage. He again criticized Jalili for his shadow state.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.etemadonline.com/%D8%A8%D8%AE%D8%B4-%D8%B3%DB%8C%D8%A7%D8%B3%DB%8C-9/665739-%D9%BE%D8%B2%D8%B4%DA%A9%DB%8C%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%A7%D9%86%D8%AA%D8%AE%D8%A7%D8%A8%D8%A7%D8%AA-%D8%B1%DB%8C%D8%A7%D8%B3%D8%AA-%D8%AC%D9%85%D9%87%D9%88%D8%B1%DB%8C|title=برنامه‌ریزی پشت درهای بسته توسط و در دولت سایه زندگی مردم را بهتر نمی‌کند|access-date=3 July 2024|archive-date=6 July 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240706103352/https://www.etemadonline.com/%D8%A8%D8%AE%D8%B4-%D8%B3%DB%8C%D8%A7%D8%B3%DB%8C-9/665739-%D9%BE%D8%B2%D8%B4%DA%A9%DB%8C%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%A7%D9%86%D8%AA%D8%AE%D8%A7%D8%A8%D8%A7%D8%AA-%D8%B1%DB%8C%D8%A7%D8%B3%D8%AA-%D8%AC%D9%85%D9%87%D9%88%D8%B1%DB%8C|url-status=live}}</ref>

During the final debate on 2 July, Pezeshkian pledged to revive a nuclear agreement with foreign powers, while Jalili called on the US to honor its commitments on par "with the commitments we fulfilled." Both candidates pledged to improve the economy, provide energy subsidies to the poor and facilitate the importation of cars while supporting [[Automotive industry in Iran|domestic automobile makers]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=3 July 2024|title=Iran's presidential candidates discuss economic sanctions and nuclear deal ahead of Friday runoff|url=https://apnews.com/article/iran-final-debate-runoff-jalili-pezeshkian-0fa6bf1e3e590f3ebf87bf1693abbccd|access-date=3 July 2024|agency=Associated Press|language=en|archive-date=3 July 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240703020146/https://apnews.com/article/iran-final-debate-runoff-jalili-pezeshkian-0fa6bf1e3e590f3ebf87bf1693abbccd|url-status=live}}</ref> Jalili made several misleading claims over the economy that sought to criticize the Rouhani administration and praise that of Raisi's.<ref>{{Cite web|title=رديه‌اي بر 5 ادعاي گمراه‌كننده|url=https://www.etemadnewspaper.ir/fa/main/detail/219138/%D8%B1%D8%AF%DB%8C%D9%87%E2%80%8C%D8%A7%DB%8C-%D8%A8%D8%B1-5-%D8%A7%D8%AF%D8%B9%D8%A7%DB%8C-%DA%AF%D9%85%D8%B1%D8%A7%D9%87%E2%80%8C%D9%83%D9%86%D9%86%D8%AF%D9%87-|access-date=2024-07-05|website=روزنامه اعتماد|archive-date=6 July 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240706101846/https://www.etemadnewspaper.ir/fa/main/detail/219138/%D8%B1%D8%AF%DB%8C%D9%87%E2%80%8C%D8%A7%DB%8C-%D8%A8%D8%B1-5-%D8%A7%D8%AF%D8%B9%D8%A7%DB%8C-%DA%AF%D9%85%D8%B1%D8%A7%D9%87%E2%80%8C%D9%83%D9%86%D9%86%D8%AF%D9%87-|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Results ==
Official results showed Pezeshkian and Jalili heading into a run-off scheduled on 5 July. Turnout in the first round was at 40 percent, the lowest for a presidential election in Iran since 1979,<ref>{{cite news|title=Iran heads to presidential run-off amid record low turnout|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/6/29/iran-poised-for-presidential-run-off-in-tight-race|publisher=Al Jazeera|date=29 June 2024|language=en|access-date=29 June 2024|archive-date=29 June 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240629093719/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/6/29/iran-poised-for-presidential-run-off-in-tight-race|url-status=live}}</ref> while a total of 1,056,159 ballots were deemed as spoiled. The election also saw the first presidential run-off vote in Iran since [[2005 Iranian presidential election|2005]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Iran to hold runoff election between reformist Masoud Pezeshkian and hard-liner Saeed Jalili|url=https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20240629-reformist-ultraconservative-lead-iran-presidential-vote|work=France 24|date=29 June 2024|language=en|access-date=29 June 2024|archive-date=29 June 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240629111104/https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20240629-reformist-ultraconservative-lead-iran-presidential-vote|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Aftermath==
In his first statements following his victory, Pezeshkian said that "the difficult path ahead will not be smooth", while pledging to serve all Iranians. In his concession statement, Jalili called for Pezeshkian to be respected. Pezeshkian is expected to assume the presidency within 30 days of the election, after he resigns from his seat in the Islamic Consultative Assembly and gains the formal approval of Khamenei.<ref>{{cite news|title=Centrist Masoud Pezeshkian will be Iran's next president|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/7/6/irans-reformist-masoud-pezeshkian-wins-run-off-presidential-vote-reports|publisher=Al Jazeera|date=6 July 2024|language=en|access-date=6 July his speech in the [[Mausoleum of Ruhollah Khomeini]], Pezeshkian thanked Khamenei for his support.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.etemadonline.com/%D8%A8%D8%AE%D8%B4-%D8%B3%DB%8C%D8%A7%D8%B3%DB%8C-9/666242-%D9%BE%D8%B2%D8%B4%DA%A9%DB%8C%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%A7%D9%86%D8%AA%D8%AE%D8%A7%D8%A8%D8%A7%D8%AA-%D8%B1%DB%8C%D8%A7%D8%B3%D8%AA-%D8%AC%D9%85%D9%87%D9%88%D8%B1%DB%8C|title=پزشکیان: 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240706181017/https://www.etemadonline.com/%D8%A8%D8%AE%D8%B4-%D8%B3%DB%8C%D8%A7%D8%B3%DB%8C-9/666242-%D9%BE%D8%B2%D8%B4%DA%A9%DB%8C%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%A7%D9%86%D8%AA%D8%AE%D8%A7%D8%A8%D8%A7%D8%AA-%D8%B1%DB%8C%D8%A7%D8%B3%D8%AA-%D8%AC%D9%85%D9%87%D9%88%D8%B1%DB%8C|url-status=live}}</ref> high, bright horizons."<ref name="apreaction">{{cite web|url=https://apnews.com/article/iran-presidential-election-runoff-jalili-pezeshkian-results-8a10c940adc1259566791b549828a3fd|title=Reformist Senior Khamenei advisor Kamal Kharazi stated that the election would not affect Iranian foreign policy as it is controlled by the {{ill|Strategic Council for Foreign Relations|fa|شورای راهبردی روابط خارجی جمهوری اسلامی ایران}}.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.isna.ir/amp/1403041208361/|title=خرازی: انتخابات بر راهبرد سیاست خارجی ایران بی‌تاثیر است – ایسنا|access-date=7 July 2024|archive-date=7 July 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240707030657/https://www.isna.ir/amp/1403041208361/|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 28 July, Pezeshkian received Khamenei's official endorsement to hold office as president. Pezeshkian also appointed as his first vice president [[Mohammad Reza Aref]], a reformist politician who had previously served in the same position under Mohammad Khatami.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20240728-iran-s-khamenei-formally-grants-pezeshkian-presidential-powers|title=Iran's Khamenei formally grants Pezeshkian presidential powers|date=12 July 2024|website=France 24}}</ref>
