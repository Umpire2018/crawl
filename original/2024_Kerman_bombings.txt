==Background==
{{further|Iran and the Islamic State}}
[[File:Women reciting Fatiha on Qasem Soleimani's grave 01.jpg|thumb|The grave of Qasem Soleimani at the Martyrs' Cemetery in [[Kerman]], 2021]]

On 3 January 2020, General [[Qasem Soleimani]] was [[Assassination of Qasem Soleimani|killed by a United States drone attack]] in Iraq. Soleimani was the commander of the [[Quds Force]] of the [[Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps|IRGC]]. Soleimani held a position of significant influence in Iran, widely considered the second most powerful figure in the country after Supreme Leader Ayatollah [[Ali Khamenei]]. As leader of the Quds Force, the overseas operations arm of the IRGC, he played a key role in shaping Iranian policy across the region. Soleimani was responsible for overseeing clandestine missions and providing guidance, funding, weapons, intelligence, and logistical support to allied governments and armed groups, including [[Hamas]] and [[Hezbollah]].<ref name="BBC"/><ref name=":0">{{Cite news|date=3 January 2024|title=Iran says at least 73 killed in 'terror' blasts near grave of IRGC general Soleimani|work=Times of Israel|url=https://www.timesofisrael.com/liveblog-january-03-2024/|access-date=3 January 2024|archive-date=3 January 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240103001122/https://www.timesofisrael.com/liveblog-january-03-2024/|url-status=live}}</ref>

During the [[Funeral of Qasem Soleimani|burial procession for Soleimani]] in Kerman on 7 January 2020, a stampede occurred, killing at least 50 mourners and causing injuries to over 200 individuals.<ref name="BBC"/> The 2024 attack came amidst heightened tensions in the Middle East following the [[2023 Israelâ€“Hamas war]] and its spillover conflicts. A day before the bombings, an [[Assassination of Saleh al-Arouri|Israeli drone strike]] in Lebanon killed Hamas's deputy leader [[Saleh al-Arouri]],<ref name="BBC"/> while a week prior, an Israeli airstrike killed Iranian Brigadier General [[Sayyed Razi Mousavi]] in Syria.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-67820538 |title=Israeli strike in Syria kills Iranian commander, Iran says |date=25 December 2023 |access-date=4 January 2024 |archive-date=1 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240101143244/https://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-67820538 |url-status=live}}</ref> Into the 2020s, the [[Islamic State]] and other [[Sunni]] extremist groups have launched similar attacks in the [[Shiite]]-majority country, including a [[2022 Shah Cheragh attack|mass shooting]] that killed 13 people in [[Shiraz]] in 2022.<ref name="ap141">{{cite news |title=Iran says at least 103 people killed, 141 wounded in blasts at ceremony honoring slain general |url=https://apnews.com/article/iran-us-soleimani-explosion-kerman-2524cfed1d040370bf98000e2b53ad5a |access-date=3 January 2024 |publisher=Associated Press |date=3 January 2024 |archive-date=3 January 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240103143210/https://apnews.com/article/iran-us-soleimani-explosion-kerman-2524cfed1d040370bf98000e2b53ad5a |url-status=live}}</ref>

===U.S. warning to Iran===
{{Blockquote|Prior to ISIS' terrorist attack on 3 January 2024, in Kerman, Iran, the US government provided Iran with a private warning that there was a terrorist threat within Iranian borders. The US government followed a longstanding '[[Duty to warn#U.S. Intelligence Community|duty to warn]]' policy that has been implemented across administrations to warn governments against potential lethal threats. We provide these warnings in part because we do not want to see innocent lives lost in terror attacks.|A U.S. official, quoted by [https://www.cnn.com/2024/01/25/politics/us-secretly-warned-iran-isis-terror-attack/index.html CNN]}}

The [[United States]] secretly warned [[Iran]] about intelligence that the [[Islamic State]] was planning a potential [[terrorism|terror attack]] inside Iran's borders before the deadly 3 January bombings in Kerman, according to a U.S. official.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Bertrand |first1=Natasha |title=US secretly warned Iran before ISIS terror attack |url=https://www.cnn.com/2024/01/25/politics/us-secretly-warned-iran-isis-terror-attack/index.html |access-date=26 January 2024 |publisher=CNN |date=25 January 2024}}</ref> The private warning was based on U.S. intelligence about ISIS' plans and was provided to Iran based on the U.S. government's "duty to warn" policy, which applies even to adversaries, to warn about potential lethal threats.<ref name="wsj">{{cite news |title=U.S. Secretly Alerted Iran Ahead of Islamic State Terrorist Attacks |url=https://www.wsj.com/world/middle-east/u-s-secretly-alerted-iran-ahead-of-islamic-state-terrorist-attack-af77a19a |work=Wall Street Journal |date=25 January 2024}}</ref>

The warning from the U.S. to Iran is notable not only because Iran is a U.S. adversary, but also because of recent [[Iran and state-sponsored terrorism|Iranian proxy attacks]] on U.S. personnel in the Middle East.<ref name="wsj" /> However, the U.S. official stated the warning was provided "in part because we do not want to see innocent lives lost in terror attacks."<ref name="wsj"/>

Despite the U.S. warning, Iran was ultimately unable to stop the 3 January ISIS attack, which killed at least 84 people and was the deadliest such incident in the country since the [[Iranian Revolution]] in 1979.<ref name="cnnisis" />

According to the [[Office of the Director of National Intelligence]], the "duty to warn" policy requires the U.S. intelligence community to warn intended victims, including non-U.S. persons, about "credible and specific" threats of intentional killing, serious injury or kidnapping.<ref name="wsj"/>

==Aftermath==
IRIB showed [[Iranian Red Crescent Society|Red Crescent]] rescuers assisting injured individuals at the ceremony. Several Iranian news agencies reported a higher count of wounded individuals. Reza Fallah, the head of the provincial Red Crescent, stated that their rapid response teams were evacuating the injured, but faced challenges due to waves of crowds obstructing roads.<ref>{{Cite news |date=3 January 2024 |title=Dozens killed in 'terrorist attacks' near tomb of Iranian Guards' Soleimani |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/iran-media-report-explosion-near-guards-commander-soleimanis-tomb-anniversary-2024-01-03/|access-date=3 January 2024}}</ref> Hospitals in Kerman and surrounding areas were placed on alert to treat victims.<ref name="guardian" />

==Suspect arrested==

The Iranian Ministry of Intelligence has announced the detention of a key figure believed to be behind the deadly terrorist bombings that struck the city of Kerman earlier this year.

In a statement released on July 13, 2024, the ministry said it had apprehended "one of the ring leaders and main plotters" of the attacks, identifying him as Abdullah Quetta.

According to the statement, the intelligence obtained from Quetta has played a "significant role" in uncovering "many Takfiri conspiracies" and identifying other major elements involved in the plot.
