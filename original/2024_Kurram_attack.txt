On 21 November 2024, gunmen attacked a large convoy of vehicles transporting [[Shia Islam|Shia Muslims]] in the [[Kurram District]] of Pakistan's [[Khyber Pakhtunkhwa]] province, killing at least 54 people and injuring 86 others. The attack was one of [[northwestern Pakistan]]'s deadliest [[Sectarian violence in Pakistan|incidents of sectarian violence]] in recent years.<ref name=":2">{{Cite news |last=ur-Rehman |first=Zia |date=21 November 2024 |title=At Least 38 Killed as Gunmen Ambush Shiite Convoys in Pakistan |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2024/11/21/world/asia/kurram-pakistan-attack.html}}</ref> The attack was described as a "[[terrorist attack]]" by Pakistani authorities.<ref name=":0" />

== Background ==
The attack, one of [[northwestern Pakistan]]'s deadliest [[Sectarian violence in Pakistan|incidents of sectarian violence]] in recent years, marked a significant escalation in sectarian tensions that had already claimed numerous lives in preceding months.{{Citation needed|date=November 2024}}

Sunni and Shia Muslims are in conflict in the region over a dispute over land.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |last=Ali |first=Mushtaq |date=21 November 2024 |title=Gunmen attack Pakistan passenger vehicles, killing at least 38 people |url=https://www.msn.com/en-gb/news/world/gunmen-attack-pakistan-passenger-vehicles-killing-at-least-38-people/ar-AA1uuSKn |access-date=22 November 2024 |website=MSN |agency=Reuters}}</ref> Half of the population of the Kurram region are Shia Muslims, while the majority of Pakistan are Sunni Muslims.<ref name=":2" />

== Attack ==
Four gunmen emerged from a vehicle and opened fire on a convoy of over 200 vehicles containing Shia Muslims traveling from [[Parachinar]] to [[Peshawar]] along a remote highway. The gunmen initially targeted police escort vehicles, who were assigned to protect the convoy due to prior instances of sectarian violence on roadways, before firing on the passengers.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Drury |first=Flora |date=22 November 2024 |title=Pakistan: Dozens dead in attack on passenger vehicles in Kurram |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cqxw18yvrndo |access-date=21 November 2024 |website=BBC News |language=en-GB |archive-date=21 November 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241121134046/https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cqxw18yvrndo |url-status=live }}</ref> Additional attackers reportedly fired from nearby farm fields.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=21 November 2024 |title=At least 42 killed as gunmen open fire on vehicles carrying Shiites in northwest Pakistan |url=https://apnews.com/article/pakistan-gunmen-opened-fire-convoy-vehicles-northwest-42c8fb3cf1f720de49efebd306d76fa1 |access-date=21 November 2024 |website=AP News |language=en |archive-date=21 November 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241121104747/https://apnews.com/article/pakistan-gunmen-opened-fire-convoy-vehicles-northwest-42c8fb3cf1f720de49efebd306d76fa1 |url-status=live }}</ref>  

The assault lasted approximately 40 minutes, claiming the lives of over 38 men, five children and eight women, while leaving dozens of victims in critical condition at local hospitals. No group initially claimed responsibility for the attack.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite news |date=November 24, 2024 |title=Death toll from Kurram gun attack rises to 54 |url=https://24newshd.tv/23-Nov-2024/death-toll-from-kurram-gun-attack-rises-to-54 |access-date=November 25, 2024 |publisher=[[24 News]] |language=en}}</ref>

== Aftermath ==
The attack triggered renewed sectarian clashes in Kurram. Rioters in the villages of Bagan and Bacha Kot set fire to shops, houses and government buildings.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web |date=23 November 2024 |title=Violence Erupts in Kurram as Clashes Leave 18 Dead, Protests Sweep Pakistan After Massacre |url=https://tnnenglish.com/violence-erupts-in-kurram-as-clashes-leave-18-dead-protests-sweep-pakistan-after-massacre |access-date=23 November 2024 |website=[[Tribal News Network]] |language=en}}</ref> Protesters in Parachinar set ablaze two police checkpoints.<ref>{{Cite web |date=23 November 2024 |title=Protests over Kurram massacre turn violent |url=https://tribune.com.pk/story/2511384/protests-over-kurram-massacre-turn-violent |access-date=23 November 2024 |website=The Express Tribune |language=en}}</ref> Internet was shut down, mobile services were suspended, schools were closed and a severe medicine and [[Famine|food shortage]] occurred after the attack.<ref>{{Cite news |date=27 November 2024 |title=Death Toll Rises To 102 As Kurram Violence Continues Amid Internet Blackout And School Closures |url=https://menafn.com/1108932433/Death-Toll-Rises-To-102-As-Kurram-Violence-Continues-Amid-Internet-Blackout-And-School-Closures |access-date=28 November 2024 |publisher=MENAFN |language=en}}</ref> Reports emerged of intense gunfire in [[Lower Kurram Agency]] between the [[Alizai (Pashtun tribe)|Alizai]] and Bagan tribes.<ref name=":3" /><ref name=":4">{{Cite web |last=Khan |first=Riaz |date=23 November 2024 |title=Fighting between armed sectarian groups in restive northwestern Pakistan kills at least 33 people |url=https://apnews.com/article/pakistan-sectarian-violence-kurram-aec6e365dd13de8e381740008fbd14b7 |access-date=23 November 2024 |website=AP News |language=en}}</ref>

By 28 November, 110 people had been killed in fighting across Kurram<ref name=":4" /> with an additional 150 being wounded as well as over 300 shops and 100 houses being razed.<ref>{{Cite news |date=28 November 2024 |title=Kurram's Struggle: Severe Shortages Of Medicine And Food Amid Ongoing Violence, Death Toll Reaches 110 |url=https://menafn.com/1108937152/Kurrams-Struggle-Severe-Shortages-Of-Medicine-And-Food-Amid-Ongoing-Violence-Death-Toll-Reaches-110 |access-date=28 November 2024 |publisher=MENAFN |language=en}}</ref><ref>[https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20241123-32-killed-in-new-sectarian-violence-in-pakistan 32 killed in new sectarian violence in Pakistan]</ref> The death toll had risen to 133 by 3 December, with 177 being wounded.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Afzaal |first=Ali Afzal |date=2024-12-03 |title=Two more succumb to injuries in Kurram; death toll hits 133 |url=https://www.thenews.com.pk/latest/1257661-two-more-succumb-to-injuries-in-kurram-bringing-tally-to-133 |access-date=December 3, 2024 |website=The News International |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Mehsud |first=Rehmat |date=2024-12-03 |title=Pakistan province forms grand jirga to broker truce after 133 killed in sectarian feuding |url=https://www.arabnews.pk/node/2581612/pakistan |access-date=2024-12-03 |website=Arab News PK |language=en}}</ref>



== Reactions ==

=== Domestic ===
The incident prompted immediate responses from Pakistan's leadership: [[Ministry of Interior (Pakistan)|Interior Minister]] [[Mohsin Naqvi]] categorized it as a terrorist attack,<ref name=":0" /> while [[Prime Minister of Pakistan|Prime Minister]] [[Shehbaz Sharif]] and [[President of Pakistan|President]] [[Asif Ali Zardari]] issued strong condemnations and promises of justice against the parties responsible. Heightened security measures were implemented.<ref name=":0" /> Nadeem Aslam Chaudhry, [[Chief Secretary of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa]], called the attack a "major tragedy" and said the death toll was likely to rise.<ref name=":1" />

Local Shia leaders criticized authorities for failing to provide adequate security despite recent threats against the Shia community. Several shop owners in Parachinar went on strike in protest against regional violence.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2024/nov/23/hundreds-flee-deadly-sectarian-violence-in-north-west-pakistan|title=Hundreds flee deadly sectarian violence in north-west Pakistan|publisher=The Guadian}}</ref>

=== Foreign governments ===
[[President of Iran|Iranian President]] [[Masoud Pezeshkian]] condemned the attack, expressing his condolences to the government  Pakistan and the families of the victims. [[Iranian Foreign Minister]] [[Abbas Araghchi]] wrote on his [[Twitter|X]] account, "Our condolences to the victims' families and the people and government of brotherly Pakistan. We ask Almighty Allah for the speedy recovery of those injured. We spare no effort in strengthening bilateral and multilateral coordination and cooperation to uproot the scourge of terrorism".<ref>{{Cite web |date=22 November 2024 |title=Pezeshkian, Araghchi condemn terrorist attack in Pakistan |url=https://en.mehrnews.com/news/224737/Pezeshkian-Araghchi-condemn-terrorist-attack-in-Pakistan |access-date=22 November 2024 |website=Mehr News Agency |language=en}}</ref>

[[United States Department of State|US State Department]] [[Spokesperson for the United States Department of State|Spokesperson]] [[Matthew Miller (spokesperson)|Matthew Miller]] expressed solidarity with Pakistan during a press briefing, condemning the attack and expressing sympathy for the victims and their families. MIller additionally highlighted the United States' cooperation with the Pakistani government in counterterrorism.<ref>{{Cite web |date=20 November 2024 |title=US condemns surge in terrorist attacks in Pakistan, reaffirms continued support |url=https://www.nation.com.pk/20-Nov-2024/us-condemns-surge-in-terrorist-attacks-in-pakistan-reaffirms-continued-support |access-date=22 November 2024 |website=The Nation |language=en-US}}</ref> Spokesperson of the [[Embassy of the United States, Islamabad|US embassy in Islamabad]] Jonathan Lalley expressed a similar sentiment to the State Department, stating that Pakistanis 'deserve to live free from harm, danger, and threat, and to feel protected and secure in their daily lives' and that the US will remain a 'steadfast partner' to the nation.<ref>{{Cite web |date=22 November 2024 |title=STATEMENT BY U.S. MISSION SPOKESPERSON JONATHAN LALLEY ON THE ATTACK IN KURRAM |url=https://pk.usembassy.gov/statement-by-u-s-mission-spokesperson-jonathan-lalley-on-the-attack-in-kurram/ |access-date=23 November 2024 |website=U.S. Embassy & Consulates in Pakistan |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Khan |first=Naimat |date=22 November 2024 |title=US vows to be ‘steadfast partner’ after deadly attack in Pakistan’s Kurram district |url=https://www.arabnews.com/node/2580357/pakistan |access-date=23 November 2024 |website=[[Arab News]] |language=en}}</ref> Additional messages of solidarity were issued by the Australian, Iranian and [[Embassy of Russia, Islamabad|Russian embassies]] in Islamabad.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Achakzai |first=Jamila |date=23 November 2024 |title=Foreign missions condemn Kurram attack |url=https://www.thenews.com.pk/print/1254138-foreign-missions-condemn-kurram-attack |access-date=23 November 2024 |website=[[The News International]] |language=en}}</ref>
