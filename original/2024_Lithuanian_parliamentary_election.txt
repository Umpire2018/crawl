The '''2024 Lithuanian parliamentary election''' was held on 13 and 27 October 2024 to elect the 141 members of the [[Seimas]]. Parliament members were elected in 71 single-member constituencies using the [[two-round system]], and the remaining 70 in a single nationwide constituency using [[proportional representation]]. The first round was held on 13 October and the second round on 27 October.<ref>{{Cite web |title=The forthcoming elections |url=https://www.vrk.lt/en/numatomi |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240229231713/https://www.vrk.lt/en/numatomi |archive-date=29 February 2024 |access-date=29 February 2024 |website=www.vrk.lt}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=28 November 2023 |title=Lithuania starts registering voters abroad for next year's elections |url=https://www.lrt.lt/en/news-in-english/19/2136462/lithuania-starts-registering-voters-abroad-for-next-year-s-elections |access-date=29 February 2024 |website=lrt.lt |publisher=[[Lithuanian National Radio and Television|LRT]] |language=en |archive-date=2 October 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241002221635/https://www.lrt.lt/en/news-in-english/19/2136462/lithuania-starts-registering-voters-abroad-for-next-year-s-elections |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=23 July 2024 |title=Lithuania's general elections: 19 parties, 18 unaffiliated politicians register to run |url=https://www.lrt.lt/en/news-in-english/19/2324948/lithuania-s-general-elections-19-parties-18-unaffiliated-politicians-register-to-run |access-date=27 July 2024 |website=lrt.lt |language=en |archive-date=17 September 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240917035103/https://www.lrt.lt/en/news-in-english/19/2324948/lithuania-s-general-elections-19-parties-18-unaffiliated-politicians-register-to-run |url-status=live }}</ref>

The elections were won by the [[Social Democratic Party of Lithuania]] (LSDP),<ref>{{cite news |title=Lithuanian Social Democratic leader hails 'historic' election victory |url=https://www.lrt.lt/en/news-in-english/19/2399206/lithuanian-social-democratic-leader-hails-historic-election-victory |access-date=28 October 2024 |work=lrt.lt |date=28 October 2024 |language=en |archive-date=11 November 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241111173825/https://www.lrt.lt/en/news-in-english/19/2399206/lithuanian-social-democratic-leader-hails-historic-election-victory |url-status=live }}</ref> which secured 19.32% of the popular vote and 52 seats, up from 9.58% and 13 seats in the [[2020 Lithuanian parliamentary election|previous elections]] in 2020. The [[Homeland Union]] (TS–LKD), the largest party in the ruling [[Šimonytė Cabinet|centre-right coalition]] in the [[Thirteenth Seimas|preceding Seimas]], finished a distant second, securing 28 seats, down from its previous 50.

Following the first round of the election, the Social Democrats entered into coalition talks with the [[Lithuanian Farmers and Greens Union]] (LVŽS) and the [[Union of Democrats "For Lithuania"]] (DSVL), which had split from the latter. After the second round, the consultations were expanded to include the [[Liberals' Movement (Lithuania)|Liberals' Movement]] (LS), which had been part of the outgoing conservative-led coalition, and [[Dawn of Nemunas]] (PPNA), a new nationalist party that finished in third place overall.<ref>{{cite news |title=Lithuanian Social Democratic leader's refusal to lead government raises trust issues |url=https://www.lrt.lt/en/news-in-english/19/2401999/lithuanian-social-democratic-leader-s-refusal-to-lead-government-raises-trust-issues |work=lrt.lt |date=31 October 2024 |language=en |access-date=10 November 2024 |archive-date=10 November 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241110161531/https://www.lrt.lt/en/news-in-english/19/2401999/lithuanian-social-democratic-leader-s-refusal-to-lead-government-raises-trust-issues |url-status=live }}</ref>

The LSDP eventually reached a deal with DSVL and PPNA to form a coalition government.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Samoškaitė |first1=Eglė |last2=Platūkytė |first2=Domantė |title=Lithuania's social democrats reach coalition deal 'in principle' |url=https://www.lrt.lt/en/news-in-english/19/2408758/lithuania-s-social-democrats-reach-coalition-deal-in-principle |work=lrt.lt |date=8 November 2024 |language=en}}</ref> The Social Democrats' decision to include the Dawn of Nemunas party, whose founder is known for making controversial statements, prompted domestic and international backlash.<ref name=openletter /><ref>{{cite news |title=Tarptautinė kritika dėl R.Žemaitaičio stiprėja: pasisakė ir Izraelis |url=https://www.15min.lt/naujiena/aktualu/lietuva/tarptautine-kritika-del-r-zemaitaicio-stipreja-pasisake-ir-izraelis-56-2339826 |work=15min.lt |date=2024-11-09 |language=lt |access-date=10 November 2024 |archive-date=12 November 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241112221551/https://www.15min.lt/naujiena/aktualu/lietuva/tarptautine-kritika-del-r-zemaitaicio-stipreja-pasisake-ir-izraelis-56-2339826 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Background==
The [[2020 Lithuanian parliamentary election|2020 election]] was won by the [[Homeland Union]], which formed a coalition with the [[Liberals' Movement (Lithuania)|Liberal Movement]] and [[Freedom Party (Lithuania)|Freedom Party]]. The [[Šimonytė Cabinet]] was appointed by President [[Gitanas Nausėda]] on 7 December 2020, after the Seimas approved [[Ingrida Šimonytė]] as Prime Minister.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020 |title=Lietuvos Respublikos Prezidentas |url=https://www.lrp.lt/data/public/uploads/2020/12/2020-12-07-nr.-1k-459.pdf |access-date=1 August 2024 |website=www.lrp.lt |archive-date=12 January 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210112022643/https://www.lrp.lt/data/public/uploads/2020/12/2020-12-07-nr.-1k-459.pdf |url-status=live }}</ref> On 11 December, the Government program was approved, thus the cabinet officially took office.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.lrt.lt/naujienos/lietuvoje/2/1296197/lietuva-turi-nauja-vyriausybe-seimas-pritare-programai-prisieks-nauji-ministrai|title = Lietuva turi naują Vyriausybę – Seimas pritarė programai, prisiekė ministrai|date = 11 December 2020|access-date = 1 August 2024|archive-date = 11 May 2024|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20240511075419/https://www.lrt.lt/naujienos/lietuvoje/2/1296197/lietuva-turi-nauja-vyriausybe-seimas-pritare-programai-prisieks-nauji-ministrai|url-status = live}}</ref> Major domestic and foreign events during the term include the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Lithuania]], [[2020–2021 Belarusian protests]] and the [[Belarus–European Union border crisis]] and the [[Russian invasion of Ukraine]]. 

The [[2023 Lithuanian municipal elections]] were won by the opposition [[Social Democratic Party of Lithuania]], with the Homeland Union at second place, and the Homeland Union candidate Ingrida Šimonytė was defeated by incumbent Gitanas Nausėda in the [[2024 Lithuanian presidential election]] with the largest share of the vote in the history of presidential elections in Lithuania. However, the Homeland Union finished at first place in the [[2024 European Parliament election in Lithuania]], with the Social Democrats at second place. The [[Lithuanian Farmers and Greens Union]], which was the largest party in the Seimas prior to the 2020 parliamentary election, split, with the defectors forming the [[Union of Democrats "For Lithuania"]], and finished third in the 2023 municipal and the 2024 [[European Parliament]] elections.

Other notable changes in the political landscape since 2020 included the establishment of [[Dawn of Nemunas]], led by presidential candidate [[Remigijus Žemaitaitis]] after his expulsion from [[Freedom and Justice]] due to his antisemitic statements, which grew in popularity after the 2024 presidential election, as well as the poor performance of the [[Labour Party (Lithuania)|Labour Party]], which failed to win a deputy in the European Parliament for the first time since its establishment in 2003, and which was subsequently abandoned by several of the party's members of parliament, which led to the collapse of the party's parliamentary group.<ref>{{cite web |title=Seimo narys Bukauskas palieka Darbo partiją, rinkimuose dalyvaus savarankiškai |url=https://www.lrt.lt/naujienos/lietuvoje/2/2324495/seimo-narys-bukauskas-palieka-darbo-partija-rinkimuose-dalyvaus-savarankiskai |website=LRT |language=lt |date=22 July 2024 |quote=Per kelis mėnesius iš DP pasitraukė buvęs jos pirmininkas pastaraisiais metais Seimo narys Andrius Mazuronis, parlamentarai Viktoras Fiodorovas, Ieva Kačinskaitė-Urbonienė, Valdemaras Valkiūnas. Dėl to subyrėjo „darbiečių" frakcija parlamente.  Po šių pokyčių Darbo partijos steigėjas ir ilgametis jos vadovas, buvęs europarlamentaras V. ===Electoral seat allocation===

There were 1740 candidates running for seats in the parliament, including 8 mayors, 4 MEPs and 128 current members of the Seimas.<ref name="LRT system 24">{{citation |url=https://www.lrt.lt/en/news-in-english/19/2354261/lithuania-s-seimas-elections-15-party-lists-and-1-740-candidates-register-to-run|title=Lithuania's Seimas elections: 15 party lists and 1,740 candidates register to run|publisher=LRT|date=6 September 2024}}</ref> Additionally, 700 politicians vied for seats in 71 single-mandate constituencies.<ref name="LRT system 24"/>

Half of the seats in the 141-member parliament are allocated to [[at-large]] party lists that will clear the 5-percent threshold.<ref name="LRT system 24"/>

===Changes to the constitution and new electoral code===

After passing amendment of Constitution's article № 56 in April 2022, to be eligible for election, candidates must be at least 21 years old on the election day, not under allegiance to a foreign state and permanently reside in Lithuania,<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://e-seimas.lrs.lt/portal/legalAct/lt/TAD/ee8f7da0c16d11ec9f0095b4d96fd400?positionInSearchResults=7&searchModelUUID=02db2542-79e2-4646-8ada-0cd5423d1fb5 | title=XIV-1030 Lietuvos Respublikos Konstitucijos 56 straipsnio pakeitimo įstatymas | access-date=28 April 2022 | archive-date=28 April 2022 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220428185854/https://e-seimas.lrs.lt/portal/legalAct/lt/TAD/ee8f7da0c16d11ec9f0095b4d96fd400?positionInSearchResults=7&searchModelUUID=02db2542-79e2-4646-8ada-0cd5423d1fb5 | url-status=live }}</ref> while previously, candidates needed to be at least 25 years old on the election day. Persons serving or due to serve a sentence imposed by the court 65 days before the election are not eligible. Also, judges, citizens performing military service, and servicemen of professional military service and officials of statutory institutions and establishments may not run for election.<ref name="SeimasLaw" /> In addition, a person who has been removed from office through impeachment may be elected after 10 year period after impeachment.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://e-seimas.lrs.lt/portal/legalAct/lt/TAD/bd1c7e80c16d11ec9f0095b4d96fd400?positionInSearchResults=9&searchModelUUID=02db2542-79e2-4646-8ada-0cd5423d1fb5 | title=XIV-1029 Lietuvos Respublikos Konstitucijos 74 straipsnio pakeitimo įstatymas | access-date=28 April 2022 | archive-date=28 April 2022 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220428185846/https://e-seimas.lrs.lt/portal/legalAct/lt/TAD/bd1c7e80c16d11ec9f0095b4d96fd400?positionInSearchResults=9&searchModelUUID=02db2542-79e2-4646-8ada-0cd5423d1fb5 | url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.delfi.lt/news/daily/lithuania/konstitucinis-teismas-r-paksui-vilties-nesuteike.d?id=64323400 |language=Lithuanian |title=Konstitucinis Teismas R. Paksui vilties nesuteikė |trans-title=The constitutional court did not give R. Paksas hope |date=20 March 2014 |website=Delfi.lt |access-date=31 July 2015 |archive-date=18 March 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220318023937/https://www.delfi.lt/news/daily/lithuania/konstitucinis-teismas-r-paksui-vilties-nesuteike.d?id=64323400 |url-status=live }}</ref>

The Electoral Code was adopted on 23 June 2022. The Code replaced various electoral acts and introduced several changes to the parliamentary elections. It allowed the establishment more than one overseas constituency and removed a ban of political parties' campaigning on Saturdays (e. g. one day prior to the election).<ref>{{cite web | url=https://e-seimas.lrs.lt/portal/legalAct/lt/TAP/aad3d3909a2111ec9e62f960e3ee1cb6?jfwid=-ggcghcmh | title=Rinkimų kodekso patvirtinimo, įsigaliojimo ir įgyvendinimo konstitucinio įstatymo projektas + Rin }}</ref> The Electoral Code introduced changes for members to be elected in single-member constituencies. Prior to change, leading candidates needed to obtain over 50 per cent of all votes (including invalid ballots) to be elected in the first round. After the change, leading candidates needed to obtain just over 20 per cent of votes from all registered voters in a particular single-member constituency.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.delfi.lt/news/daily/politics/pries-pat-rinkimus-sujudimas-del-vienmandaciu-konservatoriu-pusen-skrieja-rimtas-kaltinimas-120050672 | title=Prieš pat rinkimus – sujudimas dėl vienmandačių: Konservatorių pusėn skrieja rimtas kaltinimas | access-date=19 November 2024 | archive-date=24 September 2024 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240924160059/https://www.delfi.lt/news/daily/politics/pries-pat-rinkimus-sujudimas-del-vienmandaciu-konservatoriu-pusen-skrieja-rimtas-kaltinimas-120050672 | url-status=live }}</ref>

==Electoral system==
{{main|Elections in Lithuania}}

The Seimas has 141 members, elected to a four-year term in [[parallel voting]], with 71 members elected in single-seat [[constituency|constituencies]] using the [[two-round system]] and 70 members elected by [[proportional representation]].<ref name="SeimasLaw">{{cite web |url=http://www3.lrs.lt/pls/inter3/dokpaieska.showdoc_l?p_id=483680 |title=Law on Elections to the Seimas |date=14 May 2015 |publisher=Seimas of the Republic of Lithuania |access-date=31 July 2015 |archive-date=7 April 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220407012050/https://www3.lrs.lt/pls/inter3/dokpaieska.showdoc_l?p_id=483680 |url-status=live }}</ref> The voting in the elections is open to all citizens of Lithuania who are at least 18 years old.

Parliament members in the 71 single-seat constituencies are elected in a majority vote; or per a new provision instituted in 2022, a leading candidate in a constituency is directly elected if they receive the support of one fifth of registered voters.<ref name="x798">{{cite web | title=Seimo rinkimai 2024. LSDP triumfuoja, antri – TS-LKD, treti – "Nemuno Aušra" | website=Delfi | date=14 October 2024 | url=https://www.delfi.lt/news/daily/politics/seimo-rinkimai-2024-lsdp-triumfuoja-antri-ts-lkd-treti-nemuno-ausra-120056945 | language=lt | access-date=14 October 2024 | quote=Šiuose rinkimuose pirmą kartą norint laimėti vienmandatę nebebūtina surinkti daugiau nei pusę rinkėjų balsų. 2022 m. įsigaliojusiame Rinkimų kodekse atsirado nuostata, kad išrinktu laikomas daugiausia rinkėjų balsų gavęs kandidatas, kuris surenka ne mažiau kaip vieną penktadalį rinkėjų balsų. | archive-date=12 November 2024 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241112225224/https://www.delfi.lt/news/daily/politics/seimo-rinkimai-2024-lsdp-triumfuoja-antri-ts-lkd-treti-nemuno-ausra-120056945 | url-status=live }}</ref> A run-off is held within 15 days, if necessary. The remaining 70 seats are allocated to the participating political parties using the [[largest remainder method]]. Parties normally need to receive at least 5% (7% for multi-party electoral lists) of the votes to be eligible for a seat. Candidates take the seats allocated to their parties based on the preference lists submitted before the election and adjusted by preference votes given by the voters.<ref name="SeimasLaw" />

For the 2024 election, around 2.4 million people were eligible to vote.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://apnews.com/article/lithuania-election-parliament-economy-migrants-751fee38ec78603147eef126699be1c4 |title=Lithuania votes in a weekend general election with many looking for change despite good economy |date=10 October 2024 |work=Associated Press |access-date=10 October 2024}}</ref>

===Debates===
The Lithuanian public broadcaster [[Lithuanian National Radio and Television|LRT]] hosted six televised debate shows, organized together with the [[Central Electoral Commission (Lithuania)|Central Electoral Commission (CEC)]]. According to the CEC these were to be divided into two rounds of three debate shows.<ref name="cecdebates">{{cite web |title=Informacija apie daugiamandatės rinkimų apygardos kandidatų sąrašų atstovų agitacines laidas |publisher=Central Electoral Commission |url=https://www.vrk.lt/daugiamandates-laidu-informacija-2024-seimo |access-date=28 October 2024 |language=lt}}</ref> Each debate included five parties (or electoral lists), and was broadcast on TV and online starting at 9:00 PM. The moderators were LRT journalists Deividas Jursevičius, {{ill|Rasa Tapinienė|lt}}, and {{ill|Nemira Pumprickaitė|lt}}.<ref name="lrtdebates">{{cite news |title=Seimo rinkimai su LRT – laukia 93 debatų ir specialių laidų transliacijos |url=https://www.lrt.lt/naujienos/tavo-lrt/15/2345291/seimo-rinkimai-su-lrt-laukia-93-debatu-ir-specialiu-laidu-transliacijos |access-date=28 October 2024 |work=lrt.lt |date=23 August 2024 |language=lt |archive-date=30 September 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240930113709/https://www.lrt.lt/naujienos/tavo-lrt/15/2345291/seimo-rinkimai-su-lrt-laukia-93-debatu-ir-specialiu-laidu-transliacijos |url-status=live }}</ref>

Furthermore, the youth initiative "Žinau, ką renku" ({{literal translation|I know who I'm voting for}}) hosted a live debate in cooperation with LRT, which was held on 11 October at 8:00 PM in the courtyard of [[Lukiškės Prison]]. All of the leaders of the 15 parties were invited.<ref name="lrtdebates" /> This youth initiative also hosted individual debates for all 71 single-seat constituencies, which were broadcast on the LRT website.<ref name="lrtdebates" />

After the first round of voting on 13 October, 63 of the 71 single-member constituencies remained contested between the top two candidates. Prior to the run-off elections on 27 October to determine the final winners, debates between the run-off candidates were filmed for each of these 63 districts and broadcast on the [[15min]], [[TV3 (Lithuanian TV channel)|TV3]], [[Delfi.lt|Delfi]] and [[Lrytas]] news portals, as well as the website rinkimai2024.lt. These debates were filmed from 16 to 21 October in the studios of Delfi and {{ill|Laisvės TV|lt}}.<ref>{{cite web |title=Informacija apie vienmandačių rinkimų apygardų kandidatų agitacines laidas |url=https://www.vrk.lt/vienmandaciu-laidu-informacija-2024-seimo |publisher=Central Electoral Commission |access-date=28 October 2024 |language=lt}}</ref>

=== Turnout ===

=== Government formation ===

On the night of 14 October, [[Vilija Blinkevičiūtė]] and [[Saulius Skvernelis]], the leaders of the LSDP and the DSVL respectively, agreed to cooperate in the second round and to form a [[coalition government]] afterwards.<ref>{{cite news | title=Socialdemokratai ir S.Skvernelis telkiasi į koaliciją, bet dėl trečiojo brolio lieka intriga | work=15min.lt | date=14 October 2024 | url=https://www.15min.lt/naujiena/aktualu/lietuva/socialdemokratai-laimejo-rinkimus-prasideda-koalicijos-deliones-56-2323416 | language=lt | access-date=14 October 2024}}</ref> [[Lithuanian Farmers and Greens Union|LVŽS]] joined the agreement on 15 October.<ref>{{cite news |last=Platūkytė |first=Domantė |title=Karbauskis Blinkevičiūtę jau vadina būsima premjere, abu sako – sukirsta rankomis |work=LRT |date=15 October 2024 |url=https://www.lrt.lt/naujienos/lietuvoje/2/2386958/karbauskis-blinkeviciute-jau-vadina-busima-premjere-abu-sako-sukirsta-rankomis |language=lt |access-date=19 October 2024 |archive-date=12 November 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241112012530/https://www.lrt.lt/naujienos/lietuvoje/2/2386958/karbauskis-blinkeviciute-jau-vadina-busima-premjere-abu-sako-sukirsta-rankomis |url-status=live }}</ref> Despite the LSDP ruling out coalition with the [[Dawn of Nemunas|PPNA]],<ref>{{cite news | title=Blinkevičiūtė: nei iki rinkimų, nei šiandien nevedame derybų su "Nemuno aušra" | work=LNK | url=https://lnk.lt/straipsniai/Politika/sinkevicius-atsake-ar-sieks-uzsienio-ministro-posto/280981 | access-date=28 October 2024}}</ref> the latter party pledged to support Blinkevičiūtė becoming prime minister.<ref>{{cite news |title=Lithuanians vote in the final round of parliamentary election |work=Associated Press |date=27 October 2024 |url=https://apnews.com/article/lithuania-parliamentary-election-new-government-92531b8be1c945c8f2029d4179de5ba3 |language=en |access-date=27 October 2024}}</ref>

=== Other changes ===

On the day after the second round of the election, [[Gabrielius Landsbergis]] announced his resignation as leader of the Homeland Union and as member of the next Seimas. He endorsed [[Ingrida Šimonytė]], the outgoing Prime Minister, for the party leadership. Landsbergis also lost in the Centras-Žaliakalnis single-member constituency, which he represented since 2016, to [[Simonas Kairys]] of the [[Liberals' Movement (Lithuania)|Liberals' Movement]] and fellow member of the [[Šimonytė Cabinet]].<ref name="gabrielover">{{cite web |last1=Samoškaitė |first1=Eglė |title=Landsbergis traukiasi iš konservatorių pirmininko pareigų ir nedirbs Seime |url=https://www.lrt.lt/naujienos/lietuvoje/2/2399005/landsbergis-traukiasi-is-konservatoriu-pirmininko-pareigu-ir-nedirbs-seime |website=LRT |language=lt |date=28 October 2024}}</ref> [[Radvilė Morkūnaitė-Mikulėnienė]] became interim leader of the party.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Perminas |first1=Paulius |title=Morkūnaitė-Mikulėnienė named interim chair of TS-LKD after Landsbergis' resignation |url=https://www.lrt.lt/en/news-in-english/19/2399681/morkunaite-mikuleniene-named-interim-chair-of-ts-lkd-after-landsbergis-resignation |work=lrt.lt |date=28 October 2024 |language=en |access-date=28 October 2024 |archive-date=12 November 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241112210018/https://www.lrt.lt/en/news-in-english/19/2399681/morkunaite-mikuleniene-named-interim-chair-of-ts-lkd-after-landsbergis-resignation |url-status=live }}</ref>

After the second round of the election, various parties lost all their seats in the Seimas, including the [[Labour Party (Lithuania)|Labour Party]] and the [[Lithuanian Regions Party]]. On the other hand, the LSDP won 52 seats, its best ever result. It also won single-member constituencies in [[Kaunas]] for the first time since 1992.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://m.kauno.diena.lt/naujienos/lietuva/politika/lietuva-paraudonavo-1197805 | title=Lietuva paraudonavo: Vajus "Visi prieš vieną" suveikė |work=KaunoDiena.lt | date=29 October 2024 }}</ref>

On 3 November, [[Aušrinė Armonaitė]] announced her resignation as leader of the [[Freedom Party (Lithuania)|Freedom Party]] after it failed to win any seats.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.alfa.lt/aktualijos/politika/po-nesekmingu-rinkimu-is-laisves-partijos-vadoves-posto-traukiasi-a-armonaite/-40327190/ | title=Po nesėkmingų rinkimų iš Laisvės partijos vadovės posto traukiasi A.
