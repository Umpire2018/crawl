Presidential elections were held in [[Mauritania]] on 29 June 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-04-20 |title=Mauritanian Presidential Election Set For June 29 |url=https://www.barrons.com/news/mauritanian-presidential-election-set-for-june-29-13acef47 |access-date=2024-04-21 |website=www.barrons.com |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-04-21 |title=Mauritanie: l'élection présidentielle fixée au 29 juin |url=https://afrique.le360.ma/politique/mauritanie-lelection-presidentielle-fixee-au-29-juin_A7EENTJ2QZB3TG7UVVBGWJ2AGI/ |access-date=2024-04-21 |website=Le 360 Afrique |language=fr}}</ref> Incumbent [[Mohamed Ould Ghazouani]] won re-election for his second and final term as [[President of Mauritania|president]], increasing his vote share by four points.

==Electoral system==
Under Article 26 of the [[Constitution of Mauritania|constitution]] the [[List of heads of state of Mauritania|president]] is elected for a five-year term using the [[two-round system]]. If no candidate receives an absolute majority of the vote in the first round, a second round is held two weeks later between the two candidates who received the most votes.<ref name=Constitution>{{Cite web |title=دستور الجمهورية الإسلامية الموريتانية |trans-title=Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Mauritania |url=https://parlement.mr/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/Constitution-de-la-RIM-ar_8.pdf |access-date=2024-05-16 |website=Parliament of Mauritania |language=ar |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221117072420/https://parlement.mr/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/Constitution-de-la-RIM-ar_8.pdf |archive-date=2022-11-17}}</ref>  

Candidacy is restricted to citizens by birth aged between 40 and 75 (on the day of the first round) who have not had their civil and political rights removed. Article 23 also stipulates that the President has to be a Muslim. Article 28 establishes a [[term limit]] of two mandates, allowing the President to only be re-elected once.<ref name=Constitution/>  

The election of a new president is required to take place between 30 and 45 days before the expiration of the term of the incumbent president.<ref name=Constitution/>  

For this election, campaigning was held from 14 June to 27 June.<ref name="f24">{{cite news |date=2024-04-25 |url=https://www.france24.com/en/africa/20240425-mauritania-president-announces-second-term-bid-for-june-election |title=Mauritania president announces second term bid for June election |language=en |access-date=2024-04-25 |publisher=France 24}}</ref> Polling stations were open from 07:00 to 19:00 local time ([[Greenwich Mean Time|GMT]]).<ref>{{Cite web |title=Mauritania heads to polls as incumbent Ghazouani expected to secure second term |url=https://www.france24.com/en/africa/20240629-mauritania-heads-to-polls-as-incumbent-ghazouani-expected-to-secure-second-term |access-date=29 June 2024 |website=France 24 |date=29 June 2024 |language=en}}</ref>

==Campaign==
Ghazouani campaigned in part on a security platform, while his opponents accused his government of corruption and clientelism, with Dah Abeid denouncing what he called “a catastrophic management of the state”.<ref>{{cite news |date=27 June 2024 |url=https://apnews.com/article/mauritania-election-sahel-slavery-migration-305e303f3e65a9eb0161682c12b62368 |title=Mauritania goes to the polls with a regional security crisis and economic concerns among the issues |language=en |access-date=27 June 2024 |publisher=Associated Press}}</ref> Other issues during the election included unemployment, poverty and access to utilities such as water and electricity. Observers from the [[African Union]] and the United States monitored the vote.<ref>{{cite news |date=28 June 2024 |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/6/28/whats-at-stake-in-mauritanias-presidential-election |title=What's at stake in Mauritania's presidential election? |language=en |access-date=28 June 2024 |publisher=Al Jazeera}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=30 June 2024 |url=https://apnews.com/article/mauritania-vote-sahel-jihadism-gas-829815a5a67dbcec5db74a9ec4200d42 |title=Mauritania's President Mohamed Ould Ghazouani on track for reelection, provisional results show |language=en |access-date=30 June 2024 |publisher=Associated Press}}</ref>

==Aftermath==
{{Update|section|date=July 2024}}
After the preliminary results showed Ghazouani leading in the election, Dah Abeid said that he would not recognize the results of “Ghazouani’s [[National Independent Election Commission|Ceni]]” and would hold "peaceful" demonstrations in the streets.<ref>{{cite news |date=30 June 2024 |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/6/29/mauritanians-vote-in-presidential-election-with-incumbent-expected-to-win |title=Mauritania's President Ghazouani leads in presidential election |language=en |access-date=1 July 2024 |publisher=Al Jazeera}}</ref> A spokesperson for the electoral commission said it had recorded no anomalies or complaints regarding the vote.<ref>{{cite news |date=2 July 2024 |url=https://www.africanews.com/2024/07/02/mauritanias-president-ghazouani-re-elected/ |title=Mauritania's President Ghazouani re-elected |language=en |access-date=2 July 2024 |publisher=Africanews}}</ref>

On 2 July, three people were killed during clashes between the security forces and protesters demonstrating against the election results in [[Kaédi]], which led authorities to block mobile internet services nationwide. Protests were also held in [[Nouadhibou]], [[Rosso]],  [[Zouérate]] and [[Boghé]].<ref>{{cite news |date=3 July 2024 |url=https://apnews.com/article/mauritania-election-ghazouani-abeid-protest-7165f12ca95573c91dfaf917ebbbe4dd |title=Clashes between police and protesters disputing Mauritania's presidential election result kill 3d |language=en |access-date=3 July 2024 |publisher=Associated Press}}</ref> On July 4, the Constitutional Council confirmed Ghazouani as the winner of the election.<ref>{{cite news |date=5 July 2024 |url=https://www.jeuneafrique.com/1585057/politique/en-mauritanie-la-reelection-de-mohamed-ould-cheikh-el-ghazouani-confirmee-par-le-conseil-constitutionnel/ |title=En Mauritanie, la réélection de Mohamed Ould Cheikh El Ghazouani confirmée par le Conseil constitutionnel |language=fr |access-date=6 July 2024 |publisher=Jeune Afrique}}</ref>
