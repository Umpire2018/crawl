General elections were held in [[Mexico]] on 2 June 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Elección Federal 2024 |url=https://ine.mx/voto-y-elecciones/elecciones-2024/eleccion-federal-2024/ |access-date=15 November 2023 |website=Instituto Nacional Electoral |language=es |archive-date=8 December 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231208235951/https://ine.mx/voto-y-elecciones/elecciones-2024/eleccion-federal-2024/ |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=ELECCIONES 2024: Proceso Electoral en números |url=https://www.ine.mx/transparencia/proceso-electoral-en-numeros/ |website=Instituto Nacional Electoral |access-date=15 November 2023 |date=13 November 2023 |archive-date=15 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231115144311/https://www.ine.mx/transparencia/proceso-electoral-en-numeros/ |url-status=live}}</ref> Voters elected a new [[president of Mexico|president]] to serve a six-year term, all 500 members of the [[Chamber of Deputies (Mexico)|Chamber of Deputies]], and all 128 members of the [[Senate of the Republic (Mexico)|Senate of the Republic]]. These elections took place concurrently with the [[2024 Mexican local elections|2024 state elections]].

[[Claudia Sheinbaum]], a member of the [[left-wing]] political party [[Morena (political party)|Morena]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.france24.com/en/americas/20240603-sheinbaum-set-to-win-mexico-election-becoming-first-female-president |title=Ruling leftist party candidate Sheinbaum elected Mexico's first female president |date=3 June 2024 |access-date=3 June 2024 |archive-date=3 June 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240603063457/https://www.france24.com/en/americas/20240603-sheinbaum-set-to-win-mexico-election-becoming-first-female-president |url-status=live}}</ref> was widely regarded by her party as the top contender to succeed President [[Andrés Manuel López Obrador]] and ultimately secured the nomination of the ruling coalition, [[Sigamos Haciendo Historia]]. [[Xóchitl Gálvez]] emerged as the frontrunner of [[Fuerza y Corazón por México]] following a surge in popularity due to criticisms from López Obrador.<ref>{{Cite web |date=20 July 2023 |title=As a child, she sold street tamales; a senator now, she's shaking up Mexico's presidential race. |url=https://apnews.com/article/mexico-politics-xochitl-galvez-andres-manuel-lopez-obrador-present-politics-30ed04316ff56de326754d13b8cef1dd |access-date=29 February 2024 |website=AP News |language=en |archive-date=29 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240229152735/https://apnews.com/article/mexico-politics-xochitl-galvez-andres-manuel-lopez-obrador-present-politics-30ed04316ff56de326754d13b8cef1dd |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Shortell |first=David |date=22 July 2023 |title=Mexico's president is on the attack. == Background ==

=== Political background ===
In the [[2018 Mexican general election|2018 election]], [[Andrés Manuel López Obrador]], a three-time presidential candidate and founder of the [[Morena (political party)|National Regeneration Movement]] (Morena), ran as the candidate of the [[Juntos Haremos Historia]] alliance, which included Morena, the [[Labor Party (Mexico)|Labor Party]] (PT), and the [[Social Encounter Party]] (PES). He won in a landslide, securing 54.71% of the vote and defeating his closest rival, [[Ricardo Anaya]] of the [[Por México al Frente]] coalition, by nearly 31 percentage points. The Juntos Haremos Historia alliance also secured majorities in the [[Chamber of Deputies (Mexico)|Chamber of Deputies]] and the [[Senate of the Republic (Mexico)|Senate]].

As president, López Obrador expanded the Mexican welfare state with programs aimed at the elderly and low-income students, raised the minimum wage significantly, and invested heavily in infrastructure projects such as the [[Tren Maya]] and the [[Felipe Ángeles International Airport]].<ref name=":21" /> He combatted corruption by implementing [[austerity]] measures, including salary caps, budget cuts across the government, and the dismissal of numerous government employees.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Romero |first=Luis Gómez |date=2019-02-09 |title=López Obrador Takes on Corruption and Poverty in Mexico Through Austerity |url=https://psmag.com/economics/combatting-corruption-in-mexico-through-austerity/ |access-date=2025-01-14 |website=Pacific Standard |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news achieved a poverty reduction, it faced accusations of [[democratic backsliding]] and undermining institutional independence, including frequent attacks on journalists and judges during his morning === Political violence ===
{{Further|List of politicians killed during the 2024 Mexican elections}}

Political violence in Mexico typically escalates during election season, with candidates often facing threats, abductions, assaults, or assassinations. While this violence is most common at the municipal level, it can also occur at the federal level.<ref>{{Cite journal |last=Hernández Huerta |first=Víctor Antonio |date=2020 |title=Candidatos asesinados en México, ¿competencia electoral o violencia criminal? |url=https://www.scielo.org.mx/scielo.php?script=sci_arttext&pid=S1665-20372020000200008 |journal=Política y gobierno |language=es |volume=27 |issue=2 |issn=1665-2037}}</ref>

During the 2021 elections, 102 politicians were killed, including 36 who were nominees or candidates for public office.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Herrera |first=Por Olivia Vázquez |date=2024-01-28 |title=Elecciones 2024 se tiñen de rojo: ¿Cuántos políticos y precandidatos han sido asesinados en México? |url=https://www.infobae.com/mexico/2024/01/28/elecciones-2024-se-tinen-de-rojo-cuantos-politicos-y-precandidatos-han-sido-asesinados-en-mexico/ |access-date=2024-08-20 |website=infobae |language=es-ES}}</ref>

By January 2024, before the campaigning period started, multiple aspiring candidates for political office had been killed.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Stevenson |first=Mark |date=28 February 2024 |title=Two mayoral hopefuls of a Mexican city are shot dead within hours of each other |url=https://apnews.com/article/mexico-election-candidates-killed-mayor-66e20fe665a25a4974637d8369c7a740 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240302133719/https://apnews.com/article/mexico-election-candidates-killed-mayor-66e20fe665a25a4974637d8369c7a740 |archive-date=2 March 2024 |access-date=2 March 2024 |website=Associated Press |language=en}}</ref> The government provided security guards to around 560 candidates and election officials.<ref name="aj37">{{Cite news |title=Mayoral candidate murdered in Mexico two days before election |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/6/1/mayoral-candidate-murdered-in-mexico-two-days-before-vote |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240601092000/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/6/1/mayoral-candidate-murdered-in-mexico-two-days-before-vote |archive-date=1 June 2024 |access-date=1 June 2024 |work=Al Jazeera |language=en}}</ref> Around 27,000 [[Mexican Armed Forces|Armed Forces]] and [[National Guard (Mexico)|National Guard]] personnel were deployed to secure the electoral process.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Elections Suspended In Two Violent Mexico Municipalities |url=https://www.barrons.com/news/elections-suspended-in-two-violent-mexico-municipalities-192caf83 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240602004639/https://www.barrons.com/news/elections-suspended-in-two-violent-mexico-municipalities-192caf83 |archive-date=2 June 2024 |access-date=2 June 2024 |work=Barron's |language=en}}</ref> By May 2024, the candidate death toll had risen to 37.<ref name="aj37" />

== Electoral system ==
The [[Instituto Nacional Electoral|National Electoral Institute]] (INE) oversees federal elections in Mexico. Its responsibilities include organizing election day logistics, producing and distributing electoral materials, counting votes, and certifying the election results.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Ley General de Instituciones y Procedimientos Electorales |url=https://www.diputados.gob.mx/LeyesBiblio/pdf/LGIPE.pdf |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240602051058/https://www.diputados.gob.mx/LeyesBiblio/pdf/LGIPE.pdf |archive-date=2 June 2024 |access-date=2 June 2024 |website=diputados.gob.mx}}</ref>

Voters must present their voter ID at polling stations to cast their ballots. Voters with physical limitations or disabilities and voters in preventive detention can vote in advance, with electoral materials sent to the voter.<ref name=":10">{{Cite web |date=4 October 2023 |title=¿Sabías que en las elecciones 2024 se implementará el voto anticipado para personas con alguna discapacidad o limitación física? === Presidential election ===
The president is elected by [[plurality voting]] in a single round; there is no provision for a second run-off round.<ref>[http://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/2246/ Mexico] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180707182242/http://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/2246/|date=7 July 2018}} IFES</ref> Article 83 of the [[Mexican Constitution]] limits the president to a single six-year term, called a ''[[Sexenio (Mexico)|sexenio]]''; no one who has served as president, even on a caretaker basis, may run for or serve in the office again.<ref>''Constitución Politica de los Estados Unidos Mexicanos. Artículo 83. 1917 (México).''</ref> The new president will be sworn in on 1 October.<ref>{{cite news |date=20 January 2024 |title=Por qué junio y octubre son las nuevas fechas clave para las elecciones 2024 de México |url=https://cnnespanol.cnn.com/2024/01/20/junio-octubre-nuevas-fechas-clave-elecciones-2024-mexico-orix/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240326033457/https://cnnespanol.cnn.com/2024/01/20/junio-octubre-nuevas-fechas-clave-elecciones-2024-mexico-orix/ |archive-date=26 March 2024 |access-date=3 June 2024 |agency=CNN en Español}}</ref>

=== Legislative elections ===
{{main|2024 Mexican Senate election|2024 Mexican Chamber of Deputies election}}

The 500 members of the [[Chamber of Deputies (Mexico)|Chamber of Deputies]] are elected in two ways: 300 are elected in [[Federal electoral districts of Mexico|single-member constituencies]] by plurality vote, and the remaining 200 are elected by [[proportional representation]] in [[Electoral regions of Mexico|five multi-member districts]], with seats divided according to [[Largest remainders method|Hamilton's method]]. No party is permitted to hold more than 300 seats.<ref name="auto2">{{cite web |date=22 April 2017 |title=The Mexican Electoral System |url=https://www.ine.mx/the-mexican-electoral-system/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230908014945/https://www.ine.mx/the-mexican-electoral-system/ |archive-date=8 September 2023 |access-date=8 September 2023 |website=Instituto Nacional Electoral}}</ref><ref name="auto1">{{cite web |title=Constitution, Arts. 52 et seq. |url=https://www.diputados.gob.mx/LeyesBiblio/pdf/CPEUM.pdf |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230908070641/http://www.diputados.gob.mx/LeyesBiblio/pdf/CPEUM.pdf |archive-date=8 September 2023 |access-date=8 September 2023}}</ref> Deputies are elected for three-year terms and will serve in the [[LXVI Legislature of the Mexican Congress|66th Congress]].<ref>{{cite web |date=7 September 2023 |title=No habrá doble Congreso en agosto de 2024; se corrigió el error que pondría en jaque al Legislativo |url=https://comunicacionsocial.diputados.gob.mx/revista/index.php/a-profundidad/no-habra-doble-congreso-en-agosto-de-2024-se-corrigio-el-error-que-pondria-en-jaque-al-legislativo |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240601042825/https://comunicacionsocial.diputados.gob.mx/revista/index.php/a-profundidad/no-habra-doble-congreso-en-agosto-de-2024-se-corrigio-el-error-que-pondria-en-jaque-al-legislativo |archive-date=1 June 2024 |access-date=5 June 2024 |publisher=Cámara de Diputados}}</ref>

The 128 members of the [[Senate of the Republic (Mexico)|Senate]] are also elected in two ways: 96 are elected in 32 three-seat constituencies based on the country's [[States of Mexico|states]], and == Political parties and coalitions ==
{{See also|List of political parties in Mexico}}
Seven national political parties were registered with the INE and were eligible to participate in the federal elections: the [[National Action Party (Mexico)|National Action Party]] (PAN), the [[Institutional Revolutionary Party]] (PRI), the [[Party of the Democratic Revolution]] (PRD), the [[Labor Party (Mexico)|Labor Party]] (PT), the [[Ecologist Green Party of Mexico]] (PVEM), [[Citizens' Movement (Mexico)|Citizens' Movement]] (MC) and the [[Morena (political party)|National Regeneration Movement]] (Morena).<ref>{{Cite web |title=Partidos Políticos Nacionales |url=https://portal.ine.mx/actores-politicos/partidos-politicos-nacionales/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240401035900/https://portal.ine.mx/actores-politicos/partidos-politicos-nacionales/ |archive-date=1 April 2024 |access-date=31 May 2024 |website=Instituto Nacional Electoral |language=es-MX}}</ref>

The General Law of Political Parties stipulates that national political parties can form coalitions for elections by submitting a coalition agreement to the electoral authority. Parties cannot join coalitions in their first election.<ref name=":16">{{Cite web |date=2023-03-02 |title=Ley General de los Partidos Políticos |url=https://www.diputados.gob.mx/LeyesBiblio/pdf/LGPP.pdf |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240529170035/https://www.diputados.gob.mx/LeyesBiblio/pdf/LGPP.pdf |archive-date=29 May 2024 |access-date=2024-06-13 |website=diputados.gob.mx}}</ref> All parties were eligible to join an alliance in this election.

Parties failing to reach 3% of the popular vote in the presidential or legislative elections will lose their registration as a national political party.<ref name=":16" />

=== Fuerza y Corazón por México ===
Frente Amplio por México required prospective candidates to gather 150,000 signatures, including at least 1,000 signatures from 17 of the country's 32 states. The selection process then included a series of polls to narrow the field, with the first one, conducted from 11 to 14 August, aimed to identify the top three candidates. A second poll was scheduled for 27–30 August, contributing 50% of the points needed for nomination, with the remaining 50% determined through an online survey on 3 September.<ref>{{Cite web |last1=Monroy |first1=Jorge |last2=Lugo |first2=Eric |date=26 June 2023 |title=Frente Amplio por México arrancará el 4 de julio la búsqueda de su aspirante presidencial |url=https://www.eleconomista.com.mx/politica/Frente-Amplio-por-Mexico-arrancara-el-4-de-julio-la-busqueda-de-su-aspirante-presidencial-20230626-0051.html |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230722003059/https://www.eleconomista.com.mx/politica/Frente-Amplio-por-Mexico-arrancara-el-4-de-julio-la-busqueda-de-su-aspirante-presidencial-20230626-0051.html |archive-date=22 July 2023 |access-date=29 February 2024 |website=El Economista}}</ref>

The registration deadline for individuals wishing to run for president as independent candidates (i.e., without the backing of a registered party) expired on 7 September 2023.<ref>{{cite web |title=Candidaturas Independientes 2024 |url=https://www.ine.mx/candidaturasindependientes/ |website=Instituto Nacional Electoral |date=20 July 2023 |access-date=8 September 2023 |archive-date=7 September 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230907204102/https://www.ine.mx/candidaturasindependientes/ |url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Issues ===

==== Security ====
Polling indicated that crime and violence ranked high on voters' concerns in the election.<ref>{{Cite web |date=30 January 2024 |title=¿Qué le preocupa al mundo y a los mexicanos en 2024? |url=https://businessinsider.mx/que-le-preocupa-al-mundo-a-los-mexicanos-2024-estudio-ipsos/ |access-date=1 March 2024 |website=Business Insider México |language=es |archive-date=1 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240301202536/https://businessinsider.mx/que-le-preocupa-al-mundo-a-los-mexicanos-2024-estudio-ipsos/ |url-status=live}}</ref> During the outgoing presidency of [[Andrés Manuel López Obrador]], Mexico experienced one of its bloodiest periods. From 2018 to 2022, the number of [[List of countries by intentional homicide rate|intentional homicides]] surpassed 30,000, with the peak in 2020 at 36,773 homicides.<ref>{{Cite web |title=México – Homicidios Intencionados 2022 |url=https://datosmacro.expansion.com/demografia/homicidios/mexico |access-date=2 March 2024 |website=Datosmacro.com |language=es |archive-date=2 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240302062315/https://datosmacro.expansion.com/demografia/homicidios/mexico |url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=":6">{{Cite web |title=Informe de Seguridad |url=http://www.gob.mx/sspc/galerias/informe-de-seguridad-358359?idiom=es |access-date=1 March 2024 |website=gob.mx |language=es |archive-date=3 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240303033536/https://www.gob.mx/sspc/galerias/informe-de-seguridad-358359?idiom=es |url-status=live}}</ref> However, since 2020, homicide rates have decreased. In 2023, homicides fell below 30,000 for the first time since 2018, totaling 29,675.<ref>{{Cite web |date=16 January 2024 |title=Los homicidios dolosos caen un 4.18% en 2023 en México, primera vez por debajo de los 30,000 |url=https://www.forbes.com.mx/los-homicidios-dolosos-caen-un-4-18-en-2023-en-mexico-primera-vez-por-debajo-de-los-30000/ |access-date=1 March 2024 |website=Forbes México |language=es-MX |archive-date=1 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240301202535/https://www.forbes.com.mx/los-homicidios-dolosos-caen-un-4-18-en-2023-en-mexico-primera-vez-por-debajo-de-los-30000/ |url-status=live}}</ref> Despite this decrease, the numbers remain higher than recorded between 1990 and 2017.<ref name=":6" />

López Obrador tackled this issue by endorsing the "[[Abrazos, no balazos|hugs, not bullets]]" slogan and establishing the civilian-led [[National Guard (Mexico)|National Guard]]. Some have raised suspicions that López Obrador's administration is underreporting intentional homicides, with some cases possibly being reclassified as having undetermined intentions to bring the figure down.<ref>{{Cite web |date=25 January 2024 |title=Mexico marks another record-breaking year for murders |url=https://houstonherald.com/2024/01/mexico-marks-another-record-breaking-year-for-murders/ |access-date=1 March 2024 |website=Houston Herald |language=en-US |archive-date=1 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240301202534/https://houstonherald.com/2024/01/mexico-marks-another-record-breaking-year-for-murders/ |url-status=live}}</ref>

Xóchitl Gálvez highlighted that security would be a top priority in her government. She opposed López Obrador's "hugs, not bullets" approach. She proposed several measures to strengthen state police forces nationwide, including raising their salaries to MXN 20,000 per month, building a university for aspiring police officers, and providing state governments with increased economic resources and advanced technology to combat crime.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Figueroa |first=Héctor |date=5 March 2024 |title=Xóchitl Gálvez construirá la Gálvez also suggested doubling the numbers of prosecutors and judges and the size of the National Guard,<ref>{{Cite web |date=4 March 2024 |title=Xóchitl Gálvez presenta sus 15 propuestas de seguridad Claudia Sheinbaum has expressed her commitment to replicating her success as [[Head of Government of Mexico City]], where, in 2023, her policies brought down intentional homicides to their lowest level since 1989.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2 August 2023 |title=Claudia Sheinbaum destaca tasa más baja de homicidios dolosos desde 1989 en CDMX |url=https://heraldodemexico.com.mx/nacional/2023/8/2/claudia-sheinbaum-destaca-tasa-mas-baja-de-homicidios-dolosos-desde-1989-en-cdmx-527140.html |access-date=2 March 2024 |website=El Heraldo de México |language=es |archive-date=2 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240302062313/https://heraldodemexico.com.mx/nacional/2023/8/2/claudia-sheinbaum-destaca-tasa-mas-baja-de-homicidios-dolosos-desde-1989-en-cdmx-527140.html |url-status=live}}</ref> She explained that she would follow a similar structure, emphasizing zero impunity by strengthening coordination among the National Guard, state police forces, and prosecutors and improving intelligence and investigative services. ==== Social programs ====
President López Obrador implemented various social programs, the largest being the Pension for the Well-being of Older People (''Pensión para el Bienestar de las Personas Adultas Mayores''), targeting individuals aged 65 and above.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Pension for the Well-being of Older People / Pensión para el Bienestar de las Personas Adultas Mayores (2019–) – Non-contributory pensions – Non-contributory pensions programmes in Latin America and the Caribbean database |url=https://dds.cepal.org/bpsnc/programme?id=173 |access-date=2 March 2024 |website=dds.cepal.org |archive-date=2 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240302094059/https://dds.cepal.org/bpsnc/programme?id=173 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Soto |first=Dulce |date=13 September 2023 |title=Gasto en programas sociales creció 30% con AMLO, pero mantiene pobreza extrema |url=https://politica.expansion.mx/mexico/2023/09/12/gasto-en-programas-sociales-crecio-30-con-amlo |access-date=2 March 2024 |website=ADNPolítico |language=es |archive-date=2 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240302094059/https://politica.expansion.mx/mexico/2023/09/12/gasto-en-programas-sociales-crecio-30-con-amlo |url-status=live}}</ref> These programs are very popular among voters.<ref>{{Cite web |date=4 January 2024 |title=Approval Tracker: Mexico's President AMLO |url=https://www.as-coa.org/articles/approval-tracker-mexicos-president-amlo |access-date=2 March 2024 |website=www.as-coa.org |language=en |archive-date=18 February 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230218221357/https://www.as-coa.org/articles/approval-tracker-mexicos-president-amlo |url-status=live}}</ref>

Both Xóchitl Gálvez and Claudia Sheinbaum expressed their support for the social programs established by the outgoing administration and pledged not to abolish them. Gálvez proposed reducing the age eligibility for the Pension for the Well-being of Older People from 65 to 60.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2 March 2024 |title=Xóchitl Gálvez firma con su sangre: 'No se van a eliminar los programas sociales' |url=https://www.elfinanciero.com.mx/elecciones-mexico-2024/2024/03/01/xochitl-galvez-primer-dia-de-campana-guanajuato-firma-con-sangre/ |access-date=2 March 2024 |website=El Financiero |language=es |archive-date=2 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240302094059/https://www.elfinanciero.com.mx/elecciones-mexico-2024/2024/03/01/xochitl-galvez-primer-dia-de-campana-guanajuato-firma-con-sangre/ |url-status=live}}</ref> Sheinbaum pledged to ensure that any increases to the pensions from all social programs will always be above the inflation rate.<ref name=":8" />

Sheinbaum has proposed two new social programs: one aimed at students from [[preschool]] to [[secondary education]]<ref>{{Cite web |last=Carrillo |first=Luis |title=Propone Claudia Sheinbaum becas para niños de nivel básico |url=https://interdiario.com.mx/2024/04/01/propone-claudia-sheinbaum-becas-para-ninos-de-nivel-basico/ |access-date=26 May 2024 |language=es |archive-date=26 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240526192855/https://interdiario.com.mx/2024/04/01/propone-claudia-sheinbaum-becas-para-ninos-de-nivel-basico/ |url-status=live}}</ref> and the other targeting women aged 60 to 64, where they would receive half the amount provided by the Pension for the Well-being of Older People.<ref name=":8" />

==== Energy ====
López Obrador has pursued an energy sovereignty policy, seeking to prevent [[Pemex]] from exporting crude oil and instead refining it in Mexico. To this end, he inaugurated a new refinery in [[Paraíso, Tabasco]], canceled oil auctions, financed Pemex's debts, and used regulatory agencies to keep private firms off the market.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Lopez |first=Oscar |date=17 August 2022 |title=Mexico Sees Its Energy Future in Fossil Fuels, Not Renewables |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2022/08/17/world/americas/mexico-president-renewable-energy.html |access-date=26 May 2024 |work=The New York Times |language=en-US |issn=0362-4331 |archive-date=25 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240525120857/https://www.nytimes.com/2022/08/17/world/americas/mexico-president-renewable-energy.html |url-status=live}}</ref>

Claudia Sheinbaum is committed to following López Obrador's approach, believing that the country's energy policy should have a low rate of energy imports.<ref>{{Cite web |title=The Mexican Energy Sector after AMLO |url=https://www.wilsoncenter.org/article/mexican-energy-sector-after-amlo |access-date=26 May 2024 |website=www.wilsoncenter.org |language=en |archive-date=25 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240525120909/https://www.wilsoncenter.org/article/mexican-energy-sector-after-amlo |url-status=live}}</ref> She has claimed to be committed to diversifying Mexico's energy matrix, particularly with renewable energies.

Xóchitl Gálvez advocated for a more free-market model approach to Mexico's energy sector, proposing significant reforms to Pemex by opening it up to private investment and reinstating oil auctions and joint ventures, using [[Petrobras]] as an example.<ref>{{Cite web |date=7 September 2023 |title=Xóchitl Gálvez va por reforma radical en Pemex; invita a inversión privada |url=https://www.elfinanciero.com.mx/nacional/2023/09/07/xochitl-galvez-va-por-reforma-radical-en-pemex-invita-a-inversion-privada/ |access-date=26 May 2024 |website=El Financiero |language=es |archive-date=26 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240526235131/https://www.elfinanciero.com.mx/nacional/2023/09/07/xochitl-galvez-va-por-reforma-radical-en-pemex-invita-a-inversion-privada/ |url-status=live}}</ref> She stated that she would use the oil drilled by private companies for the [[petrochemical industry]] and would produce electricity with renewable energies.<ref>{{Cite web |author1=Max de Haldevang |author2=Maya Averbuch Averbuch |date=7 September 2023 |title=Mexican Opposition Candidate Calls for Private Investment In Pemex |url=https://www.bloomberglinea.com/english/mexican-opposition-candidate-calls-for-private-investment-in-pemex/ |access-date=26 May 2024 |website=Bloomberg Línea |language=en-US |archive-date=26 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240526235132/https://www.bloomberglinea.com/english/mexican-opposition-candidate-calls-for-private-investment-in-pemex/ |url-status=live}}</ref> Additionally, Gálvez emphasized the role of [[natural gas]] as a transitional fuel and intended to increase investments in hydrogen and solar power.<ref>{{Cite web |date=7 March 2024 |title=Gálvez apuesta por el gas natural y 'pequeñas plantas nucleares' en México |url=https://www.forbes.com.mx/galvez-apuesta-por-el-gas-natural-y-pequenas-plantas-nucleares-en-mexico/ |access-date=26 May 2024 |website=Forbes México |language=es-MX |archive-date=27 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240527141212/https://www.forbes.com.mx/galvez-apuesta-por-el-gas-natural-y-pequenas-plantas-nucleares-en-mexico/ |url-status=live}}</ref>

==== Electoral reform ====
President López Obrador unsuccessfully attempted to pass electoral reforms multiple times during his term. His latest proposal, unveiled on 5 February 2024 as one of twenty proposed constitutional reforms, aims to restructure the INE by reducing the number of counselors and requiring that electoral judges be elected by popular vote. Additionally, it would eliminate all seats allocated by [[proportional representation]], reducing the [[Chamber of Deputies (Mexico)|Chamber of Deputies]] from 500 to 300 seats and the [[Senate of the Republic (Mexico)|Senate]] from 128 to 64 seats.<ref>{{Cite web |last=López |first=Alejandro I. |date=6 February 2024 |title=Las 20 reformas constitucionales que propone López Obrador |url=https://elpais.com/mexico/2024-02-06/las-20-reformas-constitucionales-que-propone-lopez-obrador.html |access-date=1 March 2024 |website=El País México |language=es-MX |archive-date=2 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240302183146/https://elpais.com/mexico/2024-02-06/las-20-reformas-constitucionales-que-propone-lopez-obrador.html |url-status=live}}</ref>

''Fuerza y Corazón por México'' members have been critical of López Obrador's efforts to reform the electoral system and have successfully blocked previous legislative attempts, deeming them undemocratic. On 18 February 2024, the coalition organized nationwide protests, dubbed the "march for democracy", in multiple cities, with the largest one occurring at the [[Zócalo]] in [[Mexico City]]. Government figures estimate turnout at 90,000; however, organizers claim about 700,000 attendees were at the protests.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Marentes |first=Amaranta |date=19 February 2024 |title=Tens of thousands rail against Mexico's president and ruling party in 'march for democracy' |url=https://apnews.com/article/democracy-mexico-andres-manuel-lopez-obrador-opposition-48f454552fab8d52c2b17aac82d44016 |access-date=21 February 2024 |website=Associated Press |language=en |archive-date=21 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240221065707/https://apnews.com/article/democracy-mexico-andres-manuel-lopez-obrador-opposition-48f454552fab8d52c2b17aac82d44016 |url-status=live}}</ref> Gálvez lauded the protests, asserting that Mexico's institutions would remain free from interference by authoritative figures.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Marentes |first=Amaranta |date=19 February 2024 |title=Mexicans rally to 'protect democracy' ahead of elections |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/gallery/2024/2/19/mexicans-rally-to-protect-democracy-ahead-of-elections |access-date=21 February 2024 |website=Al Jazeera |language=en |archive-date=21 February 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240221120137/https://www.aljazeera.com/gallery/2024/2/19/mexicans-rally-to-protect-democracy-ahead-of-elections |url-status=live}}</ref>

Sheinbaum suggested passing López Obrador's electoral reform if the outgoing administration failed to do so, supporting reducing the INE's costs, eliminating seats allocated by proportional representation,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Jiménez |first=Fabiola Martínez, Alma Muñoz, Lilian Hernández y Néstor |date=19 May 2024 |title=Sheinbaum ofrece eliminación de pluris, Gálvez afirma que es una trampa |url=https://www.jornada.com.mx/noticia/2024/05/19/politica/sheinbaum-ofrece-eliminacion-de-pluris-galvez-afirma-que-es-una-trampa-7254 |access-date=26 May 2024 |website=La Jornada |language=es |archive-date=26 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240526235525/https://www.jornada.com.mx/noticia/2024/05/19/politica/sheinbaum-ofrece-eliminacion-de-pluris-galvez-afirma-que-es-una-trampa-7254 |url-status=live}}</ref> and advocating for counselors and electoral judges to be elected via popular vote.<ref>{{Cite web |date=6 May 2024 |title=Ministros y consejeros del INE, por voto popular; 'eso es democracia', sostiene Claudia Sheinbaum |url=https://www.elfinanciero.com.mx/elecciones-mexico-2024/2024/05/06/ministros-y-consejeros-del-ine-por-voto-popular-eso-es-democracia-sostiene-claudia-sheinbaum/ |access-date=26 May 2024 |website=El Financiero |language=es |archive-date=3 June 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240603055844/https://www.elfinanciero.com.mx/elecciones-mexico-2024/2024/05/06/ministros-y-consejeros-del-ine-por-voto-popular-eso-es-democracia-sostiene-claudia-sheinbaum/ |url-status=live}}</ref> Additionally, she proposed a constitutional amendment to prevent reelection for any popularly elected position. Furthermore, she announced her willingness to subject herself to a recall election, mirroring [[2022 Mexican presidential recall referendum|López Obrador in 2022]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=100 propuestas y discurso íntegro de Claudia Sheinbaum |url=https://www.eluniversal.com.mx/elecciones/100-propuestas-y-discurso-integro-de-claudia-sheinbaum/ |access-date=2 March 2024 |website=El Universal |language=es |archive-date=2 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240302062313/https://www.eluniversal.com.mx/elecciones/100-propuestas-y-discurso-integro-de-claudia-sheinbaum/ |url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Debates ===

==== First debate ====
The first debate was held on 7 April 2024 at the INE's headquarters. The theme of the debate was "the society we want", with questions focused on health and education, corruption and governmental transparency, discrimination against vulnerable groups, and violence against women.<ref name=":4" /> On 6 March, the INE selected journalists [[Denise Maerker]] and Manuel López San Martín as moderators of the debate.<ref>{{Cite web |date=6 March 2024 |title=Denise Maerker y Manuel López San Martín son las personas propuestas para moderar el Primer Debate Presidencial |url=https://centralelectoral.ine.mx/2024/03/06/denise-maerker-y-manuel-lopez-san-martin-son-las-personas-propuestas-para-moderar-el-primer-debate-presidencial/ |access-date=10 March 2024 |website=Central Electoral |language=es |archive-date=10 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240310221003/https://centralelectoral.ine.mx/2024/03/06/denise-maerker-y-manuel-lopez-san-martin-son-las-personas-propuestas-para-moderar-el-primer-debate-presidencial/ |url-status=live}}</ref> The debate was the first in 18 years without [[Andrés Manuel López Obrador|López Obrador]], who participated in the presidential debates during the [[2006 Mexican general election|2006]], [[2012 Mexican general election|2012]], and [[2018 Mexican general election|2018]] elections.<ref>{{Cite web |date=8 April 2024 |title=El debate presidencial 2024 será el primero sin AMLO desde 2006 |url=https://politica.expansion.mx/elecciones/2024/04/07/debate-presidencial-2024-primero-sin-amlo-desde-2006 |access-date=8 April 2024 |website=ADNPolítico |language=es |archive-date=8 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240408101824/https://politica.expansion.mx/elecciones/2024/04/07/debate-presidencial-2024-primero-sin-amlo-desde-2006 |url-status=live}}</ref>

The debate was characterized as light on proposals, with frequent personal attacks being prevalent.<ref>{{Cite web |date=8 April 2024 |author=Janetsky, Megan |title=Candidates spar in Mexico's first presidential debate ahead of June 2 election |url=https://apnews.com/article/mexico-sheinbaum-xochitl-galvez-733b34a09a7efebab7470ef8fe25912a |access-date=8 April 2024 |website=AP News |language=en |archive-date=8 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240408101824/https://apnews.com/article/mexico-sheinbaum-xochitl-galvez-733b34a09a7efebab7470ef8fe25912a |url-status=live}}</ref> Gálvez was described as attempting to attack Sheinbaum whenever she had the opportunity to do so to provoke her opponent,<ref>{{Cite web |date=7 April 2024 |title=Así le hemos contado el primer debate presidencial |url=https://elpais.com/mexico/elecciones-mexicanas/2024-04-07/primer-debate-presidencial-en-mexico-2024-en-vivo.html |access-date=8 April 2024 |website=El País México |language=es-MX |archive-date=8 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240408101824/https://elpais.com/mexico/elecciones-mexicanas/2024-04-07/primer-debate-presidencial-en-mexico-2024-en-vivo.html |url-status=live}}</ref> interlacing attacks with incidents where Sheinbaum was involved, such as the collapse of the Colegio Rébsamen during the [[2017 Puebla earthquake|2017 Mexico City earthquake]], the [[Mexico City Metro overpass collapse]], and the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Mexico|COVID-19 pandemic in Mexico City]]. Gálvez called Sheinbaum the cold and heartless "ice lady" and stated, "You're no [[Andrés Manuel López Obrador|AMLO]]. You don't even have his charisma".<ref name="auto">{{Cite web |date=8 April 2024 |title='¡¿Cómo creerle a una mentirosa?!' Las frases que 'encendieron' el debate presidencial 2024 |url=https://www.elfinanciero.com.mx/elecciones-mexico-2024/2024/04/07/debate-presidencial-2024-frases-candidatos-mentirosa-fria-sin-corazon/ |access-date=8 April 2024 |website=El Financiero |language=es |archive-date=8 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240408101825/https://www.elfinanciero.com.mx/elecciones-mexico-2024/2024/04/07/debate-presidencial-2024-frases-candidatos-mentirosa-fria-sin-corazon/ |url-status=live}}</ref> Máynez accused Sheinbaum and Gálvez of belonging to the "old politics", highlighting that Gálvez was endorsed by the "worst [[Institutional Revolutionary Party|PRI]] in history".<ref name="auto" /> Sheinbaum also briefly mentioned the [[2024 raid on the Mexican embassy in Ecuador]] and praised the diplomatic staff there for their courage during the incident.<ref>{{Cite web |date=8 April 2024 |title=Candidates spar in Mexico's first presidential debate ahead of June 2 election |url=https://apnews.com/article/mexico-sheinbaum-xochitl-galvez-733b34a09a7efebab7470ef8fe25912a |access-date=8 April 2024 |website=Associated Press |language=en |archive-date=8 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240408101824/https://apnews.com/article/mexico-sheinbaum-xochitl-galvez-733b34a09a7efebab7470ef8fe25912a |url-status=live}}</ref>

Many stated there was no clear winner and the debate would not influence polling.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Esquivel |first=Karen |date=8 April 2024 |title=Debate presidencial en México: ¿quién ganó? ¿Quién perdió? |url=https://cnnespanol.cnn.com/2024/04/08/debate-presidencial-en-mexico-quien-gano-quien-perdio-orix/ |access-date=8 April 2024 |website=CNN |language=es |archive-date=8 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240408101824/https://cnnespanol.cnn.com/2024/04/08/debate-presidencial-en-mexico-quien-gano-quien-perdio-orix/ |url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=":9">{{Cite web |date=8 April 2024 |title=¿Quién ha ganado el debate presidencial? Siete expertos dan su veredicto |url=https://elpais.com/mexico/elecciones-mexicanas/2024-04-08/quien-ha-ganado-el-debate-siete-expertos-dan-su-veredicto.html |access-date=8 April 2024 |website=El País México |language=es-MX |archive-date=8 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240408101825/https://elpais.com/mexico/elecciones-mexicanas/2024-04-08/quien-ha-ganado-el-debate-siete-expertos-dan-su-veredicto.html |url-status=live}}</ref> However, some highlighted Sheinbaum's calm and disciplined demeanor throughout the debate, even amid provocations from Gálvez.<ref name=":9" /> Máynez was described as struggling to find footing since he was overshadowed by the two better-known candidates.<ref>{{Cite web |date=8 April 2024 |title=Elecciones 2024: Postdebate 2024 |url=https://politica.expansion.mx/elecciones/2024/04/07/quien-gano-el-debate-presidencial |access-date=8 April 2024 |website=ADNPolítico |language=es |archive-date=8 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240408101824/https://politica.expansion.mx/elecciones/2024/04/07/quien-gano-el-debate-presidencial |url-status=live}}</ref> López Obrador stated that "the whole narrative of the debate was not to recognize anything" done under his administration. At the same time, sources from his government said that the president was dissatisfied with Sheinbaum for not adequately defending his policies.<ref>{{Cite web |date=9 April 2024 |title=Mexico's outgoing leader is unhappy with the first presidential debate |url=https://apnews.com/article/mexico-election-debate-president-52291460ce1346442c3366beeaf05b4e |access-date=9 April 2024 |website=Associated Press |language=en |archive-date=9 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240409164206/https://apnews.com/article/mexico-election-debate-president-52291460ce1346442c3366beeaf05b4e |url-status=live}}</ref>

==== Second debate ====
The second debate was held on 28 April 2024 at [[Estudios Churubusco]]. The theme was "the route to the development of Mexico", addressing economic growth, employment, inflation, infrastructure, poverty, climate change, and sustainable development. To ensure state inclusivity, the INE gathered questions from citizens of all 32 federal entities.<ref name=":4" /> On 28 March, the INE selected journalists Adriana Pérez Cañedo and Alejandro Cacho as moderators of the debate.<ref>{{Cite web |date=29 March 2024 |title=Adriana Pérez Cañedo y Alejandro Cacho fueron designados como moderadores para el Segundo Debate Presidencial |url=https://centralelectoral.ine.mx/2024/03/28/adriana-perez-canedo-y-alejandro-cacho-fueron-designados-como-moderadores-para-el-segundo-debate-presidencial/ |access-date=8 April 2024 |website=Central Electoral |language=es |archive-date=8 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240408101824/https://centralelectoral.ine.mx/2024/03/28/adriana-perez-canedo-y-alejandro-cacho-fueron-designados-como-moderadores-para-el-segundo-debate-presidencial/ |url-status=live}}</ref> The debate was the most watched in Mexican history, with 16.18 million viewers.<ref>{{Cite web |author=Fernando Merino |title=Segundo debate presidencial es el más visto en la historia: INE |url=https://www.elsoldemexico.com.mx/elecciones-2024/segundo-debate-presidencial-es-el-mas-visto-en-la-historia-ine-11841635.html |access-date=4 May 2024 |website=El Sol de México |language=es |archive-date=3 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240503053651/https://www.elsoldemexico.com.mx/elecciones-2024/segundo-debate-presidencial-es-el-mas-visto-en-la-historia-ine-11841635.html |url-status=live}}</ref>

Like the first debate, Gálvez was again described as the aggressor, holding placards several times while Sheinbaum spoke, prompting moderator Pérez Cañedo to reprimand her.<ref>{{Cite web |date=29 April 2024 |title=A Xóchitl Gálvez la regañaron en el segundo debate presidencial por esta razón |url=https://www.sdpnoticias.com/mexico/a-xochitl-galvez-la-reganaron-en-el-segundo-debate-presidencial-por-esta-razon/ |access-date=29 April 2024 |website=sdpnoticias |language=en |archive-date=30 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240430074514/https://www.sdpnoticias.com/mexico/a-xochitl-galvez-la-reganaron-en-el-segundo-debate-presidencial-por-esta-razon/ |url-status=live}}</ref> Gálvez questioned Sheinbaum about investigating potential corruption within López Obrador's administration, including allegations involving one of the president's sons and the [[illicit enrichment]] of [[Rocío Nahle García]], to which Sheinbaum challenged her to file a complaint.<ref>{{Cite web |date=28 April 2024 |title=Así le hemos contado el segundo debate presidencial de México 2024 |url=https://elpais.com/mexico/elecciones-mexicanas/2024-04-28/debate-presidencial-2024-en-vivo-sheinbaum-galvez-y-maynez-se-enfrentan-en-su-segundo-cara-a-cara.html |access-date=29 April 2024 |website=El País México |language=es-MX |archive-date=29 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240429025858/https://elpais.com/mexico/elecciones-mexicanas/2024-04-28/debate-presidencial-2024-en-vivo-sheinbaum-galvez-y-maynez-se-enfrentan-en-su-segundo-cara-a-cara.html |url-status=live}}</ref> Throughout the debate, Sheinbaum avoided addressing Gálvez by name, referring to her instead as the "candidate of the PRIAN" and the "corrupt one"; in retaliation, Gálvez called her the "candidate of lies" and the "narco-candidate".<ref>{{Cite web |title="La candidata de las mentiras" y "la del PRIAN": Sheinbaum y Xóchitl se ponen estos motes en debate |url=https://www.proceso.com.mx/nacional/elecciones-2024/2024/4/28/la-candidata-de-las-mentiras-la-del-prian-sheinbaum-xochitl-se-ponen-estos-motes-en-debate-328020.html |access-date=29 April 2024 |website=www.proceso.com.mx |language=spanish |archive-date=30 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240430074520/https://www.proceso.com.mx/nacional/elecciones-2024/2024/4/28/la-candidata-de-las-mentiras-la-del-prian-sheinbaum-xochitl-se-ponen-estos-motes-en-debate-328020.html |url-status=live}}</ref> Máynez was mostly ignored by the other candidates, which allowed him to flesh out proposals with periodic attacks on Gálvez.<ref>{{Cite web |date=29 April 2024 |title=Xóchitl Gálvez combate; Claudia Sheinbaum se cuida en el segundo debate |url=https://abcnoticias.mx/nacional/2024/4/29/xochitl-galvez-combate-claudia-sheinbaum-se-cuida-en-el-segundo-debate-215123.html |access-date=30 April 2024 |website=ABC Noticias |language=es |archive-date=30 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240430074514/https://abcnoticias.mx/nacional/2024/4/29/xochitl-galvez-combate-claudia-sheinbaum-se-cuida-en-el-segundo-debate-215123.html |url-status=live}}</ref> Some praise was also drawn to López Obrador's social programs, with Sheinbaum and Gálvez agreeing to maintain them.<ref>{{Cite web |date=29 April 2024 |title=Mexico's presidential candidates discuss social spending, climate change in 2nd debate |url=https://apnews.com/article/mexico-election-debate-sheinbaum-galvez-52fa1949eaee592130f378315151f405 |access-date=29 April 2024 |website=AP News |language=en |archive-date=30 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240430074514/https://apnews.com/article/mexico-election-debate-sheinbaum-galvez-52fa1949eaee592130f378315151f405 |url-status=live}}</ref>

Many stated that the debate would not influence polling as there was no clear consensus on a winner. It was described that on social media, opinions on the winner were closely linked to views about the candidates before the debate.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Elecciones en México: quién ganó el segundo debate presidencial 2024 |url=https://www.ambito.com/mexico/informacion-general/elecciones-mexico-quien-gano-el-segundo-debate-presidencial-2024-n5985598 |access-date=29 April 2024 |website=www.ambito.com |language=es-MX |archive-date=30 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240430074520/https://www.ambito.com/mexico/informacion-general/elecciones-mexico-quien-gano-el-segundo-debate-presidencial-2024-n5985598 |url-status=live}}</ref> In Google Trends, Máynez led in average interest during the debate, although there were moments when Sheinbaum had higher peaks than Máynez.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Campos |first=Mariana |date=29 April 2024 |title=¿Quién generó más interacciones en Google tras segundo debate presidencial: Gálvez, Sheinbaum o Máynez ? |url=https://www.infobae.com/mexico/2024/04/29/quien-genero-mas-interacciones-en-google-tras-segundo-debate-presidencial-galvez-sheinbaum-o-maynez/ |access-date=30 April 2024 |website=infobae |language=es-ES |archive-date=30 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240430074520/https://www.infobae.com/mexico/2024/04/29/quien-genero-mas-interacciones-en-google-tras-segundo-debate-presidencial-galvez-sheinbaum-o-maynez/ |url-status=live}}</ref>

==== Third debate ====
The third and final debate was held on 19 May 2024 at [[National Autonomous University of Mexico|UNAM's]] ''Centro Cultural Universitario Tlatelolco''. The theme was "democracy and government: constructive dialogues". Questions focused on social policy, insecurity and organized crime, migration and foreign policy, democracy, pluralism, and division of powers. On 18 April 2024, the INE selected journalists Luisa Cantú Ríos, Elena Arcila, and Javier Solórzano Zinser as moderators of the debate, each respectively representing Mexico's northern, southern, and central regions.<ref>{{Cite web |date=19 April 2024 |title=Designa INE a Luisa Cantú, Javier Solórzano y Elena Arcila como moderadores del Tercer Debate Presidencial |url=https://centralelectoral.ine.mx/2024/04/18/designa-ine-a-luisa-cantu-javier-solorzano-y-elena-arcila-como-moderadores-del-tercer-debate-presidencial/ |access-date=21 April 2024 |website=Central Electoral |language=es |archive-date=21 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240421092757/https://centralelectoral.ine.mx/2024/04/18/designa-ine-a-luisa-cantu-javier-solorzano-y-elena-arcila-como-moderadores-del-tercer-debate-presidencial/ |url-status=live}}</ref> The debate's format would have initially allowed candidates to engage with each other directly.<ref name=":4" /> However, on 9 May, the campaigns agreed to a new format where candidates would submit questions to the moderators, who would then choose and ask them.<ref>{{Cite web |last=ZEPEDA |first=AURORA |date=9 May 2024 |title=Partidos cambian formato del tercer debate presidencial |url=https://www.excelsior.com.mx/nacional/partidos-cambian-formato-del-tercer-debate-presidencial/1651185 |access-date=20 May 2024 |website=Excélsior |language=es-MX |archive-date=12 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240512234313/https://www.excelsior.com.mx/nacional/partidos-cambian-formato-del-tercer-debate-presidencial/1651185 |url-status=live}}</ref>

The debate saw Claudia Sheinbaum defend López Obrador's security policy and pledge to continue tackling the Mexican drug war from a social angle. Meanwhile, Xóchitl Gálvez said that "Hugs for criminals are over", referring to López Obrador's slogan "Hugs, not bullets" and pledged to strengthen the [[National Guard (Mexico)|National Guard]] created by López Obrador as well as state and local police forces. Jorge Álvarez Máynez focused on youth empowerment during the debate and pledged to implement a five-day workweek and create more spaces in public universities.<ref>{{Cite web |date=20 May 2024 |title=Mexico presidential candidates offer little detail to address country's violence in final debate |url=https://apnews.com/article/mexico-election-protest-president-lopez-obrador-fbcf39a5e99cbe12c24b53d7476a205e |access-date=20 May 2024 |website=Associated Press |language=en |archive-date=20 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240520064722/https://apnews.com/article/mexico-election-protest-president-lopez-obrador-fbcf39a5e99cbe12c24b53d7476a205e |url-status=live}}</ref>

Many commentators criticized the debate's format and the removal of the face-to-face portion, arguing that it hindered meaningful dialogue.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web |last=Esquivel |first=Karen |date=20 May 2024 |title=ANÁLISIS: ¿Quién ganó y quien perdió el tercer debate presidencial de México entre Sheinbaum, Gálvez y Máynez? |url=https://cnnespanol.cnn.com/2024/05/20/analisis-quien-gano-tercer-debate-presidencial-mexico-elecciones-orix/ |access-date=20 May 2024 |website=CNN |language=es |archive-date=20 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240520130944/https://cnnespanol.cnn.com/2024/05/20/analisis-quien-gano-tercer-debate-presidencial-mexico-elecciones-orix/ |url-status=live}}</ref> Opinions on the winner varied, with some finding it unclear and others declaring Sheinbaum the victor, claiming that Sheinbaum won because Gálvez had lost, as she spent too much time attacking Sheinbaum instead of presenting her proposals.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web |date=20 May 2024 |title=¿Quién ha ganado el tercer debate presidencial? === Gaffes ===
In April 2024, Xóchitl Gálvez stated that people who did not own a house by the age of 60 were poor money managers before later clarifying that the words were meant solely at Claudia Sheinbaum, who lives in a rented apartment and had attacked Gálvez for the circumstances in which she acquired her home.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Raziel |first1=Zedryk |title=Gálvez critica a Sheinbaum por vivir en un departamento alquilado |url=https://elpais.com/mexico/elecciones-mexicanas/2024-04-12/galvez-critica-a-sheinbaum-por-vivir-en-un-departamento-alquilado-si-a-los-60-anos-no-has-podido-hacer-un-patrimonio-eres-bien-guey.html |access-date=2 June 2024 |work=El País |date=11 April 2024 |archive-date=2 June 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240602053008/https://elpais.com/mexico/elecciones-mexicanas/2024-04-12/galvez-critica-a-sheinbaum-por-vivir-en-un-departamento-alquilado-si-a-los-60-anos-no-has-podido-hacer-un-patrimonio-eres-bien-guey.html |url-status=live}}</ref> Jorge Máynez also sparked outrage and apologized after posting a video in which he appeared to be drinking while criticizing electoral officials.<ref name="apgaffe" />

On 10 May 2024, Claudia Sheinbaum said during a campaign speech in [[Baja California Sur]] that "we are not going to reach the presidency like Andrés Manuel López Obrador did, out of personal ambition". Sheinbaum subsequently said that the phrase "could be misinterpreted" after being advised by a colleague, saying that she meant to say that López Obrador "transformed our country without personal ambitions". Xóchitl Gálvez said the incident showed a [[Freudian slip]] on Sheinbaum's part.<ref name="apgaffe">{{Cite web |date=14 March 2024 |title=Mexico's ruling party presidential candidate slips, says outgoing leader led by 'personal ambition' |url=https://apnews.com/article/mexico-elections-sheinbaum-lopez-obrador-e7e0b95c4812f4c4504a5e1803328aad |access-date=11 May 2024 |website=Associated Press |language=en |archive-date=11 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240511024548/https://apnews.com/article/mexico-elections-sheinbaum-lopez-obrador-e7e0b95c4812f4c4504a5e1803328aad |url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Disasters ===
{{main|San Pedro Garza García stage collapse}}

On 22 May, a stage being used by [[Jorge Álvarez Máynez]] for a campaign rally was toppled by strong winds in [[San Pedro Garza García]], [[Nuevo León]], killing ten people, including a child, and injuring 213 others. Máynez, who managed to escape, suspended his upcoming campaign events and met with hospitalized victims. Condolences were issued by his rivals and President López Obrador over the incident, while Claudia Sheinbaum canceled an event in neighboring [[Monterrey]] scheduled the next day in solidarity with the victims of the disaster.<ref>{{Cite web |date=23 May 2024 |title=Stage collapse at a campaign rally in northern Mexico kills at least 9 people and injures 121 |url=https://apnews.com/article/mexico-election-maynez-wind-stage-knocked-over-1c4745f31a5b3caf2bce2db47d7cec12 |access-date=23 May 2024 |website=Associated Press |language=en |archive-date=23 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240523050309/https://apnews.com/article/mexico-election-maynez-wind-stage-knocked-over-1c4745f31a5b3caf2bce2db47d7cec12 |url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Election day violence ===
On 1 June, authorities ordered the suspension of voting in the municipalities of [[Pantelhó]] and [[Chicomuselo]] in [[Chiapas]], citing the burning of election papers in the former by unknown individuals on 31 May and threats against poll workers by gang members.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Elections In Two Mexico Municipalities Suspended Over Violence |url=https://www.barrons.com/news/elections-in-two-mexico-municipalities-suspended-over-violence-2e9f0b98 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240602004327/https://www.barrons.com/news/elections-in-two-mexico-municipalities-suspended-over-violence-2e9f0b98 |archive-date=2 June 2024 |access-date=2 June 2024 |work=Barron's |language=en}}</ref> On election day, two people were killed in shootings at polling stations at [[Coyomeapan]] and {{ill|Tlanalapan|es|San Rafael Tlanalapan}} in [[Puebla]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Front-runner Claudia Sheinbaum poised to become Mexico's 1st woman president |url=https://www.cbc.ca/lite/story/1.7222446 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240603165521/https://www.cbc.ca/lite/story/1.7222446 |archive-date=3 June 2024 |access-date=3 June 2024 |work=CBC |language=en}}</ref>

=== Result reporting ===
[[File:Elecciones federales de México de 2024 15.jpg|alt=A person receives electoral ink on her thumb after casting her vote.|thumb|A person receives electoral ink on her thumb after casting her vote.]]
On election day, the INE uses two processes to report results, both strictly for informational purposes and not official. The Preliminary Electoral Results Program (PREP) progressively counts votes from tally sheets at polling stations, digitizing and recording them online after being delivered to INE district offices. The quick count is a statistical process that randomly selects a sample of votes to estimate voter turnout and candidate results, providing a preliminary indication of the election outcome.<ref name=":19">{{Cite web |last=Román |first=Elisa Villa |date=2024-06-03 |title=PREP, conteo rápido o cómputos distritales: ¿qué son, cuándo se publican y cuáles son las diferencias? |url=https://elpais.com/mexico/elecciones-mexicanas/2024-06-02/prep-conteo-rapido-o-computos-distritales-que-son-cuando-se-publican-y-cuales-son-las-diferencias.html |access-date=2024-10-13 |website=El País México |language=es-MX}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-06-02 |title=Elecciones en México: ¿Qué es el PREP 2024 y a qué hora ‘arranca’ este domingo 2 de junio? |url=https://www.elfinanciero.com.mx/elecciones-mexico-2024/2024/06/02/elecciones-en-mexico-que-es-el-prep-2024-y-a-que-hora-arranca-este-domingo-2-de-junio/ |access-date=2024-10-13 |website=El Financiero |language=es}}</ref>

The district tally is the official process for recording election results. Tally sheets are manually recorded, and each electoral package is certified at the 300 district offices across the country, with citizen councils and party representatives overseeing the process. These packages may be reopened for a vote recount if necessary. This procedure determines the final election result, after which a majority certificate is issued, declaring the candidate with the most votes as the winner.<ref name=":19" />

After the district tally, the validation of results and the proclamation of the "president-elect" become the exclusive responsibilities of the [[Electoral Tribunal of the Federal Judiciary]].

== Results ==
About 60 million votes were cast in each election, with most of them being cast on election day. The election saw a lower turnout than the [[2012 Mexican general election|2012]] and [[2018 Mexican general election|2018 presidential elections]], at 61.05%.<ref>{{Cite web |last=García |first=Carina |date=2024-06-03 |title=Elección 2024 tuvo menos participación que las de 2018 y 2012 |url=https://politica.expansion.mx/elecciones/2024/06/03/cuanta-gente-voto-por-amlo-2028 |access-date=2024-06-13 |website=ADNPolítico |language=es |archive-date=13 June 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240613025411/https://politica.expansion.mx/elecciones/2024/06/03/cuanta-gente-voto-por-amlo-2028 |url-status=live}}</ref> [[Claudia Sheinbaum]] received 35.9 million votes, surpassing [[Andrés Manuel López Obrador|López Obrador]]'s 2018 record of 30.1 million and making her the candidate with the most votes in Mexican history.<ref name=":13" /> She also achieved the highest vote percentage of any candidate since [[1982 Mexican general election|1982]].

Sheinbaum carried 31 out of 32 states, with [[Aguascalientes]] being the only state where a plurality voted for [[Xóchitl Gálvez]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-06-03 |title=Claudia Sheinbaum: 3 números que muestran la magnitud de su victoria (y por qué puede llegar a tener más poder que AMLO) |url=https://www.bbc.com/mundo/articles/cg33ylzwgdgo |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240603125052/https://www.bbc.com/mundo/articles/cg33ylzwgdgo |archive-date=3 June 2024 |access-date=2024-06-04 |website=BBC News Mundo |language=es}}</ref> Sheinbaum managed to flip [[Guanajuato]] – the home state of former president [[Vicente Fox]] – and a state considered a bastion for the [[National Action Party (Mexico)|National Action Party]] since 2000; it was the only state not voting for López Obrador in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-06-03 |title=Sheinbaum gana en Guanajuato con más de un millón de votos, ¿se acabó el bastión panista? |url=https://www.am.com.mx/guanajuato/2024/6/3/sheinbaum-gana-en-guanajuato-con-mas-de-un-millon-de-votos-se-acabo-el-bastion-panista-707487.html |access-date=2024-06-13 |website=www.am.com.mx |language=spanish |archive-date=13 June 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240613025410/https://www.am.com.mx/guanajuato/2024/6/3/sheinbaum-gana-en-guanajuato-con-mas-de-un-millon-de-votos-se-acabo-el-bastion-panista-707487.html |url-status=live}}</ref>

Besides Aguascalientes, Gálvez placed second in all states except [[Campeche]], where [[Jorge Máynez]] defeated her by five and a half points.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Flores |first=Por Claudia |date=2024-06-06 |title=Cuál es el estado donde Jorge Álvarez Máynez le ganó a Xóchitl Gálvez con más de 18 mil votos |url=https://www.infobae.com/mexico/2024/06/06/cual-es-el-estado-donde-jorge-alvarez-maynez-le-gano-a-xochitl-galvez-con-mas-de-18-mil-votos/ |access-date=2024-06-15 |website=infobae |language=es-ES}}</ref> She also obtained 5.4 million fewer votes than the combined total of both [[Ricardo Anaya]] and [[José Antonio Meade]], the PAN and PRI nominees for president in 2018, respectively; Gálvez obtained 16.5 million in total, while Anaya and Meade obtained 21.9 million votes combined.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-06-04 |title=Xóchitl Gálvez obtuvo menos votos que Anaya y Meade juntos en 2018, según el PREP |url=https://www.infobae.com/mexico/2024/06/04/xochitl-galvez-obtuvo-menos-votos-que-anaya-y-meade-juntos-en-2018-segun-el-prep/ |access-date=2024-06-14 |website=www.infobae.com |language=spanish |archive-date=5 June 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240605041347/https://www.infobae.com/mexico/2024/06/04/xochitl-galvez-obtuvo-menos-votos-que-anaya-y-meade-juntos-en-2018-segun-el-prep/ |url-status=live}}</ref>

Máynez's performance in the election marked the best result for [[Citizens' Movement (Mexico)|Citizens' Movement]] in its history. The party received 5.2 million more votes compared to 2018 when it received about 1 million – about a 500% increase. Citizens' Movement secured the third-highest votes among all parties in the election, behind Morena and the PAN.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Jiménez |first=Por César |date=2024-06-11 |title=Movimiento Ciudadano presume triunfo sobre el PRI y el PAN en elecciones: “Crecimos jugando limpio” |url=https://www.infobae.com/mexico/2024/06/11/movimiento-ciudadano-presume-triunfo-sobre-el-pri-y-el-pan-en-elecciones-crecimos-jugando-limpio/ |access-date=2024-10-13 |website=infobae |language=es-ES}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Jiménez |first=Elia Castillo |date=2024-06-06 |title=Del triunfalismo de Movimiento Ciudadano a la pérdida de territorio: el doble efecto Máynez |url=https://elpais.com/mexico/2024-06-06/del-triunfalismo-de-movimiento-ciudadano-a-la-perdida-de-territorio-el-doble-efecto-maynez.html |access-date=2024-10-13 |website=El País México |language=es-MX}}</ref>

[[File:Claudia Sheinbaum discurso de la victoria.jpg|thumb|Claudia Sheinbaum during her victory speech at the [[Zócalo]].]]

=== District tally and certification ===
[[File:Resultados electorales presidencia de México 2024.jpg|thumb|[[Andrés Manuel López Obrador]] showing Sheinbaum's margin of victory in one of his ''mañaneras'', 25 July 2024.]]
The district tally started on 5 June at 8:00 CST. The INE announced it would recount ballots from 60% of the polling stations, a decrease from the 75% recounted during the 2018 election.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-06-04 |title=El INE prevé recuento de votos en el 60% de las casillas; en 2018 recontaron el 75% |url=https://animalpolitico.com/elecciones-2024/electoral/ine-recuento-votos-casillas |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240613025407/https://animalpolitico.com/elecciones-2024/electoral/ine-recuento-votos-casillas |archive-date=13 June 2024 |access-date=2024-06-13 |website=Animal Politico |language=en}}</ref> Xóchitl Gálvez requested that 80% of the votes be recounted, asserting there were irregularities at some polling stations.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Staff |first=Forbes |date=2024-06-05 |title=Xóchitl Gálvez pide la revisión voto por voto en el 80% de las casillas |url=https://www.forbes.com.mx/xochitl-galvez-pide-la-revision-voto-a-voto-en-el-80-de-las-casillas/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240613025410/https://www.forbes.com.mx/xochitl-galvez-pide-la-revision-voto-a-voto-en-el-80-de-las-casillas/ |archive-date=13 June 2024 |access-date=2024-06-13 |website=Forbes México |language=es-MX}}</ref>

On 6 June, the district tally concluded for the presidential election, increasing Sheinbaum's margin of victory by 0.85%.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-06-06 |title=Claudia Sheinmbaum 'devora' en los cómputos distritales: Sería presidenta con 35.9 millones de votos |url=https://www.elfinanciero.com.mx/elecciones-mexico-2024/2024/06/06/claudia-sheinbaum-gana-eleccion-presidencial-con-35-millones-de-votos-a-su-favor-segun-computos-distritales-del-ine/ |access-date=2024-08-23 |website=El Financiero |language=es}}</ref> On 9 June, the INE released constituency seats and final vote count results for each district.<ref name=":18">{{Cite web |date=2024-06-10 |title=Morena reconoce que no alcanzó la supermayoría en el Senado |url=https://abcnoticias.mx/nacional/2024/6/10/morena-reconoce-que-no-alcanzo-la-supermayoria-en-el-senado-218407.html |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240610210012/https://abcnoticias.mx/nacional/2024/6/10/morena-reconoce-que-no-alcanzo-la-supermayoria-en-el-senado-218407.html |archive-date=10 June 2024 |access-date=2024-06-10 |website=ABC Noticias |language=es}}</ref> Projections using the final vote count to calculate party-list deputies and senators confirmed a supermajority in the Chamber of Deputies but not in the Senate, falling three seats short of the two-thirds majority needed to change the [[Constitution of Mexico|Constitution]].<ref name=":18" /><ref name=":15">{{Cite web |last=C.V |first=DEMOS, Desarrollo de Medios, S. A. de |date=2024-06-11 |title=La Jornada: Morena, a dos escaños de poder ratificar las reformas en el Senado |url=https://www.jornada.com.mx/2024/06/11/politica/005n1pol |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240611201441/https://www.jornada.com.mx/2024/06/11/politica/005n1pol |archive-date=11 June 2024 |access-date=2024-06-11 |website=www.jornada.com.mx |language=es-MX}}</ref>

On 15 August, after all challenges to the presidential election were resolved, Sheinbaum's victory was officially certified, and she received the certificate designating her as president-elect.<ref>{{Cite web |title=¿Por qué a mes y medio del 2 de junio, no se ha certificado el triunfo de Sheinbaum? |url=https://aristeguinoticias.com/1907/mexico/por-que-a-mes-y-medio-del-2-de-julio-no-se-ha-certificado-el-triunfo-de-sheinbaum/ |access-date=2024-08-23 |website=Aristegui Noticias |language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Vargas |first=Por Octavio |date=2024-08-15 |title=Claudia Sheinbaum recibe constancia como presidenta electa de México en evento histórico: "La mayoría voto por un gobierno de resultados" |url=https://www.infobae.com/mexico/2024/08/15/claudia-sheinbaum-recibe-constancia-como-presidenta-electa-en-evento-historico/ |access-date=2024-08-23 |website=infobae |language=es-ES}}</ref>

On 21 August, the INE's Commission on Prerogatives and Political Parties unanimously approved a draft assigning party-list deputies and senators.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-08-22 |title=El INE ya se definió: dará mayoría calificada a Morena y aliados |url=https://animalpolitico.com/elecciones-2024/congreso/mayoria-morena-pt-verde-congreso-ine |access-date=2024-08-23 |website=Animal Politico |language=en}}</ref>

=== Party registrations ===
On 10 June, for the first time in the party's three-decade-long history, the INE notified the [[Party of the Democratic Revolution]] (PRD) that it had failed to meet 3% of the popular vote to keep its registration as a national political party.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-06-11 |title=Notifica INE al Partido de la Revolución Democrática inicio de periodo de prevención |url=https://centralelectoral.ine.mx/2024/06/10/notifica-ine-al-partido-de-la-revolucion-democratica-inicio-de-periodo-de-prevencion/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240612184407/https://centralelectoral.ine.mx/2024/06/10/notifica-ine-al-partido-de-la-revolucion-democratica-inicio-de-periodo-de-prevencion/ |archive-date=12 June 2024 |access-date=2024-06-12 |website=Central Electoral |language=es}}</ref> On 19 June, an auditor was chosen to oversee the party's liquidation,<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-06-19 |title=INE elige al interventor encargado de la liquidación del PRD, ante la posible pérdida de su registro nacional |url=https://animalpolitico.com/politica/interventor-ine-liquidacion-prd-perdida-registro |access-date=2024-09-05 |website=Animal Politico |language=en}}</ref> and on 19 September, the INE declared that the PRD had lost its registration.<ref>{{Cite web |last=INE |date=2024-09-20 |title=Declara INE pérdida de registro del PRD |url=https://centralelectoral.ine.mx/2024/09/19/declara-ine-perdida-de-registro-del-prd/ |access-date=2024-09-29 |website=Central Electoral |language=es}}</ref>

=== Lawsuits ===
After the election, the [[National Action Party (Mexico)|National Action Party]] (PAN), the [[Institutional Revolutionary Party]] (PRI), the [[Party of the Democratic Revolution]] (PRD), and [[Citizens' Movement (Mexico)|Citizens' Movement]] (MC) collectively filed 240 challenges against the presidential election results. The PRD spearheaded most of these challenges, which sought to annul millions of ballots to meet the 3% threshold required to maintain its registration as a national political party.<ref>{{Cite web |last=López Ponce |first=Jannet |date=2024-06-17 |title=PRD arranca pelea en TEPJF para mantener su registro: no tiramos la toalla, crimen organizado nos afectó directamente |url=https://www.milenio.com/politica/elecciones/prd-arranca-pelea-en-tepjf-para-mantener-su-registro |access-date=2024-08-21 |website=Milenio}}</ref> However, by 8 August, the [[Federal Electoral Tribunal|Electoral Tribunal of the Federal Judiciary]] had rejected all of the challenges.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Pérez |first=Maritza |date=2024-08-08 |title=El TEPJF confirma resultados del triunfo de Sheinbaum |url=https://www.eleconomista.com.mx/politica/TEPJF-confirma-los-resultados-que-le-dan-el-triunfo-a-Sheibaum-desechan-todas-las-impugnaciones-de-la-oposicion-20240808-0072.html |access-date=2024-08-21 |website=El Economista}}</ref>

For the legislative elections, all political parties challenged the results for deputies and senators, citing allegations of exceeding campaign spending limits, vote buying, voter intimidation, and various irregularities in vote counting.<ref name=":17">{{Cite web |last=Pérez |first=Maritza |date=2024-08-13 |title=Descartan impugnaciones por elección al Congreso |url=https://www.eleconomista.com.mx/politica/Descartan-impugnaciones-por-eleccion-al-Congreso-20240813-0164.html |access-date=2024-08-21 |website=El Economista}}</ref> Most of these challenges, when presented to the Electoral Tribunal of the Federal Judiciary, were rejected, and any rulings from lower courts did not alter the overall election results.<ref name=":17" /> The only exception was in the 23rd district of the [[State of Mexico]], where Luis Alberto Carballo Gutiérrez's victory certificate was annulled due to his ineligibility, as he was a food debtor,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Por deudor alimentario, diputado federal electo del Verde pierde constancia de mayoría |url=https://www.proceso.com.mx/nacional/estados/2024/6/28/por-deudor-alimentario-diputado-federal-electo-del-verde-pierde-constancia-de-mayoria-331903.html |access-date=2024-08-22 |website=www.proceso.com.mx |language=spanish}}</ref> resulting in his alternate assuming the position instead.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Contreras |first=Por Jorge |date=2024-06-29 |title=Deudor alimentario del PVEM ve anulada su constancia de mayoría en Edomex |url=https://www.infobae.com/mexico/2024/06/29/deudor-alimentario-del-pvem-ve-anulada-su-constancia-de-mayoria-en-edomex/ |access-date=2024-08-22 |website=infobae |language=es-ES}}</ref>
