{{Politics of Namibia}}
General elections were held in [[Namibia]] to elect a new [[President of Namibia|president]] and members of the [[National Assembly (Namibia)|National Assembly]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Matthys |first=Donald |date=8 March 2024 |title=ECN releases timetable for 27 November elections |url=https://www.namibian.com.na/ecn-releases-timetable-for-27-november-elections/ |newspaper=[[The Namibian]] |language=en-ZA |archive-date=20 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240420080311/https://www.namibian.com.na/ecn-releases-timetable-for-27-november-elections/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=7 March 2024 |title=Namibia's 2024 general elections slated for November 27 |url=https://apanews.net/namibias-2024-general-elections-slated-for-november-27/ |website=African Press Agency |language=en-US |archive-date=18 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240318120502/https://apanews.net/namibias-2024-general-elections-slated-for-november-27/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Matthys |first=Donald |date=7 March 2024 |title=Namibia votes on 27 November |url=https://www.namibian.com.na/namibia-votes-on-27-november/ |newspaper=[[The Namibian]] |language=en-ZA}}</ref> Initially scheduled on 27 November 2024, these were later extended in some areas to 30 November due to poor planning. Opposition parties decried the move, boycotting the declaration of results and pledging to challenge the results of the election in court. They were the country's seventh general elections since gaining independence from [[Union of South Africa|apartheid South Africa]] in 1990.

On 3 December 2024, [[Netumbo Nandi-Ndaitwah]] of the ruling [[SWAPO]] party was declared the winner of the election. She is set to become Namibia's first female president. The National Assembly elections saw SWAPO reduced to 51 seats, a bare majority of three. It was SWAPO's weakest showing since Namibia's independence in 1990. 

==Background==
Outgoing president [[Hage Geingob]] died on 4 February 2024 whilst receiving treatment for [[cancer]] and was replaced by his [[vice-president of Namibia|vice-president]], [[Nangolo Mbumba]]. Mbumba announced that he had no intention of running for president.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Nyaungwa |first1=Nyasha |title=Namibia interim president says no plan to run in this year's election |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/africa/namibia-interim-president-says-no-plan-run-this-years-election-2024-02-04/ |website=Reuters  |location=Namibia |language=en | date=5 February 2024 |access-date=6 December 2024}}</ref> He will serve out the remainder of Geingob's term which expires on 21 March 2025.<ref>{{Cite news | title=Namibian President Hage Geingob dies in a hospital where he was receiving treatment, his office says | work=[[Associated Press News]] | date=4 February 2024 | url=https://apnews.com/article/namibia-president-hage-geingob-dies-hospital-f01eb59f2ea02c9459ffa96b0c25e0e0 | archive-date=4 February 2024 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240204140622/https://apnews.com/article/namibia-president-hage-geingob-dies-hospital-f01eb59f2ea02c9459ffa96b0c25e0e0 | url-status=live }}</ref> 

In 2019, the ruling [[SWAPO]] party received 56% of the vote, down from 87% in 2014. SWAPO, which gained its base fighting against [[apartheid]] in 1990, is seemingly losing support among youth voters, who are dissatisfied with low employment rates. The current unemployment rate is around 20%.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1">{{Cite news |last=Nyaungwa |first=Nyasha |date=25 November 2024 |title=Namibia to vote in toughest election yet for ruling party |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/africa/namibia-vote-toughest-election-yet-ruling-party-2024-11-25/ |work=[[Reuters]]}}</ref>

Prior to the election, online disinformation campaigns targeted various candidates. These campaigns included false allegations of opposition candidate [[Panduleni Itula]] being a "[[British national identity|British]] agent"; purported footage of another opposition candidate, [[Bernadus Swartbooi]], making tribalist statements towards Itula; and an [[Artificial intelligence|artificially-generated]] image of SWAPO candidate [[Netumbo Nandi-Ndaitwah]] fainting at rallies. Various politicians accused the [[Zimbabwe]]an [[ZANU–PF]] of spreading false information.<ref>{{Cite news |date=26 November 2024 |title=Namibia Fact Check Warns of Foreign Influence and Smear Campaigns Targeting Itula Ahead of Elections |url=https://allafrica.com/stories/202411260077.html |newspaper=[[The Namibian]] |language=en-ZA |archive-date=26 November 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241126132355/https://allafrica.com/stories/202411260077.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Electoral system ==
The [[President of Namibia]] is elected using the [[two-round system]]; if no candidate receives more than 50% in the first round of voting, a run-off will be held. No previous presidential votes in Namibia have gone to a second round.<ref>{{Cite news | title=Namibia's Ruling Party Faces Unexpectedly Challenging Vote | agency=[[Associated Press]] | newspaper=[[The New York Times]] | date=24 November 2019 | url=https://www.nytimes.com/aponline/2019/11/24/world/africa/ap-af-namibia-election-preview.html | archive-date=28 November 2019 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191128172043/https://www.nytimes.com/aponline/2019/11/24/world/africa/ap-af-namibia-election-preview.html | url-status=live }}</ref>

The 104 members of the [[National Assembly (Namibia)|National Assembly]] consist of 96 elected members and eight (non-voting) members appointed by the president.<ref>[https://www.lac.org.na/projects/grap/Pdf/Gov2_Three_Branches_of_Government.pdf The Three Branches of Government] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200913011138/https://www.lac.org.na/projects/grap/Pdf/Gov2_Three_Branches_of_Government.pdf |date=13 September 2020}} [[Hanns Seidel Foundation]]</ref> The 96 elected members are elected by [[closed list]] [[proportional representation]] from one nationwide constituency. Seats are allocated using the [[largest remainder method]].<ref name=IPU>[http://archive.ipu.org/parline-e/reports/2225_B.htm Electoral system] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190825013453/http://archive.ipu.org/parline-e/reports/2225_B.htm |date=25 August 2019}} IPU</ref>

==Presidential candidates==
As president Geingob was ineligible for re-election after having served two consecutive terms, he selected deputy prime minister [[Netumbo Nandi-Ndaitwah]] to run for president as a candidate of his party, the [[South West Africa People's Organisation]] (SWAPO), of which she was also vice-president, in 2023.<ref>{{Cite news | title=Namibia: President names Netumbo Nandi-Ndaitwah woman successor | agency=[[Africanews]] | date=13 March 2023 | url=https://www.africanews.com/2023/03/13/namibian-president-names-netumbo-nandi-ndaitwah-woman-successor/ | archive-date=4 February 2024 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240204154048/https://www.africanews.com/2023/03/13/namibian-president-names-netumbo-nandi-ndaitwah-woman-successor// | url-status=live }}</ref> Upon Geingob's death, she was appointed [[Vice-President of Namibia|vice president]] by president Mbumba. She will become the first female president of Namibia.<ref name="bbc">{{Cite news | title=Hage Geingob: Namibia's president dies aged 82 | work=[[BBC]] | date=4 February 2024 | url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-africa-68196412 |archive-date=4 February 2024 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240204151557/https://www.bbc.com/news/world-africa-68196412 | url-status=live }}</ref><ref name=":1" /> 

Fourteen others were also running for president in the election.<ref>{{Cite news | title=Is Namibia going to elect its first female leader? | work=[[BBC]] | date=26 November 2024 | url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy5z9enx0do | archive-date=27 November 2024 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241127203908/https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy5z9enx0do | url-status=live }}</ref> These included 2019 presidential candidate [[Panduleni Itula]] of the [[Independent Patriots for Change]] (IPC), the main opposition candidate, [[Popular Democratic Movement]] (PDM) leader [[McHenry Venaani]], former deputy minister [[Bernadus Swartbooi]] of the [[Landless People's Movement (Namibia)|Landless People's Movement]] (LPM), and former [[Windhoek]] mayor [[Job Amupanda]] of [[Affirmative Repositioning]] (AR).<ref name=":0">{{Cite news | last=Peyton | first=Nelly | title=Main candidates in Namibia's presidential election | work=[[CNBC]] | date=20 November 2024 | url=https://www.cnbcafrica.com/2024/main-candidates-in-namibias-presidential-election/ | archive-date=21 November 2024 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241121033720/https://www.cnbcafrica.com/2024/main-candidates-in-namibias-presidential-election/ | url-status=live }}</ref>

==Aftermath==
On 3 December 2024, Nandi-Ndaitwah was declared the victor with 57% of the vote,<ref name="F24" /><ref name="3decaj" /> avoiding a runoff election which was predicted to occur.<ref name="ap3dec">{{cite news |title=Namibia will have its first female leader after VP wins presidential election for the ruling party |url=https://apnews.com/article/namibia-election-female-leader-president-8ea8783e1aa56ea20d65970e87adc313 |access-date=4 December 2024 |work=AP News |date=3 December 2024 |language=en}}</ref><ref name="dw3dec">{{cite news |title=Namibia elects Nandi-Ndaitwah as first woman president |url=https://www.dw.com/en/namibia-elects-nandi-ndaitwah-as-first-woman-president/a-70952442 |access-date=4 December 2024 |work=DW |date=3 December 2024 |language=en}}</ref> She became the first woman to be elected as [[president of Namibia]]<ref name="nhongo" /><ref name="F24">{{cite news |title=Namibia elects its first woman president |url=https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20241203-namibia-elects-its-first-woman-president |access-date=4 December 2024 |work=France 24 |agency=AFP-Agence France Presse |date=3 December 2024 |language=en}}</ref><ref name="zane" /> and will be one of two women presidents in Africa, alongside [[Samia Suluhu Hassan]] of [[Tanzania]].<ref name="zane" /> In a speech to supporters, Nandi-Ndaitwah stated "the Namibian nation has voted for peace and stability."<ref name="dw3dec" /><ref name="ap3dec" /> The results showed the weakest showing for SWAPO since Namibia's independence, with the party barely receiving a majority in parliament.<ref name="nhongo" /><ref name="ap3dec" /><ref name="dw3dec" /> ''[[Bloomberg News|Bloomberg]]'' identified the result as "broad policy continuity in a nation that's on the verge of becoming a major hydrocarbons producer", noting the recent discovery of offshore deposits in the [[Orange Basin]].<ref name="nhongo">{{cite news |last1=Nhongo |first1=Kaula |title=Namibia Ruling Party Retains Power in Worst Election Showing |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-12-03/namibia-elections-ruling-party-retains-power-despite-slide-in-support |access-date=4 December 2024 |work=Bloomberg |date=3 December 2024 |language=en}}</ref>

[[Panduleni Itula]], the second-place candidate, declared that there was a "multitude of irregularities" and would "fight... to nullify the elections through the processes that are established within our electoral process."<ref name="F24" /> Opposition parties pledged to challenge the election extension in court.<ref name="3decaj">{{cite news |title=Namibia elects country's first woman president |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/12/3/namibia-elects-nandi-ndaitwah-as-countrys-first-woman-president |access-date=4 December 2024 |work=Al Jazeera |date=3 December 2024 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-12-01 |title=Namibia election: Opposition candidate Itula refuses to recognise poll result |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cjdld7xljk7o.amp |access-date=2024-12-06 |website=BBC News |language=en-gb}}</ref> Itula criticized the opening of only selective polling stations, which may have prevented thousands of voters from casting their vote.<ref name="ap3dec" /> The opposition parties boycotted the announcement of the official results that took place in Windhoek.<ref name="zane">{{cite news |last1=Zane |first1=Damian |title=Namibia election: Swapo's Netumbo Nandi-Ndaitwah elected first female president |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5yx0r14jrmo |access-date=4 December 2024 |work=BBC |agency=BBC News |date=3 December 2024}}</ref><ref name="ap3dec" /> Calls for a repeat of the election by the opposition was rejected by the ECN.<ref name="ap3dec" /> The Forum of German-Speaking Namibians noted that doubts caused by the extension can lead to governmental distrust, and "it is in the hands of the ECN to avoid this slippery path to chaos and violence."<ref name="mumbuu">{{cite news |last1=Mumbuu |first1=Edward |title=ECN slammed over electoral conduct |url=https://neweralive.na/ecn-slammed-over-electoral-conduct/ |access-date=4 December 2024 |work=New Era Live |date=2 December 2024}}</ref>

On 13 December, the Namibian electoral court ordered the ECN to allow the IPC and the LPM to obtain access to electoral data as part of their electoral protest.<ref>{{cite news |title=Nambian court grants opposition parties access to election data |url=https://www.africanews.com/2024/12/13/nambian-court-grants-opposition-parties-access-to-election-data/ |access-date=13 December 2024 |work=Africanews |date=13 December 2024}}</ref> On 28 February 2025, the [[Supreme Court of Namibia]] ruled that the voting extension was valid and rejected the IPC's protest.<ref>{{cite news |title=Namibia: Supreme Court throws out challenge to presidential election result |url=https://www.africanews.com/2025/02/28/namibia-supreme-court-throws-out-challenge-to-presidential-election-result/ |access-date=1 March 2025 |work=Africanews |date=28 February 2025}}</ref>
