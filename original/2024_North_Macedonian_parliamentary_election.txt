Parliamentary elections were held in [[North Macedonia]] on 8 May 2024. The slow pace of [[Accession of North Macedonia to the European Union|EU integration]] and corruption were the main issues during the campaign.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://eutoday.net/north-macedonia-elections/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=8 May 2024 |archive-date=8 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240508212412/https://eutoday.net/north-macedonia-elections/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

The right-wing opposition coalition led by the nationalist [[VMRO-DPMNE]] party decisively won the election receiving 45% of the vote and 58 seats which were three short of an outright majority. The incumbent center-left coalition led by the [[Social Democratic Union of Macedonia]] lost 28 seats and collapsed to 16% of the popular vote.<ref>{{cite news |title=North Macedonia opposition wins big on election night|url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/north-macedonia-votes-elections-crucial-eu-accession-2024-05-07/ |work=Reuters |date=May 8, 2024|author1=Aleksandar Vasovic}}</ref> The second round of the [[2024 North Macedonian presidential election|2024 presidential election]] was held on the same day which was also won by the VMRO-DPMNE candidate, [[Gordana Siljanovska-Davkova]].<ref name="apwoman"/><ref>{{Cite web|title=Parliamentary elections in North Macedonia: The date when they will be held is revealed|url=https://www.cna.al/english/kosova-bota/zgjedhjet-parlamentare-ne-maqedonine-e-veriut-zbulohet-data-kur-do-t-i382992|website=CNA|date=4 December 2023|access-date=9 January 2024|archive-date=9 January 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240109184649/https://www.cna.al/english/kosova-bota/zgjedhjet-parlamentare-ne-maqedonine-e-veriut-zbulohet-data-kur-do-t-i382992|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Electoral system==
[[File:Constituencies of North Macedonia.svg|right|thumb|North Macedonia's six constituencies]]
Of the 123 seats in the [[Assembly of North Macedonia|Assembly of the Republic]] (Sobranie), 120 are elected from six 20-seat constituencies in North Macedonia using [[closed list]] [[proportional representation]], with seats allocated using the [[d'Hondt method]]. The remaining three seats are elected by Macedonians living abroad,<ref>[http://www.ipu.org/parline-e/reports/2313_B.htm Electoral system] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304061659/http://www.ipu.org/parline-e/reports/2313_B.htm |date=4 March 2016 }} IPU</ref> but are only filled if the number of votes exceeds that of the elected candidate with the fewest votes in North Macedonia in the previous election. If a list crosses this threshold, it wins one seat; to win two seats, a list needs to win twice the number of votes, and to win three seats the threshold is three times the number of votes. These seats were not filled in the [[2016 Macedonian parliamentary election|2016 elections]] due to insufficient turnout.<ref>[https://www.osce.org/odihr/elections/fyrom/302136?download=true Early Parliamentary Elections 11 December 2016] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220223195742/https://www.osce.org/odihr/elections/fyrom/302136?download=true |date=23 February 2022 }} OSCE</ref> 

For this election, more than 1,700 candidates were running for the Sobranie.<ref name="ap">{{Cite web |date=8 May 2024 |title=North Macedonia holds presidential and parliamentary elections Wednesday |url=https://apnews.com/article/north-macedona-election-president-parliament-16d5e8870f2587b2a0172dbff07578b8 |access-date=8 May 2024 |website=Associated Press |language=en |archive-date=8 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240508023544/https://apnews.com/article/north-macedona-election-president-parliament-16d5e8870f2587b2a0172dbff07578b8 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Campaign==
Among significant issues during the election was corruption. The opposition [[VMRO-DPMNE]] accused the ruling coalition of presiding over a "pandemic" of corruption in the country, while the [[Social Democratic Union of Macedonia]], a member of the coalition, supported the confiscation of illegally acquired property from corrupt officials. The ruling government supported revising the [[Constitution of North Macedonia|Constitution]] to include recognition of a [[Bulgarians in North Macedonia|Bulgarian minority in North Macedonia]], a condition set by [[Bulgaria]] to allow the country's [[Accession of North Macedonia to the European Union|accession]] to the [[European Union]]. The VMRO-DPMNE called the acceptance of such conditions a "capitulation" to Bulgaria. It has also expressed interest in forming a coalition government with the VLEN coalition comprising [[Albanians in North Macedonia|ethnic Albanian]] parties<ref name="ap"/> but has ruled out entering into one with the [[Democratic Union for Integration]], which is part of the ruling coalition and which it had called "corrupt".<ref>{{Cite web |date=8 May 2024 |title=North Macedonia: Frustrated voters set to punish government |url=https://www.dw.com/en/north-macedonia-frustrated-voters-set-to-punish-government/a-69014477 |access-date=8 May 2024 |website=DW |language=en |archive-date=7 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240507232419/https://www.dw.com/en/north-macedonia-frustrated-voters-set-to-punish-government/a-69014477 |url-status=live }}</ref> The VMRO-DPMNE also pledged to create tens of thousands of jobs amid the country's sluggish economic situation, mass emigration and rising inflation.<ref>{{Cite web |date=9 May 2024 |title=N. Macedonia's nationalist opposition sweeps elections, setting rocky path for EU accession |url=https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240509-n-macedonia-s-nationalist-opposition-sweeps-elections-setting-rocky-path-for-eu-accession |access-date=8 May 2024 |website=France 24 |language=en |archive-date=9 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240509071209/https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240509-n-macedonia-s-nationalist-opposition-sweeps-elections-setting-rocky-path-for-eu-accession |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Results==
The election resulted in a landslide victory for the [[National conservatism|national conservative]] [[VMRO-DPMNE]] and its [[Your Macedonia]] coalition, winning 58 seats in the Sobranie, just three shy of an outright majority. Their main rivals, the incumbent [[Pro-Europeanism|pro-European]] SDSM and its [[For a European Future|For a European Future coalition]], ended up in distant third place, winning 18 seats, their worst ever result. All other parties and coalitions that won seats include: the [[European Front]] coalition with 18 seats and the [[VLEN Coalition]] with 14 seats (both representing national minorities), the [[Left-wing nationalism|left-wing nationalist]] [[The Left (North Macedonia)|The Left]] with six seats, as well as the [[Left-wing populism|left-wing populist]] [[For Our Macedonia]] Movement (ZNAM) with six seats.<ref>{{Cite web |date=8 May 2024 |title=Претседателски и парламентарни избори 2024 – 08.05.2024 |url=https://rezultati-prv.sec.mk/mk/parl/r |website=Државна Изборна Комисија |language=mk |access-date=23 May 2024 |archive-date=23 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240523114731/https://rezultati-prv.sec.mk/mk/parl/r |url-status=live }}</ref> Turnout was 55%. While initially allocated 19 and 13 seats respectively, following repollings in [[Ohrid]], [[Struga]] and other areas on 22 May 2024 led to the European Front losing one seat in favor of the VLEN coalition allowing SDSM and its For a European Future coalition to become the second biggest group in the Assembly.<ref>{{Cite web |date=22 May 2024|title=Parliamentary elections revoting - 22.05.2024 |url=https://rezultati.sec.mk/en/parl/r|website=Државна Изборна Комисија |language=mk}}</ref>

==Aftermath==
Social Democrat leader [[Dimitar Kovačevski]] conceded defeat and announced that he would resign as party leader once a successor had been elected. VMRO-DPMNE leader [[Hristijan Mickoski]] said that “the people have taught the government its most important lesson and saved their country" and pledged to hold into account “every last person who committed a crime and committed corruption".<ref name="apwoman">{{Cite web |date=9 May 2024 |title=North Macedonia elects first woman president as center-left incumbents suffer historic losses |url=https://apnews.com/article/north-macedonia-election-corruption-european-union-bulgaria-ac12182171ed7d46f160feeb469c7aa4 |access-date=9 May 2024 |website=Associated Press |language=en |archive-date=9 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240509011558/https://apnews.com/article/north-macedonia-election-corruption-european-union-bulgaria-ac12182171ed7d46f160feeb469c7aa4 |url-status=live }}</ref>

According to international observers, the elections were "competitive and an extensive and pluralistic campaign helped voters to make an informed choice, but marred by negative rhetoric with nationalistic slogans, as well as shortcomings in the legislation and insufficient oversight of campaign finances".<ref>{{cite web |title=North Macedonia's elections were competitive and voters well informed, although the process remains insufficiently regulated: international observers |url=https://www.osce.org/odihr/elections/north-macedonia/568306 |website=OSCE |access-date=10 May 2024 |date=9 May 2024 |archive-date=9 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240509231155/https://www.osce.org/odihr/elections/north-macedonia/568306 |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 6 June, Mickoski was formally asked by President [[Gordana Siljanovska-Davkova]] to form the next government, after he had unveiled a coalition agreement with the [[VLEN Coalition]] and [[For Our Macedonia|For Our Macedonia (ZNAM)]].<ref>{{cite web |title=North Macedonia's center-right leader given official mandate to form government after election win |url=https://apnews.com/article/north-macedonia-government-mickoski-c17e07ff171aa31e08fa24ee4c8733c6 |publisher=Associated Press |date=6 June 2024 |access-date=6 June 2024 |archive-date=6 June 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240606151426/https://apnews.com/article/north-macedonia-government-mickoski-c17e07ff171aa31e08fa24ee4c8733c6 |url-status=live }}</ref> The investiture vote for Mickoski took place on 24 June, with 77 votes in favor, 22 against and 21 absent.<ref>{{cite tweet |author=Europe Elects |user=europeelects |number=1804980578825248861 |title=North Macedonia: investiture vote on Hristijan Mickoski (VMRO DPMNE-EPP):<br>Yes: 77<br>No: 22<br>Abstention/Did not vote: 21<br>As a majority has voted "yes", Mickoski (VMRO DPMNE-EPP) is approved as Prime Minister. He will form a government consisting of VMRO DPMNE (EPP), VREDI alliance (~NI), ZNAM (*) and other smaller parties. |date=24 June 2024}}</ref> The cabinet is expected to have 24 members, of which sixteen from the VMRO-DPMNE-led coalition [[Your Macedonia]], six from the VLEN coalition block of Albanian opposition parties, and two from ZNAM.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rferl.org/a/north-macedonia-parliament-government-mickoski/33005997.html |title=North Macedonia's Parliament Elects New Right-Wing Government |date=23 June 2024 |access-date=25 June 2024 |work=Radio Free Europe}}</ref>
