Presidential elections were held in [[North Macedonia]] on 24 April 2024. Incumbent [[Stevo Pendarovski]] of the ruling centre-left [[Social Democratic Union of Macedonia|Social Democrats]] ran for re-election to a second term.

No candidate received a majority of votes in the first round, with right-wing challenger [[Gordana Siljanovska-Davkova]] placing first by a wide margin with 41% of the vote, while Pendarovski finished second with 20% of the vote. ==Electoral system==
The [[President of North Macedonia]] is elected using a modified [[two-round system]]; a candidate can only be elected in the first round of voting if they receive the equivalent of over 50% of the vote from all registered voters.<ref>[http://www.balkaninsight.com/en/article/polls-open-in-macedonia-presidential-elections-2 Polls Open in Macedonia Presidential Elections] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140415205029/http://www.balkaninsight.com/en/article/polls-open-in-macedonia-presidential-elections-2 |date=15 April 2014 }} Balkan Insight, 13 April 2014</ref> In the second round, voter turnout must be at least 40% for the result to be deemed valid.<ref name=XXXI>[https://www.sobranie.mk/the-constitution-of-the-republic-of-macedonia.nspx Amendment XXXI] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200319062234/https://www.sobranie.mk/the-constitution-of-the-republic-of-macedonia.nspx |date=19 March 2020 }} Constitution of North Macedonia</ref> Before 2009, the constitution required turnout in the second round to be 50% to validate the result. However, the XXXI amendment approved on 9 January 2009 lowered the threshold to 40%,<ref name=XXXI/> as the then-government feared the trend of reducing turnouts would lead to presidential elections being frequently invalidated. In the [[2009 Macedonian presidential election|2009 presidential elections]], second round turnout was just 42.6%.<ref>Anna Fruhstorfer & Michael Hein (2016) [https://books.google.com/books?id=tRhADQAAQBAJ&pg=PA325 ''Constitutional Politics in Central and Eastern Europe: From Post-Socialist Transition to the Reform of Political Systems''] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240427185033/https://books.google.com/books?id=tRhADQAAQBAJ&pg=PA325#v=onepage&q&f=false |date=27 April 2024 }} Springer, p235</ref>

In order to contest the election, potential candidates are required to collect signatures from at least 10,000 registered voters or 30 MPs.<ref>{{cite web |title=Изборен законик, член 59 |url=https://www.sec.mk/regulativa/ |access-date=4 March 2024 |archive-date=4 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240304181957/https://www.sec.mk/regulativa/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

The [[Constitution of North Macedonia|constitution]] mandates that the President must be over 40 years of age and have lived in the country for ten of the last fifteen years.

Campaigning began on 4 April 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |date=4 April 2024 |title=North Macedonia starts month-long presidential campaign season |url=https://apnews.com/article/north-macedonia-president-election-1c5fd32ef5e0daf6960bc1401aa00fe3 |access-date=4 April 2024 |website=Associated Press |language=en |archive-date=4 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240404154458/https://apnews.com/article/north-macedonia-president-election-1c5fd32ef5e0daf6960bc1401aa00fe3 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Campaign==
[[File:11 Oktomvri Skopje 1.jpg|thumb|Presidential election advertisements in Skopje.]]
Stevo Pendarovski supported revising the [[Constitution of North Macedonia|constitution]] to include recognition of a [[Bulgarians in North Macedonia|Bulgarian minority in North Macedonia]], a condition set by [[Bulgaria]] to allow the country's [[Accession of North Macedonia to the European Union|accession]] to the [[European Union]]. Gordana Siljanovska-Davkova called for negotiations with the EU under a new framework and for such amendments to be made after North Macedonia attains EU membership. Pendarovski called on fighting corruption through taking on organized criminal groups, while Siljanovska-Davkova focused on the judiciary in her anti-corruption stance.<ref>{{cite web |title=North Macedonia to elect president after campaign focused on hot-button issue of EU membership |url=https://apnews.com/article/north-macedonia-election-president-european-union-185e7e3870c4017114e6d148df0320ba |website=Associated Press |language=en |date=23 April 2024 |access-date=23 April 2024 |archive-date=23 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240423060112/https://apnews.com/article/north-macedonia-election-president-european-union-185e7e3870c4017114e6d148df0320ba |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=North Macedonia votes in presidential polls as EU membership bid looms |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/4/24/north-macedonia-votes-for-president-as-eu-membership-bid-looms |website=Al Jazeera |language=en |date=24 April 2024 |access-date=24 April 2024 |archive-date=24 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240424135608/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/4/24/north-macedonia-votes-for-president-as-eu-membership-bid-looms |url-status=live }}</ref>

Following the first round, Gordana Siljanovska-Davkova said "the hour has come for this government to go"<ref>{{cite web |title=EU membership on the table as North Macedonia holds first round of presidential vote |url=https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240424-eu-membership-on-the-table-as-north-macedonia-holds-first-round-of-presidential-vote |website=France 24 |language=en |date=24 April 2024 |access-date=25 April 2024 |archive-date=25 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240425061625/https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240424-eu-membership-on-the-table-as-north-macedonia-holds-first-round-of-presidential-vote |url-status=live }}</ref> and described the results as a "new course". She also called on voters to prove "that we have always (belonged to) Europe". Stevo Pendarovski expressed surprise and disappointment with the result, but expressed hope for improvement in the second round, including with the support of the [[Albanians in North Macedonia|Albanian community]].<ref>{{cite web |title=EU hopeful North Macedonia to hold May 8 presidential runoff with center-right candidate in the lead |url=https://apnews.com/article/north-macedonia-presidential-elections-d7c0cc09c0712de5175a6d34eacb8d83 |website=Associated Press |language=en |date=25 April 2024 |access-date=24 April 2024 |archive-date=24 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240424050655/https://apnews.com/article/north-macedonia-presidential-elections-d7c0cc09c0712de5175a6d34eacb8d83 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="f24"/>

The [[Democratic Union for Integration]], which represents ethnic Albanians and supported former Foreign Minister [[Bujar Osmani]] as a presidential candidate, offered its support in the second round to any candidate who supports having the President selected by the [[Assembly of North Macedonia]] in future elections, in the hopes of having an ethnic Albanian attaining the office. However, both Pendarovski and Siljanovska-Davkova dismissed the proposal, saying it is democratic for the Presidency to be selected through direct voting.<ref name="f24"/>

==Conduct==
Polling in the first round opened at 07:00 on 24 April and lasted until 19:00 at 3,480 polling stations nationwide. About 1.8 million people were eligible to vote,<ref>{{cite web |title=Longtime EU hopeful North Macedonia holds presidential polls centered on bloc accession, rule of law |url=https://apnews.com/article/north-macedonia-presidential-elections-d7c0cc09c0712de5175a6d34eacb8d83 |website=Associated Press |language=en |date=24 April 2024 |access-date=24 April 2024 |archive-date=24 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240424050655/https://apnews.com/article/north-macedonia-presidential-elections-d7c0cc09c0712de5175a6d34eacb8d83 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="f24">{{cite web |title=N. Macedonia's right-wing candidate wins first round presidential election |url=https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240425-n-macedonia-s-right-wing-candidate-wins-first-round-presidential-election |website=France 24 |language=en |date=25 April 2024 |access-date=25 April 2024 |archive-date=25 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240425055545/https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240425-n-macedonia-s-right-wing-candidate-wins-first-round-presidential-election |url-status=live }}</ref> while around 2,300 domestic and international observers monitored the proceedings.<ref name="apwoman"/>

==Aftermath==
Following the release of the results, Stevo Pendarovski conceded defeat, while Siljanovska-Davkova acknowledged her being the first woman to be elected as president of North Macedonia and pledged to "stand with women in taking this great step forward, a step towards reform."<ref name="apwoman"/>

According to international observers, the elections were "competitive and an extensive and pluralistic campaign helped voters to make an informed choice, but marred by negative rhetoric with nationalistic slogans, as well as shortcomings in the legislation and insufficient oversight of campaign finances".<ref>{{cite web |title=North Macedonia's elections were competitive and voters well informed, although the process remains insufficiently regulated: international observers |url=https://www.osce.org/odihr/elections/north-macedonia/568306 |website=OSCE |access-date=10 May 2024 |date=9 May 2024 |archive-date=9 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240509231155/https://www.osce.org/odihr/elections/north-macedonia/568306 |url-status=live }}</ref>
