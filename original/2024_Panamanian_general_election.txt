== Electoral system ==
Of the 71 members of the [[National Assembly (Panama)|National Assembly]], 26 are elected in single-member constituencies and 45 by proportional representation in multi-member constituencies. Each [[Districts of Panama|district]] with more than 40,000 inhabitants forms a constituency. Constituencies elect one MP for every 30,000 residents and an additional representative for every fraction over 10,000.<ref name="IPU">{{cite web|url=http://www.ipu.org/parline-e/reports/2245_B.htm |title=Panama: Asamblea Nacional (National Assembly): Electoral system|website=[[Inter-Parliamentary Union]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140429073441/http://www.ipu.org/parline-e/reports/2245_B.htm |archive-date=29 April 2014|access-date=14 May 2024}}</ref> Around three million people were eligible to vote in the election.<ref>{{cite news |title=Former minister Jose Raul Mulino wins Panama presidential race |url=https://www.france24.com/en/americas/20240505-polls-close-in-panama-presidential-elections-with-eight-contenders |work=[[France 24]] |date=6 May 2024 |access-date=6 May 2024 |archive-date=6 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240506004701/https://www.france24.com/en/americas/20240505-polls-close-in-panama-presidential-elections-with-eight-contenders |url-status=live }}</ref>

In single-member constituencies MPs are elected using the [[First-past-the-post voting|first-past-the-post system]]. In multi-member constituencies MPs are elected using party list [[proportional representation]] according to a double quotient; the first allocation of seats uses a simple quotient, further seats are allotted using the quotient divided by two, with any remaining seats are awarded to the parties with the greatest remainder.<ref name="IPU" />

The president is elected by [[plurality vote]] in a single round.

== Campaign ==
The election was held against a backdrop of voter discontent. One poll found an 86% disapproval rating for the incumbent president, Nito Cortizo, and that 9 out of 10 of Panamanians thought the country was on the wrong path.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=3 May 2024 |title=Poll Tracker: Panama's 2024 Presidential Election |url=https://www.as-coa.org/articles/poll-tracker-panamas-2024-presidential-election |access-date=4 May 2024 |website=Americas Society/Council of the Americas |language=en |archive-date=3 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240503145417/https://www.as-coa.org/articles/poll-tracker-panamas-2024-presidential-election |url-status=live }}</ref> Another found two out of three citizens wanted "radical change". The same poll found were 83% are dissatisfied with their democracy.<ref name=":1A">{{Cite news |title=Years of growth forged prosaic politics. Now Panamanians are fed up |url=https://www.economist.com/the-americas/2024/05/02/years-of-growth-forged-prosaic-politics-now-panamanians-are-fed-up |access-date=4 May 2024 |newspaper=The Economist |issn=0013-0613 |archive-date=4 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240504203623/https://www.economist.com/the-americas/2024/05/02/years-of-growth-forged-prosaic-politics-now-panamanians-are-fed-up |url-status=live }}</ref>

Among significant issues during the campaign were corruption, the economy, the environment and constitutional reform, with [[Rómulo Roux]] and [[Ricardo Lombana]] pledging to revise or replace the current [[Constitution of Panama|constitution]] enacted in 1972. Specifically, Lombana wants the presidential election to be decided by a run-off.<ref name=":1A" /> Martín Torrijos pledged to jumpstart the economy by investing in the [[Panama Canal]], while Roux pledged to implement an "aggressive" job creation scheme and improve public services.<ref name="bbc" />

The closure of the [[Cobre mine, Panama|Cobre Panamá]] copper mine in 2023 was also a significant issue, with all candidates supporting the Supreme Court's decision to annul a 20-year contract awarded to [[First Quantum Minerals]] as unconstitutional. However, Roux, Lombana and [[Maribel Gordón]] supported the total closure and conversion (reclamation) of the mine site.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Candidates confront corruption and inequality in Panama's presidential race |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/5/2/candidates-confront-corruption-and-inequality-in-panamas-presidential-race |date=3 May 2024 |access-date=3 May 2024 |website=Al Jazeera |language=en |archive-date=3 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240503003209/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/5/2/candidates-confront-corruption-and-inequality-in-panamas-presidential-race |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="bbc" />

On the topic of corruption, Lombana pledged to fight the influence of drug money in Panamanian politics.<ref name=":1A" /> According to [[Gallup, Inc.|Gallup]], 57% of Panamanians believed corruption is the most important issue ahead of the election.<ref name=":0" />

[[José Raúl Mulino]] pledged to close the [[Darién Gap]] as a route for migrants heading to the United States and repatriate migrants already in Panama, while promising to respect their rights.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Panama presidential frontrunner vows to shut jungle migrant route |url=https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20240417-panama-presidential-frontrunner-vows-to-shut-jungle-migrant-route |date=17 April 2024 |access-date=17 April 2024 |website=France 24 |language=en |archive-date=17 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240417025247/https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20240417-panama-presidential-frontrunner-vows-to-shut-jungle-migrant-route |url-status=live }}</ref> He also pledged to provide help to Ricardo Martinelli once he was in office.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Panamanians vote in election dominated by former president who was banned from running |url=https://apnews.com/article/panama-election-president-mulino-martinelli-a9954f8a3622885232771ffd51e69715 |date=5 May 2024 |access-date=5 May 2024 |website=Associated Press |language=en |archive-date=5 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240505053444/https://apnews.com/article/panama-election-president-mulino-martinelli-a9954f8a3622885232771ffd51e69715 |url-status=live }}</ref> Lombana opposed closing the Darién Gap and instead proposed to improve security conditions for migrants transiting the country.<ref name="bbc" />

Over the course of the campaign, [[Zulay Rodríguez]] attempted to resign her seat in the Assembly, without success.<ref>{{Cite web |date=9 May 2024 |title=Zulay Rodríguez anuncia que peleará por la curul en la Asamblea |url=https://www.prensa.com/politica/zulay-rodriguez-anuncia-que-peleara-por-la-curul-en-la-asamblea/ |access-date=10 May 2024 |website=La Prensa |language=es}}</ref>

On 30 April, presidential candidate [[Melitón Arrocha]] announced that he was supporting former president [[Martín Torrijos]], but did not formally withdraw his candidacy.<ref name="challenge2">{{Cite web |date=3 May 2024 |title=Panama Supreme Court rejects challenge to candidacy of presidential frontrunner days before vote |url=https://apnews.com/article/panama-election-presidential-contender-supreme-court-06509f45a5f5d35e96c0ef0ebfaab598 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240503104939/https://apnews.com/article/panama-election-presidential-contender-supreme-court-06509f45a5f5d35e96c0ef0ebfaab598 |archive-date=3 May 2024 |access-date=3 May 2024 |website=Associated Press |language=en}}</ref><ref name=":13">{{Cite web |last=De La Cruz |first=Omar |date=30 April 2024 |title=Melitón Arrocha reafirma apoyo a Martín Torrijos, pero no va renunciar a la candidatura |url=https://www.tvn-2.com/tu-decision-2024/meliton-arrocha-reafirma-apoyo-martin-torrijos_1_2132195.html |access-date=7 May 2024 |website=Tvn Panamá |language=es |archive-date=8 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240508142958/https://www.tvn-2.com/tu-decision-2024/meliton-arrocha-reafirma-apoyo-martin-torrijos_1_2132195.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

==== David ====
