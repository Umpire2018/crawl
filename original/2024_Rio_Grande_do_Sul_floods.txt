{{Use dmy dates|date=May 2024}}The '''2024 Rio Grande do Sul floods''' were severe floods caused by heavy rains and storms that hit the [[Federative units of Brazil|Brazilian state]] of [[Rio Grande do Sul]], and the adjacent [[Uruguay]]an cities of [[Treinta y Tres]], [[Paysandú]], [[Cerro Largo Department|Cerro Largo]], and [[Salto, Uruguay|Salto]]. From 29 April through to May,<ref name="AJ">{{Cite news |date=4 May 2024 |title='It's going to be worse': Brazil braces for more pain amid record flooding |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/5/4/its-going-to-be-worse-brazil-braces-for-more-pain-amid-record-flooding |access-date=4 May 2024 |publisher=[[Al Jazeera]] |archive-date=4 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240504133523/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/5/4/its-going-to-be-worse-brazil-braces-for-more-pain-amid-record-flooding |url-status=live }}</ref> it resulted in 181 fatalities (as of 7 July 2024),<ref name="rsgov" /><ref name=UN24Jul7/> widespread landslides, and a dam collapse. It is considered the country's worst flooding in over 80 years.<ref name="AJ"/><ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-08-04 |title=Jogue com responsabilidade: Dicas e recursos essenciais para jogadores brasileiros |url=https://mmabet-br.com/jogo-responsavel/ |access-date=2024-09-09 |language=pt-BR}}</ref>

The floods marked the fourth such environmental disaster in [[Brazil]] within the past 12 months, following similar calamities that killed 75 people in July, September, and November 2023.<ref name="IlPost">{{Cite web |date=5 May 2024 |title=Le disastrose alluvioni nel sud del Brasile |url=https://www.ilpost.it/2024/05/05/alluvioni-porto-alegre-morti-sfollati/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240507140743/https://www.ilpost.it/2024/05/05/alluvioni-porto-alegre-morti-sfollati/ |archive-date=7 May 2024 |access-date=6 May 2024 |website=[[Il Post]] |language=it-IT}}</ref><ref name="Guardian">{{Cite news |date=3 May 2024 |title=Brazil: 37 killed and dozens missing in worst floods in 80 years |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/article/2024/may/03/brazil-floods-rio-grande-do-sul |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240505021255/https://www.theguardian.com/world/article/2024/may/03/brazil-floods-rio-grande-do-sul |archive-date=5 May 2024 |access-date=6 May 2024 |work=[[The Guardian]] |language=en-GB |issn=0261-3077 |agency=[[Associated Press]]}}</ref>

==Meteorological history==
[[File:Porto Alegre (May 6-20, 2024).jpg|thumb|Satellite images of the affected areas on 6 May (top) and 20 April (bottom), during and before the floods]]
An [[Block (meteorology)|atmospheric block]], caused by a [[Anticyclone|high-pressure system]] in [[Centro-Sul]], prevented the displacement of typical meteorological systems (such as [[extratropical cyclone]]s, [[cold front]]s, and [[Trough (meteorology)|troughs]]) that cause precipitation.<ref>{{Cite news |date=26 April 2024 |title=Onda de calor no Brasil piorará com recordes históricos e até anticyclone was active, temperatures were {{cvt|5|to|10|C-change}} higher than the record values recorded by the [[National Institute of Meteorology]] (INMET), since [[Storm|areas of instability]] Heavy rain and strong winds started hitting the northern part of the state on 28&nbsp;April; by the following day, they had spread to almost the entire state. Storms occurring between 28&nbsp;April and 1&nbsp;May were caused by a cold front associated with a [[low-pressure area]] over the sea, while also being influenced by a [[Humidity#Climate|moisture flow]] coming from the north of the country.<ref>{{Cite news |date=1 May 2024 |title=Frente fria vai avançar pelo RS e SC com mais chuva intensa e temporais |url=https://metsul.com/frente-fria-vai-avancar-pelo-rs-e-sc-com-mais-chuva-intensa-e-temporais/ |access-date=7 May 2024 |work=MetSul Meteorologia |language=pt-BR |archive-date=4 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240504104429/https://metsul.com/frente-fria-vai-avancar-pelo-rs-e-sc-com-mais-chuva-intensa-e-temporais/ |url-status=live }}</ref> According to an INMET report, rainfall reached over {{Convert|150|mm|in|2}} in some parts of Rio Grande do Sul in 24&nbsp;hours on 30&nbsp;April;<ref name="Sky" /> they later reported that the average precipitation in the Porto Alegre area in the first twelve days of May could amount up to {{Convert|333.1|mm|in}}, almost thrice the average monthly value recorded between 1991 and 2020 ({{Convert|113|mm|in}}).<ref name="GloboReport" />

==Aftermath==
[[File:Assistência - Enchentes RS - 2024.jpg|thumb|upright|Food storage for victims in Porto Alegre]]
The floods were considered the worst to hit Brazil in over 80{{Nbsp}}years,<ref name="AJ" /> and marked the fourth such environmental disaster in a [[Tropical year|solar year]], as previous floods killed 75 people between July, September, and November 2023.<ref name="IlPost" /><ref name="Guardian" />

[[Climatologist]] Francisco Eliseu Aquino told AFP that, while the region was already prone to extreme climate events caused by the collision of [[Tropics|tropical]] and [[Geographical pole|polar]] [[air mass]]es, these events had "intensified" due to the effects of [[climate change]],<ref name="IlPost" /><ref name="Barrons" /> stating that the storms were the result of a "disastrous cocktail" of global warming and the [[El Niño–Southern Oscillation|El Niño]] climate phenomenon.<ref name="Barrons" />

====Mobilization of military resources====
On 6 May, the [[Brazilian Navy]] announced the dispatch of the largest warship in South America, the [[helicopter carrier]] {{ship|Brazilian aircraft carrier|Atlântico||2}}, which would depart Rio de Janeiro and head to the coast of Rio Grande do Sul. ''Atlântico'' was expected to carry 200 [[Brazilian Marine Corps|Marine Corps]] officers, 40 vehicles and eight between medium, and small vessels to help rescue stranded victims and transport supplies through flooded roads.<ref>{{Cite web |date=7 May 2024 |title=Marinha envia nesta quarta maior navio de guerra da América Latina para ajudar população do Rio Grande do Sul |url=https://g1.globo.com/politica/noticia/2024/05/06/marinha-envia-nesta-quarta-maior-navio-de-guerra-da-america-latina-para-ajudar-populacao-do-rio-grande-do-sul.ghtml |access-date=7 May 2024 |website=[[G1 (website)|G1]] |language=pt-BR |archive-date=7 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240507002831/https://g1.globo.com/politica/noticia/2024/05/06/marinha-envia-nesta-quarta-maior-navio-de-guerra-da-america-latina-para-ajudar-populacao-do-rio-grande-do-sul.ghtml |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name=":0" />
