Presidential elections were held in [[Slovakia]] in March and April 2024. Incumbent president [[Zuzana Čaputová]] announced in June 2023 that she would not run for a second term.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Reakcie na rozhodnutie prezidentky / Pavel vyjadril Čaputovej uznanie, Kiska cíti smútok a Smer ju kritizuje|url=https://www.postoj.sk/132724/dnes-treba-vediet|access-date=29 June 2023|website=Postoj.sk|language=sk|archive-date=29 June 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230629081249/https://www.postoj.sk/132724/dnes-treba-vediet|url-status=live}}</ref>
	
In the first round held on 23 March, [[Ivan Korčok]] led with 43% of the votes followed by [[Peter Pellegrini]] with 37%. As no candidate received a majority, a runoff was scheduled between the two on 6 April.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Korčok wins the first round of Slovakia's presidential election |url=https://spectator.sme.sk/c/23300627/korcok-wins-the-first-round-of-slovakias-presidential-election.html|website=The Slovak Spectator|date=23 March 2024|access-date=7 April 2024}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-03-23 |title=Slovakia: Presidential election proceeds to runoff |url=https://www.dw.com/en/slovakia-presidential-election-proceeds-to-runoff/a-68652919 |access-date=2024-04-07 |website=Deutche Welle}}</ref> However, in the second round, Pellegrini defeated Korčok with 53% of the vote. He was sworn in as president on 15 June.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ukraine-sceptic, PM Fico ally Pellegrini wins Slovakia presidential election|url=https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240407-ukraine-sceptic-pellegrini-wins-slovakia-presidential-election|website=France 24|date=7 April 2024}}</ref>

The election was widely regarded as a win for Slovakia's prime minister [[Robert Fico]] who saw his ally elected as President.

==Electoral system==
The [[President of Slovakia]] is elected using the [[two-round system]]; if no candidate gains a majority in the first round, a runoff will be held between the top-two candidates.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Slovak Republic: Election for president|url=https://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/3140/|website=electionguide.org|date=16 March 2019|access-date=13 November 2021|archive-date=13 November 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211113231412/https://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/3140/|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Candidates==
===Eligibility===
Slovak citizens not sentenced for an intentional crime aged 40 or more are eligible to run for the office of president.<ref>{{cite web|title=Prezidentské voľby: Kto môže kandidovať na prezidenta?|url=https://www.aktuality.sk/clanok/660240/prezidentske-volby-2024-kto-moze-kandidovat-za-prezidenta/|website=[[Aktuality.sk]]|date=23 January 2019|access-date=31 January 2024|language=sk|archive-date=31 January 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240131163723/https://www.aktuality.sk/clanok/660240/prezidentske-volby-2024-kto-moze-kandidovat-za-prezidenta/|url-status=live}}</ref> 

Each candidacy has to be supported by either 15 Members of the [[National Council of Slovakia]] or signatures of 15,000 citizens of Slovakia to be admissible. Candidates fulfilling eligibility criteria had until midnight 30 January 2024 to formally confirm their candidacy.

==Campaign==
{{Expand Slovak|Voľba prezidenta Slovenskej republiky v roku 2024|date=April 2024|section=yes|topic=debates}}
{{expand section|date=March 2024}}
Milan Náhlik, Štefan Harabin, Marian Kotleba and Peter Pellegrini have expressed statements sympathetic with Russia in relation to its [[Russian invasion of Ukraine|invasion of Ukraine]] and critical of the [[European Union]], with Pellegrini supporting Ukraine opening negotiations with Russia. Meanwhile, Ivan Korčok has been supportive of the West and Ukrainian resistance against Russia.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Slovakia's presidential election: A choice between Russia and the West|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/3/22/presidential-election-forces-slovakia-to-choose-between-russia-and-the-west|website=[[Al Jazeera]]|date=20 March 2024|access-date=22 March 2024|archive-date=22 March 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240322133922/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/3/22/presidential-election-forces-slovakia-to-choose-between-russia-and-the-west|url-status=live}}</ref> During the campaign, Pellegrini accused Korčok of being a warmonger who wants to send troops to Ukraine, which Korčok denied.<ref>{{Cite web|title=A pro-Western career diplomat faces an ally of the populist premier in Slovakia's presidential vote|url=https://apnews.com/article/slovakia-presidential-election-korcok-pellegrini-fico-126e8cf1fd04e6bb054e0d55c90ea7f9|website=[[Associated Press]]|date=5 April 2024|access-date=5 April 2024|archive-date=5 April 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240405153550/https://apnews.com/article/slovakia-presidential-election-korcok-pellegrini-fico-126e8cf1fd04e6bb054e0d55c90ea7f9|url-status=live}}</ref> Pellegrini also said that he was running for president "to rescue the government of [Prime Minister] [[Robert Fico]]." Fico also openly supported Pellegrini, calling him a “a moderate candidate who recognises the value of peace" and described Korčok as supporting "everything the West tells him without hesitation".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Slovakia holds second round of presidential vote amid deep divisions over Ukraine war|url=https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240406-slovakia-holds-second-round-of-presidential-election-amid-deep-divisions-over-ukraine-war|website=[[France 24]]|access-date=24 March 2024|archive-date=6 April 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240406073807/https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240406-slovakia-holds-second-round-of-presidential-election-amid-deep-divisions-over-ukraine-war|url-status=live}}</ref> Pellegrini and Fico's accusations were made despite the office of the president not having constitutional powers to deploy military forces abroad.<ref name="bbcresult">{{Cite news |title=Peter Pellegrini: Russia-friendly populist elected Slovak president |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-68754112 |website=[[BBC]] |date=7 April 2024 |access-date=7 April 2024 |archive-date=7 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240407001231/https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-68754112 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Korčok ran on the notion of "not letting Robert Fico take everything" 

Following the results of the first round, Korčok said that he acknowledged a "need to reach out more to voters who supported the government parties,” adding that it was also clear that “they’re not satisfied with how this government is ruling, where it’s taking Slovakia.” Pellegrini congratulated Korčok for his first place and pledged to hold “several duels, as it should be” with Korčok before the second round, and “not to succumb” to an aggressive campaign style, having only engaged once in a debate prior to the first round of voting. He also noted that a majority of voters voted for nationalist candidates and not "a liberal, progressive" candidate, and predicted the second round of voting to be a tight race.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Slovak president election: Pro-EU diplomat tops coalition stalwart in first round |url=https://www.politico.eu/article/coalition-stalwart-edges-pro-eu-opponent-in-first-round-slovak-presidential-ballot/ |access-date=2024-03-24 |website=Politico |date=23 March 2024 |language=en |archive-date=24 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240324142549/https://www.politico.eu/article/coalition-stalwart-edges-pro-eu-opponent-in-first-round-slovak-presidential-ballot/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=A pro-West diplomat and an ally of populist premier to meet in runoff for Slovakia's presidency |url=https://apnews.com/article/slovakia-president-election-fist-round-a2aedaa45c03362ccc0aa478c43404f4|website=Associated Press |date=23 March 2024 |access-date=24 March 2024|archive-date=24 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240324142549/https://apnews.com/article/slovakia-president-election-fist-round-a2aedaa45c03362ccc0aa478c43404f4 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Slovakia's pro-West diplomat Korcok to face off with Fico ally Pelligrini in presidential runoff |url=https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240324-pro-ukraine-korcok-ex-pm-pellegrini-set-for-slovakia-presidential-runoff|website=France 24|date=24 March 2024|archive-date=24 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240324152409/https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240324-pro-ukraine-korcok-ex-pm-pellegrini-set-for-slovakia-presidential-runoff |url-status=live }}</ref>

President Čaputová denounced war rhetoric used during the campaigning for the second round and defended both Pellegrini and Korčok from allegations that they were willing to deploy soldiers in Ukraine.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Slovakia elects new president amid divisions over Ukraine war |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/4/6/slovakia-elects-new-president-amid-divisions-over-ukraine-war|website=Al Jazeera|access-date=7 April 2024|archive-date=6 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240406222001/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/4/6/slovakia-elects-new-president-amid-divisions-over-ukraine-war |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Aftermath==
Following the results, Pellegrini pledged to be "a president of all Slovak citizens" and "be always by the side of Slovakia." Korčok conceded defeat and congratulated Pellegrini, while acknowledging that he was "disappointed" by the electoral result.<ref>{{Cite web |title=A close ally of populist prime minister tops a pro-Western diplomat to become Slovakia's president |url=https://apnews.com/article/slovakia-presidential-election-korcok-pellegrini-fico-c7c4873395ddae50bd4ead0304da8297|website=Associated Press |date=7 April 2024 |archive-date=7 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240407062730/https://apnews.com/article/slovakia-presidential-election-korcok-pellegrini-fico-c7c4873395ddae50bd4ead0304da8297 |url-status=live }}</ref> 

Pellegrini's victory is seen by analysts to be a gain for the government of Prime Minister Robert Fico, with Pellegrini having been acquiescent in Fico's efforts to overhaul the justice system, which had launched investigations into members of Fico's [[SMER]] party and place public broadcasting under state control, in addition to sharing his views on Ukraine and relations with Russia.<ref name="bbcresult"/>
