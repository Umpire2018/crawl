<!-- Paragraph about what they are-->
Protests followed the [[2024 Venezuelan presidential election]] on 28 July, in response to [[2024 Venezuelan presidential election#Fraud allegations|voter fraud]] and [[2024 Venezuelan presidential election#Conduct and irregularities|other irregularities during the election cycle]], as part of the [[2024 Venezuelan political crisis]]. The election and unrest occurred in the context of the ongoing [[crisis in Venezuela]].

<!--Paragraph about background and fraud-->Statistical analyses by multiple organizations<ref name=CNNAfterVote/> indicated that the election was won convincingly by [[Edmundo González Urrutia|Edmundo Gonzalez]] but those [[2024 Venezuelan presidential election#Results|results have not been recognized]] by incumbent [[Nicolás Maduro]]; the [[Democratic Unitary Platform]] (PUD), an alliance of opposition parties, released vote tallies at the precinct level indicating that González won by a wide margin, while the government-controlled [[National Electoral Council (Venezuela)|National Electoral Council]] (CNE) announced an unsubstantiated result, without any precinct-level tallies, stating Maduro won. Both candidates claimed victory, while [[International reactions to the 2024 Venezuelan presidential election|many countries recognized González]] as the winner.

<!-- Paragraph about the protests-->Demonstrations to uphold the results of the election, along with vigils for political prisoners, occurred worldwide after the July election. Spontaneous protests broke out immediately after the election, while later rallies were organized by the [[Venezuelan opposition]];<ref name=AFPMaduroYield>{{cite news|url=https://www.france24.com/en/americas/20240828-venezuela-opposition-leader-machado-vows-to-make-maduro-yield-on-election-fraud|work=[[France 24]]|title=Venezuela opposition leader Machado vows to make Maduro 'yield' on election 'fraud'|date=28 August 2024}}</ref> Maduro claimed the opposition was encouraging a [[coup d'état|coup]] and has charged demonstraters with terrorism, while initiating an unprecedented [[#Crackdown|crackdown]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Phillips |first1=Tom |last2=Torres |first2=Patricia |date=3 August 2024 |title='Maduro has lost the streets': In Venezuela's barrios, former loyal voters risk all in protests |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/article/2024/aug/03/maduro-has-lost-the-streets-in-venezuelas-barrios-former-loyal-voters-risk-all-in-protests |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |agency=[[The Observer]]}}</ref> Maduro's security forces have gone door-to-door seeking to arrest protesters, poll workers and members of the opposition in what Maduro has referred to as ''[[Operation Tun Tun]]'', and armed bands of Maduro supporters known as ''[[Colectivo (Venezuela)|colectivos]]'' have joined security forces in repressing dissent. As of 14 August 2024, at least 2,200 persons are reported to have been arrested, and 25 killed;<ref name=HundredThousand>{{cite news |first1=Antonio Maria |last1=Delgado |first2=Syra |last2=Ortiz Blanes |title=A hundred thousand Venezuelans are considering leaving the country right now, poll says |newspaper=Miami Herald |publisher=Yahoo News |date=14 August 2024 |url=https://ca.news.yahoo.com/hundred-thousand-venezuelans-considering-leaving-093000006.html}}</ref> Maduro has announced plans to continue to seek the arrest of dissenters, and to rehabilitate two prisons to house those detained.

<!--Paragraph about reactions to the protests-->The repression has been [[#International|widely condemned]] by international groups; [[Amnesty International]] penned an open letter requesting urgent action from the [[International Criminal Court]] (ICC),<ref name=AIonICC/> on the basis of an [[International Criminal Court investigation in Venezuela|ongoing ICC investigation]] of possible [[crimes against humanity]] under Maduro's regime.

== Background ==
{{Crisis in Bolivarian Venezuela short}}

== Chronology ==
During the month of July, the [[Venezuelan Observatory of Social Conflict]] (OVCS: Observatorio Venezolano de Conflictividad Social) documented 1,311 protests throughout Venezuela; 70% were on 29 or 30 July.<ref name=VenezuelaRegistered>{{cite news|url=https://www.infobae.com/venezuela/2024/08/24/venezuela-registro-1311-protestas-durante-el-mes-de-julio-impulsadas-por-el-rechazo-al-fraude-electoral-de-maduro/|work=[[Infobae]]|date=24 August 2024|access-date=24 August 2024|language=es|title=Venezuela registró 1.311 protestas durante el mes de julio, impulsadas por el rechazo al fraude electoral de Maduro|trans-title=Venezuela registered 1,311 protests during the month of July, driven by the rejection of Maduro's electoral fraud}}</ref> OVCS said most of the protests occurred in poor areas, and involved "harassment, arbitrary arrests, threats and political retaliation against leaders and sympathizers of opposition political parties".<ref name=VenezuelaRegistered/>
===2024===
====28 July: Election day ====
On 28 July 2024, the day of the election, Venezuelans in the Brazilian cities, [[Pacaraima]], [[Boa Vista, Roraima|Boa Vista]], and [[São Paulo]], demonstrated against Maduro. In São Paulo, hundreds of Venezuelan migrants participated in a demonstration in defense of democracy and calling for freedom.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Rodrigues |first=Caíque |date=29 July 2024 |title=Venezuela volta a reabrir fronteira com o Brasil após fechar por 1h |url=https://g1.globo.com/rr/roraima/noticia/2024/07/29/venezuela-reabre-fronteira-com-o-brasil.ghtml |access-date=29 July 2024 |website=G1 |language=pt}}</ref> On the same day, Julio Valerio García, a citizen of [[Táchira]], was shot dead by a group of motorcyclists. There were also four injured that day.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Hernández |first=Rosalinda |date=29 July 2024 |title=Táchira: con un fallecido y varios heridos cierra proceso electoral |url=https://elestimulo.com/elecciones-2024/2024-07-29/tachira-con-un-fallecido-y-varios-heridos-cierra-proceso-electoral/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240730121231/https://elestimulo.com/elecciones-2024/2024-07-29/tachira-con-un-fallecido-y-varios-heridos-cierra-proceso-electoral/ |archive-date=30 July 2024 |access-date=29 July 2024 |website=El Estímulo |language=es}}</ref>

Neither Machado nor González called for protests, but instead asked people to be present at the voting centers until they had records of the votes cast.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Moleiro |first=Alonso |date=29 July 2024 |title=María Corina Machado rechaza reconocer como ganador a Maduro: 'Venezuela tiene un nuevo presidente electo y es Edmundo González' |url=https://elpais.com/america/2024-07-29/maria-corina-machado-rechaza-reconocer-como-ganador-a-maduro-venezuela-tiene-un-nuevo-presidente-electo-y-es-edmundo-gonzalez.html |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240730122509/https://elpais.com/america/2024-07-29/maria-corina-machado-rechaza-reconocer-como-ganador-a-maduro-venezuela-tiene-un-nuevo-presidente-electo-y-es-edmundo-gonzalez.html |archive-date=30 July 2024 |access-date=29 July 2024 |website=El País América |language=es}}</ref>

On 28 July, which would have been the 70th birthday of [[Hugo Chávez]],<ref name=ChavezBirthday>{{cite news|url=https://elpais.com/america/2024-03-05/venezuela-anuncia-la-celebracion-de-elecciones-para-el-28-de-julio-el-dia-del-nacimiento-de-chavez.html|work=El Pais|date=5 March 2024|access-date=24 August 2024|language=es|title=Venezuela celebrará las elecciones presidenciales el 28 de julio, el día del nacimiento de Chávez|trans-title=Venezuela will hold presidential elections on July 28, Chavez's birthday|author=Quesada, Juan Diego}}</ref> nine statues of him were brought down by protesters.<ref name= ElPaisCannotPerform>{{cite news |url=https://english.elpais.com/international/2024-08-22/venezuela-election-edmundo-gonzalez-says-supreme-court-cannot-perform-the-functions-of-the-national-electoral-council.html |work=El Pais |date=22 August 2024 |title=Venezuela election: Edmundo González says Supreme Court cannot perform the functions of the National Electoral Council |last=Moleiro |first=Alonso}}</ref>

==== 17 August: Great National March for Peace ====
The [[Agence France-Presse]] (AFP) reported that "the ruling 'Chavista' movement ...&nbsp;also called demonstrations ...&nbsp;'in support of the victory' of the president".<ref name=AFPRivalProtests>{{cite news |url=https://www.france24.com/en/americas/20240817-venezuelan-opposition-holds-nationwide-rallies-against-maduro-reelection-claim |work=France 24 |publisher=AFP |title=Venezuelan opposition holds nationwide rallies against Maduro reelection claim |date=17 August 2024}}</ref>

According to the [[United Socialist Party of Venezuela]] (PSUV—of which Maduro serves as president), "the Venezuelan people overflowed with love and joy in all states of the country" in the Great National March for Peace and in support of President Nicolás Maduro (Gran Marcha Nacional por la Paz y en apoyo al President Nicolás Maduro).<ref name=PSUV>{{cite press release |url=http://www.psuv.org.ve/portada/galeria-imagenes-gran-marcha-nacional-por-paz-y-apoyo-a-victoria-presidente-nicolas-maduro/ |publisher=[[PSUV]] |title=Gran Marcha Nacional por la Paz y en apoyo a la victoria del presidente Nicolás Maduro |language=es |trans-title=The Great National March for Peace and in support of President Nicolás Maduro |date=17 August 2024}}</ref>

[[Globovisión]] ([[Venezuelan sanctions|sanctioned by the US]]<ref name="Treas8Jan2019">{{cite press release |url=https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sm583 |publisher=U.S. Department of the Treasury |date=8 January 2019 |access-date=2 April 2019 |title=Treasury targets Venezuela currency exchange network scheme generating billions of dollars for corrupt regime insiders}}</ref><ref name="NTN8Jan2019">{{cite news |date=8 January 2019 |title=EE. UU. sanciona a siete empresarios venezolanos, entre ellos los dueños de Globovisión |work=NTN24 |url=http://www.ntn24.com/america-latina/venezuela/ee-uu-sanciona-siete-empresarios-venezolanos-entre-ellos-los-duenos-de |access-date=2 April 2019}}</ref>) wrote that the large movement to support Maduro's victory began early before ending at [[Miraflores Palace]] (the presidential palace) as a celebration "in rejection of far-right fascism".<ref name=Globovision>{{cite news |url=https://www.globovision.com/nacional/26042/inicia-gran-marcha-nacional-en-apoyo-al-presidente-maduro-y-por-la-paz-de-venezuela |work=[[Globovisión]] |date=17 August 2024 |title=Así transcurrió la gran marcha nacional en respaldo a Maduro (+Fotos) |language=es |trans-title=This is how the great national march in support of Maduro took place (+Photos)}}</ref> Globovision says that the celebrations occurred in 100 Venezuelan cities and comprised 10,000 ''motorizados'' (motorcycles in Venezuelan Spanish). [[Eduardo Piñate]] led the celebrations in [[San Fernando de Apure]]; Globovisión states that he "highlighted that all of Venezuela repudiates fascism and the psychological operations of imperialism and the extreme right that has sold out to foreign interests".<ref name=Globovision/> [[Aragua]] state governor, Karina Carpio, "called on the people not to retreat and maintain the legacy of Bolívar and Chávez to a free, independent and sovereign homeland".<ref name=Globovision/>

[[Infobae]] wrote that Maduro railed against González for not being present at the opposition rally, asking "Where is he hidden?"<ref name=WhereHidden>{{cite news |url=https://www.infobae.com/venezuela/2024/08/17/el-dictador-maduro-volvio-a-arremeter-contra-edmundo-gonzalez-urrutia-donde-esta-escondido/ |work=[[Infobae]] |date=17 August 2024 |title=El dictador Maduro volvió a arremeter contra Edmundo González Urrutia: '¿Dónde está escondido?' |language=es |trans-title=The dictator Maduro once again attacked Edmundo González Urrutia: 'Where is he hidden?'}}</ref> Speaking in front of Miraflores, Maduro mocked González as hiding in a cave and plotting to flee to Miami. With respect to Machado, he stated that "she deflated, she failed, they don't even want her in the opposition".<ref name=WhereHidden/>

==== 28 August: One-month election anniversary ====
[[File:2024 Venezuelan protest 28 Aug 03.jpg|thumb|Machado addressing 28 August demonstration in Caracas]]
Machado called for a return to the street on 28 August with a rally centered at Avenida Francisco de Miranda in Caracas to reject the [[2024 Venezuelan political crisis#Transparency in vote reporting and Supreme Court ruling|TSJ validation of Maduro as victor in the election]], with a rally slogan of ''A la calle el 28'' (to the street on the 28th) and the hashtag, #ActaMataSentencia ('a record kills a sentence', referring to the vote ''actas'' being stronger than a TSJ sentence<ref name=EFENiUnPais/>).<ref name=AFPCandidateDue>{{cite news|url=https://www.barrons.com/news/venezuela-opposition-candidate-due-in-court-over-election-dispute-9e41d645|publisher= Agence France-Press|work=Barron's|title=Venezuela Opposition Candidate Due Before Prosecutors In Election Dispute|author=Tosta, Andrea|date= 25 August 2024|access-date=26 August 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.infobae.com/venezuela/2024/08/25/maria-corina-machado-convoco-a-los-venezolanos-a-movilizarse-el-proximo-28-de-agosto-a-un-mes-del-fraude-electoral-del-chavismo/ |work=[[Infobae]] |date=25 August 2024 |title=María Corina Machado convocó a los venezolanos a movilizarse el próximo 28 de agosto, a un mes del fraude electoral del chavismo |language=es |trans-title=Maria Corina Machado called on Venezuelans to mobilize on August 28, one month after the electoral fraud of Chavismo}}</ref>

Machado came out of hiding for this fourth organized rally<ref name=AFPMaduroYield/> and stated during her speech to the crowd in Caracas that no democratic countries recognize Maduro's victory and asked the Armed Forces to defend the constitution,<ref name=EFENiUnPais>{{cite web|url=https://efe.com/mundo/2024-08-28/maria-corina-machado-venezuela-protesta-maduro-elecciones/|publisher=[[EFE]]|language=es|access-date=2 September 2024|date=28 August 2024|trans-title=Maria Corina Machado: 'Not a single democratic government in the world has recognized Maduro's fraud'|title=María Corina Machado: 'Ni un solo Gobierno democrático del mundo ha reconocido el fraude de Maduro'}}</ref> speaking to a smaller group than at earlier protests.<ref name=APTakeToStreets>{{cite news|url=https://apnews.com/article/venezuela-election-maduro-protests-3a3dc0501297ba0e658a30bd022fc2a2|publisher=Associated Press|author1=Goodman, Joshua|author2=Rueda, Jorge|date=28 August 2024|access-date=3 September 2024|title=Maduro opponents take to streets to revive protests disputing Venezuelan election results}}</ref>

Maduro backers also held a rally the same day. Smaller protests against Maduro convened in San Cristobal, Barquisimeto, Valencia and [[Puerto Ordaz]];<ref name=ReutersRalliesArrests>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/dueling-rallies-expected-venezuela-mark-one-month-disputed-election-2024-08-28/|work=Reuters|date=28 August 2024|access-date=2 September 2024|title=Rallies and arrests mark one month since disputed Venezuela election|author1=Nava, Mariela|author2=Romero, Tibisay|author3=Sequera, Vivian}}</ref> protests characterized by the Associated Press as "tiny" were held in other countries of Latin America.<ref name=APTakeToStreets/>

== Siege against diplomatic mission ==
{{off topic|reason= no connection made to protests|date=September 2024}}

Patrols from [[Bolivarian National Intelligence Service]] (SEBIN) and the [[Bolivarian National Police]] were deployed in front of the embassy and the embassy's electricity was cut.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://elpais.com/argentina/2024-07-30/maduro-da-72-horas-a-los-diplomaticos-argentinos-para-abandonar-venezuela-y-deja-sin-luz-a-su-embajada.html |work=El Pais |date=30 July 2024 |language=es |title=Maduro da 72 horas a los diplomáticos argentinos para abandonar Venezuela y deja sin luz a su Embajada |trans-title=Maduro gives Argentine diplomats 72 hours to leave Venezuela and leaves the Embassy without electricity |first=Mar |last=Centenera}}</ref> Brazil agreed to take custody of the Argentinian embassy.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/brazilian-flag-raised-argentine-embassy-caracas-2024-08-01/ |work=Reuters |date=1 August 2024 |title=Brazil running Argentina, Peru embassies in Caracas after election-related expulsions |first1=Leonardo |last1=Fernandez |first2=Lisandra |last2=Paraguassu}}</ref>

Later on 6 September, the security forces again surrounded the embassy, shutting down road access to the embassy and cutting electric power; the move was widely condemned by the international community as a violation of the [[Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://edition.cnn.com/2024/09/07/americas/venezuelan-security-forces-surround-argentine-embassy-intl/index.html |title=Venezuelan security forces surround Argentine embassy after opposition members take refuge inside |last1=Torres |first1=Mauricio |last2=Alvarado |first2=Abel |date=7 September 2024 |website=cnn.com |publisher=[[CNN]] |access-date=10 September 2024}}</ref> On the same day, the Venezuelan government unilaterally revoked Brazil's custody of the Argentinian embassy. The [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Brazil)|Brazilian foreign ministry]] rejected the unilateral revocation of custody and stated that Brazil would remain in custody of the embassy until another nation was willing to assume custody of the embassy.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/9/7/venezuela-ends-brazils-management-of-argentine-affairs-amid-ongoing-spat |title=Venezuela ends Brazil's management of Argentine affairs amid ongoing spat |date=7 September 2024 |website=aljazeera.com |publisher=[[Al Jazeera]] |access-date=10 September 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://apnews.com/article/venezuela-brazil-diplomacy-f8661fdab3c154b839bc9d48954cbd72 |title=Venezuela revokes Brazil's custody of diplomatic mission that's housing 6 Maduro opponents |date=7 September 2024 |website=apnews.com |publisher=[[Associated Press]] |access-date=10 September 2024}}</ref>

===International ===
The [[Independent International Fact-Finding Mission on Venezuela|UN International Mission]] expressed "its deep concern about the violence and allegations of human rights violations reported in the country following Sunday's presidential election."<ref>{{Cite web |last=Singer |first=Florantonia |date=2 August 2024 |title=María Corina Machado se pone a resguardo por seguridad y convoca a una marcha nacional para el sábado |url=https://elpais.com/america/2024-08-02/maria-corina-machado-se-pone-a-resguardo-por-seguridad-y-convoca-a-una-marcha-nacional-para-el-sabado.html |access-date=3 August 2024 |website=[[El País]] |language=es}}</ref> The repression has been condemned by [[Human Rights Watch]].<ref>{{cite web|website=[[Human Rights Watch]]|title=Venezuela Presidential Election: International Organizations Call on Authorities to Guarantee Transparency|date=30 July 2024 |url=https://www.hrw.org/news/2024/07/30/venezuela-presidential-election-international-organizations-call-authorities}}</ref>

According to ''[[Infobae]]'' a "forceful statement following the electoral fraud in Venezuela and the criminalization of protests" was published on 1 August by [[Amnesty International]] (AI) together with eleven human rights organizations that "condemned the repressive actions of the Nicolás Maduro regime in Venezuela and demanded that it guarantee the right to protest and full respect for the rights to life, personal integrity and freedom".<ref name=InfoBaeDR/> In addition to AI, the signers of the statement were [[CIVICUS]], [[Freedom House]], [[International Commission of Jurists]], [[Robert F. Kennedy Human Rights]], [[Washington Office on Latin America]], [[World Organization Against Torture]] and others. The joint statement mentioned a "disproportionate use of force by Venezuelan security forces" and "call[ed] on [Venezuelan] authorities to refrain from criminalizing protest" and "avoid the use of speeches that encourage and incite violence" against protesters. It highlighted "the use of lethal weapons", along with "politically motivated detentions, potentially unlawful killings", restrictions on freedom of the press, and internet shutdowns". The signatories reminded that Article 68 of the Venezuelan Constitution requires the right of demonstrators to be protected, and encouraged the international community to document crimes committed in the post-electoral process to the United Nations and the ICC.<ref name=InfoBaeDR>{{cite news |url=https://www.infobae.com/venezuela/2024/08/01/prestigiosas-organizaciones-de-ddhh-condenaron-en-conjunto-las-acciones-represivas-del-regimen-de-nicolas-maduro/ |work=Infobae |date=1 August 2024 |first=Gastón |last=Calvo |language=es |title=Organizaciones de Derechos Humanos condenaron en conjunto las acciones represivas del régimen de Nicolás Maduro |trans-title=Human Rights Organizations jointly condemned the repressive actions of the Nicolás Maduro regime}}</ref><ref>{{cite press release |url=https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2024/08/venezuela-organizaciones-internacionales-condenan-represion-masiva-exigen-autoridad-protejer-protesta |publisher=Amnesty International |date=1 August 2024 |title=Venezuela: International organizations condemn the high levels of violence and repression and demand that the authorities guarantee the right to protest and full respect for the rights to life, personal integrity and freedom}}</ref>

[[Brian A. Nichols]], of the US Department of State, condemned the ongoing repression and violence.<ref>{{Cite web |date=3 August 2024 |title=EEUU condenó la escalada represiva en Venezuela tras el fraude electoral: 'Demuestra la dependencia de Maduro del miedo para aferrarse al poder' |url=https://www.infobae.com/venezuela/2024/08/03/eeuu-condeno-la-escalada-represiva-en-venezuela-tras-el-fraude-electoral-demuestra-la-dependencia-de-maduro-del-miedo-para-aferrarse-al-poder/ |access-date=3 August 2024 |website=[[infobae]] |language=es-AR}}</ref>

In an emergency session of [[Organization of American States]], the secretary general [[Luis Almagro]] said he would file a report against Maduro to the [[International Criminal Court]] (ICC), which is [[International Criminal Court investigation in Venezuela|investigating Venezuela]] for [[crimes against humanity]], and ask the ICC to issue an arrest warrant against Maduro for the repression that is being committed after the election.<ref>{{Cite news |date=31 July 2024 |title=Secretario general de la OEA pedirá a Corte Penal Internacional el arresto de Nicolás Maduro |url=https://www.milenio.com/internacional/titular-de-oea-pedira-a-corte-penal-internacional-el-arresto-de-maduro |access-date=1 August 2024 |website=Milenio |language=es}}</ref> On 12 August, [[Karim Ahmad Khan]], who is [[Prosecutor of the International Criminal Court]], stated that he had received reports regarding the repression and violence that has been committed by the Maduro regime and stated that the ICC is actively monitoring the situation.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.voanews.com/a/icc-prosecutor-monitors-venezuela-tensions-amid-election-protests/7739227.html |title=ICC prosecutor monitors Venezuela tensions amid election protests |date=12 August 2024 |website=voanews.com |publisher=Voice of America |access-date=14 August 2024 |quote=}}</ref> [[Amnesty International]] penned an open letter requesting urgent action from the ICC<ref name=AIonICC>{{cite web |publisher=[[Amnesty International]] |title=Open letter to the Prosecutor of the International Criminal Court: Urgent call for action on Venezuela |url=https://www.amnesty.org/en/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/AMR5384152024ENGLISH.pdf |date=9 August 2024}}</ref> on the basis of the ongoing ICC investigation of Venezuela under Maduro's regime. On 18 August, a group of international jurists filed a complaint to the ICC to demand an arrest warrant be issued against Maduro.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.infobae.com/venezuela/2024/08/18/juristas-internacionales-presentaron-ante-la-cpi-una-denuncia-por-crimenes-de-lesa-humanidad-en-venezuela-y-exigieron-la-emision-de-ordenes-de-arresto/ |title=Juristas internacionales presentaron ante la CPI una denuncia por crímenes de lesa humanidad en Venezuela y exigieron la emisión de órdenes de arresto |date=18 August 2024 |website=infobae.com |publisher=Infobae |access-date=19 August 2024 |quote=}}</ref> Individually, seven nations in the region (Argentina, Canada, Colombia, Chile, Paraguay, Peru, and Uruguay) have denounced Maduro in 2018 to the ICC.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2 September 2024 |title=Uruguay se adherirá a denuncia contra Maduro por presuntos crímenes de lesa humanidad ante la CPI |url=https://registrationcdnaws-1.com/lo-ultimo/uruguay-se-adherira-a-denuncia-contra-maduro-por-crimenes-de-lesa-humanidad-ante-la-cpii/ |access-date=3 September 2024 |website=VPI TV |language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.dw.com/en/argentina-calls-on-icc-to-order-arrest-of-venezuelas-maduro/a-70160517 |title=Argentina calls on ICC to order arrest of Venezuela's Maduro |date=7 September 2024 |publisher=[[Deutsche Welle]]}}</ref>

The [[Permanent Council of the Organization of American States|Permanent Council of the OAS]] met on 16 August and approved a resolution that called for the vote tallies to be protected and published, respect for the sovereignty of the election results, and emphasized the rights to basic freedoms without reprisals and arbitrary arrests.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.barrons.com/news/keep-up-the-fight-venezuela-opposition-on-eve-of-protests-a699a5ab |work=Barron's |publisher=Agence France Press |date=16 August 2024 |title='Keep Up The Fight:' Venezuela Opposition On Eve Of Protests}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.ntn24.com/noticias-politica/la-oea-aprueba-por-consenso-resolucion-que-pide-la-publicacion-de-las-actas-electorales-de-venezuela-508356 |work=NTN 24 |date=16 August 2024 |title=La OEA aprueba por consenso resolución que pide la publicación de las actas electorales de Venezuela |language=es |trans-title=The OAS approves by consensus a resolution that calls for the publication of Venezuela's electoral records}}</ref> The resolution urged the Venezuelan election authorities to end state violence.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://dominicantoday.com/dr/world/2024/08/16/oas-approves-resolution-urging-release-of-venezuelas-electoral-records/ |title=OAS approves resolution urging release of Venezuela's electoral records |date=16 August 2024 |website=dominicantoday.com |publisher=Dominican Today}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://voz.us/en/world/240817/15530/oas-and-european-union-countries-sign-resolutions-to-condemn-human-rights-violations-and-maduro-s-electoral-fraud-in-venezuela.html |title=OAS and European Union countries sign resolutions to condemn human rights violations and Maduro's electoral fraud in Venezuela |date=16 August 2024 |website=voz.us |publisher=Voz Media |access-date=17 August 2024}}</ref>
