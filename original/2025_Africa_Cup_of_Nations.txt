The '''2025 [[Africa Cup of Nations]]''', also referred to as '''AFCON 2025''' or '''CAN 2025''', is scheduled to be the 35th edition of the biennial African [[association football|football]] tournament organised by the [[Confederation of African Football]] (CAF).<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-06-21 |title=AFCON moves to winter due to Club World Cup |url=https://africa.espn.com/football/story/_/id/40402895/africa-cup-nations-2025-winter-club-world-cup |access-date=2024-06-24 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref> It will be hosted by [[Morocco]] for the second time and the first since [[1988 African Cup of Nations|1988]].<ref>{{cite web|date=21 February 2024|title=AFCON 2025 gets summer dates in Morocco|url=https://www.afrik-foot.com/en-ng/afcon-2025-gets-summer-dates-in-morocco|website=Afrik Foot|access-date=10 March 2024}}</ref> Morocco was originally scheduled to host [[2015 Africa Cup of Nations|the 2015 edition]], but withdrew due to fears stemming from the [[Western African Ebola virus epidemic]].<ref>{{cite web|date=20 February 2024|title=AFCON 2025 to be scheduled forJuly-August 2025 in Morocco|url=https://www.africanews.com/2024/02/20/afcon-2025-to-be-scheduled-forjuly-august-2025-in-morocco|website=[[africanews]]|access-date=10 March 2024}}</ref>

Due to [[FIFA]] expanding its [[FIFA Club World Cup|Club World Cup]] competition to 32 teams and having it [[2025 FIFA Club World Cup|scheduled for June and July that year]], this edition of the tournament will be played between 21 December 2025 and 18 January 2026.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Aarons |first=Ed |last2=Molina |first2=Romain |last3=Aarons |first3=Exclusive by Ed |date=2024-06-12 |title=Afcon could move to December 2025 to avoid Club World Cup clash |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/article/2024/jun/12/afcon-could-move-to-december-2025-to-avoid-club-world-cup-clash |access-date=2024-06-21 |work=The Guardian |language=en-GB |issn=0261-3077}}</ref> The situation has been further complicated by the addition of two extra match days scheduled for the last two weeks of January in the expanded Champions League that begins this season in Europe.<ref>{{cite press release|date=3 July 2022|title=CAF President Dr Motsepe announces African Super League launch details, AFCON 2023 and Champions League key decisions|url=https://www.cafonline.com/press-release/news/caf-president-dr-motsepe-announces-african-super-league-launch-details-afcon2023|publisher=[[Confederation of African Football|CAFOnline.com]]|access-date=3 June 2023|quote=...the Executive Committee has decided that this edition of the tournament (the 2023 edition) will be postponed to the months of January and February 2024. The postponement is as a direct and sole result of the adverse weather conditions in the country and after also having received further technical opinion from experts on adverse effects of staging the matches in that period, as June and July are rainy seasons in Ivory Coast. |archive-date=3 July 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220703210736/https://www.cafonline.com/press-release/news/caf-president-dr-motsepe-announces-african-super-league-launch-details-afcon2023 |url-status=live}}</ref>

This edition of the tournament was scheduled to be the second after [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations|2019]] to take place during the northern hemisphere's summer (June-July), in order to reduce scheduling conflicts with European club teams and competitions;<ref>{{cite web|date=21 July 2017|title=Africa Cup of Nations to switch from January staging to June in edition]] was moved to January and February 2024 owing to the adverse summer weather conditions in Ivory Coast.<ref>{{cite web|last=Southby|first=Ben|date=3 July 2022|title=Africa Cup of Nations 2023 finals have been postponed and moved to January 2024 due to weather concerns in Ivory Coast|url=https://www.eurosport.co.uk/football/africa-cup-of-nations-2023-finals-have-been-postponed-and-moved-to-january-2024-due-to-weather-conce_sto9017466/story.shtml|website=[[Eurosport]]|access-date=3 process,<ref name="Reopen" /> Morocco was named as the new hosts on 27 September 2023.<ref name="2025Host">{{cite web|date=27 September 2023|title=Morocco to host 2025 Africa Cup of national football team|Ivory Coast]] are [[2023 Africa Cup of Nations final|the defending champions]].<ref name="MOTM">{{cite web |date=12 February 2024 |title=Nigeria 1–2 Ivory Coast: Sebastien ==Host selection==
CAF stripped Cameroon from hosting [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations|the 2019 edition]] of the tournament on 30 November 2018 due lack of speed of progress in preparations,<ref>{{cite web|date=30 November 2018|title=Cameroon stripped of hosting 2019 Africa Cup of Nations|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/46407474|website=[[BBC Sport]]|access-date=1 December 2018}}</ref> but accepted former CAF president [[Ahmad Ahmad]]'s request to stage [[2021 Africa Cup of Nations|the next edition in 2021]]. Consequently, the original hosts of 2021, Ivory Coast, became hosts of [[2023 Africa Cup of Nations|the 2023 edition]] with Guinea instead hosting the 2025 edition, which until then had no hosts.<ref>{{cite news|date=10 December 2018|title=CAN 2019 : le pays hôte sera connu le 9 janvier|trans-title=CAN 2019: the host country will be known on January 9|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/afrique/article/2018/12/10/can-2019-le-pays-hote-sera-connu-le-9-janvier_5395283_3212.html|website=[[Le Monde]]|language=fr-FR|access-date=1 February 2019}}</ref> The CAF President confirmed the timetable shift after a meeting with Ivorian president [[Alassane Ouattara]] in [[Abidjan]], Ivory Coast on 30 January 2019.<ref>{{cite news|date=30 January 2019|title=Cote d'Ivoire agrees CAF timetable shift|url=https://www.cafonline.com/news-center/news/cote-d-ivoire-agrees-caf-timetable-shift|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220111091908/https://www.cafonline.com/news-center/news/cote-d-ivoire-agrees-caf-timetable-shift|archive-date=11 January 2022|website=[[Confederation of African Football|CAFOnline.com]]|access-date=1 February 2019}}</ref> On 30 September 2022, current CAF president Patrice Motsepe announced that [[Guinea]] had been stripped as host for the 2025 edition due to inadequacy and speed of progress in hosting preparations.<ref name="Withdraw">{{cite web|date=30 September 2022|title=CAF President discusses withdrawal by CAF of AFCON Guinea 2025 with Guinea Transition President|url=https://www.cafonline.com/press-release/news/caf-president-discusses-withdrawal-by-caf-of-afcon-guinea-2025-with-guinea-trans|website=[[Confederation of African Football|CAFOnline.com]]|access-date=1 February 2023 |archive-date=27 January 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230127181626/https://www.cafonline.com/press-release/news/caf-president-discusses-withdrawal-by-caf-of-afcon-guinea-2025-with-guinea-trans |url-status=live }}</ref> Consequently, a new process was re-opened for a replacement host bidder.<ref name="Reopen">{{cite news|date=26 October 2022|title=CAF re-opens bidding process for the TotalEnergies Africa Cup of Nations 2025 hosting rights|url=https://www.cafonline.com/press-release/news/caf-re-opens-bidding-process-for-the-totalenergies-africa-cup-of-nations-2025-ho|website=[[Confederation of African Football|CAFOnline.com]]|access-date=1 November 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Diallo|first=Abubakr|date=1 October 2022|title=CAF : qui va organiser la CAN 2025 retirée à la Guinée ?|trans-title=CAF: who will organize the CAN 2025 withdrawn from Guinea?|url=https://www.afrik.com/caf-qui-va-organiser-la-can-2025-retiree-a-la-guinee|website=Afrik Foot|access-date=1 December 2022|language=fr-FR}}</ref> On 27 September 2023, the 2025 edition was awarded to [[Morocco]]<ref name="2025Host" /> and the 2027 edition to [[Kenya]], [[Uganda]], and [[Tanzania]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=27 September 2023|title=Tanzania, Uganda & Kenya to host 2027 TotalEnergies CAF Africa Cup of Nations|url=https://www.cafonline.com/news/tanzania-uganda-kenya-to-host-2027-totalenergies-caf-africa-cup-of-nations/|website=[[Confederation of African Football|CAFOnline.com]]|access-date=11 ==Qualification==
{{main|2025 Africa Cup of Nations qualification}}
[[File:2025 Africa Cup of Nations qualification map.png|thumb|{{Legend|#0000FF|Team qualified}}{{Legend|#FFB400|Team failed to qualify}}{{Legend|#000000|Team banned or did not enter}}{{Legend|#C1C1C1|Not a member of [[Confederation of African Football|CAF]]}}|300px]]

The qualifiers were held between 20 March and 19 November 2024, starting with the [[2025 Africa Cup of Nations qualification preliminary round|preliminary round]] (20–26 March 2024) and then the [[2025 Africa Cup of Nations qualification#Group stage|group stage]] (2 September–19 November 2024).<ref>{{Cite web |last=Oyebola |first=Mike |date=25 December 2023 |title=CAF announces kick-off date for AFCON 2025 qualifiers |url=https://dailypost.ng/2023/12/25/caf-announces-kick-off-date-for-afcon-2025-qualifiers/ |access-date=4 April 2024 |website=Daily Post Nigeria}}</ref> The preliminary round draw was held on 20 February 2024, 14:00 [[Central Africa Time|CAT]] ([[UTC+02:00|UTC+2]]) at the CAF headquarters in [[Cairo]], [[Egypt]].<ref name="draw result">{{cite web |date=20 February 2024 |title=TotalEnergies CAF Africa Cup of Nations Morocco 2025 Preliminary Draw conducted |url=https://www.cafonline.com/caf-africa-cup-of-nations/news/totalenergies-caf-africa-cup-of-nations-morocco-2025-preliminary-draw-conducted |access-date=27 March 2024 |website=[[Confederation of African Football|CAFOnline.com]]}}</ref> The eight involved national teams were seeded into two pots of four based on the [[FIFA World Rankings]] from 15 February 2024, <ref>{{Cite web |date=15 February 2024 |title=Men's Ranking |url=https://inside.fifa.com/fifa-world-ranking/men?dateId=id14289 |access-date=27 March 2024 |publisher=FIFA}}</ref> [[Seychelles national football team|Seychelles]] and [[Eritrea national football team|Eritrea]] were excluded from the qualifiers. The eight teams were split into four ties which were played in home-and-away [[Two-legged tie|two-legged]] format.<ref name="draw result" /> The four winners: [[Chad national football team|Chad]], [[Eswatini national football team|Eswatini]], [[Liberia national football team|Liberia]] and [[South Sudan national football team|South Sudan]] advanced to the group stage to join the 44 teams which entered directly.<ref name="result">{{Cite web |date=26 March 2024 |title=Chad and Eswatini reach group stage of TotalEnergies AFCON 2025 qualifiers |url=https://www.cafonline.com/caf-africa-cup-of-nations/news/chad-and-eswatini-reach-group-stage-of-totalenergies-afcon-2025-qualifiers/ |access-date=27 March 2024 |publisher=Confederation of African Football}}</ref> The group stage draw took place on 4 July 2024, 14:30 [[Central Africa Time|CAT]] ([[UTC+02:00|UTC+2]]) in [[Johannesburg]], South Africa. The 48 national teams involved were divided into twelve groups of four each, which consisted of the 44 teams that entered directly, in addition to the four winners of the preliminary round, and were seeded into four pots of twelve each based on the June 2024 [[FIFA Men's World Rankings|FIFA World Rankings]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Draw for TotalEnergies Africa Cup of Nations Morocco 2025 Qualifiers set for 04 July in Johannesburg |url=https://www.cafonline.com/caf-africa-cup-of-nations/news/draw-for-totalenergies-africa-cup-of-nations-morocco-2025-qualifiers-set-for-04-july-in-johannesburg/ |access-date=2024-06-13 |website=Draw for TotalEnergies Africa Cup of Nations Morocco 2025 Qualifiers set for 04 July in Johannesburg |language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=20 June 2024 |title=Men's Ranking |url=https://inside.fifa.com/fifa-world-ranking/men |access-date=20 June 2024 |publisher=FIFA}}</ref>

=== Qualified teams ===
The following teams qualified for this edition, all of them have previously participated in the tournament.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Last Day of TotalEnergies CAF Africa Cup of Nations Morocco 2025 Qualifiers provides drama and thrill |url=https://www.cafonline.com/caf-africa-cup-of-nations/news/last-day-of-totalenergies-caf-africa-cup-of-nations-morocco-2025-qualifiers-provides-drama-and-thrill/ |access-date=2024-11-21 |website=Last Day of TotalEnergies CAF Africa Cup of Nations Morocco 2025 Qualifiers provides drama and thrill |language=en-GB}}</ref> [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]], the host country, played in the qualifiers in [[2025 Africa Cup of Nations qualification Group B|Group B]] despite qualifying automatically. [[Ghana national football team|Ghana]], four-time African champions, failed to qualify after finishing bottom of the [[2025 Africa Cup of Nations qualification Group F|Group F]], missing out on the Africa Cup of Nations for the first time since [[2004 African Cup of Nations|2004]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-11-15 |title=Ghana fail to reach AFCON for 1st time since '04 |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/42412488/ghana-fail-qualify-afcon-africa-cup-nations |access-date=2024-11-16 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Millar |first=Colin |title=Ghana fail to qualify for AFCON 2025 after draw against Angola |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/5922531/2024/11/15/ghana-fail-afcon-qualification-sudan/ |access-date=2024-11-16 |work=The New York Times |language=en-US |issn=0362-4331}}</ref> [[Tunisia national football team|Tunisia]] qualified for the 22nd time and extended their record for consecutive participations, reaching the tournament for the 17th time in a row, having not been absent since [[1994 African Cup of Nations|1994]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Tunisia qualify for the 2025 AFCON in dramatic fashion |url=https://supersport.com/football/africa-cup-qual/news/75be85b8-e8e5-4404-8def-c40943a87378/tunisia-qualify-for-the-2025-afcon-in-dramatic-fashion |access-date=2024-11-16 team|Zimbabwe]] made their return to the continental tournament after missing out on [[2023 Africa Cup of Nations|2023]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Comoros claim top spot as Tunisia stumble in TotalEnergies CAF AFCON qualifiers |url=https://www.cafonline.com/caf-africa-cup-of-nations/news/comoros-claim-top-spot-as-tunisia-stumble-in-totalenergies-caf-afcon-qualifiers/ [[Botswana national football team|Botswana]] qualified for the second time after their first participation in [[2012 Africa Cup of Nations|2012]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Botswana return to AFCON after 12-year absence - Mauritania, Cape Verde miss out |url=https://www.cafonline.com/caf-africa-cup-of-nations/news/botswana-return-to-afcon-after-12-year-absence-mauritania-cape-verde-miss-out/ |access-date=2024-11-21 |website=Botswana return to AFCON after 12-year absence - Mauritania, Cape Verde miss out |language=en-GB}}</ref> [[Cape Verde national football team|Cape Verde]], [[Gambia national football team|Gambia]], [[Guinea national football team|Guinea]], [[Guinea-Bissau national football team|Guinea-Bissau]], [[Mauritania national football team|Mauritania]] and [[Namibia national football team|Namibia]] also failed to qualify after appearing in [[2023 Africa Cup of Nations|2023]].

==Group stage==
{{Hatnote|All times are local, [[Central European Time|CET]] ([[UTC+01:00|UTC+1]]).}}

CAF announced the tournament schedule on 31 January 2025, which did not include kick-off times for matches.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.cafonline.com/caf-africa-cup-of-nations/news/caf-announce-match-schedule-for-totalenergies-caf-africa-cup-of-nations-morocco-2025/ |title=CAF announce match schedule for TotalEnergies CAF Africa Cup of Nations, Morocco 2025 |website=cafonline.com |publisher=Confederation of African Football |date=31 January 2025 |access-date=1 February 2025 |archive-date=1 February 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250201094623/https://www.cafonline.com/caf-africa-cup-of-nations/news/caf-announce-match-schedule-for-totalenergies-caf-africa-cup-of-nations-morocco-2025/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cafonline.com/media/50ykqklv/match_schedule_totalenergies-caf-africa-cup-of-nations_morocco-25.pdf |title=2025 Africa Cup of Nations match schedule |website=cafonline.com |publisher=Confederation of African Football |date=31 January 2025 |access-date=1 February 2025 |archive-date=1 February 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250201094759/https://www.cafonline.com/media/50ykqklv/match_schedule_totalenergies-caf-africa-cup-of-nations_morocco-25.pdf |url-status=live }}</ref> The kick-off times for all matches are not yet announced.

The top two teams of each group, along with the best four third-placed teams, will advance to the round of 16.

===Tiebreakers===
Teams will be ranked according to the [[three points for a win]] system (3 points for a win, 1 for a draw, 0 points for a loss), and if tied on points, the following tiebreaking criteria are applied, in the order given, to determine the rankings (Regulations Article 74):<ref name="regulations">{{cite web|last=Nassar|first=Khaled|date=21 October 2021|title=Regulations of the Africa Cup of Nations|url=https://www.cafonline.com/media/bl2lhb3v/bm58fa2qjh76asriri5s.pdf|website=[[Confederation of African Football|CAFOnline.com]]|access-date=11 January 2022}}</ref>
# Points in head-to-head matches among tied teams;
# [[Goal difference]] in head-to-head matches among tied teams;
# Goals scored in head-to-head matches among tied teams;
# If more than two teams were tied, and after applying all head-to-head criteria above, if two teams are still tied, all head-to-head criteria above are applied exclusively to these two teams;
# Goal difference in all group matches;
# Goals scored in all group matches;
# Drawing of lots.
