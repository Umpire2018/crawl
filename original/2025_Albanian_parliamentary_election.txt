[[Elections in Albania|Parliamentary elections]] are scheduled to be held in [[Albania]] on 11 May 2025 to elect the 140 members of [[Parliament of Albania]].

==Background==
In the [[2021 Albanian parliamentary election|2021 parliamentary elections]] the [[Socialist Party of Albania|Socialist Party]] (PS) won 74 of the 140 seats in the parliament, resulting in [[Edi Rama]] being re-elected as Prime Minister. The PD-AN alliance, led by the centre-right [[Democratic Party of Albania|Democrats]], increased their seat-share from 43 to 59 seats. However 9 out of the 59 seats went to smaller parties that ran within the Democratic Party coalition.

During the same period, [[President of Albania|President]] [[Ilir Meta]] faced widespread allegations of violating the constitution, particularly in his intervention in the electoral process and the overt partisan stance against the PS he adopted during the 2021 elections.<ref>{{cite web |publisher=Deutsche Welle (DW) |title=Albania parliament impeaches President Meta == Electoral system ==

{{Politics of Albania}}

The electoral system of Albania is constructed upon the principles defined in the [[Constitution of Albania|constitution]] and the electoral code. As a [[parliamentary republic]], Albania implements a regional [[proportional representation]] method that allocates seats in the [[Parliament of Albania|parliament]] according to the proportion of votes garnered by political parties in a [[multi-party system]].{{sfn|OSCE|1998|p=1|loc=Article 1}}{{sfn|OSCE|1998|p=12|loc=Article 64}}{{sfn|OSCE|2021|p=6}} The parliament is composed of 140 representatives with a term of four years.{{sfn|OSCE|1998|p=12|loc=Article 64}} The constitution codifies substantial democratic principles, stipulating that voting rights are equal, free, and conducted through [[secret ballot]].{{sfn|OSCE|1998|p=9|loc=Article 45}}{{sfn|OSCE|1998|p=14|loc=Article 73}} All citizens of Albania, upon reaching the age of 18 and have not been deprived of voting rights, are permitted to participate in elections.{{sfn|OSCE|1998|p=8|loc=Article 45}} The electoral code also establishes comprehensive procedures for voter registration, ensuring that all eligible citizens can participate fully in the process. The allocation of representatives in the parliament is integrated on population size within 12 electoral districts, which correspond to the [[Counties of Albania|administrative regions]] of Albania.{{sfn|OSCE|2021|p=6}} To ensure proportional representation, the [[d'Hondt method]] is employed for seat distribution to parties, contingent upon an [[electoral threshold]] of 1%.{{sfn|OSCE|2021|p=6}} Nationally, each party is required to run 46 candidates in the closed lists and 140 candidates in the open lists. For example, if a party wins 15 seats in Tirana, the candidates elected in the Parliament would be the 12 candidates from the closed lists and the 3 most voted candidates from the open lists.

=== Amendments ===

On 26 July 2024, the parliament approved the amendments to the new electoral code, which included provisions for [[Albanian diaspora|diaspora]] [[Non-resident citizen voting|voting]] and the allocation of mandates.<ref name="Shqiptarja">{{cite web |work=Shqiptarja |title=106 vota pro, PS-PD miratojnë ndryshimet në Kodin Zgjedhor: Lista të hapura në 2/3 dhe votë të diasporës! 'Përplasen' demokratët, akuza për pazar Berisha-Rama |url=https://shqiptarja.com/lajm/kuvend-pakti-mes-pdps-sot-pritet-te-miratohet-kodi-i-ri-zgjedhor-ne-rend-dite-edhe-ligji-per-policine-e-shtetit-e-per-prodhimin-e-armeve |access-date=26 October 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240728050541/https://shqiptarja.com/lajm/kuvend-pakti-mes-pdps-sot-pritet-te-miratohet-kodi-i-ri-zgjedhor-ne-rend-dite-edhe-ligji-per-policine-e-shtetit-e-per-prodhimin-e-armeve |archive-date=28 July 2024 |language=sq |date=26 July 2024 |url-status=live}}</ref> A significant focus centered on the formula employed for candidate lists, which was no longer entirely open; instead, one-third was designated as closed and subject to the discretion of the party leader, thereby exempting it from preferential voting. Furthermore, the new regulations stipulated that one out of every three candidates elected from the open list was required to be a woman, thereby reflecting a commitment to enhancing gender representation within the electoral framework.<ref name="Shqiptarja"/>

=== Parliamentary parties ===
{{See also|List of political parties in Albania}}

As of the electoral code, a parliamentary party is defined as a political organisation that has achieved representation in the parliament by obtaining a minimum of one seat in the most recent electoral contest.{{sfn|Electoral Code|2024|p=2}} Subsequent to the [[2021 Albanian parliamentary election|2021 parliamentary election]], the PS obtained 74 of the 140 seats, emerging as the majority party.<ref name="CSIS"/><ref>{{cite web |publisher=Parliament of Albania |title=Mandatet Parlamentare 2021–2025 |url=https://kuvendiwebfiles.blob.core.windows.net/webfiles/mandatet.pdf |access-date=20 December 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240711072337/https://kuvendiwebfiles.blob.core.windows.net/webfiles/mandatet.pdf |archive-date=11 July 2024 |location=Tirana |language=sq |url-status=live}}</ref> The PD–AN secured 59 seats, while LSI acquired 4 seats and the PSD attained 3 seats.<ref name="CSIS"/>

== Parties and coalitions ==

According to the electoral code, each political party is mandated to submit a registration request to the [[Central Election Commission (Albania)|CEC]] no later than 70 days prior to the scheduled election date.{{sfn|Electoral Code|2024|p=34|loc=Article 64}} This request must comprehensively contain several critical components, including the identity of the party chair authorised to present candidates, the official name and acronym of the party, a specimen of the official stamp, and the contact information for both the finance officer and communication officer.{{sfn|Electoral Code|2024|p=34|loc=Article 64}} Registered political parties have the option to form coalitions, which must also be registered with the KQZ at least 60 days before the election.{{sfn|Electoral Code|2024|p=34|loc=Article 65}} A political party that is part of a coalition is restricted from engaging in another coalition or presenting candidates independently.{{sfn|Electoral Code|2024|p=34|loc=Article 65}} The coalition is required to designate a leading party, which assumes the responsibility for fulfilling the legal obligations formed in the electoral code, and to document inter-party relations in a formal coalition agreement submitted to the KQZ.{{sfn|Electoral Code|2024|p=34|loc=Article 65}} It is essential that both political parties and coalitions submit a multi-name list of candidates for the parliament election no later than 50 days prior to the election.{{sfn|Electoral Code|2024|p=35|loc=Article 67}}

== Campaign ==

Election campaigns in Albania are regulated to initiate 30 days before the scheduled election date and must conclude 24 hours prior to voting.{{sfn|Electoral Code|2024|p=39|loc=Article 77}} The day preceding and election day are designated as periods of [[Election silence|electoral silence]], during which all campaigning is prohibited.{{sfn|Electoral Code|2024|p=39|loc=Article 77}}

For the first time in history, the [[Albanian diaspora]] is able to vote through mail. As result, political parties have extended their campaign rallies to other countries, especially in [[Italy]] and [[Greece]] where there is a higher number of Albanian citizens residing. As of 28 February 2025, as 250,000 voters have registered to vote from overseas.

===TikTok ban===
On 21 December 2024, Albanian Prime Minister Edi Rama announced that TikTok would be banned in Albania for at least one year, starting from the beginning of 2025. Rama cited the death of a teenager in a brawl linked to a conflict on TikTok and concerns over social media promoting youth violence. Alongside this ban, the Albanian government plans to initiate educational programs for students and parents.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Albania to ban TikTok for a year, blaming it for promoting violence among children |url=https://www.abc.net.au/news/2024-12-22/albania-bans-tiktok-for-one-year/104755308?future=true&}}</ref>

On 13 March 2025, the Electronic and Postal Communications Authority (AKEP) and the National Cybersecurity Authority (AKSK), per the decisions released by the Albanian government, instructed all internet service providers in Albania to block TikTok's IPs, DNS resolution, and Server Name Indications, as well as ByteDance's IP addresses.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Zbulohet data, ja kur do të mbyllet rrjeti social ‘TikTok’ në Shqipëri! |url=https://www.panorama.com.al/qeveria-njoftoi-mbylljen-e-tiktok-zbulohet-data-ja-kur-nis-bllokimi-i-aksesit-ne-vendin-tone-per-platformen-e-rrjeteve-sociale/}}</ref>

Opposition leader Sali Berisha criticized the ban, arguing it would hinder their ability to run online campaigns ahead of the elections. Hundreds of Democrat protesters gathered in front of the Kryeministria building on 15 March 2025 to protest the ban.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Albanian opposition protests TikTok ban alleging censorship ahead of election |url=https://apnews.com/article/albania-opposition-tiktok-ban-protest-election-db108a58f84307596e257e41567248e7#:~:text=Last%20week%2C%20the%20Albanian%20Cabinet,and%20bullying%2C%20especially%20among%20children.}}</ref> Arlind Qori, leader of Together Movement, considered the ban as an act of censorship.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Rama mbyll Tiktok, SHPËRTHEN Arlind Qori: AKT CENSURE, dy muaj para ZGJEDHJEVE! |url=https://top-channel.tv/video/rama-mbyll-tiktok-shperthen-arlind-qori-akt-censure-dy-muaj-para-zgjedhjeve-breaking/}}</ref>

=== Other parties ===

In August 2024, Basha of the Democratic Party–Euroatlantic Democrats presented an anti-corruption strategy for the elections, including the formation of a dedicated unit to trace and recover misappropriated public funds, alongside reinforcing the roles of the [[Special Structure against Corruption and Organized Crime]] (SPAK) and the Special First Instance Court for Corruption and Organized Crime (GJKKO).<ref name="LBBS">{{cite web |work=Bota Sot |title=Basha publikon programin për zgjedhjet 2025, premton 'luftë të egër' ndaj korrupsionit |url=https://www.botasot.info/shqiperia/2219680/basha-publikon-programin-per-zgjedhjet-2025-premton-lufte-te-eger-ndaj-korrupsionit/ |access-date=15 November 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240828025404/https://www.botasot.info/shqiperia/2219680/basha-publikon-programin-per-zgjedhjet-2025-premton-lufte-te-eger-ndaj-korrupsionit/ |archive-date=28 August 2024 |language=sq |date=23 August 2024 |url-status=live}}</ref> Basha further advocates for an extension of an anti-mafia law to encompass corrupt public-private partnerships, the confiscation of illicit assets from public officials, and the removal of parliamentary immunity to facilitate comprehensive investigations.<ref name="LBBS"/> Additionally, he proposes a vetting process aimed at cleansing both central and local government institutions.<ref name="LBBS"/>

In September 2024, the Democratic Party appointed [[Flamur Noka]] to lead the party's the coordination of campaign activities for the elections.<ref>{{cite web |last=Barjamemaj |first=Anesti |publisher=[[A2 CNN]] |title=Zgjedhjet 2025, zbulohet kush do drejtojë fushatën e Pd |url=https://a2news.com/shqiperia/politike/zgjedhjet-2025-zbulohet-kush-do-drejtoje-fushaten-e-pd-i1127497 |access-date=15 November 2024 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20241115115656/https://a2news.com/shqiperia/politike/zgjedhjet-2025-zbulohet-kush-do-drejtoje-fushaten-e-pd-i1127497 |archive-date=15 November 2024 |language=sq |date=4 September 2024 |url-status=live}}</ref> Also in September, Adriatik Lapaj of Lëvizja Shqipëria Bëhet stated that over the past year, they have been actively engaging citizens through meetings in Tirana, regional areas, and the diaspora, underscoring the role of the electorate in challenging the existing government.<ref name="ALBW">{{cite web |work=BalkanWeb |title=Zgjedhjet e 2025/ Lapaj: Do të kemi rezultat surprizë. Fitorja më e madhe e shqiptarëve do të ishte |url=https://www.balkanweb.com/zgjedhjet-e-2025-lapaj-do-te-kemi-rezultat-surprize-fitorja-me-e-madhe-e-shqiptareve-do-te-ishte/#gsc.tab=0 |access-date=14 November 2024 |archive-url= |archive-date= |language=sq |date=10 September 2024 }}</ref> Lapaj criticized both the Socialist Party and Democratic Party, claiming that their leaders exploit criminal networks to manipulate elections and maintain power.<ref name="ALBW"/> Lapaj's vision seeks to dismantle this system, promising an alternative model based on honesty and accountability, with the ultimate goal of empowering the Albanian people to enact change.<ref name="ALBW"/>
