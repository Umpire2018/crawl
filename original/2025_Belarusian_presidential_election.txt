Presidential elections were held in [[Belarus]] on 26 January 2025. The [[President of Belarus|president]] is directly elected to serve a five-year term.

Incumbent president [[Alexander Lukashenko]] has won every [[Elections in Belarus|presidential election]] since 1994, with all but the first being deemed by international monitors as neither [[Free and fair election|free nor fair]].<ref name=":1"/> Prior to the elections, independent media was closed down or forced to operate from abroad.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |last=Allnutt |first=Luke |date=2025-01-24 |title=Could Belarus's Presidential Election Be Lukashenka's Last? |url=https://www.rferl.org/a/belarus-election-lukashenka-tsikhanouskaya/33285221.html |publisher=[[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]] }}</ref> Aside from Lukashenko, there were four candidates on the ballot. Three of them represented pro-government parties. The fourth candidate, [[Hanna Kanapatskaya]], was the only formally independent candidate, but opposition figures accused her of being a [[Dummy candidate|stooge]], which her supporters have denied.<ref name=":0" />

Following [[exit poll]] results indicating Lukashenko's decisive victory, the election was described as a [[Sham election|sham]] by critics.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Svirnovskiy |first=Gregory |date=2025-01-26 |title=Lukashenko wins 'sham' election in Belarus with 87.6 percent of vote |url=https://www.politico.eu/article/alexander-lukashenko-wins-sham-election-in-belarus/ |access-date=2025-01-26 |website=POLITICO}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Higgins |first=Andrew |date=2025-01-26 |title=Belarus's Strong-Arm Leader, Aleksandr Lukashenko, Cruises to Re-election |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2025/01/26/world/europe/belarus-lukashenko-re-election.html |access-date=2025-01-26 |work=The New York Times |language=en-US |issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2025-01-26 |title=Belarus strongman set to win a 7th term in an election the opposition calls a farce |url=https://apnews.com/article/belarus-lukashenko-election-crackdown-opposition-putin-ae937352a1340ad5ca02ab55744487e0 |access-date=2025-01-26 |website=AP News |language=en}}</ref> Lukashenko faced no serious challenge on the ballot from the other candidates and received over 87% of the vote.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Trevelyan |first1=Mark |title=Putin ally Lukashenko declared winner of Belarus vote that West calls a charade |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/lukashenko-pockets-massive-win-belarus-election-scorned-by-west-2025-01-27/ |work=Reuters |date=27 January 2025}}</ref>

==Background==
[[2020 Belarusian protests|Mass protests]] alleging widespread vote-rigging erupted after incumbent president [[Alexander Lukashenko]] was declared the winner of the [[2020 Belarusian presidential election|2020 presidential election]]. He had won every [[Elections in Belarus|presidential election]] since 1994, with all but the first being deemed by international monitors as neither [[Free and fair election|free nor fair]].<ref name=":1">{{cite news |last1=Vasilyeva |first1=Nataliya |title=His Opponents Sidelined, Lukashenko Is Set for a 7th Term in Belarus |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2025/01/25/world/europe/belarus-election-lukashenko.html |work=The New York Times |date=25 January 2025}}</ref> Opposition figure [[Sviatlana Tsikhanouskaya]], who claimed to have won the election, went into exile.<ref>{{cite news |title=Belarus' Lukashenko to Run for Seventh Presidential Term in 2025 |url=https://www.voanews.com/a/belarus-lukashenko-to-run-for-seventh-presidential-term-in-2025/7502067.html |publisher=[[Voice of America]] |date=25 February 2024 }}</ref><ref name="BBC_BY_exiled_leader_peaceful_rallies" /> Tsikhanouskaya formed government-in-exile institutions consisting of the [[Coordination Council (Belarus)|Coordination Council]], created in August 2020,<ref name="BBC_BY_exiled_leader_peaceful_rallies" /> and the [[United Transitional Cabinet of Belarus|United Transitional Cabinet]], created in August 2022.<ref name="RFERL_second_anniversary" /><ref name="DW_BY_opposition_leader_says" />

On 17 August 2020, Lukashenko stated that the next presidential election could be held earlier than 2025 if a new constitution were to be adopted.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rferl.org/a/calls-for-strikes-in-belarus-keep-pressure-on-lukashenka-after-huge-minsk-rally/30787556.html|title=Heckled And Jeered, Lukashenka Says New Election Could Be Held Tsikhanouskaya stated that she was ready to lead a transitional government and to hold early elections under international supervision.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.aa.com.tr/en/europe/belarus-opposition-leader-says-ready-to-lead-country/1944479 |title=Belarus: Opposition leader says 'ready to lead country' |last=Teslova |first=Elena |date=17 On 23 October 2024, the [[Central Election Commission of Belarus]] (CEC) announced that the election would be held on 26 January 2025,<ref>{{cite web |title=Belarus to Hold Next Presidential Election on Jan. 26 |website=[[The Moscow Times]] |url=https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2024/10/23/belarus-to-hold-next-presidential-election-on-jan-26-a86783 |access-date=23 October 2024 |date=23 October 2024}}</ref> with a potential second round to be held on 12 February.<ref name="round2">{{cite news |title=Belarus presidential election: Who's taking on Lukashenko, does it matter? |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/1/26/belarus-presidential-election-whos-taking-on-lukashenko-does-it-matter |access-date=26 January 2025 |publisher=[[Al Jazeera]] |date=26 January 2025}}</ref>

On 6 November 2024, the [[Viasna Human Rights Centre]] reported that Belarusian authorities had started a wave of arrests with over a hundred people detained in a week, many linked to online chats, at a time when Belarusian prisons were already overcrowded.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://kyivindependent.com/belarus-cracks-down-ahead-of-january-election-over-100-people-detained/ |title=Belarus cracks down ahead of January election, over 100 people detained |website=[[The Kyiv Independent]] |date=2024-11-07 |access-date=2024-11-07}}</ref>

===Non-recognition===
On 23 October 2024, the same day that the CEC announced the election, Tsikhanouskaya, the United Transitional Cabinet, and the Coordination Council stated that they would consider the election to be invalid unless all [[human rights in Belarus#Political dissidents and prisoners|political prisoners]] were released, participation by all Belarusians regardless of residence was implemented, and other conditions such as [[human rights in Belarus#Freedom of association|freedom of assembly]] and equal access to the media were fulfilled.<ref name="Tsikhanouskaya_joint_statement">{{cite Q|Q131857853|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="s468">{{cite web |last=Jochecová |first=Ketrin |date=2024-10-23 |title=Belarus announces date for Lukashenko's next election win |url=https://www.politico.eu/article/belarus-announces-date-for-lukashenkos-re-election/ |access-date=2025-01-24 |newspaper=[[Politico]]}}</ref><ref name="q655">{{cite web |website=EU Today |date=2024-10-23 |title=Belarus Sets Presidential Election Date for January 2025 |url=https://eutoday.net/belarus-sets-presidential-election-date-for-january-2025/ |access-date=2025-01-24}}</ref> Tsikhanouskaya later called the vote a "farce" and called for a boycott,<ref>{{cite news |title=Belarus election is poised to extend the 30-year rule of 'Europe's last dictator' |url=https://apnews.com/article/belarus-election-lukashenko-crackdown-e9fdb16a721022364cf5108cf5e94762 |access-date=24 January 2025 |publisher=[[Associated Press]] |date=24 January 2025}}</ref> but advised against protests "until the real moment of possibility".<ref name="round2"/>

In November, Estonian foreign minister [[Margus Tsahkna]] stated that the election would not be recognised by Estonia, saying it would not meet democratic standards.<ref name="EER_EE_not_recognize_charade">{{cite Q|Q131862682|url-status=live}}</ref> On 9 January 2025, [[Małgorzata Kidawa-Błońska]], [[Marshal of the Senate of Poland|Marshal]] of the [[Senate of Poland|Polish Senate]], stated that the chamber would not recognise the election.<ref name="PolskieRadio_PL_not_recognize_upper_house">{{cite Q|Q131862725|url-status=live}}</ref> On 22 January, the [[European Parliament]] called for the [[European Union]] (EU) and EU member states to refuse to recognise Lukashenko as president after the vote.<ref name="EuroParl_EP_denounces_upcoming">{{cite Q|Q131857947|url-status=live}}</ref>

Outgoing [[US Secretary of State]] [[Antony Blinken]] said the election cannot be free or fair in an "environment where censorship is ubiquitous and independent media outlets no longer exist".<ref name="round2"/> [[Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe]] rapporteur [[Ryszard Petru]] said on 24 January that the election was lacking in debate, free choice and transparency, which meant that it "cannot and will not meet internationally recognised standards of fairness and legitimacy".<ref>{{cite news |title=Belarus' Lukashenko Is a Shoo-In for His Seventh Election as President |url=https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2025/01/24/belarus-lukashenko-is-a-shoo-in-for-his-seventh-election-as-president-a87722 |access-date=24 January 2025 |work=The Moscow Times |date=24 January 2025}}</ref> The [[European Commission]] also called the vote "a total sham".<ref name="euronews"/> On 25 January, EU foreign policy chief [[Kaja Kallas]] also called the election a "sham".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20250126-lukashenko-set-to-extend-30-year-rule-in-belarus |title=Polls open in Belarus with Lukashenko set to extend 30-year rule |date=26 January 2025 |access-date=26 January 2025 |publisher=[[France 24]]}}</ref> A joint EU statement rejecting the legitimacy of the election was blocked by [[Hungary]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Hungary Blocks Joint EU Statement On Presidential Elections In Belarus – Sources |url=https://www.rferl.org/a/belarus-hungary-kallas-european-union-presidential-election/33289953.html |access-date=2025-01-27 |work=RadioFreeEurope/RadioLiberty |language=en}}</ref>

==Campaign==
Syrankov supported the rebuilding of monuments to [[Joseph Stalin]] and the criminalisation of [[LGBTQ rights in Belarus|LGBT activities]] in Belarus.<ref>{{Cite web |date=27 January 2025 |title=Belarus strongman wins a 7th term in an election the opposition calls a farce |url=https://apnews.com/article/belarus-lukashenko-election-crackdown-opposition-putin-ae937352a1340ad5ca02ab55744487e0 |access-date=27 January 2025 |publisher=AP News }}</ref> His campaign slogan was "Not instead of, but together with Lukashenko!". In an interview with the [[BBC]], Syrankov said "there is no alternative to Alexander Lukashenko as the leader of our country", adding that "we are taking part in the election with the president's team". He also called Lukashenko the "main Communist" in Belarus. Likewise, Gaidukevich said "it's obvious that Lukashenko will win"<ref>{{Cite web |date=25 January 2025 |title=Belarus election: 'There is no alternative to Lukashenko' |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cp9x0mjzl4mo |access-date=25 January 2025 |website=BBC News}}</ref> and urged other candidates to "make Lukashenko's enemies nauseous". [[Alexander Khizhnyak]] pledged to prevent a "repeat of disturbances" in the 2020 presidential election, while [[Hanna Kanapatskaya]] presented herself as the "only democratic alternative to Lukashenko" and pledged for the release of political prisoners while warning supporters against "excessive initiative".<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 January 2025 |title=Voting under way in Belarus with Lukashenko set to extend 30-year rule |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/1/26/voting-under-way-in-belarus-with-lukashenko-set-to-extend-30-year-rule |access-date=26 January 2025 |publisher=Al Jazeera }}</ref> She added "I do not fight with police officers because it is a crime. But that doesn't mean I support the policies of the current leadership of the country."<ref name="euronews">{{Cite web |date=26 January 2025 |title=Polls open in Belarus presidential election with chance of change at the top slim |url=https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2025/01/26/polls-open-in-belarus-presidential-election-with-chance-of-change-at-the-top-slim |access-date=26 January 2025 |publisher=[[Euronews]] }}</ref>

== Results ==
According to an exit poll shown on state television, Lukashenko won with 88% of the vote, with 82% turnout. EU foreign policy chief [[Kaja Kallas]] called the election a "blatant affront to democracy". Exiled Belarus opposition leader [[Sviatlana Tsikhanouskaya]] called the election "a senseless farce, a Lukashenko ritual".<ref>{{Cite web |last= |date=January 26, 2025 |title=Lukashenko on track to win with 88% in Belarus election scorned by West as a sham |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/west-cries-foul-over-belarus-election-lukashenko-eyes-seventh-term-2025-01-26/ |archive-url= |archive-date= |access-date=January 26, 2025 |website=Reuters}}</ref>

==Conduct==
The CEC did not conduct overseas voting for the election, citing a decrease in the number of diplomatic staff posted abroad.<ref>{{cite news |title=Belarus will not open overseas polling stations in this yearʼs presidential election |url=https://babel.ua/en/news/114379-belarus-will-not-open-overseas-polling-stations-in-this-year-s-presidential-election |access-date=26 January 2025 |publisher=Babel |date=10 January 2025}}</ref> The decision disenfranchised many exiled critics and others who had left the country since 2020.<ref>{{cite news |title=Polls Open in Belarus With Lukashenko Set to Extend 30-Year Rule |url=https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2025/01/26/polls-open-in-belarus-with-lukashenko-set-to-extend-30-year-rule-a87742 |access-date=26 January 2025 |publisher=The Moscow Times |date=26 January 2025}}</ref> Early voting began on 21 January,<ref>{{cite news |title=Early voting begins in Belarusian presidential election |url=https://kyivindependent.com/early-voting-in-presidential-election-begins-in-belarus/ |access-date=22 January 2025 |work=The Kyiv Independent |date=21 January 2025}}</ref> with turnout reaching 41.81% by 26 January. Opposition groups said that ballot boxes used in early voting were left unguarded until election day, allowing irregularities to take place.<ref>{{cite news |title=Belarus strongman set to win a 7th term in an election the opposition calls a farce|url=https://apnews.com/article/belarus-lukashenko-election-crackdown-opposition-putin-ae937352a1340ad5ca02ab55744487e0 |access-date=26 January 2025 |publisher=Associated Press |date=26 January 2025}}</ref> The Belarusian government invited the [[Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe]] to monitor the vote, but the latter was unable to send observers as the invitation was issued only ten days before the election.<ref>{{cite news |title=EU rejects election in Belarus and threatens new sanctions |url=https://apnews.com/article/eu-rejects-belarus-election-7398527b65def27b6927b0286ebeef83 |access-date=26 January 2025 |publisher=Associated Press |date=26 January 2025}}</ref> On March 25, 2025, Alexander Lukashenko was sworn in for a seventh term as President of Belarus after winning 85.7% of the vote in the election.<ref>{{cite news |title=His Opponents Sidelined, Lukashenko Is Set for a 7th Term in Belarus |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2025/01/25/world/europe/belarus-election-lukashenko.html |access-date=26 January 2025 |work=The New York Times |date=25 March 2025}}</ref>
