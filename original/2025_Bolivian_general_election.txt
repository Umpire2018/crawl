General elections are scheduled to take place in Bolivia on 17 August 2025. Voters will elect the [[President of Bolivia]] and [[Vice President of Bolivia]], 130 members of the [[Chamber of Deputies (Bolivia)|Chamber of Deputies]], and 36 members of the [[Chamber of Senators (Bolivia)|Chamber of Senators]].

==Background==
The election is being held amid a schism within the ruling [[Movimiento al Socialismo]] (MAS) between incumbent president [[Luis Arce]] and one of his predecessors, [[Evo Morales]]. The former allies fell out following Arce's [[2020 Bolivian general election|election in 2020]], when Morales, who had been in exile following [[2019 Bolivian political crisis|political unrest]] caused by his [[2019 Bolivian general election|reelection bid in 2019]], returned from Mexico and tried to regain control of the party's leadership.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Meakem |first=Allison |date=2 January 2025 |title=Elections to Watch in 2025 |url=https://foreignpolicy.com/2025/01/02/top-world-elections-2025-canada-germany-ecuador-australia-poland-bolivia/ |access-date=7 April 2025 |website=Foreign Policy |language=en}}</ref>

Right-leaning political parties formed a Unity Bloc to oppose Arce. Its candidates included [[Samuel Doria Medina]] and [[Jorge Quiroga|Jorge "Tuto" Quiroga]]. Meanwhile, Morales announced his intention to run under the [[Front for Victory (Bolivia)|Front for Victory]] (FPV) despite a ban on him running again for president by the [[Plurinational Constitutional Tribunal]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Bueno |first=Boris |date=2025-03-15 |title=Doria Medina y Tuto Quiroga aceleran la conformación de alianzas para sostener su probable candidatura por el bloque de unidad |url=https://eju.tv/2025/03/doria-medina-y-tuto-quiroga-aceleran-la-conformacion-de-alianzas-para-sostener-su-probable-candidatura-por-el-bloque-de-unidad/ |access-date=2025-03-18 |website=EJU |language=es}}</ref>
