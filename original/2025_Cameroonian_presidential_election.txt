Presidential elections are due to be held in [[Cameroon]] on 5 October 2025.<ref>{{Cite web|title=EISA African Election Calendar 2025|url=https://www.eisa.org/election-calendar/|access-date=2023-03-31|website=EISA}}</ref>

==Background==
The [[2018 Cameroonian presidential election|previous presidential elections]] on 7 October 2018 saw incumbent president [[Paul Biya]] elected for another seven-year term following a 2008 constitutional amendment that removed term limits, allowing Biya to run again.<ref>[http://www.france24.com/en/20080411-cameroon-parliament-paul-biya-term-limit-extension Cameroon parliament extends Biya's term limit] France 24, 11 April 2008</ref>

==Electoral system==
The [[President of Cameroon]] is elected by [[first-past-the-post voting]]; the candidate with the most votes is declared the winner with no requirement to achieve a majority.<ref>[http://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/2205/ Cameroon] IFES</ref> Registration for voting ended on 31 August 2024, with more than seven million people estimated to have signed up.<ref name="29dec">{{Cite news |date=31 August 2024 |title=Voter registration ends in Cameroon as polls near |language=en |work=Africanews |url=https://www.africanews.com/2024/08/31/voter-registration-ends-in-cameroon-as-polls-near/ |access-date=1 September 2024}}</ref>

==Candidates==
=== Declared ===
* '''[[Paul Biya]]''', aged 92, incumbent president and candidate for the [[Cameroon People's Democratic Movement]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mimmy |date=2024-08-24 |title=Cameroon's President declares candidacy for eighth term in 2025 election |url=https://mbaretimes.com/2024/08/cameroons-president/ |access-date=2024-08-25 |website=Mbare Times |language=en-US}}</ref>
* '''[[Maurice Kamto]]''', aged 71, is a retired academic and lawyer, representing the [[Cameroon Renaissance Movement]] (MRC). Kamto, who previously ran in the [[2018 Cameroonian presidential election|2018 presidential election]], is also supported by the Political Alliance for Reform (APC) and the Front for Change in Cameroon (FCC).<ref>{{Cite web |last=Tagne |date=2024-05-17 |title=Maurice Kamto declares candidacy for the 2025 presidential election |url=https://www.actucameroun.com/2023/12/11/maurice-kamto-alliance-politique-pour-le-changement/ |access-date=2024-08-31 |website=Actu Cameroun |language=fr}}</ref>
* '''[[Akere Muna]]''', aged 72, is an international Cameroonian lawyer and candidate for the Univers party.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://theguardianpostcameroon.com/post/4406/en/in-first-official-outing-akere-muna-vows-2025-presidential-election-wont-like-others-/ |website=The Guardian Post |access-date=14 January 2025 |title=DRC: In first official outing: Akere Muna vows 2025 presidential electionwon't be like others! }}</ref> Akere is globally renowned especially in the areas of anti-corruption and good governance and previously a candidate for Now!.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theafricareport.com/26470/drc-felix-tshisekedi-asks-cameroonian-akere-muna-help-to-fight-corruption/ |website=The Africa Report |access-date=4 June 2023 |title=DRC: President Tshisekedi and Cameroonian Akéré Muna working to fight corruption }}</ref>
* '''Eric Essono Tsimi''', aged 43, is an academic based in New York and a writer. He announced his "civil and civic" candidacy in May 2024 during an interview with journalist Jean-Bruno Tagne on [[Naja TV]]. His campaign is being carried out under the platform ''Nous Sommes Le Changement / We Are the Change''. Tsimi has also been engaged in negotiations with various political parties, including the FDC and the PAL.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Tagne |first=Jean-Bruno |date=2024-05-24 |title=Eric Essono Tsimi announces candidacy for 2025 |url=https://naja.tv/eric-essono-tsimi/ |access-date=2024-08-31 |website=Naja TV |language=fr}}</ref>
