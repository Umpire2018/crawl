{{Politics of Ecuador}}
General elections were held in [[Ecuador]] on 9 February 2025.<ref name="date">{{cite web |date=9 February 2024 |title=Las elecciones presidenciales serán el 9 de febrero de 2025 |url=https://www.primicias.ec/noticias/politica/elecciones-presidenciales-presidente-fecha-cne-calendario2025/ |accessdate=30 July 2024 |publisher=Primicias |archive-date=17 December 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241217185234/https://www.primicias.ec/noticias/politica/elecciones-presidenciales-presidente-fecha-cne-calendario2025/ |url-status=live }}</ref> As no presidential candidate secured an outright majority, a run-off was conducted on 13 April 2025. Incumbent President [[Daniel Noboa]] was re-elected for a full term, defeating [[Luisa González]] of the [[Citizen Revolution Movement]] in a rematch.<ref name="noboa" /><ref name="hope">{{Cite web |title=Daniel Noboa: Hope for Ecuador? |url=https://www.dw.com/en/daniel-noboa-hope-for-ecuador/a-67127822 |access-date=15 February 2025 |website=dw.com |language=en |archive-date=7 April 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240407011713/https://www.dw.com/en/daniel-noboa-hope-for-ecuador/a-67127822 |url-status=live }}</ref> The elections also included selecting members of the [[National Assembly (Ecuador)|National Assembly]], the 21 [[Provinces of Ecuador|provincial]] assemblies, and Ecuador's representatives to the [[Andean Parliament]] to serve full four-year terms.

In the first round, Noboa had received 44.17% of the vote, slightly ahead of González, who received 44.0%.<ref name="advance1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.wric.com/news/u-s-world/ap-ecuadors-conservative-incumbent-and-a-leftist-lawyer-advance-to-presidential-runoff/|title=Ecuador's conservative incumbent and a leftist lawyer advance to presidential runoff &#124; WRIC ABC 8News|access-date=10 February 2025|archive-date=4 March 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250304093504/https://www.wric.com/news/u-s-world/ap-ecuadors-conservative-incumbent-and-a-leftist-lawyer-advance-to-presidential-runoff/|url-status=live}}</ref> The coalition formed by the Citizen Revolution Movement and the [[RETO Movement]], led by former President [[Rafael Correa]], secured a narrow plurality in the [[National Assembly (Ecuador)|National Assembly]] with 67 seats, followed closely by Noboa's [[National Democratic Action (Ecuador)|National Democratic Action]] party with 66 seats. Voter turnout for the first round was approximately 82%.<ref name="CNEpresturnout" />

On 12 April, a state of emergency was declared leading up to the second round.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web |date=2025-04-12 |title=Ecuador imposes state of emergency before razor-close election |url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/ecuador-imposes-state-of-emergency-election/ |access-date=2025-04-17 |website=CBS News |language=en-US}}</ref> In the second round, Noboa received 55.6% of the vote, defeating González by 11.2 percentage points. The result exceeded expectations, with Noboa's campaign notable for its focus on young voters.<ref name=":4"/><ref name=":5"/> González demanded a recount,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Rios |first=Michael |date=2025-04-13 |title=Ecuador's President Daniel Noboa declared election winner as rival demands recount |url=https://www.cnn.com/2025/04/13/americas/ecuador-vote-noboa-gonzalez-latam-intl/index.html |access-date=2025-04-17 |website=CNN |language=en |archive-date=14 April 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250414054402/https://www.cnn.com/2025/04/13/americas/ecuador-vote-noboa-gonzalez-latam-intl/index.html |url-status=live }}</ref> claiming the results had discrepancies with pre-election polls<ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Press |first=Associated |date=2025-04-14 |title=Ecuador Re-Elects President Daniel Noboa |url=https://time.com/7277460/ecuador-presidential-election-results-daniel-noboa-crime-fraud/ |access-date=2025-04-17 |magazine=TIME |language=en}}</ref> and exit polls<ref>{{Cite web |title=Cover-Up in Ecuador? Disputed Presidential Election Rocked by New Allegation from 2023 Assassination |url=https://www.democracynow.org/2025/4/15/ecuador_daniel_noboa |access-date=2025-04-17 |website=Democracy Now! |language=en}}</ref> and were fraudulent.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2025-04-16 |title=Ecuador re-elects Daniel Noboa. This is the balance of power in Latin America |url=https://aldianews.com/en/politics/elected-officials/ecuador-re-elects-noboa |access-date=2025-04-17 |website=Al Día News |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Ecuador: Conservative President Daniel Noboa reelected |url=https://www.dw.com/en/ecuador-conservative-president-daniel-noboa-reelected/a-72231689 |access-date=2025-04-17 |website=DW |language=en}}</ref> Several media outlets said that González's claims of fraud lacked evidence.<ref name=":4" /><ref>{{Cite news |title=Ecuador's President Daniel Noboa wins reelection |url=https://www.npr.org/2025/04/13/nx-s1-5363690/ecuadors-president-daniel-noboa-wins-re-election |access-date=2025-04-17 |work=NPR |language=en}}</ref><ref name=":5" /><ref>{{Cite web |date=2025-04-13 |title=Noboa wins Ecuador presidential runoff, rival claims fraud |url=https://www.rfi.fr/en/international-news/20250413-polls-close-in-ecuador-s-razor-tight-presidential-runoff |access-date=2025-04-17 |website=RFI |language=en |quote=She did not immediately provide evidence for her claims of fraud.}}</ref> International observers, including the [[European Union]] and the [[Organization of American States|Organisation of American States]], determined the elections were free and fair, rejecting claims of fraud.<ref name=":8"/><ref>{{Cite web |date=15 April 2025 |title=EU observers "categorically" reject Ecuador election fraud allegations |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/eu-observers-categorically-reject-ecuador-election-fraud-allegations-2025-04-15/ |website=[[Reuters]]}}</ref>  

== Background ==
The 2025 election was held after an early election in 2023 after former President [[Guillermo Lasso]] invoked the '[[muerte cruzada]]', just days before a congressional impeachment vote. That constitutional mechanism dissolved the National Assembly and triggered a [[snap election]]. In that election, Daniel Noboa was elected to complete Lasso's unfinished term, which runs until May 2025.<ref name="referendum">{{Cite news |title=A referendum on crime? What to know about Ecuador's presidential election |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/2/6/a-referendum-on-crime-what-to-know-about-ecuadors-presidential-election |access-date=5 March 2025 |work=Al Jazeera |language=en |archive-date=5 March 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250305011613/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/2/6/a-referendum-on-crime-what-to-know-about-ecuadors-presidential-election |url-status=live }}</ref>

The 2025 election saw Ecuador returning to its regular electoral schedule to determine the president and vice president for a full four-year term, alongside members of the National Assembly.<ref name="referendum"/>

==Electoral system==
The president is elected using a modified [[two-round system]], with a candidate required to get over 50% of the vote, or get over 40% of the vote and be 10 points ahead of their nearest rival, to be elected in the first round.<ref name="guide"/> The president is limited to two consecutive four-year terms.<ref name="guide">{{Cite web|url=https://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/2265/|title=Elections: Ecuador Pres 2013|website=IFES Election Guide|access-date=23 June 2023|archive-date=31 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190331044326/http://www.electionguide.org/elections/id/2265/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="PDBA">{{Cite web|url=https://pdba.georgetown.edu/ElecSys/Ecuador/ecuador.html|title=Ecuador: Sistemas Electorales / Electoral Systems|website=pdba.georgetown.edu|access-date=23 June 2023|archive-date=19 April 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230419091911/https://pdba.georgetown.edu/ElecSys/Ecuador/ecuador.html|url-status=live}}</ref> However, Noboa is serving the remaining term of [[Guillermo Lasso]], whose invoking of ''[[muerte cruzada]]'' called for the [[2023 Ecuadorian general election|2023 snap general elections]] and his early conclusion of his presidency.<ref name="BBC Mundo">{{cite news |title=Qué es la 'muerte cruzada' invocada por el presidente Lasso en Ecuador y qué pasa ahora |url=https://www.bbc.com/mundo/noticias-america-latina-65624119 |access-date=17 May 2023 |publisher=BBC Mundo |date=17 May 2023 |archive-date=22 May 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230522180644/https://www.bbc.com/mundo/noticias-america-latina-65624119 |url-status=live}}</ref>

Members of the [[National Assembly (Ecuador)|National Assembly]] are elected by three methods.<ref name="PDBA"/> Fifteen are elected by [[closed list]] [[proportional representation]] in a nationwide constituency. Six are elected by overseas voters (two each from Canada/United States, Latin America and Asia/Europe/Oceania). The remaining 116 members are elected from multi-member constituencies by closed list proportional representation, with all seats allocated using the [[Webster/Sainte-Laguë method|Webster method]].<ref name="57lkF">{{Cite web|title=La Asamblea aprobó reformas al Código de la Democracia|url=http://www.elcomercio.com/actualidad/reformas-elecciones-asamblea-codigo-democracia.html|access-date=15 January 2021|website=El Comercio|date=22 January 2020 | archive-date = 28 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210128084532/https://www.elcomercio.com/actualidad/reformas-elecciones-asamblea-codigo-democracia.html|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="IJBs7">{{Cite web|title=Electores no podrán votar por candidatos de distintas listas|url=http://www.elcomercio.com/actualidad/cne-electores-vota-candidatos-listas.html|access-date=15 January 2021|website=El Comercio|date=11 December 2019 |archive-date=9 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210109054952/https://www.elcomercio.com/actualidad/cne-electores-vota-candidatos-listas.html|url-status=live}}</ref> Members of the National Assembly are limited to two four-year terms, regardless if they are consecutive or not.<ref name="IJBs7"/> There are gender quotas for the party lists, meaning there is [[Zipper system|alternation between men and women]]. There are no quotas for minority representation.<ref name="PDBA"/>

==Results==
Preliminary results in the first round showed no candidate in the presidential election had won an outright majority.<ref>{{Cite web |date=9 October 2023 |title=Event Data now with added references |url=https://doi.org/10.5555/tjrfyv6htr |access-date=18 February 2025 |website=doi.org}}</ref> Daniel Noboa received 44.17% of the vote, followed by Luisa González at 43.97%. Turnout was estimated at around 82%.<ref name=CNEpresturnout>{{Cite web |title=CONSEJO NACIONAL ELECTORAL – Elecciones 2025 |url=https://elecciones2025.cne.gob.ec/ |access-date=10 February 2025 |website=elecciones2025.cne.gob.ec}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://apnews.com/article/ecuador-presidential-election-gonzalez-noboa-correa-crime-650a730693168df58914266fe3faa82c |title=Ecuador's conservative incumbent and a leftist lawyer advance to presidential runoff |date=10 February 2025 |publisher=AP News |accessdate=10 February 2025 |archive-date=22 February 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250222110518/https://apnews.com/article/ecuador-presidential-election-gonzalez-noboa-correa-crime-650a730693168df58914266fe3faa82c |url-status=live }}</ref> On 24 February 2025, the Plenary of the CNE approved the numerical results of the 2025 general elections. After approval, three days were given for political organizations to file appeals with the CNE or the TCE if they considered it necessary.<ref>{{Cite AV media |url=https://www.youtube.com/live/IDSQH2_66LA?si=-vr1Q78OKWfQ5UBQ |title=REINSTALACIÓN SESION ORDINARIA 08-PLE-CNE-2025 – AUDIENCIA PÚBLICA DE ESCRUTINIO |language=en |access-date=25 February 2025 |via=YouTube |archive-date=1 March 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250301110648/https://www.youtube.com/live/IDSQH2_66LA%3Fsi%3D-vr1Q78OKWfQ5UBQ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite AV media |url=https://www.youtube.com/live/jgw7SS9cj8c?si=vYPD9iN2VlY7Ffyg |title=REINSTALACIÓN SESIÓN ORDINARIA 08-PLE-CNE-2025 – AUDIENCIA PÚBLICA DE ESCRUTINIO |language=en |access-date=25 February 2025 |via=YouTube}}</ref><ref>{{Cite AV media |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?si=pcGGt5uVdKi-aiSY&v=Kw4_XB11K-k&feature=youtu.be |title=Pleno del CNE proclamó resultados numéricos de las Elecciones Generales 2025 |date=24 February 2025 |last=CNE Ecuador |access-date=25 February 2025 |via=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> The final results for the presidential candidates were released on 12 March 2025. Luisa González achieved 44.00% (4,510,860 votes) and Daniel Noboa 44.17% (4,527,606 votes). The difference between them was of 16,746 votes.<ref>{{Cite web |date=12 March 2025 |title=CNE proclama resultados de la primera vuelta: Daniel Noboa superó con 16.746 votos a Luisa González |url=https://www.primicias.ec/elecciones/ecuador2025/presidenciales/resultados-definitivos-primera-vuelta-cne-daniel-noboa-luisa-gonzalez-91634/ |access-date=26 March 2025 |website=Primicias}}</ref> During the election, Noboa's mother, [[Anabella Azín]] was elected to the National Assembly to represent the national constituency.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://kchcomunicacion.com/2025/03/22/annabella-azin-es-la-carta-de-adn-para-presidir-la-asamblea-nacional-dice-el-ministro-de-gobierno-jose-de-la-gasca/|title=Annabella Azín es la 'carta' de ADN para presidir la Asamblea Nacional, dice el ministro de Gobierno, José de la Gasca|date=22 March 2025 |publisher=KCH Comuncacion|accessdate=15 April 2025}}</ref>

In the second round, Noboa received 55.6% of the vote, defeating González by 11.2 percentage points. The result exceeded expectations based on pre-election polling, with Noboa's campaign notable for its focus on young voters.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web |date=14 April 2025 |title=Daniel Noboa: Centre-right leader wins re-election in Ecuador |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4gr8dw20evo |access-date=14 April 2025 |website=BBC |language=en-GB |quote=His left-wing challenger, Luisa González, said she did not accept the result and claimed fraud, without providing evidence. |archive-date=15 April 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250415131558/https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4gr8dw20evo |url-status=live }}</ref> Noboa was congratulated by various international leaders. González refused to concede and has requested a recount, alleging fraud without providing evidence.<ref>{{Cite web |date=14 April 2025 |title=Daniel Noboa: Centre-right leader wins re-election in Ecuador |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4gr8dw20evo |access-date=14 April 2025 |website=www.bbc.com |language=en-GB |quote=His left-wing challenger, Luisa González, said she did not accept the result and claimed fraud, without providing evidence. |archive-date=15 April 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250415131558/https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4gr8dw20evo |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name=":5">{{Cite news |date=14 April 2025 |title=Incumbent Noboa wins Ecuador presidential vote, opposition claims fraud |url=https://www.lemonde.fr/en/international/article/2025/04/14/noboa-wins-ecuador-presidential-vote-election-authority_6740191_4.html |access-date=14 April 2025 |language=en |quote=His left-wing rival, Luisa Gonzalez, accused Noboa of fraud without providing evidence for her claims. In the National Assembly, the coalition formed by the Citizen Revolution Movement and the [[RETO Movement]], led by former President [[Rafael Correa]], secured a narrow plurality with 67 seats, followed closely by Noboa's [[National Democratic Action (Ecuador)|National Democratic Action]] party with 66 seats. Mónica Salazar soon after left the Citizen Revolution Movement, reducing RC-RETO to 66 seats.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2025-04-15 |title=Mónica Salazar dejó la bancada correísta en la Asamblea y asegura que sus compañeros la marginaban |url=https://www.ecuavisa.com/noticias/politica/monica-salazar-dejo-bancada-correista-asamblea-CX9167455 |access-date=2025-04-17 |website=Ecuavisa |language=es-EC}}</ref> Most smaller parties collapsed in seat share, save for [[Pachakutik]], which won 9 seats. In the Andean Parliament, the ADN won three of Ecuador's five seats to RC-RETO's two.

== Aftermath ==
=== First round ===
After the results, ADN cancelled a planned party celebration in a hotel in northern Quito, and Noboa did not make public declarations.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Guerrero |first=Ana |date=9 February 2025 |title=En Quito se esperaba el pronunciamiento de Daniel Noboa tras las elecciones 2025 |url=https://www.elcomercio.com/elecciones/quito-pronunciamiento-daniel-noboa-elecciones-ecuador-2025.html |access-date=10 February 2025 |website=El Comercio |language=es}}</ref> The next day, he posted a written statement on [[Twitter]] thanking his voters.<ref>{{Cite web |date=10 February 2025 |title=Noboa: 'Ganamos la primera vuelta contra todos los partidos del viejo Ecuador' |url=https://www.teleamazonas.com/daniel-noboa-reaccion-resultados-primera-vuelta-elecciones-2025/ |access-date=10 February 2025 |website=Teleamazonas |language=es |archive-date=11 February 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250211091806/https://www.teleamazonas.com/daniel-noboa-reaccion-resultados-primera-vuelta-elecciones-2025/ |url-status=live }}</ref> González, in a party meeting in [[Quito]], said she would have dialogues with the other candidates.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Cáceres |first=Moisés |date=10 February 2025 |title=Luisa González habló sobre el desarrollo de la primera vuelta |url=https://www.elcomercio.com/elecciones/luisa-gonzalez-denuncia-recursos-publicos-campana-daniel-noboa.html |access-date=10 February 2025 |website=El Comercio |language=es |archive-date=11 February 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250211020933/https://www.elcomercio.com/elecciones/luisa-gonzalez-denuncia-recursos-publicos-campana-daniel-noboa.html |url-status=live }}</ref> In the context of the polarization in the National Assembly between left- and right-wing political factions, the nine elected Pachakutik deputies play a crucial role. The Citizen's Movement can only achieve an absolute majority of 76 seats by forming a formal alliance with the Pachakutik Party.<ref name=indigena/> On the other hand, Noboa can secure a majority if he obtains support from at least one dissident deputy from the opposition.<ref name=indigena>{{Cite web |title=El voto indígena, clave en la segunda vuelta: ¿hacia dónde se inclinará Pachakutik? |url=https://www.lahora.com.ec/pais/pachakutik-movimiento-indigena-segunda-vuelta-elecciones-ecuador/ |access-date=18 February 2025 |website=La Hora |language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=10 February 2025 |title=Elecciones 2025: Aunque muy lejos de los líderes, Pachakutik será la tercera fuerza política |url=https://www.primicias.ec/elecciones/ecuador2025/presidenciales/elecciones2025-pachakutik-leonidas-iza-fuerza-politica-correismo-adn-89451/ |access-date=18 February 2025 |website=Primicias |archive-date=14 February 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250214063558/https://www.primicias.ec/elecciones/ecuador2025/presidenciales/elecciones2025-pachakutik-leonidas-iza-fuerza-politica-correismo-adn-89451/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

On the election day, Diego Tello Flores, a senior high-ranking official, announced the results of a post-electoral voter poll.<ref name=frauddt/> He claimed to be the only one of the four pollsters authorized by his former employer, the CNE, to conduct this poll. According to his findings, Daniel Noboa was leading Luisa González by nearly 8 points, securing 50.12% of the votes and winning the election in the first round.<ref name=frauddt/> This announcement led to significant controversy, with officials from the Citizen Revolution Movement accusing him of fraud, as such polls typically have a minimal margin of error.<ref name=frauddt>{{Cite web |title=Diario Frontera – Elecciones en Ecuador: Noboa lidera Exit poll y primeros datos del CNE |url=https://fronteradigital.com.ve/entrada/55949 |access-date=24 February 2025 |website=fronteradigital.com.ve}}</ref><ref>{{Cite AV media |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?si=mAS6eloysIFbtcB_&v=f8F-EA1fUyI&feature=youtu.be |title=GONZÁLEZ y NOBOA al BALOTAJE {{!}} ATAMAINT NO RESPONDIÓ DUDAS {{!}} DIEGO TELLO, un FRAUDE ESTADÍSTICO |date=10 February 2025 |last=En Retrospectiva |access-date=24 February 2025 |via=YouTube}}</ref> Tello reiterated in a live interview on Teleamazon that he had been compensated by the firm Estrategas and acted independently from the government. |language=en}}</ref>

On 11 February, in his first interview after the elections, Noboa claimed he had evidence of fraud, which he alleged was confirmed by an [[Organization of American States]] report. However, officials from the OAS dismissed this claim, clarifying that no abnormalities had been detected at that time.<ref name=OAS>{{Cite web |last=Cuevas |first=Carlos |date=11 February 2025 |title=OEA desmiente a Noboa y asegura que no hubo "indicios de irregularidades" en las elecciones |url=https://www.bloomberglinea.com/latinoamerica/ecuador/oea-desmiente-a-noboa-y-asegura-que-no-hubo-indicios-de-irregularidades-en-las-elecciones/ |access-date=18 February 2025 |website=Bloomberg Línea |language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |author1=Abel Alvarado |author2=Max Saltman |date=12 February 2025 |title=Ecuador's president questions first-round election results in a claim ruled out by election observers |url=https://edition.cnn.com/2025/02/12/americas/ecuador-election-irregular-latam-intl/index.html |access-date=21 February 2025 |website=CNN |language=en |archive-date=21 February 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250221010907/https://edition.cnn.com/2025/02/12/americas/ecuador-election-irregular-latam-intl/index.html |url-status=live }}</ref> Noboa also alleged that armed groups associated with drug trafficking were intentionally released from prisons to intimidate voters into supporting the Citizens' Revolution. The CNE said that several people, including poll workers, were arrested during the run-off vote on 13 April for electoral anomalies that included double voting and reports of counterfeit, pre-marked ballots. Seventeen people were caught taking photos of their ballots despite a ban imposed by the CNE due to reports of voter coercion by criminal groups.<ref>{{Cite web |date=14 April 2025 |title=Daniel Noboa is reelected Ecuador's president by voters weary of crime |url=https://apnews.com/article/ecuador-presidential-election-noboa-luisa-gonzalez-correa-1f638a952e5cc1135dcff5d8e99dc259 |access-date=14 April 2025 |website=AP News |language=en}}</ref>

====Endorsement negotiations====
On 12 February, Leonidas Iza categorically rejected any negotiations with Noboa's party, the ADN. He announced that several meetings would take place within CONAIE ([[Confederation of Indigenous Nationalities of Ecuador]]) and its political arm, the [[Pachakutik]] Party, to determine their stance on a potential alliance with the Citizens' Revolution for the second presidential round and beyond.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Segunda vuelta Ecuador 2025: Leonidas Iza descarta diálogo con el movimiento político de Daniel Noboa |url=https://www.vistazo.com/politica/elecciones-ecuador-2025/2025-02-12-segunda-vuelta-ecuador-2025-leonidas-iza-descarta-dialogo-daniel-noboa-HK8792449 |access-date=18 February 2025 |website=Vistazo |language=es-EC}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Gupta |first=Malvika |date=10 February 2025 |title=Ecuador election heads to runoff – Indigenous movement now holds key to the outcome |url=https://theconversation.com/ecuador-election-heads-to-runoff-indigenous-movement-now-holds-key-to-the-outcome-248974 |access-date=18 February 2025 |website=The Conversation |language=en-US}}</ref> On 13 February, Diego Borja, the vice-presidential candidate for Citizens Revolution, expressed a desire to initiate a joint program with Pachakutik. The primary objective is to establish a National Constituent Assembly that includes the proposal to redefine Ecuador as a [[Plurinational State]], akin to Bolivia.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Jijón |first=Eduardo José |date=13 February 2025 |title=Borja: Estamos dispuestos a conversar con los Movimientos |url=https://www.larepublica.ec/blog/2025/02/13/borja-dice-que-esta-dispuesto-a-conversar-con-todos-los-movimientos-politicos/ |access-date=18 February 2025 |language=es |archive-date=14 February 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250214063115/https://www.larepublica.ec/blog/2025/02/13/borja-dice-que-esta-dispuesto-a-conversar-con-todos-los-movimientos-politicos/ |url-status=live }}</ref> On 19 February, Leonidas Iza announced the convocation of an Expanded Grand Council for 7 March 2025, where indigenous peoples would make the final decision on currently debated proposals for a programmatic political alliance with the Citizens' Revolution Movement.<ref>{{Cite web |date=19 February 2025 |title=Movimiento indígena de Ecuador anunciará el 7 de marzo su postura para la segunda vuelta |url=https://www.swissinfo.ch/spa/movimiento-ind%C3%ADgena-de-ecuador-anunciar%C3%A1-el-7-de-marzo-su-postura-para-la-segunda-vuelta/88895417 |access-date=19 February 2025 |website=SWI swissinfo.ch |language=es |archive-date=1 March 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250301110650/https://www.swissinfo.ch/spa/movimiento-ind%C3%ADgena-de-ecuador-anunciar%C3%A1-el-7-de-marzo-su-postura-para-la-segunda-vuelta/88895417 |url-status=live }}</ref> 

On 14 February the [[Christian Social Party (Ecuador)|Social Christian Party]] announced that they would support Daniel Noboa in the second round, saying "We have never supported or voted for Rafael Correa in any election".<ref name=SCPend>{{Cite web |date=14 February 2025 |title=El Partido Social Cristiano apoyará a Daniel Noboa en la segunda vuelta: "En ninguna elección hemos apoyado o votado por Rafael Correa" |url=https://www.aldia.com.ec/el-partido-social-cristiano-apoyara-a-daniel-noboa-en-la-segunda-vuelta-en-ninguna-eleccion-hemos-apoyado-o-votado-por-rafael-correa/ |access-date=10 April 2025 |website=ALDIA |language=es |archive-date=10 April 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250410162947/https://www.aldia.com.ec/el-partido-social-cristiano-apoyara-a-daniel-noboa-en-la-segunda-vuelta-en-ninguna-eleccion-hemos-apoyado-o-votado-por-rafael-correa/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 12 March, CONAIE's branch in the Amazon region, CONFENIAE, endorsed Noboa, triggering a dispute within the organization and in Pachakutik.<ref name=indi/> On 30 March, Pachakutik endorsed Luisa González after she had agreed to 25 demands listed by the party.<ref name=indi>{{Cite web |date=11 April 2025 |title='Choosing who to fight': Ecuador's Indigenous movement splinters over presidential election support |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/longform/2025/4/10/ecuadors-indigenous-movement-splinters-over-presidential-election-support |access-date=11 April 2025 |website=Al Jazeera |language=en |archive-date=11 April 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250411014245/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/longform/2025/4/10/ecuadors-indigenous-movement-splinters-over-presidential-election-support |url-status=live }}</ref>
