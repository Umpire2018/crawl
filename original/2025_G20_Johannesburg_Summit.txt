The '''G20 Johannesburg Summit''' will be the [[List of G20 summits|twentieth]] meeting of the [[G20|Group of Twenty]] (G20), a meeting of heads of state and government held from 22 to 23 November 2025.<ref>{{Cite web |title=G20 Leaders'Summit |url=https://g20.org/event/g20-leaders-summit/ |access-date=2024-12-03 |language=en-ZA}}</ref> It will be the first G20 summit held in [[Johannesburg]], [[South Africa]] and on the African continent.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-11-08 |title=Political events set to boost tourism in 2025 |url=https://www.tourismupdate.co.za/article/political-events-set-boost-tourism-2025 |access-date=2025-02-24 |website=Southern & East African Tourism Update |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Brederode |first=William |title=Cape Town 'disappointed' as Joburg gets to host G20 Summit thanks to airports, hotels |url=https://www.news24.com/fin24/economy/g20-summit-joburg-pips-cape-town-ethekwini-as-host-city-thanks-to-airports-hotels-20241028 |access-date=2025-02-24 |website=Business |language=en-US}}</ref> The summit will cost the South African government [[South African rand|R]]691 million.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Lamola says hosting G20 summit in 2025 to cost SA nearly R700m |url=https://www.businesslive.co.za/bd/national/2024-09-11-lamola-says-hosting-g20-summit-in-2025-to-cost-sa-nearly-r700m/ |archive-url=http://web.archive.org/web/20241013144013/https://www.businesslive.co.za/bd/national/2024-09-11-lamola-says-hosting-g20-summit-in-2025-to-cost-sa-nearly-r700m/ |archive-date=2024-10-13 |access-date=2025-02-24 |work=BusinessLIVE |language=en-ZA}}</ref>

== Presidency ==
South Africa assumed the G20 presidency from 1 December 2024 to November 2025, approximately five years ahead of the [[United Nations]] (UN) [[Sustainable Development Goals|Agenda 2030]] deadline.

There are high expectations that South Africa will lead a progressive, people-centred, development-oriented and solution-oriented presidency, in a fractured global geopolitical context, until it hands over the presidency to the United States on 1 December 2025.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |title=G20 Presidency |url=https://g20.org/g20-south-africa/g20-presidency/ |access-date=2024-12-03 |language=en-ZA}}</ref>

South Africa’s participation in the G20 is guided by its four strategic foreign policy pillars (national interests, the African Agenda, South-South Cooperation and Multilateralism).<ref name=":0" />

== Summit topic ==
For the G20 to make a meaningful contribution in addressing the polycrisis confronting the world, South Africa has adopted the theme '''“Solidarity, Equality, Sustainability”'''.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-12-05 |title=2025 G20 Summit {{!}} Johannesburg to host global event |website=eNCA |url=https://www.enca.com/videos/2025-g20-summit-johannesburg-host-global-event |access-date=2025-02-24 |language=en}}</ref>
[[File:Block Solidarity-Equality-Sustainability.png|left|179x179px]]
This theme reflects South Africa’s intention to build on the efforts and successes of the last three G20 Presidencies of the [[Global North and Global South|Global South]] and to advance the development agenda.

Through '''''solidarity''''', we seek to achieve a people-centred, development-oriented and inclusive future. In an interconnected world, the challenges facing one nation affect all nations. By promoting '''''equality''''', we seek to ensure fair treatment, opportunities and progress for all people and nations, regardless of their economic status, gender, race, geographic location or other characteristics. '''''Sustainability''''' is about meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own. <ref name=":0" />

== Agenda priorities ==
South Africa will continue to use the G20 as a premier forum for economic and financial cooperation, bringing together developed and developing countries, and emerging markets to find solutions to global challenges. During the 2024 State of the Nation Address, President Cyril Ramaphosa stated that “we will put Africa’s development at the top of the agenda when we host the G20 in 2025.”<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |date=2025-02-03 |title=Saudi Sherpa Office holds first G20 workshop in preparation for 2025 summit |url=https://www.saudigazette.com.sa/article/649090/SAUDI-ARABIA/Saudi-Sherpa-Office-holds-first-G20-workshop-in-preparation-for-2025-summit-nbsp |access-date=2025-02-24 |website=Saudigazette |language=English}}</ref>

South Africa will use its G20 Presidency to advocate and mobilise support for developing economies in Africa and the Global South, building on the efforts and successes of the Indonesian, Indian and Brazilian Presidencies to champion the development agenda.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Lebuso |first=Sthembiso |title=Ramaphosa sets bold agenda for South Africa's G20 presidency |url=https://www.news24.com/citypress/news/ramaphosa-sets-bold-agenda-for-south-africas-g20-presidency-20250121 |access-date=2025-02-24 |website=City Press |language=en-US}}</ref>

=== High-level priorities ===
South Africa’s G20 Presidency will drive the following high-level deliverables, and priorities, which will find expression in the work of the Sherpa and Finance Tracks:<ref name=":0" />
* '''Priority 1''' – Inclusive economic growth, industrialisation, employment and reducing inequality<ref>{{Cite web |last=Opinion |title=G20 summit an opportunity to fix ailing Joburg metro |url=https://www.iol.co.za/news/politics/opinion/g20-summit-an-opportunity-to-fix-ailing-joburg-metro-6b30a1c4-4a68-4c69-a34d-0c221ee78e9b |access-date=2025-02-24 |website=iol.co.za |language=en}}</ref>
* '''Priority 2''' – Food security<ref>{{Cite web |last=Tembo |first=Patricia |date=2025-01-06 |title=G20 Summit sparks optimism for Mzansi agriculture in 2025 |url=https://www.foodformzansi.co.za/agricultures-hopeful-outlook-insights-from-leading-economists/ |access-date=2025-02-24 |website=Food For Mzansi |language=en-US}}</ref>
* '''Priority 3''' – Artificial intelligence and innovation for sustainable development
