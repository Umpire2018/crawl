In the early morning of February 10, 2025, a bus carrying around 70 passengers<ref name="f24" /> veered off a bridge and fell into the [[Las Vacas River]] after colliding with other vehicles in Zone 6, on the outskirts of [[Guatemala City]], Guatemala.<ref name=cnn>{{Cite web |url=https://edition.cnn.com/2025/02/10/americas/guatemala-city-bus-accident-intl/index.html |title=At least 50 die in Guatemala after bus plunges off bridge |date=February 10, 2025 |website=CNN |access-date=February 10, 2025}}</ref><ref name=f24(es)>{{Cite web |url= https://www.france24.com/es/américa-latina/20250210-al-menos-50-muertos-en-guatemala-tras-la-caída-de-un-autobús-desde-un-puente |title= Al menos 55 muertos en Guatemala tras la caída de un autobús desde un puente |date=February 10, 2025 |website=France 24 |language=es |access-date=February 11, 2025}}</ref> The crash killed at least 55 people<ref name=":0" /> and seriously injured nine.<ref name=":1"> {{cite web | url= https://tvaztecaguate.com/nacionales/2025/02/10/sobrevivientes-del-accidente-en-puente-belice-reciben-atencion-medica/ | title=Listado de sobrevivientes del accidente en Puente Belice reciben atención médica |date=February 10, 2025 |language=es|website=TV Azteca GUATE|access-date=February 11, 2025}}</ref>

The vehicle was operating as a [[commuter bus]],<ref name="bbc">{{Cite web |url= https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c8d9ndjypv9o.amp |title= Guatemala police investigate bus crash which killed 55 |date=February 11, 2025 |website=BBC News |access-date=February 11, 2025}}</ref> but outside of its approved route.<ref name="dc" /> Although the bus was licensed correctly,<ref name="f24" /> the driver did not have an appropriate license.<ref name=":3" /> Investigators are exploring a wide range of causes, including overloading,<ref name="f24" /> weapons,<ref name="bbc" /> drugs,<ref name=":4" /> or mechanical problems.<ref name="bbc" /> In the aftermath of the crash, updated legislation was proposed to improve road safety,<ref name=":5" /> after other relief efforts were underway.<ref name="alj" />

==Background==
The bus was an extra-urban transport unit owned by {{Lang|es|Transportes Tineca Sociedad Anónima}}.<ref name=":2" /> It was densely occupied with more than 70 people,<ref name="f24" /> mostly patients, pupils, as well as other passengers going to work.<ref name="bbc" /> It departed from [[El Progreso Department]]<ref name=":2" />{{efn|Divergent sources reported that the bus departed from either [[San Agustín Acasaguastlán]] or [[San Antonio La Paz]] in that department.<ref name=f24 /><ref name=bbc />}} at around 4:00&nbsp;a.m. ([[UTC−06:00|GMT-6]]).<ref name="bbc" />

==Victims==
Despite the initial report of 56 casualties,{{Efn|Guatemala City Fire Department spokesman Carlos Hernández said that 52 died at the scene, and four died in hospital.}}<ref name=cbs>{{Cite web |url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/puente-belice-guatemala-deadly-bus-crash/ |title=Bus plunges off a Guatemala bridge, killing at least 56 passengers |website=CBS News |access-date=February 10, 2025 |archive-date=February 11, 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250211003821/https://www.cbsnews.com/amp/news/puente-belice-guatemala-deadly-bus-crash/ |url-status=live }}</ref> a list of 54 fatal victims and their ages was released the following day.<ref name=pl>{{cite web | url= https://www.prensalibre.com/vida/los-rostros-de-una-tragedia-el-duelo-nacional-por-las-54-victimas-de-un-viaje-sin-retorno/ | title= Los rostros de una tragedia: el duelo nacional por las 54 víctimas de un viaje sin retorno |date= February 11, 2025 |language=es|website=Prensa Libre|access-date=February 12, 2025}}</ref> Fifty-three died at the scene, reportedly including the 24-year-old driver, and two died in hospital after being rescued, bringing the number of fatalities to 55.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=2025-02-11 |title=Guatemala police investigate bus crash which killed 55 |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c8d9ndjypv9o |access-date=2025-02-18 |website=BBC |language=en-GB}}</ref> Nine people remain hospitalized as of February 10, with five in one hospital and four alive in another hospital where two of the victims died.<ref name=":1" />

Mynor Ruano, a spokesman for the city's fire service, said that efforts were underway "to save other people who are trapped" in the wreckage. The bodies of 36 men and 15 women have been sent to a provincial morgue.<ref name="alj" />

==Investigation==
[[Ministry of Communications, Infrastructure and Housing|Minister of Communications, Infrastructure and Housing]] Miguel Ángel Díaz Bobadilla initiated an investigation that revealed the bus had been active for 30 years but still had a valid license to operate. He said that the cause of the accident was still unknown and that investigators were looking into whether the bus was overloaded with passengers.<ref name="f24" />

Investigators said they would check if the driver could have lost control due to [[brake fade]].<ref name="bbc" />

On the same day, the [[Attorney General of Guatemala|Public Ministry of Guatemala]] ({{Lang|es|Ministerio Público}}, MP) requested a [[autopsy|post-mortem examination]] on the body of Billy Aníbal Foronda Azañón, the 24-year-old bus driver, to [[drug test|detect the presence of possible drugs]].<ref name=":4">{{cite web |date=February 10, 2025 |title=MP solicita análisis toxicológicos al piloto del bus accidentado en Puente Belice |url=https://tvaztecaguate.com/nacionales/2025/02/10/mp-solicita-analisis-toxicologicos-al-piloto-del-bus-accidentado-en-puente-belice/ |access-date=February 11, 2025 |website=TV Azteca GUATE |language=es}}</ref><ref name="bbc" /> Preliminary investigations determined that his [[driver's license]] was for light noncommercial vehicles, not mass transit.<ref name=":3">{{cite web |title=Piloto del bus accidentado en el puente Belice conducía con licencia tipo C, confirman autoridades |url=https://www.prensalibre.com/guatemala/comunitario/piloto-del-bus-accidentado-en-el-puente-belice-conducia-con-licencia-tipo-c-confirman-autoridades/ |access-date=February 11, 2025 |website=Prensa Libre |language=es}}</ref><ref name="lh" /> The bus also was at an unapproved route.<ref name=dc>{{Cite web |title=Church expresses solidarity with victims' families, demands answers after Guatemala bus crash |url=https://www.detroitcatholic.com/news/church-expresses-solidarity-with-victims-families-demands-answers-after-guatemala-bus-crash |access-date=2025-02-18 |website=Detroit Catholic |language=en-US}}</ref>

In addition, police found a gun in the wreckage, raising speculation that the driver may have been threatened with the gun. National Civil Police deputy director-general Helver Beltetón said the weapon was registered to a deceased passenger who worked as a [[private security guard]], adding that "the brakes could have failed but we're also not ruling out an accident or a criminal act; all that will be investigated".<ref name="bbc" />

By February 15, the wreckage still remained at the accident site as the MP prosecutors continued to inspect it.<ref>{{cite web |date=February 15, 2025 |title=Bus accidentado bajo el puente Belice sigue bajo resguardo del MP por peritajes |url=https://www.prensalibre.com/guatemala/justicia/bus-accidentado-bajo-el-puente-belice-sigue-bajo-resguardo-del-mp-por-peritajes/ |access-date=February 16, 2025 |website=Pensa Libre |language=es}}</ref>

==Reactions==
===Domestic===
President [[Bernardo Arévalo]] declared three days of national mourning and deployed the [[Armed Forces of Guatemala|Guatemalan army]] along with the disaster agency to provide response efforts.<ref name=alj>{{Cite web |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/2/10/dozens-killed-in-guatemala-bus-crash-rescuers-say |title=At least 51 people killed in Guatemala bus crash |website=Al Jazeera |access-date=February 10, 2025 |archive-date=February 10, 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250210165504/https://www.aljazeera.com/amp/news/2025/2/10/dozens-killed-in-guatemala-bus-crash-rescuers-say |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=February 10, 2025 |title=Qué significa luto nacional en Guatemala: la medida que decretó Bernardo Arévalo por el accidente de bus que dejó 54 fallecidos |url=https://www.prensalibre.com/guatemala/politica/que-significa-luto-nacional-en-guatemala-la-medida-que-decreto-bernardo-arevalo-por-el-accidente-de-bus-que-dejo-54-fallecidos/ |access-date=February 10, 2025 |website=Prensa Libre |language=es}}</ref>

President of the [[Congress of the Republic of Guatemala|Congress of Guatemala]] [[Nery Ramos]] lamented that "more than four dozen Guatemalans lost their lives in search of daily sustenance".<ref name=alj />

During an interview on [[TV Azteca Guate]], deputy César Dávila stressed that a proposal to support victims' families was submitted at a meeting with [[parliamentary group]] leaders of the Congress. He also pointed out that they have discussed updating the [[traffic code]], in force since 1996, to improve [[road traffic safety|road safety]] and hold carriers responsible.<ref name=":5">{{cite web | url= https://tvaztecaguate.com/nacionales/2025/02/12/estado-de-la-nacion-transporte-publico-y-el-seguro-contra-terceros/ | title=Estado de la Nación; Transporte público y el seguro contra terceros|date=February 12, 2025|language=es|website=TV Azteca GUATE|access-date=February 13, 2025}}</ref>

Father Rigoberto Pérez, a [[vicar]] in Guatemala City and a longtime communications officer in the [[Catholic Church in Guatemala|Guatemalan Catholic Church]], stated the tragedy revealed the risks and disorganization that many Guatemalan workers face everyday.<ref name=dc />
