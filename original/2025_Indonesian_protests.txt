Public and student-led anti-government demonstrations are being held throughout several cities in Indonesia. They were launched on 17 February 2025 by the [[All-Indonesian Students' Union]] (BEM SI),<ref name="detik">{{cite web |last=Syarifudin |first=Taufiq |date=17 February 2025 |title=Massa Mahasiswa Aksi Indonesia Gelap di Patung Kuda Terus Bertambah |trans-title=Mass of 'Dark Indonesia' Action Students at the Horse Statue Continues to Grow |url=https://news.detik.com/berita/d-7782635/massa-mahasiswa-aksi-indonesia-gelap-di-patung-kuda-terus-bertambah |access-date=17 February 2025 |website=[[Detikcom]] |language=id}}</ref> together with individual students' unions.<ref>{{cite web |last=Safitri |first=Rahma Dwi |date=17 Feb 2025 |title=BEM UI & BEM SI akan Demo Tolak Pemangkasan Anggaran Hari Ini |trans-title=BEM UI & BEM SI will demonstrate against budget cuts today |url=https://tirto.id/bem-ui-bem-si-akan-demo-tolak-pemangkasan-anggaran-hari-ini-g8od |access-date=17 February 2025 |website=Tirto |language=id}}</ref> 

According to the central coordinator of BEM SI, Herianto, the alliance had called for protests all over the country on 17 and 18 February (cancelled at Jakarta), while they would hold the protest centrally at Jakarta on 19 (cancelled) and 20 February.<ref name="detik"/> The Civil Society Coalition had also called for civilians to participate in demonstrations on 21 February following Friday prayers.<ref>{{cite web |last=Dewi |first=Santi |date=19 Feb 2025 |title=Koalisi Warga Sipil Ajak Cuti Bersama Jumat, Demo Kebijakan Prabowo |trans-title=Civil Citizen Coalition invites Friday Mutual Leave to Demo Prabowo's Policy |url=https://www.idntimes.com/news/indonesia/santi-dewi/koalisi-warga-sipil-ajak-cuti-bersama-jumat-demo-kebijakan-prabowo?page=all&_gl=1*1ry28gd*_ga*UElYTXBOMG15VnU4cE9qN1hBaENwUXZ5NVhLemJnVzJiTjJTcFhEODRSanM5Z0prY29FT2lqLWxxQVpDaF8ySA..*_ga_TT180KERFB*MTczOTk3MzIzMC4xOS4wLjE3Mzk5NzMyMzAuMC4wLjA. |access-date=19 February 2025 |website=[[IDN Times]] |language=id}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |date=18 Feb 2025 |title=Demonstrasi 'Indonesia Gelap' Siap Kembali Digelar Jumat 21 Februari 2025: Memanggil Seluruh Warga Sipil Sekalian! |trans-title='Dark Indonesia' Demonstration Ready to Return on Friday, February 21, 2025: Calling All Civilians! |url=https://poskota.co.id/2025/02/18/demonstrasi-indonesia-gelap-siap-kembali-digelar-jumat-21-februari-2025-memanggil-seluruh-warga-sipil-sekalian |access-date=18 February 2025 |website=[[Pos Kota]] |language=id}}</ref> BEM SI projected that around 5,000 students would participate in the protests,<ref>{{cite web |last=Bayu Wisesa |first=Yosafat Diva |date=17 Feb 2025 |title=Gelar Demo di Berbagai Daerah, BEM SI: 5 Ribu Mahasiswa Turun ke Jalan |trans-title=Holding Demonstrations in Various Regions, BEM SI: 5 Thousand Students Take to the Road |url=https://www.idntimes.com/news/indonesia/yosafat-diva-bagus/gelar-demo-di-berbagai-daerah-bem-si-5-ribu-mahasiswa-turun-ke-jalan |access-date=18 February 2025 |website=IDN Times |language=id}}</ref> and they also threatened further actions if the government does not react positively.<ref>{{cite web |last=Tysara |first=Laudia |date=20 Feb 2025 |title=Setelah Aksi Puncak 'Indonesia Gelap', Ini Rencana Lanjutan BEM SI |trans-title=After the 'Dark Indonesia' Summit, This is BEM SI's Next Plan |url=https://www.liputan6.com/hot/read/5927529/setelah-aksi-puncak-indonesia-gelap-ini-rencana-lanjutan-bem-si |access-date=20 February 2025 |website=[[Liputan 6]] |language=id}}</ref>

The second wave of protests began in March 2025 following the ratification of newly-revised [[Indonesian National Armed Forces Law]], which increased the number of civilian positions that soldiers are allowed to hold, from 10 to 14. Generally, most of the protests were held in front of the building of respective legislatures (national or regional), with its participants usually having worn black clothing, marked by the [[tire fire|burning of used tires]] and clashes with policemen.

=== February protests ===
The protests occurred as a response over controversial policies by Indonesian president [[Prabowo Subianto]] and his vice-president [[Gibran Rakabuming Raka]], which include the {{ill|free nutritious meal|id|Makan Bergizi Gratis}} (MBG) programme and the enactment of [[Presidential Instruction Number 1 of 2025]], which led into massive budget cuts, supposedly to fund the free nutritious meal programme.<ref>{{cite web |date=February 17, 2025 |title=Questioning budget cuts |url=https://www.thejakartapost.com/opinion/2025/02/17/questioning-budget-cuts.html |access-date=18 February 2025 |website=[[The Jakarta Post]]}}</ref> The nickname {{lang|id|#IndonesiaGelap}} itself originates as a [[hashtag activism|hashtag]] popular on [[Twitter]] (X). According to ''Beautynesia'', the hashtag had been used by more than 760 thousand tweets,<ref>{{cite web |last=Quamila |first=Nadya |date=18 Feb 2025 |title=Menggema di Daring dan Luring, Mahasiswa Demo "Indonesia Gelap": Ini Makna hingga 13 Tuntutannya |trans-title=Echoing Online and Offline, Students Demo “Dark Indonesia”: The Meaning and 13 Demands |url=https://www.beautynesia.id/life/menggema-di-daring-dan-luring-mahasiswa-demo-indonesia-gelap-ini-makna-hingga-13-tuntutannya/b-301733 |access-date=18 February 2025 |website=Beautynesia |language=id}}</ref> but within 24 hours, this number has increased up to 14 million tweets.<ref>{{cite web |last=Aswan |first=Desi Triana |date=18 February 2025 |title=Indonesia Gelap Trending X Capai 14 Juta Cuitan 24 Jam, Perkara Aksi Demo Mahasiswa Gegara Efisiensi |trans-title=Indonesia Dark Trending X Reaches 14 Million Tweets in 24 Hours, Student Protests Concerned with Efficiency |url=https://sultra.tribunnews.com/2025/02/18/indonesia-gelap-trending-x-capai-14-juta-cuitan-24-jam-perkara-aksi-demo-mahasiswa-gegara-efisiensi |access-date=18 February 2025 |website=[[Tribun News]] |language=id}}</ref> While according to coordinator of BEM SI, Satria Naufal, it represents "the fears, worries, and well-being of the citizens".<ref name="tempo">{{cite web |last=Ayu |first=Rizki Dewi |date=17 February 2025 |title=Ramai Tagar Indonesia Gelap, Apa Maksudnya? |trans-title=Dark Indonesia hashtag, what does it mean? |url=https://www.tempo.co/politik/ramai-tagar-indonesia-gelap-apa-maksudnya--1208171 |access-date=17 February 2025 |website=[[Tempo (Indonesian magazine)|Tempo]] |language=id}}</ref> Digital research company Jangkara found that during February 2025, the hashtag has received 81% negative sentiment, followed by 13% neutral and 6% positive, based on their analysis on 64,816 comments on X.<ref>{{cite web |last=Yaputra |first=Hendrik |date=19 March 2025 |title=Tagar Indonesia Gelap Dapat 81 Persen Sentimen Negatif |trans-title=Dark Indonesia hashtag gets 81 percent of negative sentiment |url=https://www.tempo.co/politik/tagar-indonesia-gelap-dapat-81-persen-sentimen-negatif-1221552/ |access-date=19 March 2025 |website=Tempo |language=id}}</ref>

In March 2025, protests began to renew after [[Indonesian National Armed Forces Law]] underwent revision by the DPR. The important points of this version, include:<ref>{{cite web |date=18 Mar 2025 |title=Infografis: Poin-poin Krusial Perubahan dalam RUU TNI |trans-title=Infographic: Crucial Points of Change in the TNI Bill |url=https://www.cnnindonesia.com/nasional/20250317194605-15-1209886/infografis-poin-poin-krusial-perubahan-dalam-ruu-tni |access-date=2025-03-28 |website=[[CNN Indonesia]] |language=id}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Anjelina |first1=Chella Defa |last2=Adhi |first2=Irawan Sapto |date=2025-03-20 |title=Apa Isi UU TNI Terbaru? * Chapter 3:
** Armed Forces, previously subordinated to both the [[President of Indonesia]] and [[Ministry of Defense (Indonesia)|Ministry of Defense]] (Kemhan), is now fully subordinated to Kemhan.
* Chapter 7:
** Non-war military operations (OMSP) were increased from previous 14 into 16 tasks. The newly-added two tasks include protection efforts against cyberattacks, and protection of Indonesian citizens and national interests overseas.
* Chapter 47:
** The number of civilian positions TNI members are allowed to occupy were increased from previous 10 into 14 positions. Five of them include [[National Agency for Disaster Countermeasure]] (BNPB), [[National Border Management Agency]] (BNPP), [[National Counter Terrorism Agency]] (BNPT), [[Indonesian Maritime Security Agency|Maritime Security Agency]] (Bakamla), and [[Attorney General's Office of Indonesia|Attorney General's Office]] (Kejagung). * Chapter 53:
** Retirement ages vary by the rank of a soldier:
** [[Non-commissioned officer]]s ({{lang|id|bintara}}) and [[Private (rank)|private]] ({{lang|id|tamtama}}): 55 years
** [[Officer (armed forces)|Commissioned officers]] ({{lang|id|perwira}}) up to [[colonel]] ({{lang|id|kolonel}}): 58 years
** One-star high officer ({{lang|id|perwira tinggi bintang 1}}): 60 years
** Two-star high officer ({{lang|id|perwira tinggi bintang 2}}): 61 years
** Three-star high officer ({{lang|id|perwira tinggi bintang 3}}): 62 years
** Four-star high officer ({{lang|id|perwira tinggi bintang 4}}): 63 years, or can be extended twice by a presidential decree

=== Second phase: ''#TolakRUUTNI'' protests ===
A second wave of demonstrations against the Draft Law on the Indonesian National Army (RUU TNI) is currently taking place across multiple regions, including Yogyakarta and Jakarta, on 19–20 March 2025. The protests involved students, lecturers, and civil society organizations, who expressed concerns that the bill could undermine democratic principles and civilian supremacy over the military. In Yogyakarta, protesters from the [[University of Gadjah Mada]] (UGM) and the [[Islamic University of Indonesia]] (UII) presented several key demands:<ref>{{Cite web |last=Prodjo |first=Wahyu Adityo |date=19 March 2025 |title=Ratusan Mahasiswa dan Dosen Gelar Demo Tolak RUU TNI, Sampaikan 5 Tuntutan |url=https://www.kompas.com/edu/read/2025/03/19/200858971/ratusan-mahasiswa-dan-dosen-gelar-demo-tolak-ruu-tni-sampaikan-5-tuntutan |access-date=20 March 2025 |website=kompas.com |language=id}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=gusti.grehenson |date=19 March 2025 |title=Dosen dan Mahasiswa UGM Gelar Aksi Tolak RUU TNI |url=https://ugm.ac.id/id/berita/dosen-dan-mahasiswa-ugm-gelar-aksi-tolak-ruu-tni/ |access-date=20 March 2025 |website=Universitas Gadjah Mada |language=id-ID}}</ref>

# Demand that the Government and DPR cancel the revision of the TNI Law, which lacks transparency, is rushed, and disregards public opinion, as it constitutes a constitutional violation.
# Demand that the Government and DPR uphold the constitution and remain committed to the Reform Agenda by preserving the principles of civilian supremacy and equality before the law, while firmly rejecting the dual function of the TNI/Polri (''[[dwifungsi]]'').
# Demand that the TNI/Polri, as state institutions, undergo internal reforms and enhance their professionalism to restore public trust.

On 19 March 2025, students from [[Trisakti University]] staged a demonstration at Gerbang Pancasila, within the Parliament Complex in Jakarta, opposing the proposed revision of the Indonesian National Army Law (UU TNI). They presented four main demands, expressing concerns that the revisions could weaken civilian supremacy and potentially reintroduce the military's dual function (''dwifungsi'' TNI) in politics and governance.<ref>{{Cite AV media |url=https://www.metrotvnews.com/play/b3JCp2aW-menkum-dicegat-mahasiswa-tolak-revisi-uu-tni |title=Menkum Dicegat Mahasiswa, Tolak Revisi UU TNI |language=id |access-date=20 March 2025 |via=www.metrotvnews.com}}</ref> The demands were presented to Minister of Law, [[Supratman Andi Agtas]]. In [[Banda Aceh]], students from [[Syiah Kuala University]] (USK) organised a protest in front of the Aceh Regional House of Representatives (DPRA). They additionally demanded transparency of the use of a special autonomy fund. The head of [[NasDem Party]]'s fraction and member of the DPRA third commission, Nurchalis, promised that the students' demands would be discussed at the legislature and carried to the national level.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rmolaceh.id/aksi-indonesia-gelap-mahasiswa-aceh-tolak-efesiensi-anggaran-dan-mbg|title=Aksi Indonesia Gelap, Mahasiswa Aceh Tolak Efesiensi Anggaran dan MBG|website=rmolaceh.id|access-date=19 February 2025 |author1=Rmolnetwork }}</ref> The protesters left significant rubbish, ranging from used drinking bottles to banners. Since the protesters ignored the requests by journalists to clean the rubbish, it was cleaned by the police.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rmolaceh.id/sampah-berserakan-di-halaman-dpr-aceh-usai-aksi-demo-indonesia-gelap|title=Sampah Berserakan di DPR Aceh Usai Aksi Demo Indonesia Gelap|website=rmolaceh.id|access-date=19 February 2025 |author1=Rmolnetwork }}</ref>
[[File:Unjuk rasa mahasiswa di Medan 2025.jpg|thumb|Student protestors from the [[State Islamic University of North Sumatera|State Islamic University of North Sumatra]] in front of the North Sumatra DPRD building in Medan]]
In [[Medan]], students from [[State Islamic University of North Sumatera|State Islamic University of North Sumatra]] protested front of the building of North Sumatra Regional House of Representatives (DPRD) on 20 February. They carried several banners, and the building was guarded by dozens of personnels. After hours, representatives from DPRD met them and guaranteed that their demands will be brought to North Sumatra DPRD and national DPR.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://medan.kompas.com/read/2025/02/20/164405778/diguyur-hujan-mahasiswa-demo-indonesia-gelap-tetap-bertahan-di-dprd-sumut|title=Diguyur Hujan, Mahasiswa Demo "Indonesia Gelap" Tetap Bertahan di DPRD Sumut|website=medan.kompas.com|access-date=20 February 2025 |last1=Media |first1=Kompas Cyber }}</ref> Protests resumed next day, not long after, a representative of Commission C of the legislature, Palacheta Subies Subianto met them. Several posters were attached to the fences of North Sumatra DPRD,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.detik.com/sumut/berita/d-7789313/didemo-mahasiswa-poster-indonesia-gelap-mejeng-di-pagar-dprd-sumut|title=Didemo Mahasiswa, Poster 'Indonesia Gelap' Mejeng di Pagar DPRD Sumut|website=detik.com|access-date=21 February 2025}}</ref> and used tires were burned during the event.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sumut.inews.id/berita/demo-indonesia-gelap-di-depan-dprd-sumut-memanas-mahasiswa-bakar-ban|title=Demo Indonesia Gelap di Depan DPRD Sumut Memanas, Mahasiswa Bakar Ban|website=sumut.inews.id|access-date=21 February 2025 |last1=Siregar |first1=Wahyudi Aulia }}</ref> On 26 March, students from North Sumatra Students' Union Alliance began to organise a sit-in front of North Sumatra DPRD building at 03:00&nbsp;p.m., while some others were inside the building's hall after successfully jumping over its fence. The protests heated up after they burned used tires and clashed with policemen.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.detik.com/sumut/berita/d-7843171/puluhan-mahasiswa-demo-di-dprd-sumut-tuntut-pencabutan-uu-tni|title=Puluhan Mahasiswa Demo di DPRD Sumut Tuntut Pencabutan UU TNI|website=detik.com|access-date=26 March 2025}}</ref> Next day, Accumulation of People's and Labour's Anger (AKBAR) held a protest in the same building, but later clashed with the police. According to the [[Commission for Missing Persons and Victims of Violence]] (KontraS), dozens of people were injured during the event.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cnnindonesia.com/nasional/20250327163816-20-1213794/demo-tolak-uu-tni-di-medan-ricuh-massa-aksi-diduga-dipukuli-aparat|title=Demo Tolak UU TNI di Medan Ricuh, Massa Aksi Diduga Dipukuli Aparat|website=cnnindonesia.com|access-date=27 March 2025}}</ref>

In [[Pematangsiantar]], on 27 March, students from the University of Simalungun clashed with policemen at Pematangsiantar DPRD, after attempting to forcefully enter the building. Three protesters were arrested by the police,<ref name="kompas6">{{cite web|url=https://www.kompas.tv/nasional/583551/demo-tolak-uu-tni-mahasiswa-coba-terobos-gedung-dprd-pematang-siantar|title=Demo Tolak UU TNI, Mahasiswa Coba Terobos Gedung DPRD Pematang Siantar|website=kompas.tv|access-date=2025-03-31}}</ref> meanwhile, one of the students, Afriadi Lesmana, was beaten up by a DPRD member and municipal policemen.<ref name="detik5">{{cite web|url=https://www.detik.com/sumut/hukum-dan-kriminal/d-7848566/ulah-anggota-dprd-pematangsiantar-diduga-puluk-mahasiswa-yang-demo-uu-tni|title=Ulah Anggota DPRD Pematangsiantar Diduga Puluk Mahasiswa yang Demo UU TNI|website=detik.com|access-date=2025-03-31}}</ref>

In [[Padang]], students from [[Andalas University]] (UNAND) and [[Padang State Polytechnics]] (PNP) protested front of the entrance gate into UNAND.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tempo.co/politik/sederet-keresahan-mahasiswa-unand-dan-pnp-diungkapkan-dalam-aksi-indonesia-gelap-1208672|title=Sederet Keresahan Mahasiswa Unand dan PNP Diungkapkan dalam Aksi Indonesia Gelap|website=tempo.co|access-date=18 February 2025}}</ref> Later, hundreds of students under the All-West Sumatra Alliance of Students' Unions protested in front of the building of the West Sumatra DPRD on 18 February 2025 at 3&nbsp;p.m., and also held several banners.<ref name="ayoindonesia">{{cite web|url=https://www.ayoindonesia.com/nasional/0114576485/berlanjut-ini-daftar-aksi-indonesia-gelap-di-berbagai-daerah-hari-ini-18-februari-2025-semarang-malang-sumbar|title=Berlanjut! Ini Daftar Aksi Indonesia Gelap di Berbagai Daerah Hari Ini 18 Februari 2025: Semarang, Malang, Sumbar|website=ayoindonesia.com|access-date=18 February 2025}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.liputan6.com/regional/read/5926319/aksi-unjuk-rasa-indonesia-gelap-di-sumbar-mahasiswa-orasi-di-depan-gedung-dprd?page=2|title=Aksi Unjuk Rasa 'Indonesia Gelap' di Sumbar, Mahasiswa Orasi di Depan Gedung DPRD|website=liputan6.com|access-date=18 February 2025}}</ref>

In [[Bukittinggi]], a number of community elements and the Bukittinggi student alliances, including the [[Indonesian Muslim Student Union]] (SEMMI) of Bukittinggi City, held a demonstration at the Bukittinggi DPRD building on Thursday, 20 March 2025.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Aksi Demonstrasi di DPRD Bukittinggi: Kenapa Rapat RUU TNI Dilakukan Tertutup Di Hotel? |url=https://sumbartime.com/aksi-demonstrasi-di-dprd-bukittinggi-kenapa-rapat-ruu-tni-dilakukan-tertutup-di-hotel/ |access-date=23 March 2025 |website=Sumbartime.com |language=id}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=20 March 2025 |title=Tolak RUU TNI, Aliansi Mahasiswa Gelar Demo ke DPRD Bukittinggi |url=https://bacalahnews.com/tolak-ruu-tni-aliansi-mahasiswa-gelar-demo-ke-dprd-bukittinggi/ |access-date=23 March 2025 |website=BacalahNews.com |language=en-US}}</ref>

In [[Pekanbaru]], students from the Islamic University of Riau protested front of the Riau DPRD building on 26 February. They additionally demanded for the reimbursement of scholarships for 6,000 students. The protesters dispersed after submitting their demands to the parliament members.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://m.halloriau.com/read-14605016-2025-02-26-indonesia-gelap-di-dprd-massa-aksi-desak-pencairan-beasiswa-6000-mahasiswa-riau-dan-sahkan-uu-perampasan-aset.html|title=Indonesia Gelap di DPRD, Massa Aksi Desak Pencairan Beasiswa 6.000 Mahasiswa Riau dan Sahkan UU Perampasan Aset|website=halloriau.com|access-date=27 February 2025}}</ref>

In [[Tanjung Pinang]], 62 students protested front of the Riau Islands DPRD building. They were guarded by 150 joint personnels from regional and municipal police.<ref name="hariankepri">{{cite web|url=https://www.hariankepri.com/150-aparat-amankan-demo-kapolresta-apresiasi-aksi-mahasiswa-yang-tertib/|title=150 Aparat Amankan Demo, Kapolresta Apresiasi Aksi Mahasiswa yang Tertib|website=hariankepri.com|access-date=20 February 2025}}</ref>

In [[Palembang]], on 20 February, hundreds of students, mainly from [[Raden Fatah State Islamic University]] (UIN Raden Fatah), walked from Campus A at 10&nbsp;a.m. and arrived in front of the building of South Sumatra DPRD at 10:30&nbsp;a.m.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tempo.co/politik/ratusan-mahasiswa-demo-indonesia-gelap-di-depan-kantor-drpd-sumsel--1209652|title=Ratusan Mahasiswa Demo Indonesia Gelap di Depan Kantor DRPD Sumsel|website=tempo.co|access-date=20 February 2025}}</ref> They attempted to bring used tires top of a command car, but they were obstructed by forces. After negotiations, they were allowed to do so. However, after failing to meet the speaker of South Sumatra DPRD, Andi Dinialdie, protesters brought down the tires and burned them in front of the building.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://regional.kompas.com/read/2025/02/20/132528478/demo-indonesia-gelap-di-palembang-mahasiswa-bakar-ban-karena-tak-diterima|title=Demo Indonesia Gelap di Palembang, Mahasiswa Bakar Ban Karena Tak Diterima Ketua DPRD Sumsel|website=regional.kompas.com|access-date=20 February 2025 |last1=Media |first1=Kompas Cyber }}</ref> It was previously projected that it would be attended by 1,000 people.<ref name="tribunnews">{{cite web|url=https://palembang.tribunnews.com/2025/02/18/kritisi-kebijakan-prabowo-seribu-mahasiswa-di-palembang-akan-gelar-demo-indonesia-gelap-20-februari|title=Kritisi Kebijakan Prabowo, Seribu Mahasiswa di Palembang Akan Gelar Demo Indonesia Gelap 20 Februari|website=palembang.tribunnews.com|access-date=18 February 2025}}</ref> The protesters also gave an ultimatum of 3 × 24 hours to the government to take position regarding the budget cuts. Then, deputy speaker Ilyas Panji and member of parliament M Nasir met the protesters and agreed to reject budget cuts and to fulfill their demands.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://regional.kompas.com/read/2025/02/20/141130878/demo-indonesia-gelap-di-palembang-dprd-sumsel-diberi-waktu-3x24-jam-untuk|title=Demo "Indonesia Gelap" di Palembang, DPRD Sumsel Diberi Waktu 3X24 Jam untuk Ambil Sikap|website=regional.kompas.com|access-date=18 February 2025}}</ref>

In [[Pangkal Pinang]], students from [[University of Bangka Belitung]] together with Bangka Belitung Students' Union Alliance, protested front of the building of Bangka Belitung DPRD. They arrived at 3:20&nbsp;p.m.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://belitung.tribunnews.com/2025/02/19/ratusan-mahasiswa-ubb-demo-di-dprd-babel-suarakan-kondisi-indonesia-gelap-akibat-kebijakan-prabowo|title=Ratusan Mahasiswa UBB Demo di DPRD Babel Suarakan Kondisi Indonesia Gelap Akibat Kebijakan Prabowo|website=belitung.tribunnews.com|access-date=19 February 2025}}</ref>

In [[Bandar Lampung]], hundreds of students from various local universities protested in front of the Lampung provincial government complex. They stated that they also held protest in Padang Kuas, [[Seluma Regency]] while stating that the student organizations there are consolidating for a bigger protest that will be held later.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Mahasiswa UMB Gelar Aksi, Tolak Efisiensi Program Makan Bergizi Gratis |url=https://www.bengkulunews.co.id/mahasiswa-umb-gelar-aksi-tolak-efisiensi-program-makan-bergizi-gratis |access-date=19 February 2025 |website=www.bengkulunews.co.id |language=id}}</ref>

==== Jakarta ====
[[File:Demonstrasi_Mahasiswa_bertema_Indonesia_Gelap_2025.jpg|thumb|Students from the [[University of Indonesia]] participating in the protest]]
In [[Jakarta]], the protests started on 17 February at Horse Statue, [[Central Jakarta]], but then spread into South Medan Merdeka and West Medan Merdeka Roads, causing traffic jams at [[Bundaran HI]]. Thousands<ref>{{cite web|url=https://wartakota.tribunnews.com/2025/02/17/ribuan-mahasiswa-ui-demo-indonesia-gelap-di-patung-kuda-efisiensi-anggaran-hingga-mbg-jadi-sorotan|title=Ribuan Mahasiswa UI Demo Indonesia Gelap di Patung Kuda, Efisiensi Anggaran hingga MBG Jadi Sorotan|website=wartakota.tribunnews.com|access-date=17 February 2025}}</ref> of protesters shouted {{lang|id|hidup mahasiswa}} ('long live students') and carried various banners and posters that represent their demands, such as {{lang|id|#krisisiklimkrisisdemokrasi}} ('a climate crisis is a democratic crisis') and {{lang|id|kenyang kagak, bego iya}} ('not yet full, stupid yes').<ref name="detik"/>

Previously, student union of the [[University of Indonesia]] (BEM UI) instructed its members to wear black clothing and yellow alma maters, which is characteristic of the university.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tirto.id/info-lokasi-demo-indonesia-gelap-hari-ini-dan-isi-tuntutan-g8nB|title=Info Lokasi Demo Indonesia Gelap Hari Ini dan Isi Tuntutannya|website=tirto.id|access-date=18 February 2025}}</ref> A total of 1,623 joint personnels were deployed for security,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://news.detik.com/berita/d-7781612/bem-si-demo-di-patung-kuda-hari-ini-1-623-personel-dikerahkan|title=BEM SI Demo di Patung Kuda Hari Ini, 1.623 Personel Dikerahkan|website=detik.com|access-date=17 February 2025}}</ref> and traffic at West Medan Merdeka Road towards [[Merdeka Palace]] was closed, except for the [[Transjakarta]] Bus.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://news.detik.com/berita/d-7782115/bem-si-aksi-indonesia-gelap-lalin-patung-kuda-arah-istana-mulai-ditutup|title=BEM SI Aksi 'Indonesia Gelap', Lalin Patung Kuda Arah Istana Mulai Ditutup|website=news.detik.com |access-date=17 February 2025}}</ref>

In [[Yogyakarta]], the Jogja Is Calling Alliance has called students and civilians to participate in the protests. Protesters also condemned Prabowo's response during [[Gerindra]]'s anniversary event on 15 February 2025, where he reportedly said ''"Ndasmu!"'' (a Javanese insult) to critics. Just over 100 days into the new administration, the group claims the government has already caused significant harm, leading them to call for its immediate resignation and structural overhaul.<ref>{{Cite news |author=Pribadi Wicaksono |author2=Nabiila Azzahra |author3=Yuni Rohmawati |author4=Alif Ilham Fajriadi |date=22 February 2025|title=Dark Indonesia Action Continues in Jakarta, Palembang, and Yogyakarta|url=https://www.tempo.co/politik/aksi-indonesia-gelap-terus-menyala-di-jakarta-palembang-dan-yogyakarta-1210565 |work=Tempo |access-date=24 March 2025}}</ref> The protestors would arrive at the Special Region of Yogyakarta DPRD building at 12:15&nbsp;p.m. Not long after, they burned used tires, threw red liquid on the building's gate and stuck a poster with the inscription {{lang|id|Pemerintah Tolol}} ('The Government Is Stupid').<ref>{{cite web |title=Demo Indonesia Gelap di Yogyakarta, Massa Lempas Cat dan Bakar Ban di Depan DPRD DIY |url=https://www.tvonenews.com/daerah/yogyakarta/302404-demo-indonesia-gelap-di-yogyakarta-massa-lempar-cat-dan-bakar-ban-di-depan-dprd-diy |access-date=20 February 2025 |website=tvonenews.com|date=20 February 2025 }}</ref>

==== Censorship and repression ====
The government's reaction has also been marred by censorship, mainly targeting journalists and creative industry workers. It was widely reported that the Indonesian new wave duo Sukatani, who wrote a song criticising the police forces, was intimidated, and was later forced to make an apology video as part of the intimidation. American music critic [[Anthony Fantano]] condemned the repression, describing the situation as being "not good".<ref>{{Cite web |title=Polda Jateng: Kita Klarifikasi Band Sukatani, Hasilnya Kita Hargai |url=https://www.cnnindonesia.com/nasional/20250221165051-32-1201082/polda-jateng-kita-klarifikasi-band-sukatani-hasilnya-kita-hargai |access-date=21 February 2025 |website=CNN Indonesia |language=id-ID}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Kritikus Musik Amerika Tanggapi Pencekalan Sukatani Band |url=https://indopop.id/kritikus-musik-amerika-tanggapi-pencekalan-sukatani-band/ |access-date=21 February 2025 |website=Indopop |language=id-ID}}</ref> [[Amnesty International]] also urged the [[Indonesian National Police]] to investigate the intimidation case.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nasional.sindonews.com/read/1533287/15/amnesty-international-indonesia-desak-polri-ungkap-pengintimidasi-band-sukatani-1740204124|title=Amnesty International Indonesia Desak Polri Ungkap Pengintimidasi Band Sukatani|website=nasional.sindonews.com|access-date=22 February 2025}}</ref> On X, a video surfaced showing a foreign citizen journalist being apprehended by police for "not having a permit".<ref>{{Cite tweet |user=RadioElshinta |number=1892491132568772710 |title=Salah seorang influencers asing tanpa surat tugas peliputan diamanakan pihak kepolisian, Kamis (20/2) siang.}}</ref>

In response to the headlines, two CNN Indonesia journalists were doxed by social media users sympathetic to the protesters, and several others called for a boycott of Trans Media-affiliated outlets.<ref>{{cite web |last= |first= |date=25 February 2025|title=Dua Jurnalis CNN Indonesia Kena Doxing Usai Liput Aksi Indonesia Gelap, Apa Sanksi Bagi Pelakunya |url=https://www.tempo.co/hukum/dua-jurnalis-cnn-indonesia-kena-doxing-usai-liput-aksi-indonesia-gelap-apa-sanksi-bagi-pelakunya-1212008 |trans-title=Two CNN Indonesia Journalists Get Doxed After Covering Indonesia Gelap Action, What Are the Sanctions for the Perpetrators |website= tempo.co |language=id |location= |publisher= |access-date=2 March 2025}}</ref>

=== International ===

HRW also urged the parliament to drop the proposed amendment that would enable military to fill the civilian positions in the judiciary and state-owned enterprises.<ref>{{cite web |last= |first= |date=19 March 2025 |title=Indonesia: Proposed Military Law Amendments Threaten Rights|url=https://www.hrw.org/news/2025/03/19/indonesia-proposed-military-law-amendments-threaten-rights |website= |location= |publisher=Human Rights Watch |access-date=20 March 2025}}</ref>

[[Amnesty International]] via its representative in Indonesia also criticised the passing of military law bill. Amnesty International then explained the danger of the military law that have been passed by the parliament and found misconducts within its passing.<ref>{{cite web |last= |first= |date=17 March 2025 |title=Amnesty International Indonesia Ungkap Kejanggalan Proses Revisi UU TNI |url=https://investortrust.id/national/59876/amnesty-international-indonesia-ungkap-kejanggalan-proses-revisi-uu-tni |website=investortrust.id |location= |publisher= |access-date=21 March 2025}}</ref> Earlier, Amnesty International secretary general [[Agnès Callamard]] visited Indonesia to evaluate the human rights situation in Indonesia.<ref>{{cite web |last= |first= |date=7 March 2025 |title=Sekjen Amnesty Agnes Callamard berkunjung ke Indonesia mengevaluasi situasi HAM dan maraknya praktik-praktik otoriter |url=https://www.amnesty.id/kabar-terbaru/siaran-pers/sekjen-amnesty-agnes-callamard-berkunjung-ke-indonesia-mengevaluasi-situasi-ham-dan-maraknya-praktik-praktik-otoriter/03/2025/ |website= |location= |publisher=Amnesty International |access-date=21 March 2025}}</ref>

[[Reporters Without Borders]] (RSF) through the director of its Asia-Pacific bureau, Cédric Alviani, has also issued condemnation of violence against journalists during Indonesian protests.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://rsf.org/en/indonesia-rsf-condemns-police-violence-against-journalists-covering-protests?s=08|title=Indonesia: RSF condemns police violence against journalists covering protests|website=rsf.org|date=27 March 2025 |access-date=2025-03-28}}</ref> Other international NGOs such as the German and Belgian chapters of [[International Federation of Action by Christians for the Abolition of Torture]] (ACAT), All Arakan Students' & Youths's Congress, Accountability Counsel, and Advocacy Forum-Nepal also published a joint statement condemning the passing of the amendment.<ref>{{Cite tweet |user=KontraSupdates |number=1902586451625447578 |title=Join Statement: 234 International Organizations Say #TolakRUUTNI! }}</ref>

=== Market crash ===
[[File:Jakarta Stock Exchange Composite Index March 18 2025.jpg|thumb|[[Indonesia Stock Exchange]] Composite Index during closing around 9:37 AM on 18 March 2025]]
In March 2025, Indonesia's financial markets faced a severe jolt as the [[Jakarta Composite Index]] (JCI) plunged by over 7% in a single trading session. This sharp [[intraday]] decline—recorded on 18 March—was the steepest since 2011. The plunge triggered an automatic 30-minute trading halt as markets faced a decline of 6.146% during closure at around 9:37&nbsp;AM,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Novalius |first=Feby |date=18 March 2025 |title=IHSG Trading Halt, Perdagangan Saham Dihentikan 30 Menit : Okezone Economy |url=https://economy.okezone.com/read/2025/03/18/278/3123542/ihsg-trading-halt-perdagangan-saham-dihentikan-30-menit |access-date=27 March 2025 |website=Okezone |language=id-ID}}</ref> a mechanism designed to stabilize markets at 5% during periods of excessive volatility. After a 30 minute halt, trading resumed but the index continued to decline, reaching a 7.1% drop at one point. The sharp decline in Indonesia’s stock market quickly rippled across other sectors, with technology and manufacturing companies posting the biggest losses. Shares of {{ill|DCI Indonesia|id}}, a leading provider of data center infrastructure, plunged around 20 percent, falling to 115,800 [[Indonesian rupiah|rupiah]] (approximately $7). Meanwhile, {{ill|Chandra Asri Pacific|id}}, a major player in energy and chemicals, saw its stock price drop nearly 19 percent by the afternoon.<ref>{{Cite web |date=18 March 2025 |title=Indonesia: Stock market plunges 7 percent, trading suspended |url=https://www.agenzianova.com/en/news/Indonesia-Stock-Market-Drops-7-Percent-Trading-Suspended/?utm_source=chatgpt.com |access-date=27 March 2025 |website=Agenzia Nova |language=en}}</ref> During trading, foreign investors made a net sell of IDR 2.48 trillion, continuing the trend of outflows totalling IDR 29.41 trillion since the beginning of the year. Foreign ownership in the JCI also fell to 2.9%, the lowest since 2011, reflecting a decline in global interest in the Indonesian market. This contrasted with other Asian bourses that rallied.<ref name=":0">{{cite news |last=Nasition |first=Edy Irawan |date=22 March 2025 |title=Indonesia Gelap! IHSG Ambles dan Trading Halt Diberlakukan Lagi |url=https://emitennews.com/news/indonesia-gelap-ihsg-ambles-dan-trading-halt-diberlakukan-lagi |access-date=5 April 2025 |work=EmitenNews}}</ref>  The negative market sentiment was fuelled by concerns over national economic conditions. The sudden downturn reflected a deepening crisis of investor confidence, rooted not only in macroeconomic uncertainties but also in political anxieties and shifting fiscal policies under President Prabowo Subianto’s administration.<ref>{{Cite news |last1=Mariska |first1=Diana |last2=Lakshmi |first2=A. Anantha |date=18 March 2025 |title=Indonesian stocks tumble 4% on concerns over economy |url=https://www.ft.com/content/aae80518-0e3b-4d79-a2a3-339dfa162478?utm_source=chatgpt.com |access-date=27 March 2025 |work=Financial Times}}</ref> In February alone, the state budget (APBN) recorded a deficit of IDR 31.2 trillion, while the [[consumer confidence index]] and [[Average propensity to save|savings ratio]] fell, indicating weak purchasing power. Layoffs increased, with major factories such as [[Yamaha Motor Company|Yamaha]] and PT [[:id:Sanken_Indonesia|Sanken Indonesia]] closing. Tax revenue also weakened, with Income Tax 21 falling 39.5% YoY and IPR declining by 0.5% signalling consumption pressure. Systemic risk looms over the capital market, given the potential impact on the financial sector.<ref name=":0" /> 

Although [[Macroeconomics|macroeconomic]] factors and controversial [[Fiscal policy|fiscal policies]] of the Prabowo Subianto administration were the main culprits, public discourse and political debates also highlighted the revision of the TNI Law (RUU TNI) as a factor that worsened market sentiment.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Hadi |first=Aditya |date=18 March 2025 |title=Indonesia's stock market plunges while others shine – Markets |url=https://www.thejakartapost.com/business/2025/03/18/indonesias-stock-market-plunges-while-others-shine.html#:~:text=Analysts%20suggest%20a%20downturn%20of%20the%20Indonesia%20Stock,sense%20of%20uncertainty%20rather%20than%20by%20fundamental%20weakness. |access-date=27 March 2025 |website=The Jakarta Post |language=en}}</ref> According to a [[Tempo (Indonesian magazine)|Tempo]] report, economist from CELIOS, Nailul Huda, stated that the decline in the JCI has been occurring since 14 March, and the TNI bill is one of the triggers. He highlighted concerns that the military's expanded role in the civilian and economic spheres could lead to legal uncertainty, erode [[meritocracy]], and cause investor fear. One example is the appointment of an active military officer as President Director of [[Perum Bulog]], which fuelled perceptions of military intervention in the business sector.<ref>{{Cite web |date=18 March 2025 |title=IHSG Anjlok, Ada Dampak dari RUU TNI? |url=https://www.tempo.co/ekonomi/ihsg-anjlok-ada-dampak-dari-ruu-tni--1221130 |access-date=27 March 2025 |website=Tempo |language=id}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Scholastica |first=Clara Anna |date=20 March 2025 |title=Celios: IHSG dan Rupiah Kompak Terjun Bebas Gara-gara Pengesahan RUU TNI |url=https://www.inilah.com/celios-ihsg-dan-rupiah-kompak-terjun-bebas-gara-gara-pengesahan-ruu-tni |access-date=29 March 2025 |work=Inilah}}</ref> Deputy Chairman of Commission I of the House of Representatives [[Budi Djiwandono]] immediately reassured and denied that the TNI Bill was the direct cause of the JCI crash, asserting that there are no articles in the revised draft that explicitly regulate the placement of active TNI officers in state-owned enterprises.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Shafira |first=Monique Handa |date=19 March 2025 |title=DPR Bantah RUU TNI Bikin IHSG Anjlok Parah |url=https://www.beritasatu.com/ekonomi/2878220/dpr-bantah-ruu-tni-bikin-ihsg-anjlok-parah |access-date=29 March 2025 |work=Beritasatu}}</ref> On the other hand, economists such as [[Wijayanto Samirin]] cited the revision of the TNI Law as one of the five factors that caused the market crash, citing public concerns over the potential return of ABRI's dual function. This view reflects the market's sensitivity to the issue of militarisation, even if only as a perception.<ref>{{Cite news |title=DPR Bantah RUU TNI Jadi Sentimen IHSG Anjlok |url=https://www.cnnindonesia.com/ekonomi/20250319121149-92-1210610/dpr-bantah-ruu-tni-jadi-sentimen-ihsg-anjlok |archive-url=http://web.archive.org/web/20250322001448/https://www.cnnindonesia.com/ekonomi/20250319121149-92-1210610/dpr-bantah-ruu-tni-jadi-sentimen-ihsg-anjlok? |archive-date=22 March 2025 |access-date=27 March 2025 |work=CNN Indonesia |language=id-ID}}</ref>

The response from Indonesian authorities was swift but indicative of broader systemic stress. The [[Financial Services Authority (Indonesia)|Financial Services Authority]] (OJK) introduced temporary measures allowing publicly listed firms to buy back shares without requiring prior shareholder approval—a move aimed at supporting valuations and signaling confidence. Concurrently, [[Bank Indonesia]] intervened in the foreign exchange market to stabilize the rupiah, deploying a combination of rate tools and reserves. These interventions did moderate the pace of capital outflows but did little to resolve the underlying concerns surrounding fiscal direction and political stability.<ref>{{Cite news |last1=Sulaiman |first1=Stefanno |last2=Suroyo |first2=Gayatri |date=19 March 2025 |title=Indonesia takes currency, market measures after rupiah and shares fall |url=https://www.reuters.com/markets/asia/indonesia-takes-currency-stock-market-measures-after-rupiah-index-fall-2025-03-19/?utm_source=chatgpt.com |access-date=29 March 2025 |work=Reuters}}</ref> 

Amidst US President [[Donald Trump]]'s [[Tariffs in the second Trump administration|reciprocal import duty policy]] has the potential to trigger foreign capital outflows from Indonesia, putting pressure on the rupiah exchange rate and stock market. Economist Bhima Yudhistira of Celios highlighted that this threat is exacerbated by proposed legislation such as the currently protested Police Bill and the Criminal Procedure Code (KUHAP) Bill, which may contribute to legal uncertainty and investor anxiety. He warned that these overlapping pressures could result in a trading halt in the stock market following the ''[[Lebaran]]'' holiday. Furthermore, the depreciation of the rupiah could drive up the cost of imported goods, particularly essentials and electronics, thereby triggering imported inflation and reducing the public’s purchasing power.<ref>{{cite news |date=3 April 2025 |title=Kebijakan Tarif Trump Bisa Bikin IHSG Trading Halt Lagi? Simak Penjelasannya |url=https://money.kompas.com/read/2025/04/03/134442026/kebijakan-tarif-trump-bisa-bikin-ihsg-trading-halt-lagi-simak-penjelasannya?page=all |access-date=5 April 2025 |work=Kompas.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Avisena |first=M Ilham Ramadhan |date=3 April 2025 |title=Ekonomi Indonesia Terancam Resesi Gegara Tarif Resiprokal Trump |url=https://www.metrotvnews.com/read/KXyCQYjD-ekonomi-indonesia-terancam-resesi-gegara-tarif-resiprokal-trump |access-date=5 April 2025 |work=Metro TV News}}</ref>
