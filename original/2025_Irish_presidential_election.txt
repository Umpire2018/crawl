The '''2025 Irish presidential election''' must take place by Tuesday, 11 November 2025.<ref>{{cite web |author=<!-- not stated --> |date= |title=Presidential elections|url=https://www.electoralcommission.ie/presidential-elections/|website= |location= |publisher=[[Electoral Commission (Ireland)|Electoral Commission]]|access-date=18 March 2025|quote=Michael D. Higgins, the current President was inaugurated for his second term on the 11 November 2018. This means that the next presidential election is scheduled to take place on or up to 60 days before 11 November 2025.}}</ref> The incumbent [[President of Ireland|president]], [[Michael D. Higgins]], is [[term limit]]ed, having served the maximum two terms permitted under the [[Constitution of Ireland]]. He was elected in [[2011 Irish presidential election|2011]] with the support of the [[Labour Party (Ireland)|Labour Party]], and in [[2018 Irish presidential election|2018]] on his own nomination as an [[Independent politician (Ireland)|independent]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.presidentialelection.ie/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/Public-Notice-Nomination-of-Candidates-24-September-2018-1.pdf|website=Presidential Election 2018|title=Public Notice Presidential Election 2018 Nomination of Candidates|date=24 September 2018|access-date=28 September 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180928200713/https://www.presidentialelection.ie/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/Public-Notice-Nomination-of-Candidates-24-September-2018-1.pdf|archive-date=28 September 2018|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

==Procedure==
{{Main|Irish presidential election}}
To stand for election as [[president of Ireland]], candidates must:
*be a citizen of Ireland
*be at least 35 years of age
*be nominated by:
**at least twenty of the 234 serving members of the Houses of the [[Oireachtas]], or
**at least four of the 31 [[Local government in the Republic of Ireland|county or city council]]s, or
**themselves, in the case of a former or retiring president who has served one 7-year term.

If a member of the Oireachtas or a County or City council nominate more than one candidate, only the first nomination paper received from them will be deemed valid.<ref name=1993act/> Presidential elections are conducted in line with Article 12 of the [[Constitution of Ireland|Constitution]]<ref name=art12>Article 12 of the [[Constitution of Ireland]]. {{cite web|url=http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/cons/en/html|title=Constitution of Ireland|date=October 2015|website=[[Irish Statute Book]]|publisher=[[Attorney General of Ireland]]|access-date=16 September 2017|archive-date=3 May 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190503055502/http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/cons/en/html|url-status=live}}</ref> and under the Presidential Elections Act 1993.<ref name=1993act>{{cite web|url=https://www.presidentialelection.ie/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/1993-Presidential-Elections-Act-1993-Consolidated-January-2016.pdf|title=Presidential Elections Act 1993|website=Irish Presidential Election|publisher=Presidential Returning Officer|access-date=21 September 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180921153354/https://www.presidentialelection.ie/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/1993-Presidential-Elections-Act-1993-Consolidated-January-2016.pdf|archive-date=21 September 2018|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> The [[President of Ireland|president]] is elected through [[instant-runoff voting]] (described in the constitution as [[proportional representation]] by means of the [[single transferable vote]]).<ref>{{cite news |last=Power |first=Jack |date=25 October 2018 |title=Presidential election: Everything you need to know before voting |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/news/politics/presidential-election-everything-you-need-to-know-before-voting-1.3675937 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181026080400/https://www.irishtimes.com/news/politics/presidential-election-everything-you-need-to-know-before-voting-1.3675937 |archive-date=26 October 2018 |access-date=28 October 2018 |newspaper=[[The Irish Times]]}}</ref> All [[Irish citizen]]s entered on the current [[Electoral roll|electoral register]] are eligible to vote.<ref name=art12/> Under the Electoral (Amendment) Act 2011, there is a spending limit by each candidate of €750,000.<ref>{{Cite ISB|title=Electoral (Amendment) Act 2011|year=2011|number=14|access-date=2 March 2025|date=25 July 2011|section=6|stitle=Limitation of presidential election expenses.}}</ref> Candidates who are elected or who reach 12.5% of the vote on their elimination are entitled to a reimbursement of expenses up to €200,000.<ref>{{Cite ISB|title=Electoral (Amendment) Act 2011|year=2011|number=14|access-date=2 March 2025|date=25 July 2011|section=5|stitle=Reimbursement of expenses at presidential elections.}}</ref>
