The next parliamentary elections in [[Moldova]] are to be held on 28 September 2025.<ref>{{Cite web |date=16 Apr 2025 |title=Alegerile parlamentare vor avea loc pe 28 septembrie |url=https://tribuna.md/2025/04/16/alegerile-parlamentare-vor-avea-loc-pe-28-septembrie/ |access-date=16 Apr 2025 |website=tribuna.md}}</ref>

== Background ==
The [[2021 Moldovan parliamentary election|last parliamentary election]] in Moldova was held on 11 July 2021, resulting in a landslide victory for the [[Pro-Europeanism|pro-European]] [[Party of Action and Solidarity]] (PAS), led by President [[Maia Sandu]]. PAS secured 63 out of 101 seats, allowing it to form a single-party majority government. The election marked a significant shift towards [[Moldova–European Union relations|European integration]], as PAS defeated the [[Bloc of Communists and Socialists]], a [[pro-Russian]] electoral alliance.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Pro-Western party wins Moldova election, preliminary data shows |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/moldovans-elect-new-parliament-amid-concerns-over-graft-stalled-reforms-2021-07-11/ |archive-url=http://web.archive.org/web/20231117133610/https://www.reuters.com/world/moldovans-elect-new-parliament-amid-concerns-over-graft-stalled-reforms-2021-07-11/ |archive-date=2023-11-17 |access-date=2025-02-20 |work=Reuters |language=en-US}}</ref>

Since coming to power, the [[Party of Action and Solidarity|PAS]]-led government has pursued [[anti-corruption]] measures, judicial reforms, and [[European Union]] integration.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Moldova: Start of Formal Accession Negotiations with the EU |url=https://dgap.org/en/research/publications/moldova-start-formal-accession-negotiations-eu |access-date=2025-02-20 |website=DGAP }}</ref> Pro-Russian opposition forces have organized protests against the government’s economic policies.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-02-20 |title=Pro-Russia protesters take to streets in Moldova to denounce new government |url=https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20230220-pro-russia-protesters-take-to-streets-in-moldova |access-date=2025-02-20 |website=France 24 |language=en}}</ref>

In the [[2024 Moldovan presidential election|2024 presidential election]], incumbent Maia Sandu was re-elected with 55.3% of the vote, defeating pro-Russian candidate Alexandr Stoianoglo.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Balmforth |first=Tom |last2=Tanas |first2=Alexander |date=November 4, 2024 |title=Moldova's pro-Western Sandu claims election win after meddling allegations |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/moldova-holds-presidential-runoff-election-amid-claims-russian-meddling-2024-11-03/ |website=Reuters}}</ref> The election was overshadowed by allegations of Russian interference, with authorities seizing illegal propaganda materials.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Luxmoore |first=Matthew |date=October 21, 2024 |title=In Moldova, a Pro-Russian Oligarch With a Criminal Past Tries to Derail Path to Europe |url=https://www.wsj.com/world/europe/in-moldova-a-pro-russian-oligarch-with-a-criminal-past-tries-to-derail-path-to-europe-a000bf12? |website=The Wall Street Journal}}</ref> Alongside the election, Moldova held a [[2024 Moldovan European Union membership referendum|referendum on EU integration]], which narrowly passed with 50.4% support.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2024-10-21 |title=Moldova narrowly votes for EU membership amid claims of 'unprecedented' Russian interference |url=https://www.abc.net.au/news/2024-10-22/moldova-votes-for-eu-membership-amid-russian-meddling-claims/104500056 |access-date=2025-02-20 |work=ABC News |language=en-AU}}</ref>

Moldova is grappling with major security and economic challenges, notably including energy security issues due to disrupted [[Russia in the European energy sector|gas supplies]] from Russia.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-12-31 |title='No heating, no light': Moldovans fear looming energy shortage as Russia halts gas supplies |url=https://apnews.com/article/moldova-energy-crisis-russia-transnistria-gas-europe-3cb90f12ad5274bda67f99a1c4619abf |access-date=2025-02-20 |website=AP News |language=en}}</ref> The [[Russian invasion of Ukraine|2022 Russian invasion]] of neighboring Ukraine has  triggering a massive influx of refugees into Moldova.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Balmforth |first=Tom |last2=Tanas |first2=Alexander |date=November 4, 2024 |title=Moldova's Sandu promises to bring change after narrow runoff win |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/moldovas-sandu-says-she-will-press-change-after-narrow-runoff-win-2024-11-04/ |website=Reuters}}</ref> These difficulties have amplified the significance of the 2025 parliamentary elections; for President Sandu, according to [[Euronews]], the election would be "a final battle" on Moldova's road to [[European Union|EU]] membership.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-12-31 |title=From Germany to Romania: Elections that will define Europe in 2025 |url=https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2024/12/31/from-germany-to-romania-the-elections-that-will-define-europe-in-2025 |access-date=2025-02-20 |website=euronews |language=en}}</ref>

==Electoral system==
The 101 members of [[Parliament of Moldova|Parliament]] are elected by [[party-list proportional representation]] in a single nationwide constituency. The electoral threshold at the national level varies according to the type of list; for parties or individual organizations it is 5%; for an electoral bloc it is 7%. For independent candidates, the threshold is 2%.<ref>{{Cite web |title=CE325/2022 |url=https://www.legis.md/cautare/getResults?doc_id=134589&lang=ro |archive-url=http://web.archive.org/web/20240913105927/https://www.legis.md/cautare/getResults?doc_id=134589&lang=ro |archive-date=2024-09-13 |access-date=2025-04-16 |website=www.legis.md |language=ro}}</ref>

== Campaign ==
According to Moldova’s intelligence chief, Alexandru Musteata, the election is expected to face significant interference from [[Russia]]. He warned that Moscow is likely to employ the tactics used during the [[2024 Moldovan presidential election|2024 presidential election]] and the [[2024 Moldovan European Union membership referendum|EU referendum]], including disinformation campaigns, financial support for [[Russophilia|pro-Russian]] parties, and other covert efforts to destabilize Moldova’s democratic process. The Moldovan government has expressed its determination to counter these threats.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-12-13 |title=Moldovan spy chief: Russia will try to interfere in 2025 parliamentary election, too |url=https://www.politico.eu/article/moldovan-spy-chief-russia-will-try-interfere-parliamentary-elections-next-year-alexandru-musteata/ |access-date=2025-02-21 |website=POLITICO |language=en-GB}}</ref>

Moldovan opposition leader [[Alexandr Stoianoglo]], who lost the [[2024 Moldovan presidential election|2024 presidential election]] to President [[Maia Sandu]], announced{{when|date=April 2025}} the formation of a new coalition to challenge the [[pro-Western]] ruling majority. The coalition, called [[Alternative (political bloc)|Alternative]], unites three political parties led by Chisinau Mayor [[Ion Ceban]], former Prime Minister [[Ion Chicu]], and former lawmaker [[Mark Tkachuk]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |last=Tanas |first=Alexander |date=February 1, 2025 |title=Moldovan opposition leader forms coalition to challenge Sandu at parliament election |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/moldovan-opposition-leader-forms-coalition-challenge-sandu-parliament-election-2025-01-31/ |website=Reuters}}</ref>

While Stoianoglo campaigns on a foreign policy that includes both Russia and the West, he has leaned toward [[Russophilia|Russia]], with his past presidential candidacy backed by the pro-Moscow [[Party of Socialists of the Republic of Moldova|PSRM]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-10-30 |title=Sandu and Stoianoglo face off in crucial Moldova presidential runoff |url=https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2024/10/30/moldovas-presidential-race-pro-eu-and-russia-friendly-candidates-face-off-in-crucial-runof |access-date=2025-02-21 |website=euronews |language=en}}</ref> Meanwhile, tensions between [[Moldova]] and [[Russia]] remain high, with accusations of Russia attempting to interfere in past elections and of worsening Moldova's [[2025 Moldovan energy crisis|energy crisis]].<ref name=":1" />
