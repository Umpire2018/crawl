Presidential elections will be held in [[Poland]] on {{date|2025-05-18|DMY}}. If no candidate secures more than 50% of the vote, a second round will be held on {{date|2025-06-01|DMY}}.<ref name="PAP-20250108">{{Cite news |title=Marszałek Sejmu ogłosił datę wyborów prezydenckich |date={{date|2025-01-08|DMY}} |url=https://www.pap.pl/aktualnosci/marszalek-sejmu-oglosil-date-wyborow-prezydenckich |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/20250112003134/https://www.pap.pl/aktualnosci/marszalek-sejmu-oglosil-date-wyborow-prezydenckich |archive-date={{date|2025-01-12|DMY}} |access-date={{date|2025-01-12|DMY}} |work=[[Polish Press Agency|Polska Agencja Prasowa]] |trans-title=Marshal of the Sejm announced the presidential election day |language=pl}}</ref> Incumbent president [[Andrzej Duda]] is ineligible for re-election.

== Electoral system ==
The president is elected for a five-year term using the [[two-round system]]; if no candidate receives a majority of the vote in the first round, a run-off is held between the top two candidates. Presidents serve a five-year term and can be re-elected once. The second term of [[Andrzej Duda]] expires on {{date|2025-08-06|DMY}}, and the president-elect will take the oath of office on that day, before the [[National Assembly (Poland)|National Assembly]] (a joint session of [[Sejm]] and [[Senate of Poland|Senate]]).

== Background ==
=== Tusk's cabinet ===
In December 2023 as a result of the [[2023 Polish parliamentary election]], [[Third Cabinet of Donald Tusk|Donald Tusk's cabinet]], comprising of [[Civic Coalition (Poland)|Civic Coalition]], [[Poland 2050]], the [[Polish People's Party]], and the [[New Left (Poland)|New Left]], began governing the country. Tusk's coalition does not have enough votes to bypass the presidential veto, for which it would need 276 votes.<ref>{{cite news |date=2023-10-17 |title=Ile głosów potrzeba, by odrzucić weto prezydenta? Opozycja ociera się o tę liczbę |url=https://wiadomosci.onet.pl/kraj/ile-glosow-potrzeba-aby-odrzucic-weto-prezydenta-wyniki-wyborow-2023/ccj1plz |access-date=2024-12-14}}</ref>

==== Economic policy ====
Since Tusk's rise to power, steps have been taken to [[deregulation|deregulate the economy]], cut [[welfare spending]] and [[balanced budget|balance the budget]]. In February 2025, Tusk invited billionaire [[Rafał Brzoska]] and [[Google]] CEO [[Sundar Pichai]]<ref>{{cite news |date=2025-02-13 |title=Tusk: Google może być częścią projektu deregulacji w Europie i Polsce |url=https://www.gazetaprawna.pl/wiadomosci/kraj/artykuly/9738129,tusk-google-moze-byc-czescia-projektu-deregulacji-w-europie-i-polsce.html |access-date=2025-02-13}}</ref> to deregulate the Polish economy and cut [[labor rights|labor regulations]]. Tusk's proposal of Brzoska has led to media labelling him as the "Polish [[Elon Musk]]".<ref>{{cite news |date=2025-02-13 |title=Jaki kraj, taki Elon Musk, czyli Tuskowy trumpizm na miarę naszych możliwości |url=https://oko.press/jaki-kraj-taki-elon-musk-czyli-tuskowy-trumpizm-na-miare-naszych-mozliwosci |access-date=2025-02-13}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=2025-02-10 |title="Polski Elon Musk", trzy razy słowo na "i" oraz energetyka. Co powiedział Donald Tusk? |url=https://next.gazeta.pl/next/7,151003,31680453,polski-elon-musk-trzy-razy-slowo-na-i-oraz-energetyka.html |access-date=2025-02-13}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=2025-02-10 |title=Rafał Brzoska jak Elon Musk wesprze rząd w deregulacji gospodarki. Donald Tusk mówi Żabką, chce uwolnić czas i zasoby przedsiębiorców |url=https://www.wiadomoscihandlowe.pl/gospodarka-i-finanse/donald-tusk-zarzadza-wielka-deregulacje-polskiego-biznesu-wystosowal-prosbe-do-rafala-brzoski-prosze-sie-za-to-wziac-2523395 |access-date=2025-02-13}}</ref> Tusk has been accused of granting Google a [[monopoly]] over the [[Artificial Intelligence]] sector in Poland via his investment agreements with Pichai.<ref>{{cite news |date=2025-02-13 |title=Tusk ogłasza sukces. A naprawdę? ==== Social policy ====
The social policy of Tusk's cabinet has been seeking to expand [[Abortion-rights movement|abortion rights]]. In July 2024, the Sejm held a vote to decriminalize abortion. However, with a defection of a large section of the [[Polish People's Party]] against the government line, the vote failed and the Sejm ruled to keep abortion illegal, with 218 votes against and 215 for decriminalization.<ref>{{cite news |date=2024-07-12 |title=Koalicja rządząca przegrała głosowanie w sprawie aborcji. Kto jak głosował? |url=https://www.pap.pl/aktualnosci/koalicja-rzadzaca-przegrala-glosowanie-w-sprawie-aborcji-kto-jak-glosowal |access-date=2025-02-13}}</ref>

== Campaign ==
=== First round ===
Sławomir Mentzen was the first candidate to begin an electoral campaign on 31 August 2024, drawing criticism and accusations of illegality from politicians of other parties for its early start.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2024-09-03 |title=Mentzen ruszył z kampanią,"łamiąc prawo"?
