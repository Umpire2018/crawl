Presidential elections will be held in [[Poland]] on {{date|2025-05-18|DMY}}. If no candidate secures more than 50% of the vote, a second round will be held on {{date|2025-06-01|DMY}}.<ref name="PAP-20250108">{{Cite news |title=Marsza≈Çek Sejmu og≈Çosi≈Ç datƒô wybor√≥w prezydenckich |date={{date|2025-01-08|DMY}} |url=https://www.pap.pl/aktualnosci/marszalek-sejmu-oglosil-date-wyborow-prezydenckich |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/20250112003134/https://www.pap.pl/aktualnosci/marszalek-sejmu-oglosil-date-wyborow-prezydenckich |archive-date={{date|2025-01-12|DMY}} |access-date={{date|2025-01-12|DMY}} |work=[[Polish Press Agency|Polska Agencja Prasowa]] |trans-title=Marshal of the Sejm announced the presidential election day |language=pl}}</ref> Incumbent president [[Andrzej Duda]] is ineligible for re-election.

== Electoral system ==
The president is elected for a five-year term using the [[two-round system]]; if no candidate receives a majority of the vote in the first round, a run-off is held between the top two candidates. Presidents serve a five-year term and can be re-elected once. The second term of [[Andrzej Duda]] expires on {{date|2025-08-06|DMY}}, and the president-elect will take the oath of office on that day, before the [[National Assembly (Poland)|National Assembly]] (a joint session of [[Sejm]] and [[Senate of Poland|Senate]]).

== Background ==
=== Tusk's cabinet ===
In December 2023 as a result of the [[2023 Polish parliamentary election]], [[Third Cabinet of Donald Tusk|Donald Tusk's cabinet]], comprising of [[Civic Coalition (Poland)|Civic Coalition]], [[Poland 2050]], the [[Polish People's Party]], and the [[New Left (Poland)|New Left]], began governing the country. Tusk's coalition does not have enough votes to bypass the presidential veto, for which it would need 276 votes.<ref>{{cite news |date=2023-10-17 |title=Ile g≈Ços√≥w potrzeba, by odrzuciƒá weto prezydenta? Opozycja ociera siƒô o tƒô liczbƒô |url=https://wiadomosci.onet.pl/kraj/ile-glosow-potrzeba-aby-odrzucic-weto-prezydenta-wyniki-wyborow-2023/ccj1plz |access-date=2024-12-14}}</ref>

==== Economic policy ====
Since Tusk's rise to power, steps have been taken to [[deregulation|deregulate the economy]], cut [[welfare spending]] and [[balanced budget|balance the budget]]. In February 2025, Tusk invited billionaire [[Rafa≈Ç Brzoska]] and [[Google]] CEO [[Sundar Pichai]]<ref>{{cite news |date=2025-02-13 |title=Tusk: Google mo≈ºe byƒá czƒô≈õciƒÖ projektu deregulacji w Europie i Polsce |url=https://www.gazetaprawna.pl/wiadomosci/kraj/artykuly/9738129,tusk-google-moze-byc-czescia-projektu-deregulacji-w-europie-i-polsce.html |access-date=2025-02-13}}</ref> to deregulate the Polish economy and cut [[labor rights|labor regulations]]. Tusk's proposal of Brzoska has led to media labelling him as the "Polish [[Elon Musk]]".<ref>{{cite news |date=2025-02-13 |title=Jaki kraj, taki Elon Musk, czyli Tuskowy trumpizm na miarƒô naszych mo≈ºliwo≈õci |url=https://oko.press/jaki-kraj-taki-elon-musk-czyli-tuskowy-trumpizm-na-miare-naszych-mozliwosci |access-date=2025-02-13}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=2025-02-10 |title="Polski Elon Musk", trzy razy s≈Çowo na "i" oraz energetyka. ==== Social policy ====
The social policy of Tusk's cabinet has been seeking to expand [[Abortion-rights movement|abortion rights]]. In July 2024, the Sejm held a vote to decriminalize abortion. However, with a defection of a large section of the [[Polish People's Party]] against the government line, the vote failed and the Sejm ruled to keep abortion illegal, with 218 votes against and 215 for decriminalization.<ref>{{cite news |date=2024-07-12 |title=Koalicja rzƒÖdzƒÖca przegra≈Ça g≈Çosowanie w sprawie aborcji.
