A [[Snap election|snap legislative election]] will take place in [[Portugal]] on 18 May 2025<ref name="Data">{{cite news |last1=Pinto |first1=Salomé |last2= |first2= |date=13 March 2025 |url=https://eco.sapo.pt/2025/03/13/e-oficial-marcelo-marca-eleicoes-para-18-de-maio/|title=É oficial. Marcelo marca eleições para 18 de maio |language=pt |newspaper=Eco|location=Lisbon |access-date=13 March 2025}}</ref> to elect members of the [[Assembly of the Republic (Portugal)|Assembly of the Republic]] to the 17th Legislature. All 230 seats to the Assembly of the Republic will be at stake.

Following accusations and allegations of alleged [[Conflict of interest|conflicts of interest]] in relation to the Prime Minister's family business,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Portugal cabinet to study conflict of interest allegations against PM |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/portugal-cabinet-study-conflict-interest-allegations-against-pm-2025-02-28/ |access-date=14 March 2025 |date=28 February 2025 |website=Reuters |language=en }}</ref> the incumbent government called a confidence vote, which it lost on 11 March 2025.<ref name="CNN">{{Cite web |title=Moção de confiança rejeitada. Governo de Montenegro cai |url=https://cnnportugal.iol.pt/mocao-de-confianca/chumbada/mocao-de-confianca-chumbada-governo-de-montenegro-cai/20250311/67d09465d34ef72ee4434fc6 |access-date=11 March 2025 |date=11 March 2025 |website=CNN Portugal |language=pt }}</ref> The [[President of Portugal|President]], [[Marcelo Rebelo de Sousa]], then called an election for 18 May, making this the third legislative election in less than four years.<ref name="Data" />

== Background ==
The [[Democratic Alliance (Portugal, 2024)|Democratic Alliance]] (AD), then composed by the [[Social Democratic Party (Portugal)|Social Democratic Party]] (PSD), the [[CDS – People's Party]] (CDS–PP) and the [[People's Monarchist Party (Portugal)|People's Monarchist Party]] (PPM), and led by PSD leader [[Luís Montenegro]], won the [[2024 Portuguese legislative election|2024 legislative election]] by a very narrow margin, winning almost 29 percent of the votes and 80 seats in the 230 seat [[Assembly of the Republic (Portugal)|Assembly of the Republic]]. The [[Socialist Party (Portugal)|Socialist Party]] (PS), in power between 2015 and 2024 and led by [[Pedro Nuno Santos]], elected in the aftermath of the resignation of then [[Prime Minister of Portugal|Prime Minister]] [[António Costa]] due to an investigation around alleged corruption involving the award of contracts for lithium and hydrogen businesses,<ref>{{Cite web |title=António Costa demite-se: "Obviamente" |url=https://cnnportugal.iol.pt/antonio-costa/governo/antonio-costa-apresenta-a-demissao/20231107/654a3b7fd34e65afa2f7496e |access-date=7 November 2023 |website=CNN Portugal |language=pt |archive-date=12 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231112104825/https://cnnportugal.iol.pt/antonio-costa/governo/antonio-costa-apresenta-a-demissao/20231107/654a3b7fd34e65afa2f7496e |url-status=live }}</ref> saw a significant decrease in support, winning only 28 percent of the vote and 78 seats. The populist and [[Far-right politics|far-right]] party [[Chega (political party)|Chega]] (CH) surged in the elections, gathering 18 percent of the votes and 50 seats in Parliament, becoming  [[kingmaker]] with the best result for a third party in decades.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Chega é um dos grandes vencedores destas eleições |url=https://www.rtp.pt/noticias/politica/chega-e-um-dos-grandes-vencedores-destas-eleicoes_v1556990 |access-date=2024-03-28|date=2024-03-11 |website=RTP}}</ref> The [[Liberal Initiative]] (IL) was able to hold on to its eight seats and gather five percent of the vote. The left-wing/far-left parties, the [[Portuguese Communist Party]] (PCP) and the [[Left Bloc (Portugal)|Left Bloc]] (BE), achieved, again, disappointing results, with BE holding on to its five seats and four percent of the votes, while the Communists' alliance got its worst result ever with just three percent of the votes and four seats. [[LIVRE]] nearly surpassed PCP by gathering also three percent of the votes and four seats. [[People Animals Nature]] (PAN) was able to win just one seat.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Diário da República, 1.ª série, n.º 59-A/2024 |url=https://diariodarepublica.pt/dr/detalhe/diario-republica/59-a-2024-857147314 |access-date=2024-03-23 |website=diariodarepublica.pt}}</ref>

Eleven days after election day, on 21 March 2024, Luis Montenegro was asked by [[President of Portugal|President of the Republic]] [[Marcelo Rebelo de Sousa]] to form a government, a minority one in this case.<ref name=":1" >{{Cite web |date=21 March 2024 |title=Portugal's centre-right leader Luis Montenegro appointed prime minister |url=https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240321-portugal-s-centre-right-leader-luis-montenegro-appointed-prime-minister |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240321051332/https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20240321-portugal-s-centre-right-leader-luis-montenegro-appointed-prime-minister |archive-date=21 March 2024 |access-date=21 March 2024 |website=France 24 |language=en}}</ref> The new government was sworn into office on 2 April 2024.<ref name=":1" />

=== 2025 budget crisis ===
{{See also|2024 Portuguese legislative election#2025 budget crisis}}
With the lack of a workable majority, the AD minority government was forced to negotiate with opposition parties to pass major legislation and this created problems regarding the prospects of a budget for 2025.<ref>{{Cite web |date=29 September 2024 |title=Marcelo aumenta pressão: "Um Governo que está pendurado por um fio fraco, que é uma maioria fraca, sem orçamento fica pendurado por linhas" 2024 |website=Observador |language=pt}}</ref> The Government decided to negotiate with the Socialist Party (PS),<ref>{{Cite web |date=11 September 2024 |title=Governo e PS tentam aproximar posições sobre o Orçamento do Estado para 2025  |url=https://eco.sapo.pt/2024/09/11/governo-e-ps-tentam-aproximar-posicoes-sobre-o-orcamento-do-estado-para-2025/ |access-date=30 September 2024 |website=ECO |language=pt}}</ref> however, the odds of a positive outcome from these negotiations were slim as the PS rejected the [[corporate tax]] cuts and the proposed "Youth IRS" scheme, which would provide an [[income tax]] rate cut for young people under the age of 35, and accused the government of not giving in.<ref>{{Cite web |date=27 September 2024 |title=PS só viabiliza Orçamento para 2025 sem IRS Jovem e IRC do Governo |url=https://pt.euronews.com/my-europe/2024/09/27/ps-so-viabiliza-orcamento-para-2025-sem-irs-jovem-e-irc-do-governo |access-date=30 September 2024 |website=[[Euronews]] |language=pt}}</ref> President Marcelo Rebelo de Sousa warned that a lack of a deal could lead to snap legislative elections,<ref>{{Cite web |date=19 September 2024 |title=Marcelo deve avançar para Marcelo volta a instar Governo e PS a entenderem-se no Orçamento |url=https://www.rtp.pt/noticias/economia/estou-a-fazer-pressao-marcelo-volta-a-instar-governo-e-ps-a-entenderem-se-no-orcamento_n1603463 |access-date=30 September 2024 |website=RTP |language=pt}}</ref>

On 3 October 2024, [[Luís Montenegro]] "dropped" several parts of his government's Youth IRS scheme and corporate tax cuts by bringing his new proposals closer to policies defended by the PS, calling it an "irrefutable proposal" for the Socialists.<ref>{{Cite web |date=3 October 2024 |title=A "proposta irrecusável" de Montenegro: IRC com recuo de 1%, IRS Jovem custa 645 milhões de euros |url=https://www.tsf.pt/4141384549/a-proposta-irrecusavel-de-montenegro-irc-com-recuo-de-1-irs-jovem-com-custo-de-645-milhoes-de-euros/ |access-date=9 October 2024 |website=TSF |language=pt}}</ref> [[Pedro Nuno Santos]] recognized the concessions made by the government, but pressed for more conditions on corporate tax cuts, mainly on their timing.<ref>{{Cite web |date=4 October 2024 |title=OE2025: Pedro Nuno Santos reforça que "estamos a caminho da solução do impasse", tendo já entregue contraproposta ao Governo|url=https://24.sapo.pt/atualidade/artigos/oe2025-pedro-nuno-santos-fala-sobre-contraproposta-ao-governo |access-date=9 October 2024 |website=Sapo |language=pt}}</ref> The [[Prime Minister of Portugal|Prime Minister]] rejected these last conditions made by the PS, but said he was "confident" in the budget being approved by Parliament.<ref>{{Cite web |date=8 October 2024 |title=A grande entrevista de Luís Montenegro à SIC, nas vésperas da entrega do Orçamento do Estado|url=https://sicnoticias.pt/especiais/orcamento-do-estado/2024-10-08-a-grande-entrevista-de-luis-montenegro-a-sic-nas-vesperas-da-entrega-do-orcamento-do-estado-30b2e9c2 |access-date=9 October 2024 |website=[[SIC Notícias]] |language=pt}}</ref>

On 17 October 2024, the general-secretary of the PS, Pedro Nuno Santos, announced that the Socialist Party would abstain in the budget vote, thus ensuring the approval of the document with the sole votes of the AD coalition.<ref>{{Cite web |date=17 October 2024 |title=OE2025: a declaração de Pedro Nuno Santos |url=https://sicnoticias.pt/especiais/orcamento-do-estado/2024-10-17-video-oe2025-a-declaracao-de-pedro-nuno-santos-b033ceec |access-date=17 October 2024 |website=[[SIC Notícias]] |language=pt}}</ref> On 31 October, Parliament passed the budget in its first general reading by an 80–72 vote, with the 78 PS members abstaining. On the final vote, on 29 November 2024, the budget was confirmed by a 79–72 vote, with 77 PS members abstaining.<ref>{{Cite web |date=29 November 2024 |title=Orçamento aprovado. Abstenção do PS viabilizou documento|url=https://www.rtp.pt/noticias/economia/orcamento-aprovado-abstencao-do-ps-viabilizou-documento_v1618429 |access-date=30 November 2024 |website=RTP |language=pt}}</ref>

=== ''Spinumviva'' case ===
On 15 February 2025, [[Correio da Manhã (Portugal)|''Correio da Manhã'']] newspaper published an investigation in which it was stated that Prime Minister Luís Montenegro's family had a business with real estate interests, called Spinumviva.<ref>{{Cite news |date=15 February 2025 |title=Mulher e filhos de Luís Montenegro têm uma empresa imobiliária|url=https://cnnportugal.iol.pt/montenegro/luis-montenegro/mulher-e-filhos-de-luis-montenegro-tem-uma-empresa-imobiliaria/20250215/67b0a925d34ef72ee4424edf |access-date=1 March 2025 |work=CNN Portugal |language=Portuguese}}</ref> Montenegro confirmed the existence of the company, but denied any [[conflict of interest]], saying he had sold his shares to his wife in 2022.<ref>{{Cite news |date=15 February 2025 |title=Montenegro nega conflito de interesse por família deter imobiliária |url=https://eco.sapo.pt/2025/02/15/montenegro-nega-conflito-de-interesse-por-familia-deter-imobiliaria/ |access-date=1 March 2025 |work=Eco |language=Portuguese}}</ref> [[Chega (political party)|Chega]] criticized the lack of answers from Montenegro and presented a [[motion of no confidence]]. This was rejected by Parliament by 171 against to 49 in favour.<ref>{{Cite news |date=21 February 2025 |title=Moção de censura. === Date ===
According to the Portuguese Constitution, an election must be called between 14 September and 14 October of the year that the legislature ends. The election is called by the [[President of Portugal]] but is not called at the request of the [[Prime Minister of Portugal|Prime Minister]]; however, the President must listen to all of the parties represented in Parliament and the election day must be announced at least 60 days before the election.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Wayback Machine |url=http://app.parlamento.pt/site_antigo/ingles/cons_leg/Constitution_VII_revisao_definitive.pdf |access-date=2024-03-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141022091934/http://app.parlamento.pt/site_antigo/ingles/cons_leg/Constitution_VII_revisao_definitive.pdf |archive-date=22 October 2014 }}</ref> If an election is called during an ongoing legislature (dissolution of parliament) it must be held at least after 55 days. Election day is the same in all multi-seats constituencies, and should fall on a Sunday or national holiday. In normal circumstances, the next legislative election would, therefore, have taken place no later than 8 October 2028,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Electoral law to the Assembly of the Republic |url=http://www.cne.pt/sites/default/files/dl/legis_lear_2015_0.pdf}}</ref> but it was called early for 18 May 2025.<ref name="Data" />

The President of Portugal has the power to dissolve the Assembly of the Republic by his/her own will. Unlike in other countries, the President can refuse to dissolve the parliament at the request of the Prime Minister or the Assembly of the Republic and all the parties represented in Parliament. If the Prime Minister resigns, the President can appoint a new Prime Minister after listening to all the parties represented in Parliament and then the government programme must be subject to discussion by the Assembly of the Republic, whose members of parliament may present a motion to reject the upcoming government, or dissolve Parliament and call new elections.

=== Electoral system ===
The [[Assembly of the Republic (Portugal)|Assembly of the Republic]] has 230 members elected to four-year terms. Governments do not require absolute majority support of the Assembly to hold office, as even if the number of opposers of government is larger than that of the supporters, the number of opposers still needs to be equal or greater than 116 (absolute majority) for both the Government's Programme to be rejected or for a [[motion of no confidence]] to be approved.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Constitution of the Portuguese Republic |url=http://www.en.parlamento.pt/Legislation/CRP/Constitution7th.pdf|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303222446/http://www.en.parlamento.pt/Legislation/CRP/Constitution7th.pdf |archive-date=3 March 2016 }}</ref>

The number of seats assigned to each constituency, a total of 22, depends on the [[district magnitude]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Effective threshold in electoral systems |url=http://www.tcd.ie/Political_Science/staff/michael_gallagher/ElSystems/Docts/effthresh.php |access-date=2015-10-21 |website=Trinity College, Dublin}}</ref> The use of the [[d'Hondt method]] makes for a higher effective threshold than certain other allocation methods such as the [[Largest remainder method|Hare quota]] or [[Sainte-Laguë method]], which are more generous to small parties.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Gallagher |first=Michael |date=1992 |title=Comparing Proportional Representation Electoral Systems: Quotas, Thresholds, Paradoxes and Majorities |url=https://www.tcd.ie/Political_Science/staff/michael_gallagher/BJPS1992.pdf}}</ref>

== Campaign ==
=== Issues ===
The campaign is being dominated by several issues, from the ''Spinumviva'' case, mainly regarding ethics and transparency topics, to the Housing crisis, the state of the [[Healthcare in Portugal#National Health Service (SNS)|National Health Service]] and the increase in Defense spending.<ref>{{cite news |date=26 March 2025 |url=https://www.publico.pt/2025/03/26/politica/noticia/spinumviva-defesa-temas-marcar-campanha-eleitoral-2126940 |title=Da Spinumviva à defesa: os temas que devem marcar a campanha eleitoral |language=pt |work=Público |access-date=16 April 2025}}</ref> Plus, just like in 2024, the issue of governability and stability after the elections is also being central, with the two main parties debating whether or not to allow the winner to form a minority government.<ref>{{cite news |date=10 April 2025 |url=https://expresso.pt/politica/eleicoes/legislativas-2025/2025-04-10-pedro-duarte-em-entrevista-psd-deve-dar-condicoes-de-governabilidade-ao-ps-ece4a244 |title=Pedro Duarte em entrevista: “PSD deve dar condições de governabilidade ao PS” |language=pt |work=Expresso |access-date=16 April 2025}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=4 April 2025 |url=https://www.rtp.pt/noticias/politica/responsavel-alexandra-leitao-afirma-que-ps-garante-governabilidade_v1645597 |title="Responsável". Alexandra Leitão afirma que PS garante governabilidade |language=pt |work=RTP |access-date=16 April 2025}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=14 April 2025 |url=https://www.publico.pt/2025/04/14/politica/entrevista/miguel-relvas-psd-ficar-segundo-maioria-direita-assumir-responsabilidades-2129773 |title=Miguel Relvas: “Se o PSD ficar em segundo com maioria de direita, tem de assumir as responsabilidades”|language=pt |work=Público |access-date=16 April 2025}}</ref> [[Voter fatigue]] and [[Political apathy|apathy]] is also a source of concern due to the sucession of elections and cases involving politicians.<ref>{{cite news |date=21 March 2025 |url=https://eco.sapo.pt/2025/03/21/abstencao-nas-legislativas-arrisca-subir-face-ao-cansaco-dos-eleitores-e-casos-do-chega-campanha-sera-decisiva/ |title=Abstenção nas legislativas arrisca subir face ao cansaço dos eleitores e casos do Chega. Campanha será decisiva|language=pt |work=Eco |access-date=17 April 2025}}</ref>

On 16 April 2025, in the middle of the pre-election campaign and a month before election day, the [[Public Prosecution Service (Portugal)|Public Prosecution Service]] announced that it was analyzing complaints against Pedro Nuno Santos, leader of the PS, in relation to his properties and the way he paid for them.<ref>{{cite news |date=26 March 2025 |url=https://www.publico.pt/2025/04/16/politica/noticia/ministerio-publico-abre-averiguacao-preventiva-pedro-nuno-compra-dois-imoveis-2130055 |title=Ministério Público abre averiguação preventiva a Pedro Nuno por compra de dois imóveis  |language=pt |work=Público |access-date=16 April 2025}}</ref> The PS leader rejected any irregularities and said he was not afraid of scrutiny.<ref>{{cite news |date=16 April 2025 |url=https://www.rtp.pt/noticias/politica/denuncias-anonimas-pedro-nuno-diz-que-ao-contrario-de-montenegro-nao-teme-escrutinio_v1648469 |title=Denúncias anónimas. Pedro Nuno diz que ao contrário de Montenegro não teme escrutínio  |language=pt |work=RTP |access-date=16 April 2025}}</ref>

Montenegro reage às críticas da oposição |url=https://www.rtp.pt/noticias/politica/nao-fujo-a-nenhum-debate-montenegro-reage-as-criticas-da-oposicao_n1643600 |access-date=27 March 2022 |website=www.rtp.pt |language=pt-PT |date=26 March 2025}}</ref> Parties criticized this position from AD, with LIVRE and BE refusing to debate Nuno Melo.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Rui Tavares recusa ir a debate com Nuno Melo e critica líderes e televisões |url=https://www.publico.pt/2025/03/27/politica/noticia/rui-tavares-recusa-ir-debate-nuno-melo-critica-lideres-televisoes-2127535 |access-date=27 March 2022 |website=www.publico.pt |language=pt-PT |date=27 March 2025}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Mariana Mortágua recusa debater com Melo em substituição de Montenegro |url=https://www.rtp.pt/noticias/politica/mariana-mortagua-recusa-debater-com-melo-em-substituicao-de-montenegro_a1644142 |access-date=29 March 2022 |website=www.rtp.pt |language=pt-PT |date=28 March 2025}}</ref> The two debates between all the leaders of the parties, or coalitions, represented in Parliament will be broadcast by RTP1 and by [[List of radio stations in Portugal|radio stations]].<ref name="radiodebates" />
