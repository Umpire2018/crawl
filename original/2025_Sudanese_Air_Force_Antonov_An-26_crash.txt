On 25 February 2025, a [[Sudanese Air Force]] [[Antonov An-26]] crashed into a residential area in [[Omdurman]], Sudan, shortly after takeoff from [[Wadi Seidna Air Base]]. At least 46 fatalities have been confirmed by Sudanese authorities.<ref>{{cite news |last=Orban |first=André |title=Sudan military plane crash kills 46 amid intensifying conflict |url=https://www.aviation24.be/manufacturers/antonov/sudan-military-plane-crash-kills-46-amid-intensifying-conflict/ |access-date=26 February 2025 |work=Aviation24.be |date=26 February 2025 }}</ref><ref name="bbc">{{Cite news |date=26 February 2025 |title=Sudan military plane crashes in residential area |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyn914g249o |access-date=26 February 2025 |work=[[BBC News]] }}</ref>

== Background ==
The Sudanese military has been engaged in a [[Sudanese civil war (2023–present)|conflict]] against the paramilitary [[Rapid Support Forces]] (RSF) since April 2023, resulting in a widespread humanitarian crisis.<ref name=":3"/> As of February 2025, nearly 13 million people are thought to be displaced.<ref>{{Cite web |date=25 February 2025 |title=Maps show Sudan conflict's shifting frontlines as SAF regains territory |url=https://news.sky.com/story/maps-show-sudan-conflicts-shifting-frontlines-as-saf-regains-territory-13316893 |access-date=26 February 2025 |website=Sky News }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Owoseni |first=Opeyemi |date=26 February 2025 |title=Sudanese Military Plane Crash Kills 46 in Khartoum Outskirts |url=https://www.tv360nigeria.com/sudanese-military-plane-crash-kills-46-in-khartoum-outskirts/ |access-date=26 February 2025 |website=TV360 Nigeria |language=en-GB }}</ref>

Sudan is considered to have a poor aviation safety record,<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 February 2025 |title=Death toll in Sudan military plane crash rises to 46 |url=https://apnews.com/article/sudan-war-miliary-rsf-4311a36df4d48680770253a8bf13dcff |access-date=26 February 2025 |website=AP News |language=en }}</ref> largely due to poor maintenance and aging aircraft.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Orton |first=Daniel |date=26 February 2025 |title=At least 46 dead in military plane crash |url=https://www.newsweek.com/least-46-dead-military-plane-crash-2036425 |access-date=26 February 2025 |website=Newsweek |language=en }}</ref>

==Passengers and crew==
The [[Antonov An-26]] had been reported to be carrying several high-ranking officers of the [[Sudanese Armed Forces]] and a crew alongside civilians.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web |date=26 February 2025 |title=Death toll in Sudan military plane crash rises to 46 |url=https://apnews.com/article/sudan-war-miliary-rsf-4311a36df4d48680770253a8bf13dcff |access-date=26 February 2025 |website=AP News }}</ref>

It was reportedly on its way to [[Port Sudan]].<ref name="tribune">{{Cite web |date=25 February 2025 |title=Sudanese military plane crashes in Omdurman, killing crew, civilians on ground |url=https://sudantribune.com/article297916/ |access-date=26 February 2025 |website=Sudan Tribune }}</ref> Among the passengers were Senior Commander Major General Bahr Ahmed, a high-ranking commander who was formerly the commander of forces in the capital [[Khartoum]] and senior military officer Lt. Col. Awad Ayoub.<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 February 2025 |title=Death toll in Sudan military plane crash rises to 46 |url=https://krdo.com/news/2025/02/26/death-toll-in-sudan-military-plane-crash-rises-to-46/ |access-date=26 February 2025 |publisher=[[KRDO-TV|KRDO]] |language=en }}</ref>

==Accident==
The aircraft crashed at around 20:40 local time,<ref>{{Cite web |date=27 February 2025 |title=Antonov crash in northern Omdurman kills at least 19 people from [[Wadi Seidna Air Base]] into a house in Block 75 of the al-Thawra housing area in the Al-Iskan area of [[Karary District|Karari]], [[Omdurman]].<ref name=":2"/><ref name=":4">{{Cite news the plane was flying at relatively low altitude before it plummeted and caught fire.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=25 February 2025 |title=Officers killed in Sudan plane crash: Military source |access-date=26 February 2025 |website=Xinhua }}</ref> Military sources told ''[[Reuters]]'' that a technical failure was the likely cause.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web |date=26 February 2025 }}</ref>

Twenty-nine civilians on the ground were also reported to be killed in the crash, while damage was reported to nearby residential homes in the neighborhood the plane crashed in.<ref>{{Cite web |date=25 February 2025 |title=Sudanese military plane crash in Omdurman kills senior officers |url=https://www.albawaba.com/news/sudanese-military-plane-crash-omdurman-1600460 |access-date=26 February 2025 |website=Al Bawaba }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=26 February 2025 |title=Sudanese military plane crashes in Omdurman, crew killed |url=https://www.thenews.com.pk/latest/1286536-sudanese-military-plane-crashes-in-omdurman-crew-killed |access-date=26 February 2025 |website=The News }}</ref> Among the dead were women and children.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Magdy |first=Samy |date=26 February 2025 |title=19 killed in Sudan military plane crash after aircraft's takeoff |url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/19-killed-sudan-military-plane-crash-after-aircrafts-119197809 |access-date=26 February 2025 |website=ABC News |language=en }}</ref> The crash also caused power outages in nearby neighborhoods.<ref name="bbc"/> An additional 10 people were wounded.<ref name=":4"/>

== Aftermath ==
The Sudanese Health Ministry stated that search efforts were underway.<ref name=":3"/> Firefighters put out a fire confined to the location.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Peters |first=Luke |date=27 February 2025 |title=Sudanese military aircraft crashes on take-off killing 46 |url=https://www.aerotime.aero/articles/sudan-military-antonov-accident-multiple-fatalities |access-date=27 February 2025 |website=Aerotime |language=en-US }}</ref>
