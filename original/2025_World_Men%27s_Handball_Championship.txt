The '''2025 [[IHF World Men's Handball Championship]]''' was the 29th such event hosted by the [[International Handball Federation]]. It was held in Croatia, Denmark and Norway from 14 January to 2 February 2025.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ihf.info/media-center/news/ihf-council-awards-events-2027|title=IHF Council awards events up to 2027|date=28 February 2020|work=IHF}}</ref> This edition marked the first time the [[IHF World Men's Handball Championship|world championship]] was held in three countries.

Co-host [[Denmark men's national handball team|Denmark]] were the triple defending champions, having won the [[2019 World Men's Handball Championship|2019]], [[2021 World Men's Handball Championship|2021]] and [[2023 World Men's Handball Championship|2023]] editions, and successfully defended their title by defeating co-host [[Croatia men's national handball team|Croatia]] 32–26 in the final. In doing so, Denmark became the first men's national handball team to win four consecutive world titles.<ref name=records>{{cite news|url=https://sport.tv2.dk/haandbold/2025-02-01-her-er-danmarks-utrolige-rekorder|publisher=[[TV2 Danmark]]|title=Her er Danmarks utrolige rekorder|author=Christian Ipsen|date=2 February 2025|access-date=22 February 2025|language=da|trans-title=Here are Denmark's unbelievable records}}</ref> [[France men's national handball team|France]] beat [[Portugal men's national handball team|Portugal]] to win the bronze medals, while third co-host [[Norway men's national handball team|Norway]] finished 10th.

Denmark beat the record for the highest average win margin after 7 matches at a World Championship played with +13.4 goals. They also extended their record of most World Championship matches in a row without defeat to 31.<ref name=records />

== Qualification ==
{{main|2025 World Men's Handball Championship qualification}}
The World Championship hosts were directly qualified, along with the reigning world champions.
Since there were three organisers, all from Europe, the number of compulsory places for Europe was reduced by two: 2 instead of default 4. The number of compulsory places awarded to other continental confederation remained unchanged. In regards to the 12 performance spots, and based on the results of the [[2023 World Men's Handball Championship|2023 Men's World Championship]], [[European Handball Federation|Europe]] received 11 more spots, while [[African Handball Confederation|Africa]] took 1.

The slot allocation was as follows:
*[[Asian Handball Federation|AHF]] (Asia): 4 slots
*[[African Handball Confederation|CAHB]] (Africa): 5 slots
*[[European Handball Federation|EHF]] (Europe): 17 slots (including the three co-hosts)
*[[North America and Caribbean Handball Confederation|NACHC]] (North America and the Caribbean): 1 slot
*[[Oceania Continent Handball Federation|OCHF]] (Oceania): 0 slots
*[[South and Central America Handball Confederation|SCAHC]] (South and Central America): 3 slots
*Wildcards: 2 slots

Qualifying occurred between February 2023 and June 2024. Each region's continental championships acted as world championship qualification, although Europe also has its own qualification process. With the exception of [[Belarus men's national handball team|Belarus]] and [[Russia men's national handball team|Russia]], who are banned of the IHF due to the [[Russian invasion of Ukraine]], all remaining [[International Handball Federation|IHF]] member associations were eligible to enter qualification. So far, 76 nations have entered the continental championships and European qualifiers, which act as world championship qualification.

[[Guinea men's national handball team|Guinea]] will make their debut at the championship. Of the returnees [[Italy men's national handball team|Italy]] made their second appearance ever and first since [[1997 World Men's Handball Championship|1997]], [[Cuba men's national handball team|Cuba]] and [[Kuwait men's national handball team|Kuwait]] are returning for the first time since the [[2009 World Men's Handball Championship]], [[Czech Republic men's national handball team|Czech Republic]] made the championship after [[2015 World Men's Handball Championship|ten years]], while [[Austria men's national handball team|Austria]], [[Japan men's national handball team|Japan]] and [[Switzerland men's national handball team|Switzerland]] qualified after missing out on [[2023 World Men's Handball Championship|2023]]. The [[United States men's national handball team|United States]] made back to back appearances for the first time in 30 years. [[Netherlands men's national handball team|Netherlands]] qualified on merit for the first time ever.

[[Iran men's national handball team|Iran]], [[Montenegro men's national handball team|Montenegro]], [[Morocco men's national handball team|Morocco]], [[Saudi Arabia men's national handball team|Saudi Arabia]], [[Serbia men's national handball team|Serbia]], [[South Korea men's national handball team|South Korea]] and [[Uruguay men's national handball team|Uruguay]] all failed to make this edition after participating in [[2023 World Men's Handball Championship|2023]]. After making their debut in [[2023 World Men's Handball Championship|2023]], [[Belgium men's national handball team|Belgium]] failed to qualify.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ihf.info/media-center/news/estonia-lithuania-italy-and-slovakia-progress-european-qualification-2025-ihf|title=Estonia, Lithuania, Italy and Slovakia progress in the European Qualification for the 2025 IHF Men's World Championship|website=IHF|date=19 March 2024}}</ref>

* '''{{hb|SUI}}'''
* '''{{hb|USA}}'''

Godine | website=Croatian Handball Federation | date=9 January 2024|language=hr}}</ref> it was changed a few weeks later to 29 May.<ref name="auto2"/> The guests for the draw were Croatian goalkeeper [[Dominik Kuzmanović]], head coach of the Croatian national team, [[Dagur Sigurðsson]], Norwegian national team player, [[Alexander Blonz]] and former Danish international player, [[Morten Stig Christensen]], who all assisted the draw.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ihf.info/media-center/news/star-studded-draw-deliver-answers-2025-ihf-mens-world-championship|website=ihf.info|title=Star studded draw deliver answers 2025 IHF Men's World Championship}}</ref>

=== Seeding ===
Ahead of the draw, the 32 finalist teams were seeded into four pots according to IHF rankings in May 2024.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ihf.info/sites/default/files/2021-01/Regulations%20for%20IHF%20Competitions_E_4.pdf|title=V. Regulations for IHF Competitions (Indoor Handball)|publisher=International Handball Federation|date=9 December 2020|accessdate=1 January 2022}}</ref><ref name="auto3"/> Although, in regards to the European teams in pots 1 and 2, the [[2024 European Men's Handball Championship|2024 European Championship]] decided the teams' positions. In addition, five teams were pre-assigned by the three host nations into each of the available vacant groups: Germany in Group A, Austria in Group C, Hungary in Group D, Sweden in Group F and Slovenia in Group G.<ref name="auto3"/><ref>{{cite news |title=Македонија ќе оди во ждрепка за СП 2025 |url=https://www.g-sport.mk/vest-statija/110278/makedonija-kje-odi-vo-zhdrepka-za-sp-2025/ |work=G-Sport |date=21 May 2024}}</ref>

== Final ranking and awards==
Places 1 to 4 and 25 to 32 were decided by play-off or knock-out. The losers of the quarter finals were ranked 5th to 8th according to the places in the main round, points gained and goal difference. Teams finishing third in the main round were ranked 9th to 12th, teams finishing fourth in the main round were ranked 13th to 16th, teams finishing fifth in the main round were ranked 17th to 20th and teams ranked sixth were ranked 21st to 24th. In case of a tie in points gained, the goal difference of the main round was taken into account, then number of goals scored. If teams were still equal, number of points gained in the preliminary round was considered followed by the goal difference and then number of goals scored in the preliminary round.

== Preparations ==
Croatian host city, [[Varaždin]] signed their hosting contract on 26 June 2024.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://hrs.hr/2024/06/26/aktivnosti-hrs-a-potpisan-ugovor-o-suorganizaciji-sp-2025-s-gradom-varazdinom/ | title=Aktivnosti HRS-a: Potpisan Ugovor o suorganizaciji SP 2025 s Gradom Varaždinom &#124; HRS | date=26 June 2024|language=hr|publisher=Croatian Handball Federation }}</ref> In November 2024, host city [[Poreč]] also signed their contract.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://hrs.hr/2024/11/12/porec-potpuno-spreman-za-svjetsko-rukometno-prvenstvo-u-sijecnju/ | title=Poreč potpuno spreman za Svjetsko rukometno prvenstvo u siječnju &#124; HRS | date=12 November 2024|language=hr|publisher=Croatian Handball Federation }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://istarski.hr/node/107407-porec-spreman-za-svjetsko-prvenstvo-gledat-ce-se-vrhunski-rukomet-a-stizu-i-legendarni-francuzi | title=Poreč spreman za Svjetsko prvenstvo: Gledat će se vrhunski rukomet, a stižu i legendarni Francuzi|language=hr|website=istarski.hr }}</ref> In December 2024, [[Croatian Handball Federation]], [[Varaždin County]], [[Međimurje County]] and [[Čakovec]] signed a joint co-hosting contract.<ref name=Ugovor>{{cite web|url=https://sport.hrt.hr/rukomet/potpisani-ugovori-o-suradnji-u-suorganizaciji-svjetskog-prvenstva-11920767|title=Potpisani ugovori o suradnji u suorganizaciji Svjetskog prvenstva|website=sport.hrt.hr|publisher=Croatian Radio Television (HRT)|date=20 December 2024|language=hr}}</ref>

On 22 October 2024, the official ball was released.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.ihf.info/competitions/men/308/29th-ihf-mens-world-championship-2025/177649/news/198389 | title=IHF &#124; News }}</ref>

On 22 November 2024, the Norwegian government allotted 10 million [[Norwegian krone|NOK]] for the tournament.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.handball.no/nyheter/2024/11/regjeringen-gir-10-mill.-til-vm-pa-hjemmebane/ | title=Regjeringen gir 10 mill. Til VM på hjemmebane &#124; handball.no }}</ref>
