The '''2025 [[IHF World Women's Handball Championship]]''' will be 27th edition of the championship to be jointly hosted by Germany and the Netherlands under the aegis of [[International Handball Federation]] (IHF) from 26 November to 14 December 2025. It will be fourth time in history that the championship is jointly hosted.<ref>{{cite web|title=IHF Council awards events up to 2027 |url=https://www.ihf.info/media-center/news/ihf-council-awards-events-2027 |date=28 February 2020 |publisher=IHF |access-date=26 August 2020}}</ref> "Catch the Dream" will be the official motto of the competition.<ref name="Motto and Dutch venues">{{cite web|url=https://www.ihf.info/media-center/news/catch-dream-2025-ihf-womens-world-championship-final-venue-announced |title="Catch the dream" at the 2025 IHF Women's World Championship, as Final venue is announced |publisher=International Handball Federation|date=5 April 2023|access-date=8 April 2023}}</ref>

[[France women's national handball team|France]] are the defending world champions, having won the 2023 edition.

==Qualification==
{{main|2025 World Women's Handball Championship qualification}}

The World Championship hosts will be directly qualified, along with the reigning world champions.
Since there are two organisers, all from Europe, the number of compulsory places for Europe is reduced by one: 3 instead of default 4. The number of compulsory places awarded to other continental confederation remains unchanged. In regards to the 12 performance spots, and based on the results of the [[2023 World Women's Handball Championship|2023 Women's World Championship]], [[European Handball Federation|Europe]] receives 11 more spots, while [[South and Central America Handball Confederation|South and Central America]] takes 1.

The slot allocation is as follows:
*[[Asian Handball Federation|AHF]] (Asia): 4 slots 
*[[African Handball Confederation|CAHB]] (Africa): 4 slots
*[[European Handball Federation|EHF]] (Europe): 17 slots (including the two co-hosts and defending champion)
*[[North America and Caribbean Handball Confederation|NACHC]] (North America and the Caribbean): 1 slot
*[[Oceania Continent Handball Federation|OCHF]] (Oceania): 0 slots
*[[South and Central America Handball Confederation|SCAHC]] (South and Central America): 4 slots
*Wildcards: 2 slots

Qualifying occurred between October 2024 and April 2025. Each region's continental championships acted as world championship qualification, although Europe also has its own qualification process. With the exception of [[Belarus women's national handball team|Belarus]] and [[Russia women's national handball team|Russia]], who are banned of the IHF due to the [[Russian invasion of Ukraine]], all remaining [[International Handball Federation|IHF]] member associations were eligible to enter qualification.

[[Egypt women's national handball team|Egypt]], [[Faroe Islands women's national handball team|Faroe Islands]] and [[Switzerland women's national handball team|Switzerland]] will make their debut at the championship, with the Faroe Islands becoming the smallest nation to ever qualify, overtaking [[Greenland women's national handball team|Greenland]]. Of the returnees, after receiving a wildcard, [[Uruguay women's national handball team|Uruguay]] return for the first time since [[2011 World Women's Handball Championship|2011]]. [[Cuba women's national handball team|Cuba]] came back after their last appearance was in [[2019 World Women's Handball Championship|2019]]. [[Tunisia women's national handball team|Tunisia]], return after missing out on [[2023 World Women's Handball Championship|2023]]. [[Iceland women's national handball team|Iceland]] and [[Senegal women's national handball team|Senegal]] qualified for two consecutive finals for the first time. Originally, [[China women's national handball team|China]] failed to qualify after finishing outside the top four at the Asian Championship, however, they were belatedly given a wildcard, continuing run of making every world championship since [[1986 World Women's Handball Championship|1986]].<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.ihf.info/media-center/news/peoples-republic-china-granted-wild-card-2025-ihf-womens-world-championship|title=IHF &#124; People&#039;s Republic of China granted wild card for the 2025 IHF Women&#039;s World Championship|website=www.ihf.info}}</ref>

Notable absentees include [[Slovenia women's national handball team|Slovenia]] whose last failure to qualify was [[2015 World Women's Handball Championship|2015]], while [[Croatia women's national handball team|Croatia]], [[Cameroon women's national handball team|Cameroon]], [[Congo women's national handball team|Congo]] and [[Paraguay women's national handball team|Paraguay]] all failed to qualify having participated at the previous two championships. 2023 particpants, [[Greenland women's national handball team|Greenland]] will not qualify after failing to enter the [[2025 Nor.Ca. Women's Handball Championship]] for financial reasons.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dr.dk/sporten/haandbold/tom-pengekasse-koster-groenlands-landshold-vm-mulighed|title=Tom pengekasse koster Grønlands landshold VM-mulighed|date=February 22, 2025|website=DR}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ihf.info/media-center/news/five-teams-battle-coveted-title-2025-nachc-womens-handball-championship|title=IHF &#124; Five teams to battle for coveted title at the 2025 NACHC Women&#039;s Handball Championship|website=www.ihf.info}}</ref> [[Chile women's national handball team|Chile]] failed to qualify after taking part in [[2023 World Women's Handball Championship|2023]].

===Wildcards===
After no Oceanian team achieved a top 5 finish in the [[2024 Asian Women's Handball Championship|Asian Championship]], two wildcards were given out by the [[International Handball Federation|IHF]].

* '''{{hbw|CHN}}'''

On 31 March 2025, China was given the first wildcard due to good performances and country's position, making it a big market for the sport.<ref name="auto"/> [[United States women's national handball team|United States]] is likely to receive the other wildcard, so the team can use this opportunity to prepare for the [[2028 Summer Olympics]] which they will be hosting.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ihf.info/sites/default/files/2019-09/New%20Markets_Strategic%20plan%20USA.pdf|publisher=IHF|title=Strategic plan {{!}} USA}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.handball.no/globalassets/nhf-sentralt/om-oss/organisasjon/protokoller/ehf-og-ihf/ihf-council/20181119ihf-minutes-council-1810.pdf|title=Council Meeting No. 3|publisher=IHF|location=Doha, Qatar|date=18 October 2018|pages=8–9}}</ref>

==Final ranking==
Places 1 to 4 and 25 to 32 will be decided by play-off or knock-out. The losers of the quarter finals will be ranked 5th to 8th according to the places in the main round, points gained and goal difference. Teams finishing third in the main round will be ranked 9th to 12th, teams finishing fourth in the main round 13th to 16th, teams finishing fifth in the main round 17th to 20th and teams ranked sixth 21st to 24th. In case of a tie in points gained, the goal difference of the main round will be taken into account, then number of goals scored. If teams will still be equal, number of points gained in the preliminary round will be considered followed by the goal difference and then number of goals scored in the preliminary round.
