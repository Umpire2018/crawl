On 18 January 2025, an attack took place at the [[Supreme Court of Iran]], located inside the [[Courthouse of Tehran|Palace of Justice]] in [[Tehran]], [[Iran]].<ref name="APNews20250119" /> Initial reports stated that the shooting occurred when a man armed with a knife entered, attacking a bodyguard before taking control of the bodyguard's handgun and opening fire. Two senior [[Qadi]] ([[Sharia]] judges), {{ill|Ali Razini|fa|علی رازینی}} and {{ill|Mohammad Moghiseh|fa|محمد مقیسه}}, died after being shot in their offices. A third judge survived his injuries.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news |last=George |first=Susannah |date=January 18, 2025 |title=Two Iranian Supreme Court judges shot dead in rare Tehran attack |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2025/01/18/iran-supreme-court-judges-killed/ |newspaper=The Washington Post}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=2025-01-18 |title=Two Iranian Judges Killed in Tehran in Possible Foreign Plot |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-01-18/two-iranian-judges-killed-in-tehran-in-possible-foreign-plot |access-date=2025-01-21 |work=Bloomberg.com |language=en}}</ref> 

The judges were reportedly adjudicating cases of student protesters, artists, intellectuals and activists<ref name="CNN20250118">{{Cite web |last=Salem |first=Mostafa |date=2025-01-18 |title=Two high-profile Iranian judges killed in Tehran in a 'planned assassination' |url=https://www.cnn.com/2025/01/18/middleeast/iranian-judges-killed-in-tehran-intl/index.html |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250119033914/https://www.cnn.com/2025/01/18/middleeast/iranian-judges-killed-in-tehran-intl/index.html |archive-date=19 January 2025 |access-date=2025-01-18 |publisher=CNN |language=en}}</ref> in [[bench trial]]s and had roles in the [[1988 executions of Iranian political prisoners]].<ref name="APNews20250119" /><ref name="IndianExpress20250119">{{Cite web |date=2025-01-19 |title=Judges shot dead in Iran's supreme court building |url=https://indianexpress.com/article/news-today/iran-supreme-court-judges-shot-dead-9786331/ |access-date=2025-01-18 |website=The Indian Express |language=en}}</ref>

The perpetrator killed himself soon after the shooting, before he could be apprehended.<ref name=":0" />  No potential motive has been released.<ref name="RFERL20250118" />

== Background ==
According to Article 161 of the 1979 [[Constitution of Iran]], the [[Supreme Court of Iran|Supreme Court]] ({{Langx|fa|دیوان عالی کشور|Divan-e 'Ali-ye Keshvar}}) is the [[Supreme court|highest judicial authority]] in the country, established for "the purpose of supervising the correct implementation of the laws by the courts, ensuring uniformity of [[Judicial system of the Islamic Republic of Iran|judicial procedure]], and fulfilling any other responsibilities assigned to it by law."<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.iranonline.com/iran/iran-info/Government/constitution-11.html |title=iranonline.com Iranian government constitution, The Judiciary |access-date=2021-01-26 |archive-date=2008-04-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080410013835/http://www.iranonline.com/iran/iran-info/Government/constitution-11.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> Iran's legal system is based on [[Islamic law]]. According to [[Constitution of Iran#Preamble|Preamble]] 10, titled ''The Judiciary in the Constitution'', [[Faqīh|jurist]]s must operate "with meticulous knowledge of the Islamic laws."<ref>{{Cite web |title=Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Iran - Wikisource, the free online library |url=https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Islamic_Republic_of_Iran#Preamble_10_:_The_Judiciary_in_the_Constitution |access-date=2025-01-22 |website=en.wikisource.org |language=en |archive-date=5 March 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210305102724/https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Islamic_Republic_of_Iran#Preamble_10_:_The_Judiciary_in_the_Constitution |url-status=live }}</ref> In 2005, judge [[Hassan Moghaddas]] was assassinated.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2025-01-18 |title=2 judges shot dead at Iran's supreme court: media |url=https://www.voanews.com/a/judges-shot-dead-at-iran-s-supreme-court-media/7941542.html |access-date=2025-01-18 |publisher=Voice of America |language=en |archive-date=18 January 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250118224338/https://www.voanews.com/a/judges-shot-dead-at-iran-s-supreme-court-media/7941542.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

=== Mohammad Moghiseh ===
'''Mohammad Moghiseh''' ({{langx|fa|محمد مقیسه}}; 1956 – 18 January 2025) was an Iranian judge. He served on the Supreme Court of Iran from 2020 until his death.<ref>{{cite news |date=June 2024 |title=Mohammad Moghiseh: The Iranian Supreme Court's Hanging Judge |url=https://www.unitedagainstnucleariran.com/sites/default/files/Judge%20Mohammad%20Moghiseh%20Update_06302024.pdf |trans-title= |publisher=www.unitedagainstnucleariran.com |language= |location= |access-date=18 January 2025 |archive-date=19 January 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250119001046/https://www.unitedagainstnucleariran.com/sites/default/files/Judge%20Mohammad%20Moghiseh%20Update_06302024.pdf|url-status=live}}</ref> Moghiseh was the sentencing judge of [[Nasrin Sotoudeh]] and adjudicated the case of [[Hossein_Rajabian#Apprehension_and_court_case|Hossein Rajabian]]. He was [[2019–2020 Iranian protests#Sanctions after November uprising|sanctioned in December 2019]] by the [[United States Department of the Treasury]]'s [[Office of Foreign Assets Control]] (OFAC) "pursuant to [[List_of_executive_orders_in_the_first_presidency_of_Donald_Trump#2018|E.O. 13846]] for engaging in censorship and other activities with respect to Iran on or after 12 July 2009 that prohibit, limit, or penalize the exercise of freedom of expression or assembly by citizens of Iran".<ref name="CNN20250118"/><ref>{{Cite web |title=Treasury Sanctions Two Judges Who Penalize Iranians for Exercising Freedoms of Expression and Assembly |website=home.treasury.gov |date=23 December 2024 |url=https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sm862 |access-date=19 January 2025 |archive-date=18 January 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250118185242/https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sm862 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Sanctions List Search |url=https://sanctionssearch.ofac.treas.gov/Details.aspx?id=23471 |access-date=2025-01-19 |website=sanctionssearch.ofac.treas.gov |archive-date=18 January 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250118150536/https://sanctionssearch.ofac.treas.gov/Details.aspx?id=23471 |url-status=live }}</ref> He was also sanctioned by the European Union and Canada over alleged human rights abuses. At the time of his assassination, he was aged 68.<ref name=":0">{{cite news |title=Iran: Two supreme court judges shot dead in Tehran |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/cy4mvllgd1mo |access-date=18 January 2025 |publisher=BBC News |date=18 January 2025}}</ref><ref name="RFERL20250118"/>

== Aftermath ==
In a message, Supreme Leader [[Ali Khamenei]] offered his condolences for the "[[Shahid|martyrdom]]" of both judges.<ref name="IndianExpress20250119"/> Khamenei also lauded them as [[Mujahid]] brothers.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Khamenei|first=Sayyid Ali|date=January 18, 2025|title=Leader's message of condolence following martyrdom of two eminent judges|url=https://english.khamenei.ir/news/11395/Leader-s-message-of-condolence-following-martyrdom-of-two-eminent|website=Khamenei.ir|archive-date=19 January 2025|access-date=18 January 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250119001153/https://english.khamenei.ir/news/11395/Leader-s-message-of-condolence-following-martyrdom-of-two-eminent|url-status=live}}</ref> President [[Masoud Pezeshkian]] said the "terrorist and cowardly" act must be followed up quickly by security forces and law enforcement.<ref name="Al Jazeera" />

The Iranian judiciary spokesman Asghar Jahangir stated that the judges were working on "national security cases, including espionage and terrorism."<ref name="Reuters">{{Cite news|date=January 18, 2025|title=Two Supreme Court judges shot dead in Tehran, Iranian judiciary says|url=https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/two-supreme-court-judges-shot-dead-tehran-iranian-judiciary-says-2025-01-18/|publisher=[[Reuters]]|archive-date=18 January 2025|access-date=18 January 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250118105505/https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/two-supreme-court-judges-shot-dead-tehran-iranian-judiciary-says-2025-01-18/|url-status=live}}</ref> According to the state-affiliated newspaper ''[[Tehran Times]]'', several people working at the court building where the attack took place were detained.<ref name="Al Jazeera" />

Prosecutor-General [[Mohammad Jafar Montazeri]] described the killings as the "price" the Islamic Republic paid for its "survival". He stated that "capable young judges" would soon replace them.<ref>{{Cite web |date=January 20, 2025 |title=Iran's Prosecutor-General Calls Judges' Killings a 'Price' for Survival |url=https://iranwire.com/en/news/138296-irans-prosecutor-general-calls-judges-killings-a-price-for-survival/ |website=Iranwire}}</ref>
