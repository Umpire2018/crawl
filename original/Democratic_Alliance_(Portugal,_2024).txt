The '''AD – Democratic Alliance''' ({{langx|pt|AD – Aliança Democrática}}, '''AD''') is a [[Centre-right politics|centre-right]] [[political alliance]] in [[Portugal]]. Composed of the [[Social Democratic Party (Portugal)|Social Democratic Party]] (PSD), [[CDS – People's Party]] (CDS–PP) and [[People's Monarchist Party (Portugal)|People's Monarchist Party]] (PPM), the alliance is a relaunch of the [[Democratic Alliance (Portugal, 1979)|eponymous alliance]] which contested elections between [[1979 Portuguese legislative election|1979]] and [[1983 Portuguese legislative election|1983]].

== First attempts ==
[[Marcelo Rebelo de Sousa]] led an attempt to establish a new Democratic Alliance in 1998, between the PSD and the [[CDS – People's Party|People's Party]] (CDS–PP; the former CDS), led by [[Paulo Portas]].  It contested the [[2004 European Parliament election in Portugal|2004 European elections]] as ''[[Força Portugal]]'', but was subsequently dissolved. However, both the PSD and CDS–PP later agreed to contest the [[2014 European Parliament election in Portugal|2014 European elections]] under a joint list called the [[Portugal Alliance]].

In the [[2015 Portuguese legislative election|2015 legislative election]], PSD and CDS-PP ran together in a coalition called [[Coalition PSD/CDS|Portugal Ahead]].

The Democratic Alliance was revived in the [[Azores]] only to contest the [[2022 Portuguese legislative election|2022 elections]]. The coalition polled second with 34% of the votes and elected two MPs to Parliament.<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.legislativas2022.mai.gov.pt/resultados/globais| title=Resultados Globais| lang=pt| work=[[Ministry of Internal Administration (Portugal)|Ministry of Internal Administration]]| access-date=2 February 2022| archive-date=2 February 2022| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220202000000/https://web.archive.org/web/20220202070059/https://www.legislativas2022.mai.gov.pt/resultados/globais| url-status=live}}</ref>

== 2024 Democratic Alliance ==
After the [[2022 Portuguese legislative election|2022 legislative election]] and for the first time in history, [[CDS – People's Party|CDS–PP]] failed to win any seats and was wiped out of parliament. In December 2023, [[Luís Montenegro]] and [[Nuno Melo (politician)|Nuno Melo]] announced a coalition for the [[2024 Portuguese legislative election|2024 legislative]] and [[2024 European Parliament election in Portugal|European Parliament elections]], including the [[Social Democratic Party (Portugal)|Social Democratic Party]] (PPD/PSD), the [[CDS – People's Party]] (CDS–PP) and some independent politicians under the name ''Democratic Alliance'' (AD).<ref>{{Cite web |last=Lusa |first=Jornal Económico com |date=2023-12-21 |title=PSD e CDS-PP vão avançar com coligação pré-eleitoral para as This coalition granted CDS–PP two easily eligible seats and four potentially eligible seats, thus making the return of CDS-PP to the Parliament after the 2024 election certain.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Nuno Melo confirma que coligação garante pelo menos dois deputados ao CDS |url=https://www.dn.pt/2581061900/nuno-melo-confirma-que-coligacao-garante-pelo-menos-dois-deputados-ao-cds/ |access-date=2024-01-06 |website=Diário de Notícias |language=pt |archive-date=2024-01-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240106230544/https://www.dn.pt/2581061900/nuno-melo-confirma-que-coligacao-garante-pelo-menos-dois-deputados-ao-cds/ |url-status=live }}</ref> The coalition also granted one possibly eligible seat to PPM.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Antunes |first=Rui Pedro |title=PPM em lugar difícil de eleger na nova AD. The coalition won the 2024 Azorean regional election, with 42 percent of the votes. It was the first time in 32 years that a PSD-led coalition polled first in an Azorean regional election.<ref>[https://www.publico.pt/2024/02/04/politica/noticia/eleicoes-acores-cne-declaracoes-bolieiro-nao-violam-lei-2079235 Açores: “Governarei em maioria relativa. Não cedo a chantagens”, afirma Bolieiro] ''Público'', 4 February 2024. Retrieved 5 February 2024.</ref> The AD coalition went also to win, albeit by a narrow margin, the 2024 March legislative election, gathering 29 percent of the votes and electing 80 seats to Parliament.<ref>{{Cite web |date=11 March 2024 |title=AD ganha, mas com berbicacho, PS e esquerda ficam na "oposição" |url=https://expresso.pt/politica/eleicoes/legislativas-2024/resultados/2024-03-11-AD-ganha-mas-com-berbicacho-PS-e-esquerda-ficam-na-oposicao-bcba5700 |access-date=12 March 2024 |website=Expresso |language=pt |archive-date=11 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240311111700/https://expresso.pt/politica/eleicoes/legislativas-2024/resultados/2024-03-11-AD-ganha-mas-com-berbicacho-PS-e-esquerda-ficam-na-oposicao-bcba5700 |url-status=live }}</ref> PSD leader [[Luís Montenegro]] was sworn in as [[Prime Minister of Portugal|Prime Minister]], under a minority government, on 2 April 2024.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2 April 2024 |title=Presidente deu posse ao primeiro-ministro e ministros do XXIV Governo Constitucional |trans-title=President swears in the Prime Minister and ministers of the XXIV Constitutional Government |url=https://www.sapo.pt/noticias/atualidade/presidente-deu-posse-ao-primeiro-ministro-e_660c3efa708c9d4acd938c86 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240402185229/https://www.sapo.pt/noticias/atualidade/presidente-deu-posse-ao-primeiro-ministro-e_660c3efa708c9d4acd938c86 |archive-date=2 April 2024 |access-date=2 April 2024 |work=Lusa |language=Portuguese |via=Sapo}}</ref> The Democratic Alliance increased its share of vote in the [[2024 European Parliament election in Portugal|June 2024 European Parliament election]], up to 31 percent, but was narrowly defeated by the [[Socialist Party (Portugal)|Socialist Party]] (PS) which gathered 32 percent of the votes.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Expresso |date=2024-06-09 |title=Pedro Nuno canta vitória. A new election was called for [[2025 Portuguese legislative election|May 2025]], after a vote of confidence in the AD minority government was rejected, following the revelations of the [[2025 Portuguese legislative election#Spinumviva case|''Spinumviva'' case]], involving Luís Montenegro's family business.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Pinto |first1=Salomé |last2= |first2= |date=13 March 2025 |url=https://eco.sapo.pt/2025/03/13/e-oficial-marcelo-marca-eleicoes-para-18-de-maio/|title=É oficial.
