He pledged allegiance to [[Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi]] along with a small band of followers. == Background ==

=== Qandala taken over by Islamic State ===
On October 26, 2016, fighters aligned with the Islamic State captured the small port town of [[Qandala]] in Puntland's [[Bari, Somalia|Bari region]]. This was the first town Islamic State had taken control of in Somalia, the first notable expansion of their influence beyond their usual mountainous hideouts.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Gaffey |first=Conor |date=2016-10-26 |title=ISIS-Aligned Militants Seize Town in Somali Region |url=https://www.newsweek.com/somalia-isis-aligned-militants-seize-first-town-puntland-513890 |access-date=2025-01-02 |website=Newsweek |language=en |archive-date=2025-01-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250102214950/https://www.newsweek.com/somalia-isis-aligned-militants-seize-first-town-puntland-513890 |url-status=live }}</ref>

The takeover led to the displacement of over 25,700 civilians and the humanitarian impact of the conflict.<ref name="reliefweb20161206">{{Cite web |date=2016-12-06 |title=OCHA Somalia Flash Update ‚Äì Humanitarian impact of fighting in Qandala, Puntland {{!}} 6 December 2016 ‚Äì Somalia {{!}} ReliefWeb |url=https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/ocha-somalia-flash-update-humanitarian-impact-fighting-qandala-puntland-6-december |access-date=2025-01-02 |website=reliefweb.int |language=en |archive-date=2025-01-08 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250108144040/https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/ocha-somalia-flash-update-humanitarian-impact-fighting-qandala-puntland-6-december |url-status=live }}</ref>

=== Counter-Offensive ===
{{Main|Qandala campaign}}
[[Puntland Security Force]]s (PSF) launched a [[Counterattack|counter-offensive]] on December 3, 2016, which took aim at retaking Qandala. This operation involved both land and sea movements and faced resistance from Islamic State fighters, who had set up defenses around the town.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.reuters.com/article/world/islamic-state-aligned-group-takes-somali-town-say-officials-idUSKCN12Q0VZ/ | title=Islamic State-aligned group takes Somali town, say officials | work=Reuters | date=27 October 2016 | last1=Omar | first1=Feisal | last2=Sheikh | first2=Abdi }}</ref>

On December 7, 2016, Puntland forces successfully retook Qandala, with no resistance encountered as Islamic State had retreated to the mountainous areas south of the town.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2016-12-07 |title=Forces Retake Somali Town Held by Pro-Islamic State Fighters |url=https://www.voanews.com/a/forces-retake-somali-town-held-by-pro-islamic-state-fighters/3626738.html |access-date=2025-01-11 |website=Voice of America |language=en |archive-date=2019-04-13 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190413143906/https://www.voanews.com/a/forces-retake-somali-town-held-by-pro-islamic-state-fighters/3626738.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

After retaking Qandala, Puntland forces continued their operations into the nearby mountains, where they attacked and destroyed an Islamic State base at El Ladid on December 18, 2016. This was part of efforts to prevent Islamic State from regrouping.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web |date=2016-12-19 |title=Regional Somali Forces 'Destroy' Islamic State Base |url=https://www.voanews.com/a/regional-somali-forces-destroy-islamic-state-base/3642109.html |access-date=2025-01-11 |website=Voice of America |language=en |archive-date=2024-09-30 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240930072311/https://www.voanews.com/a/regional-somali-forces-destroy-islamic-state-base/3642109.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

Qandala's capture was both strategically and symbolically important for Islamic State. It provided them with a port for potential supply lines from Yemen and was a symbol of resistance due to historical significance in Somali culture.<ref name=":3" />

The conflict exacerbated humanitarian issues, with schools closed, and the local population facing increased hardships amidst an ongoing drought. Humanitarian aid was provided to some of the displaced in nearby areas that included Buruc village.<ref name="reliefweb20161206" />

=== Puntland police arrested Islamic State members ===
On 26 June 2023, the [[Puntland Police Force]] arrested nine foreign individuals from [[Ethiopia]], [[Sudan]] and [[Syria]] in Amaamo, [[Balidhidhin District|Balidhidin]] of the [[Bari, Somalia|Bari region]] and released their names.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-06-27 |title=Top ISIS Commander Among Suspects Apprehended in Puntland Anti-Terror Operations |url=https://thesomalidigest.com/top-isis-commander-among-suspects-apprehended-in-puntland-anti-terror-operations/ |access-date=2025-01-02 |website=The Somali Digest |archive-date=2025-01-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250102184412/https://thesomalidigest.com/top-isis-commander-among-suspects-apprehended-in-puntland-anti-terror-operations/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Recent Clashes ==

=== Dharjaale battle ===
On the early morning of the 31st of December 2024, 12 [[Inghimasi|InghimƒÅsƒ´yy≈´n]] from the [[Islamic State - Somalia Province|Islamic State]] attacked an amassing [[Puntland Dervish Force|Dervish force]] prepped for the Hilaac campaign. The assault began with a [[SVBIED|suicide vehicle-borne improvised explosive device (SVBIED)]] targeting the supply camp, causing both casualties and material damage. This was shortly followed by another [[SVBIED]], which included two [[suicide bombers]], targeting the main camp. After the initial explosions, the remaining [[Inghimasi|InghimƒÅsƒ´yy≈´n]] began engaging [[Puntland Dervish Force|Puntland forces]] for several hours until they were eliminated.<ref>{{Cite web |last=W√≥jcik |first=Pawe≈Ç |date=December 2024 |title=Initial reports of the attack in Dharjaale |url=https://x.com/SaladinAlDronni/status/1874000874613444961 |website=X}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |first= |date=Jan 2025 |title=Islamic State claims responsibility for attack on Somalia's Puntland military base |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/africa/islamic-state-claims-responsibility-attack-somalias-puntland-military-base-2025-01-01/ |website=Reuters}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Kaaman |first=Hugo |date=Jan 2025 |title=IS-Somalia carried out its most audacious attack ever yesterday |url=https://x.com/HKaaman/status/1874494841746591861}}</ref> [[Puntland Dervish Force|Puntland forces]] said they had killed 8 fighters and had "foiled" the attack.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-06-30 |title=Foreign Fighters Killed in Foiled ISIS Attack in Somalia's Puntland |url=https://www.garoweonline.com/en/news/somalia/foreign-fighters-killed-in-foiled-isis-attack-in-somalia-s-puntland |access-date=2025-01-02 |website=Garowe Online |language=en |archive-date=2025-01-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250102025306/https://www.garoweonline.com/en/news/somalia/foreign-fighters-killed-in-foiled-isis-attack-in-somalia-s-puntland |url-status=live }}</ref> Following the attack, multiple videos and images were released by reporters showing a collapsed building resulting from the SVBIED blasts, several burned-out vehicles, and the bodies of the [[Inghimasi|Islamic State fighters]]. [[Amaq News Agency|Amaq]] later released a report stating to have killed 20+ [[Puntland Dervish Force|Puntland forces]] and wounded over 10, and inflicted significant material damage, which aligned with what had been reported.<ref>{{Cite web |date=Jan 2025 |title=Amaq News report on the attack that occurred on the 31st of December 2024. |url=https://x.com/SaladinAlDronni/status/1874493184522490210 |archive-url=http://web.archive.org/web/20250119130208/https://x.com/SaladinAlDronni/status/1874493184522490210 |archive-date=2025-01-19 |access-date=2025-02-07 |website=X (formerly Twitter)}}</ref> Puntland's claim of foiling the attack was questioned by some security analysts, as the role of an [[Inghimasi|InghimƒÅsƒ´y]] is to inflict as much damage as possible, expecting to be killed. Given that the [[Islamic State ‚Äì Somalia Province|Islamic State]] successfully achieved this objective, it could be seen that the Dharjaale assault was a victory for t[[Islamic State|he]] [[Islamic State]]. This attack was the first of its kind in terms of scale and sophistication for the [[Islamic State ‚Äì Somalia Province|Islamic State in Somalia]], occurring just after [[Puntland]] announced a major offensive against both the [[Islamic State ‚Äì Somalia Province|Islamic State]] and [[Al-Shabaab (militant group)|al-Shabaab]]. [[Said Abdullahi Deni|President Said Abdullahi Deni]] has been actively involved, meeting with security officials in [[Bosaso]] to prepare for this campaign. The offensive is seen as part of a broader strategy to eliminate the [[Islamic State ‚Äì Somalia Province|Islamic State]] presence in the [[Outline of Puntland|region]], with some implications for [[Islamic State]] operations in Western & Central Africa.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Hummel |first=Kristina |date=2024-09-11 |title=Islamic State-Somalia: A Growing Global Terror Concern |url=https://ctc.westpoint.edu/islamic-state-somalia-a-growing-global-terror-concern/ |access-date=2025-02-07 |website=Combating Terrorism Center at West Point |language=en-US}}</ref>

After the Islamic State attack, high-ranking officials, including members of the Task Force, [[Puntland Parliament|Puntland lawmakers]], the Director of the [[Puntland Maritime Police Force]], and presidential aides, were at the airport to manage the evacuation of the injured soldiers. The soldiers were flown out of [[Bosaso Airport]] to [[Ethiopia]] for medical care due to the complication of injuries resulting from the explosions. In addition to this, some of the wounded soldiers were given $30 in compensation.<ref>{{Cite web |date=Jan 2025 |title=Soldiers wounded in the ISIS attack receive $30 from Puntland. |url=https://x.com/VancitySam/status/1875134667550281941 |url-status= |website=X}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2025-01-03 |title=Puntland Evacuates Injured Soldiers to Ethiopia After ISIS Attack |url=https://hornobserver.com/articles/3109/Puntland-Evacuates-Injured-Soldiers-to-Ethiopia-After-ISIS-Attack |access-date=2025-01-05 |website=hornobserver.com |language=English |archive-date=2025-01-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250105175858/https://hornobserver.com/articles/3109/Puntland-Evacuates-Injured-Soldiers-to-Ethiopia-After-ISIS-Attack |url-status=live }}</ref>

=== Islamic State mining gold in the Cal Miskaad mountains ===
Puntland officials said they are investigating how Islamic State uses gold mining in Cal-Miskaad of the Bari region to fund its activities. The group is mining gold and other resources in the mountains and taking advantage of the area's rich mineral deposits. Islamic State hires local workers for the mining operations.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=2025-01-05 |title=ISIS Believed to Engage in Gold Mining in Puntland to Fund Its Activities |url=https://hornobserver.com/articles/3113/ISIS-Believed-to-Engage-in-Gold-Mining-in-Puntland-to-Fund-Its-Activities |access-date=2025-01-05 |website=hornobserver.com |language=English |archive-date=2025-01-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250105020336/https://hornobserver.com/articles/3113/ISIS-Believed-to-Engage-in-Gold-Mining-in-Puntland-to-Fund-Its-Activities |url-status=live }}</ref> At the same time, people living in areas controlled by Islamic State are forced to pay money to avoid interference with their lives and work.<ref name=":0" />
